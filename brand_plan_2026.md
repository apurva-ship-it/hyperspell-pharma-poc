# Nexorel (nexoratinib) — Annual Brand Plan 2026
**Therapeutic Area:** Oncology — Non-Small Cell Lung Cancer (NSCLC)
**Indication:** 1st-line treatment of metastatic NSCLC with EGFR exon 19 deletions or exon 21 L858R mutations
**Manufacturer:** Aether Oncology, Inc.
**Brand Lead:** Sarah Kimura, VP Brand Strategy
**Approval Date:** March 2024 (FDA), June 2024 (EMA)

---

## Executive Summary

Nexorel (nexoratinib) is a 3rd-generation, CNS-penetrant EGFR tyrosine kinase inhibitor (TKI) approved for first-line treatment of metastatic NSCLC harboring classical EGFR mutations. Its differentiated profile — superior CNS efficacy, once-daily oral dosing, and a favorable tolerability versus osimertinib — positions it to capture 18–22% of the addressable 1L EGFR+ NSCLC market by end of 2026.

**2026 Revenue Target:** $1.2B (US) | $480M (EU5)
**Patient Target:** ~14,500 new patients in the US; ~9,200 in EU5
**Key Markets:** US, Germany, France, UK, Japan

## Situation Analysis

### Disease Landscape
- NSCLC accounts for ~85% of all lung cancer cases; approximately 238,000 new cases/year in the US
- EGFR mutations present in ~15% of NSCLC patients in the US (50–60% in Asian populations)
- Classical EGFR mutations (exon 19 del + L858R) represent ~85% of all EGFR mutations
- CNS metastases develop in 25–40% of EGFR+ NSCLC patients — a critical unmet need
- Median OS in 1L EGFR+ NSCLC: ~38 months with current standard of care (osimertinib)

### SWOT Analysis

**Strengths**
- Superior intracranial PFS vs. osimertinib (NEXUS-1 trial: mPFS 21.4 vs. 14.1 months in CNS-M+ patients)
- Better rash/dermatologic tolerability (Grade 3+ rash: 3% vs. 11% for osimertinib)
- Once-daily oral, 100mg tablet — strong patient convenience
- Robust real-world evidence program underway (NEXUS-RWE)

**Weaknesses**
- No adjuvant indication (unlike osimertinib/ADAURA) — limits earlier-stage opportunity
- Shorter market history vs. Tagrisso (10+ years of data)
- Lower brand awareness among community oncologists

**Opportunities**
- CNS metastases sub-segment is poorly served — 35% of EGFR+ patients at diagnosis
- Expanding biomarker testing rates (NGS adoption increasing 12% YoY)
- New combination data with anti-VEGF agents expected Q3 2026

**Threats**
- Tagrisso LOE not until 2030 — strong branded competition continues
- Payer access challenges: step edits requiring osimertinib failure in some plans
- Potential new entrants: aumolertinib (China approval, US filing 2026)

## Strategic Imperatives

### SI-1: Establish Nexorel as the Standard of Care for CNS-Active EGFR+ NSCLC
- Own the CNS narrative with "Think Brain First" medical education campaign
- Drive NGS + brain MRI at diagnosis as a standard workup protocol
- Publish NEXUS-1 CNS sub-group data in top-tier journals (NEJM, Lancet Oncology)

### SI-2: Accelerate Diagnosis-to-Treatment Velocity
- Partner with diagnostic companies (Foundation Medicine, Guardant Health) for biomarker co-promotion
- Shorten time from EGFR result to prescription to <7 days (current median: 18 days)
- Deploy Navigator program: dedicated patient case managers at top 200 oncology centers

### SI-3: Defend and Expand Market Access
- Achieve preferred formulary status on 70% of commercial plans by Q2 2026
- Remove step edits on remaining 30% of plans via NEXUS-1 CNS data package
- Patient Assistance Program (PAP): $0 co-pay for patients earning <400% FPL

### SI-4: Build Real-World Evidence Moat
- NEXUS-RWE: 2,000-patient real-world registry targeting publication Q1 2027
- Outcomes-based contracts with 3 major PBMs contingent on real-world OS data

## Target Customer Segments

### Primary: Thoracic Oncologists (Tier 1 — ~1,800 physicians)
High-volume NSCLC treaters at academic and large community centers
- Message priority: CNS PFS data, tolerability profile, patient selection
- Channel: Medical rep (F2F), peer-to-peer programs, congress presence

### Secondary: Medical Oncologists (Tier 2 — ~6,400 physicians)
General oncologists treating lung cancer in community settings
- Message priority: Simplified dosing, patient support program, biomarker testing support
- Channel: Inside sales, digital, speaker programs

### Tertiary: Pulmonologists & Interventional Radiologists
Involved in initial diagnosis and biopsy — biomarker testing advocacy focus
- Message priority: NGS testing importance, early referral to oncology
- Channel: Digital, targeted congress presence (CHEST, ATS)
