## 3. Patient Journey — Rheumatoid Arthritis (MTX-Inadequate Responder)

### Stage 1: Disease Progression Recognition
- **Patient Experience**: Increasing joint pain and stiffness despite MTX; functional decline (HAQ-DI worsening); frustration
- **Barriers**: Long waiting times for rheumatology (4–6 months in some US regions); normalization of pain ("it's just arthritis")
- **Opportunities**: Treat-to-target education; DAS28/SDAI patient calculator tools
- **HCP Touchpoint**: Rheumatologist recognizing inadequate MTX response; DAS28 measurement

### Stage 2: Biologic Initiation Decision
- **Patient Experience**: Fear of injection; concerns about infection risk; anxiety about insurance approval
- **Barriers**: Self-injection anxiety (~35% of patients cite as major barrier); insurance PA process (avg 7.4 days for HUMIRA)
- **Opportunities**: myAbbVie Assist injection training nurse; pen device demonstration; PA support
- **HCP Touchpoint**: Rheumatologist counseling on biologic selection; specialty pharmacy referral

### Stage 3: Onboarding and First Injection
- **Patient Experience**: Anxiety about first injection; concern about injection site reactions; learning curve
- **Barriers**: Injection site burning/stinging (citrate formulation historical issue — addressed by citrate-free); needle-related anxiety
- **Opportunities**: myAbbVie Assist CitysMD/nurse home visit program; injection training video via app; pen vs. syringe preference counseling
- **HCP Touchpoint**: Infusion nurse educator; rheumatology nurse practitioner

### Stage 4: Weeks 4–12 Response Assessment
- **Patient Experience**: Hopeful waiting; monitoring for response; possible honeymoon period followed by adjustment
- **Barriers**: Unrealistic response expectations (patients expect rapid complete remission); early discontinuation if no dramatic improvement
- **Opportunities**: Expectation management at initiation; SDAI/DAS28 monitoring tools
- **HCP Touchpoint**: 4-week and 12-week rheumatology follow-up visits

### Stage 5: Long-Term Maintenance (6+ months)
- **Patient Experience**: Fatigue with EOW injections; injection site fatigue (same sites); risk of immunogenicity over time
- **Barriers**: Injection schedule adherence; secondary failure (loss of response due to AAAs); depression comorbidity
- **Opportunities**: Reminder app; refill coordination by myAbbVie Assist; therapeutic drug monitoring (adalimumab levels)
- **HCP Touchpoint**: Annual DAS28; labs (CBC, LFTs, TB monitoring); X-rays/MRI for structural damage assessment

## 4. Indication-Specific Patient Journeys

### Hidradenitis Suppurativa (HS)
**Average Diagnosis Delay**: 7–10 years
**Patient Journey Highlights**:
- Patients typically see dermatologist, surgeon, and/or GP before HS diagnosis
- Significant psychosocial burden: depression in ~40% of HS patients; social withdrawal
- Prior treatment: Antibiotics (doxycycline, clindamycin), incision/drainage, surgical excision common before biologic consideration
- HUMIRA is the ONLY FDA-approved biologic for HS — critical positioning
- Barriers: Insurance companies requiring prior documentation of failed antibiotics; stigma about discussing disease (groin, axillae involvement)

### Crohn's Disease / Ulcerative Colitis
**Patient Journey Highlights**:
- IBD patients often diagnosed in late teens/twenties — chronic lifelong disease management
- Patient priority: Steroid-free remission; avoiding surgery; bowel control; quality of life
- HUMIRA's role: Step 2 after 5-ASA failure (UC) or immunosuppressant failure (CD/UC)
- Challenge: Lower remission rates vs. vedolizumab and ustekinumab — frank discussion needed
- Patient concern: Black box infection risk; long-term malignancy concern — especially in IBD patients already at higher CRC risk

## 6. Payer and Patient Barriers

### Payer Barriers
| Barrier | Plan Type | Severity | Response |
|---------|----------|---------|---------|
| Biosimilar mandatory substitution policy | PBM / MCO | HIGH | HUMIRA rebate structures; value-based contracts |
| PA requiring failed NSAID (AS) or DMARDs (RA) | Commercial | HIGH | PA support services; NCCL/ACR guideline letters |
| Step therapy: HUMIRA required before Skyrizi/Rinvoq | Many commercial plans | MEDIUM | Supports HUMIRA trial; once stable, may continue |
| Quantity limits (30-day supply restrictions) | Medicaid | MEDIUM | Specialty pharmacy coordination |
| Interchangeable biosimilar automatic substitution | Retail pharmacy | HIGH | Branded prescription request education for patients |

### Patient Barriers
- **Financial**: HUMIRA WAC ~$73,000/year; biosimilar WAC potentially 50–80% lower but net pricing comparable after rebates for insured patients; myAbbVie Assist addresses commercial patient OOP
- **Injection Anxiety**: ~35% of new biologic patients cite injection fear as barrier; pen device and nurse training program critical
- **Black Box Fear**: Malignancy and infection warnings create patient hesitancy — particularly cancer survivors, immunocompromised patients
- **Biosimilar Confusion**: "Is the generic version safe?" — patients may receive biosimilar without awareness; nocebo concern (30% of patients who know they switched report worsened symptoms regardless of medication change)
