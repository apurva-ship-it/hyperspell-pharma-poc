## 1. Core Brand Messages for Global Adaptation

The following messages represent the globally aligned core of the Entresto brand. Each market adapts these for local regulatory, language, and cultural requirements. The underlying clinical claims remain consistent — they derive from the same published trial data across all markets.

### Core Message 1 (Global)
**English master:**
> "Entresto is the only ARNI proven to reduce cardiovascular death by 20% vs. an ACE inhibitor in patients with HFrEF."

**Data anchor:** PARADIGM-HF; HR 0.80, p<0.0001; N=8,442

**Adaptation notes:**
- EU: Comparison language vs. enalapril must be explicit; use of "ACE inhibitor" as class claim requires local MLR review in some markets (DE, FR)
- Japan: "Cardiovascular death" wording maps directly; HR and p-value format accepted by PMDA-regulated materials
- Emerging markets: Simplify for primary care audiences; lead with "most proven HF medicine" rather than technical HR language in some markets (IN, BR, MX)

### Core Message 2 (Global)
**English master:**
> "Entresto reduced heart failure hospitalizations by 21% and all-cause mortality by 16% vs. enalapril in PARADIGM-HF."

**Adaptation notes:**
- EU: Use "hospitalisation" (British spelling) in UK materials; French, German, Spanish translations pre-approved
- Hospitalization claim resonates strongly in markets with DRG or bundled payment systems (UK, Germany, France, Australia)
- Japan: "Cardiac events" wording may need adaptation; mortality data is highly persuasive to Japanese cardiologists

### Core Message 3 (Global)
**English master:**
> "Entresto's dual mechanism — inhibiting neprilysin while blocking the AT1 receptor — delivers comprehensive neurohormonal protection unavailable from ACE inhibitors or ARBs alone."

**Adaptation notes:**
- Scientific message resonates well with academic-oriented European and Japanese specialists
- Primary care audiences in EU and EM benefit from simplified "two ways to protect the heart" framing
- In Japanese: "dual neurohormonal modulation" (二重神経体液性抑制) is the preferred MOA descriptor per PMDA-aligned materials

### Core Message 4 (Global — Guideline)
**English master:**
> "Major international HF guidelines — including ESC, ACC/AHA, CCS, and JHFS — recommend Entresto as preferred neurohormonal therapy in HFrEF."

**Adaptation notes:**
- Guideline citations must be market-specific; ACC/AHA is US-primary; ESC 2021 HF guidelines (McDonagh et al.) for EU
- Japan: JHFS 2017 guidelines (updated 2021) recommend ARNI; use PMDA-approved indication language
- Canada: CCS 2021 HF guidelines; Class I recommendation for ARNI
- Australia: NHFA/CSANZ 2018 guidelines; update citations with 2023 revision
- Brazil/Latin America: SBC (Brazilian Society of Cardiology) 2021 HF guidelines include Entresto

## 2. Regional Notes and Adaptation Requirements

### 2.1 United States

**Regulatory environment:** FDA; 21 CFR Part 202 (prescription drug advertising)
**Current indication:** Broadened 2021 label (HF with LVEF below normal; most clearly in LVEF below normal; not established in LVEF ≥60%)
**Key adaptation requirements:**
- All promotional materials must carry full ISI or brief summary
- Boxed Warning (Fetal Toxicity) must appear verbatim and prominently
- PARADIGM-HF claims use "vs. enalapril" — must not imply superiority over other drugs not studied
- PARAGON-HF messaging limited to labeled indication (not HFpEF/LVEF ≥60%)
- Fair balance requirement: hypotension (14%), angioedema (0.45%), fetal toxicity must be balanced against efficacy

**Cultural/communication adaptation:**
- US HCPs respond to NNT framing ("1 in 21 patients prevented a CV death or hospitalization")
- Visual aids: Clean, data-dense style with Kaplan-Meier curves; scientific credibility is primary driver
- Patient materials: Plain language (6th grade reading level); English and Spanish versions mandatory

### 2.2 European Union

**Regulatory environment:** EMA; country-specific MLR processes; EFPIA code
**Current EU indication:** Symptomatic chronic HF with reduced ejection fraction (LVEF ≤40%); EU label does not include 2021 US broadened label language
**EU EMA label indication (key difference from US):**
> "Adults with symptomatic chronic heart failure with reduced ejection fraction"
- EU label is more restricted than US 2021 label — LVEF ≥40% messaging NOT permitted in EU promotional materials
- Separate EU sNDA filing for HFmrEF/HFpEF indication ongoing; expected 2025

**Country-specific notes:**

| Country | Key Considerations |
|---------|------------------|
| Germany | AMNOG HTA assessment; benefit-tiering by Gemeinsamer Bundesausschuss (G-BA); Entresto has "proof of additional benefit" (Anhaltspunkt) in HFrEF vs. NYHA III/IV; reimbursement agreed via Schiedsstelle |
| France | HAS (Haute Autorité de Santé) assessment; ASMR (Amélioration du Service Médical Rendu) rating; ASMR IV (moderate benefit) established; reimbursement at 65% |
| UK | NICE Technology Appraisal TA388 (2016) — recommended for HFrEF (LVEF ≤35%); NHS restricted vs. US label; NICE review TA388 update expected 2024 |
| Spain | AEMPS; National formulary with autonomía-level variation; Catalonya has broadest access |
| Italy | AIFA — on Class A (fully reimbursed) for HFrEF per PARADIGM-HF indication |
| Netherlands | Zorginstituut Nederland; positive reimbursement; cost-effectiveness accepted |

**EU MLR requirements:**
- Use SmPC (Summary of Product Characteristics) rather than US PI as reference
- ABPI (UK), FSA (France), and country-specific advertising codes apply
- Comparative claims vs. ACE-I permitted where PARADIGM-HF supports; vs. ARB permitted where PARAGON-HF arms are noted

### 2.3 Japan

**Regulatory environment:** PMDA; MHW; JPMA code
**Approval date in Japan:** 2020 (Novartis)
**Japanese indication:** Heart failure with reduced ejection fraction (LVEF ≤40%); standard of care includes ARNI in JHFS 2021 guidelines
**Key adaptation requirements:**
- All claims must align with Japanese prescribing information (Pharmaceutical Interview Form)
- PARADIGM-HF data is the primary evidence accepted by PMDA for promotional claims
- Japanese HCPs respond to morbidity/mortality data; quality of life messaging less prominent than in Western markets
- NT-proBNP monitoring preferred (same as US/EU — scientific rationale required)
- Pediatric indication filed separately; PMDA review ongoing

**Cultural adaptation notes:**
- Formal communication style; KOL academic presentations highly influential
- Nemawashi (consensus-building) approach for hospital formulary adoption
- Japanese prescribing style: conservative titration; low-dose starting approach resonates strongly with TITRATION study data
- Seniority hierarchy: Engage department heads (科長) and academic KOLs before prescribers

### 2.4 Emerging Markets (Brazil, India, China, Mexico, Turkey)

**General principles for EM adaptation:**
- Local regulatory approval required before any promotion; timelines vary (6 months–3 years post-US approval)
- Cost-sensitivity is paramount; access programs and tiered pricing essential
- Specialist-driven markets: cardiologists are primary targets; PCP engagement secondary
- Digital channels dominant in India, China; in-person rep-led in Brazil, Mexico, Turkey

| Country | Regulatory Body | Approval Status | Key Access Consideration |
|---------|----------------|----------------|-------------------------|
| Brazil | ANVISA | Approved | SUS reimbursement complex; private market focus |
| India | CDSCO/DCGI | Approved | Price caps under DPCO; generic ARNI competition emerging |
| China | NMPA | Approved 2017 | NRDL (National Reimbursement Drug List) 2022 inclusion; broad access |
| Mexico | COFEPRIS | Approved | IMSS/ISSSTE formulary access limited; private market dominant |
| Turkey | TITCK | Approved | SGK reimbursement with LVEF ≤35% restriction |

**China-specific note:** Entresto is on China's NRDL as of 2022 negotiations — significant price concession (estimated 60% reduction from list price) in exchange for national reimbursement. Volume-driven strategy appropriate.

## 3. Global Terminology Glossary

### 3.1 Core Scientific Terminology

| English Term | Definition | Notes for Adaptation |
|-------------|-----------|---------------------|
| ARNI | Angiotensin Receptor–Neprilysin Inhibitor | Acronym accepted globally; define on first use in all materials |
| HFrEF | Heart Failure with Reduced Ejection Fraction | Use "réduite" in French; "reduzierter" in German |
| HFmrEF | HF with Mildly Reduced Ejection Fraction | "Mid-range" terminology varies by guideline era; use market-relevant term |
| HFpEF | HF with Preserved Ejection Fraction | Distinguish from HFmrEF carefully per market label |
| LVEF | Left Ventricular Ejection Fraction | Universal; fraction ejection ventriculaire gauche (FR) |
| NT-proBNP | N-Terminal Pro-B-Type Natriuretic Peptide | Note: rises on Entresto; use for monitoring (not BNP) |
| NNT | Number Needed to Treat | Requires denominator context; "21 patients treated for 27 months" |
| HR | Hazard Ratio | Define for non-statistical audiences |
| CV death | Cardiovascular death | Not synonymous with "cardiac death" — include vascular deaths |
| NYHA Class | New York Heart Association Classification | Universal; NYHA 機能分類 in Japan |
| GDMT | Guideline-Directed Medical Therapy | US term; "optimal medical therapy" more common in EU |

## 4. Localization Standards and Quality Control

### 4.1 Translation Requirements
- Medical translations must be performed by certified medical translators with cardiology background
- Back-translation required for Japanese, Chinese, Korean, and Arabic materials
- Independent MLR review in each country before use
- Novartis Global Brand Center review required for visual aid design changes
- Local medical representative review before finalization

### 4.2 Cultural Adaptation Principles
- Do not translate idiomatic claims literally; adapt to culturally appropriate equivalents
- Mortality claims: Acceptable in US/EU; more sensitively framed in Japan ("extends life" preferred over explicit death statistics in patient-facing materials)
- NNT communication: In EM markets, convert to absolute terms ("in a study of 100 patients, 5 more survived 2 years on Entresto vs. ACE inhibitor")
- Visual aid imagery: Use culturally representative patient photography; avoid all-White patient imagery in materials for non-Western markets
