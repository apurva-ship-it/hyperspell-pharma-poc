## 2. Competitor Profile Deep Dives

### Opdivo (nivolumab) — Primary Threat

**Strategic Positioning**: BMS positions Opdivo as the "foundational immunotherapy" with depth of data in RCC, HCC, and esophageal cancers. Opdivo+Yervoy dual checkpoint combo is differentiated in MSS/TMB-H tumors.

**Key Strengths**:
- CheckMate-9LA and -227: Opdivo+Yervoy combination in 1L NSCLC; OS benefit regardless of PD-L1 and TMB
- CheckMate-577: Adjuvant esophageal cancer post-chemoradiation — strong position that KEYTRUDA lacks FDA approval for
- CheckMate-9ER: Opdivo+Cabometyx in 1L RCC, ORR 55.7% vs. sunitinib 27.1%
- Subcutaneous nivolumab: FDA approval filed 2025; SC challenge to QLEX differentiation
- Biosimilar strategy: BMS exploring Opdivo Rybrevant combo for EGFR-mutant NSCLC (Opdivo-AMG232)

**Key Weaknesses**:
- No tumor-agnostic approval (KEYTRUDA advantage)
- Lower PD-L1 testing requirement for most indications creates lack of precision
- Opdivo+Yervoy combination toxicity profile is more severe than KEYTRUDA monotherapy
- IP: Nivolumab compound patent expired in some jurisdictions; biosimilar horizon 2027–2028
- Revenue declining in NSCLC vs. KEYTRUDA penetration

**Battle Card — KEYTRUDA vs. Opdivo**:
| Dimension | KEYTRUDA | Opdivo |
|-----------|---------|--------|
| 1L NSCLC (PD-L1 ≥50%) | KEYNOTE-024: mOS 26.3m | CheckMate-026: No superiority to chemo |
| Tumor-agnostic | Yes (MSI-H/dMMR, TMB-H) | No FDA tumor-agnostic approval |
| Indications | 40+ | ~27 |
| SC formulation | Approved (QLEX, Sep 2025) | Filed 2025, pending FDA action |
| Pediatric approvals | ≥6 months (select) | ≥6 months (select) |

### Opdualag (nivolumab + relatlimab) — Melanoma-Specific Threat

**Approval**: March 2022; unresectable/metastatic melanoma

**Key Data (RELATIVITY-047)**:
| Endpoint | Opdualag | Opdivo Monotherapy | HR |
|----------|---------|-------------------|-----|
| Median PFS | 10.1 months | 4.6 months | HR 0.75 (95% CI 0.62–0.92) |
| ORR | 43% | 33% | — |
| LAG-3 high subgroup PFS | 12.6 vs 4.2 months | — | HR 0.66 |

**KEYTRUDA Competitive Response**: KEYNOTE-716 adjuvant melanoma data; superiority in higher stage patients. KEYTRUDA combination with favezelimab (anti-LAG-3) in clinical development.

### Libtayo (cemiplimab) — PD-L1-High NSCLC Threat

**Key Data (EMPOWER-Lung 1)**:
| Endpoint | Cemiplimab | Chemotherapy | HR |
|----------|-----------|--------------|-----|
| Median OS (PD-L1 ≥50%) | 26.1 months | 13.3 months | HR 0.57 |
| Median PFS | 8.2 months | 5.7 months | HR 0.54 |

**KEYTRUDA Competitive Response**:
- KEYNOTE-024 comparable OS data (26.3 vs 13.4 months) with longer follow-up
- KEYTRUDA breadth across 40+ indications vs. Libtayo's limited portfolio
- Libtayo lacks TNBC, urothelial, GI portfolio — KEYTRUDA wins on breadth
- Pricing: Libtayo WAC slightly lower; however KEYTRUDA's rebate structures maintain competitiveness

---

### Imfinzi (durvalumab) — Stage III NSCLC and SCLC

**Key Data**:
- PACIFIC trial: Unresectable Stage III NSCLC after CRT — landmark; 5-year OS 42.9% vs 33.4% (HR 0.72)
- CASPIAN trial: 1L extensive-stage SCLC + platinum/etoposide — OS 12.9 vs 10.5 months (HR 0.75)
- TOPAZ-1: 1L BTC + gem/cis — OS 12.8 vs 11.5 months; KEYTRUDA KEYNOTE-966 BTC comparison similar

**KEYTRUDA Gap**: KEYTRUDA does NOT have a consolidation indication in unresectable Stage III NSCLC post-CRT (Imfinzi's exclusive territory). This is a meaningful competitive disadvantage in this specific setting.

**KEYTRUDA Strategy**: KN-867 trial: pembro as consolidation in Stage III NSCLC after CRT — ongoing; results pending.

### Long-Term Structural Threats (2028+)
- **Pembrolizumab Biosimilars**: Up to 12 applicants with FDA biosimilar applications; price erosion modeled at 30–70% post-exclusivity. Core IV formulation exposed; QLEX SC formulation protected to ~2041.
- **CAR-T Expansion into Solid Tumors**: ADP-A2AFP (AFP CAR-T, Adaptimmune), IL-13Rα2 targeting GBM — potential to displace ICI in specific solid tumor niches
- **Bispecific Antibodies**: KN-046 (anti-PD-L1/CTLA-4), cadonilimab — may capture market in PD-L1 low tumors
- **Oral PD-1 Inhibitors**: Early-stage molecules from Incyte, AstraZeneca — SC would compete vs. potential oral convenience

## 6. Battle Cards Summary

### KEYTRUDA vs. Opdivo: 1L NSCLC Setting
**If HCP asks: "Why KEYTRUDA over Opdivo in PD-L1 high NSCLC?"**
- KEYNOTE-024: 5-year OS of 31.9% — historic benchmark vs. chemotherapy; CheckMate-026 failed to demonstrate superiority
- Tumor-agnostic MSI-H/TMB-H: No Opdivo equivalents; relevant when tissue agnostic testing is positive
- Pediatric approvals broader in KEYTRUDA (MSI-H tumors ≥6 months; melanoma ≥12 years)
- QLEX SC currently ahead of Opdivo SC in regulatory timeline

### KEYTRUDA vs. Libtayo: 1L NSCLC PD-L1 ≥50%
**If HCP asks: "Cemiplimab shows similar data in PD-L1 ≥50% NSCLC. Why choose KEYTRUDA?"**
- Longer-term follow-up and survival data available for KEYTRUDA (5+ years vs. 3 years for Libtayo)
- KEYTRUDA has 40+ indications — if patient's disease progresses and needs retreatment in different tumor type, physician familiar with single agent
- KEYTRUDA + chemo combinations for PD-L1 low or <50% patients well-established; Libtayo combination data limited
- Broader payer coverage and formulary positioning for KEYTRUDA

### KEYTRUDA vs. Imfinzi: Stage III NSCLC
**Acknowledged Gap**: KEYTRUDA does not have Stage III NSCLC consolidation approval. Response:
- Redirect to 1L Stage IV data superiority
- Position KN-867 trial as ongoing commitment
- For patients who fail Imfinzi consolidation — KEYTRUDA-based 1L systemic therapy is the clear SOC next step
