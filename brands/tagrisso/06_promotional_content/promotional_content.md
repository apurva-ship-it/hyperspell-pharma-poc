## 1. CORE KEY MESSAGES (MLR-APPROVED)

### KM-1: The First-Line OS Standard
**Message**: "TAGRISSO is the only first-line EGFR TKI with a demonstrated Overall Survival benefit in the FLAURA trial — a median OS of **38.6 months vs 31.8 months** with standard-of-care EGFR TKI (HR 0.80; p=0.046) — redefining expectations for patients with EGFR-mutant metastatic NSCLC."

**Supporting Data**:
- FLAURA (N=556): mOS 38.6 vs 31.8 months; HR 0.80 (95% CI 0.64–0.99); p=0.046
- PFS: 18.9 vs 10.2 months; HR 0.46 (p<0.001)
- No other 1L EGFR TKI has shown a positive OS result in Phase 3 against an active comparator
- NCCN Category 1 recommendation; ASCO/ESMO guidelines: preferred agent 1L

**Approved for**: Medical oncology field force; KOL programs; congress presentations
**Indication**: First-line metastatic NSCLC (EGFR Ex19del/L858R)

---

### KM-2: Protecting What Matters Most — The Brain
**Message**: "TAGRISSO's CNS penetration — with a CSF/plasma ratio of approximately 2.5% — delivers superior CNS protection. In the FLAURA trial, CNS progression-free survival was **not reached** with TAGRISSO vs **11.7 months** with SOC EGFR TKI, providing patients with a meaningful reduction in the risk of brain metastasis progression."

**Supporting Data**:
- FLAURA CNS PFS: Not reached vs 11.7 months (HR 0.48; p<0.001)
- CNS ORR (patients with baseline CNS mets): 91% vs 68%
- AURA3: CNS ORR 70% in T790M+ patients with CNS involvement
- ADAURA: CNS DFS 98% vs 85% at 5 years (adjuvant setting)
- Brain metastases occur in ~30–40% of EGFR-mutant NSCLC patients — clinically meaningful differentiator

**Approved for**: All oncology field force; neuro-oncology-focused programs
**Indication**: First-line and 2L metastatic (CNS context); mention in adjuvant context with ADAURA data

### KM-3: The ADAURA Adjuvant Landmark
**Message**: "In the landmark ADAURA trial, adjuvant TAGRISSO after complete tumor resection demonstrated a **5-year DFS rate of 65.8% vs 26.2%** with placebo (Stage II–IIIA; HR 0.23; p<0.0001) AND a proven **Overall Survival benefit (HR 0.49; 88% vs 78% at 5 years; p=0.0039)** — establishing TAGRISSO as the first targeted agent to demonstrate OS benefit in the adjuvant NSCLC setting."

**Supporting Data**:
- ADAURA Stage II–IIIA 5yr DFS: 65.8% vs 26.2%; HR 0.23 (p<0.0001)
- ADAURA OS: 88% vs 78% at 5yr; HR 0.49 (p=0.0039)
- CNS DFS: 98% vs 85% at 5 years
- Treatment duration: 3 years of 80mg once daily
- NCCN Category 1 recommendation for Stage IB–IIIA adjuvant with EGFR mutation

**Approved for**: Thoracic surgery + medical oncology field force; tumor board programs
**Indication**: Adjuvant post-resection (Stage IB–IIIA, EGFR Ex19del/L858R)

### KM-4: Proven Efficacy Against T790M Resistance
**Message**: "When first- or second-generation EGFR TKI therapy fails and T790M resistance is identified, TAGRISSO delivers a meaningful second chance — with a **PFS of 10.1 months vs 4.4 months** with platinum-pemetrexed (HR 0.30; p<0.001) and a **CNS ORR of 70%** in the AURA3 trial."

**Supporting Data**:
- AURA3 (N=419): PFS 10.1 vs 4.4 months; HR 0.30 (p<0.001)
- ORR: 71% vs 31%
- CNS ORR: 70% vs 31%
- Duration of response: 9.7 vs 4.1 months
- T790M testing: Required before initiation; Guardant360 CDx (liquid) and cobas (tissue) FDA-approved

**Approved for**: Medical oncology field force (2L call messaging)
**Indication**: 2L T790M+ NSCLC after 1st/2nd gen TKI failure

### KM-5: Tolerability That Enables Long-Term Treatment
**Message**: "TAGRISSO's selective targeting of mutant over wild-type EGFR (approximately 200× selectivity) translates to a tolerability profile significantly better than first- and second-generation EGFR TKIs — with Grade ≥3 rash in just **1%** vs **10–12%** with erlotinib, enabling sustained, long-term treatment without the dose interruptions that compromise outcomes."

**Supporting Data**:
- FLAURA: Grade ≥3 rash: 1% (Tagrisso) vs 10% (SoC TKI)
- FLAURA: Grade ≥3 AEs overall: 34% vs 45%
- FLAURA: Treatment discontinuation due to AEs: 5% (Tagrisso) vs 13% (SoC TKI)
- ADAURA: Grade ≥3 AEs: 20% (Tagrisso) vs 10% (placebo) — lower than chemotherapy regimens
- Diarrhea Grade ≥3: 1–2% (Tagrisso) vs 8–10% (erlotinib)

**Approved for**: All oncology field force; patient-centric programs; HCP awareness
**Indication**: All approved indications (context-dependent)

### KM-6: Biomarker Testing — The Gateway to Precision Therapy
**Message**: "TAGRISSO is only accessible to patients with EGFR Ex19del or L858R mutations detected by an FDA-approved companion diagnostic test. Comprehensive molecular testing at diagnosis — tissue NGS or liquid biopsy — is the critical first step to ensuring every eligible patient receives the treatment they deserve."

**Supporting Data**:
- EGFR mutation prevalence: ~15% of NSCLC in Western populations; ~35–50% in East Asian ancestry
- FDA-approved CDx tests: FoundationOne CDx, cobas EGFR v2, FoundationOne Liquid CDx, Guardant360 CDx, Oncomine Dx, therascreen
- NCCN guidelines: Broad molecular profiling (NGS) recommended for all advanced NSCLC at diagnosis
- ctDNA sensitivity for Ex19del/L858R: ~65–80%; tissue testing recommended when ctDNA negative
- Testing gap: ~22% of advanced NSCLC patients globally not receiving molecular testing

**Approved for**: Pathology/laboratory medicine; tumor board discussions; advocacy for testing
**Indication**: Cross-indication; testing enablement message

## 3. OBJECTION HANDLERS (MLR-APPROVED RESPONSES)

### Objection 1: "MARIPOSA shows longer PFS — shouldn't I use the lazertinib combination first-line?"
**Approved Response**:
"MARIPOSA is an important trial — and yes, the combination shows a PFS advantage: 23.7 months vs 16.6 months with Tagrisso (HR 0.70). Here's the full context I'd want you to have. First, overall survival data for MARIPOSA are still immature — no OS benefit has been shown. Meanwhile, Tagrisso has mature OS data from FLAURA: 38.6 months, HR 0.80, p=0.046 — the only 1L EGFR TKI to show an OS benefit. Second, Grade ≥3 toxicity in MARIPOSA was 75% vs 27% with Tagrisso monotherapy. VTE rate was 11% — every patient needs anticoagulation consideration. One in ten patients discontinued the combination. For a patient with a good PS and moderate disease burden, the quality-of-life cost of the combination may outweigh the PFS gain — especially without OS data. Tagrisso remains NCCN Category 1, Preferred, for a reason."

---

### Objection 2: "My patient already had surgery — why would I add 3 years of Tagrisso?"
**Approved Response**:
"That question goes to the heart of what ADAURA tells us. The surgeon did an excellent job — complete resection is critical. But we know that despite R0 resection, EGFR-mutant NSCLC recurs in 40–60% of Stage II–IIIA patients within 5 years without adjuvant therapy. ADAURA showed that 3 years of Tagrisso changes that dramatically: 65.8% DFS at 5 years vs 26.2% with placebo — HR 0.23. And now we have OS: 88% vs 78% alive at 5 years, HR 0.49. This is the first targeted agent to show OS benefit in adjuvant NSCLC. Three years of a once-daily oral pill — with a tolerability profile far better than chemotherapy — gives your patient the best chance at long-term survival. The question is: why wouldn't you?"

---

### Objection 3: "Brain mets are manageable with radiation — I don't need to prioritize CNS protection."
**Approved Response**:
"Whole-brain radiation is effective, but it comes at a cost — cognitive decline, fatigue, quality-of-life impact. And the data show that Tagrisso's CNS penetration may delay or prevent the need for WBRT. In FLAURA, CNS PFS was not reached with Tagrisso vs 11.7 months with SOC TKI. CNS ORR in patients with brain mets at baseline was 91% vs 68%. And in ADAURA, CNS DFS at 5 years was 98% vs 85% in the adjuvant setting. The goal isn't to manage brain mets when they arrive — it's to prevent them from becoming the dominant clinical problem in the first place. Tagrisso helps you do that."

### Objection 5: "I'm not sure I need to order EGFR testing post-surgery — we 'got it all.'"
**Approved Response**:
"I understand that instinct — and it reflects excellent surgical outcomes. But EGFR testing post-resection is now a critical step in the standard of care, because it's the only way to identify which patients will benefit from 3 years of adjuvant Tagrisso. ADAURA showed HR 0.23 for DFS — but only in EGFR-mutant patients. Testing identifies those patients. NCCN guidelines and ASCO recommend EGFR molecular testing for ALL resected NSCLC, Stage IB and above. The good news is that the resection specimen provides excellent tissue quality — it's usually the most reliable EGFR test you'll ever run on that patient. Would it help if I worked with your pathology department to set up a reflex EGFR testing protocol for all resected NSCLC cases?"

---

### Objection 6: "Tagrisso has ILD risk — how do I manage that?"
**Approved Response**:
"ILD with Tagrisso is real but infrequent — it occurred in 3.3% of patients in clinical trials, with fatal events in 0.6%. To put that in context, ILD with erlotinib and gefitinib in Japanese populations has historically run 2–4%, and Tagrisso's rate is similar. The key is vigilance: any new or progressive dyspnea, cough, or fever warrants a CT scan. If ILD is confirmed, withhold Tagrisso immediately; Grade 2+ that doesn't resolve in 3 weeks leads to dose reduction; Grade 3+ means permanent discontinuation. The management algorithm is clearly defined in the PI. For most patients, with appropriate monitoring, ILD is a manageable — if serious — event. I have a one-page monitoring guide if that would be useful."

---

### Objection 7: "My patient progressed on Tagrisso — what now?"
**Approved Response**:
"Progression on Tagrisso is unfortunately inevitable in metastatic disease, and the post-Tagrisso landscape is evolving. The first step is re-biopsy — both tissue and liquid biopsy — to identify the resistance mechanism. Approximately 20% will have MET amplification (savolitinib + osimertinib under investigation), ~15% will have C797S (4th-gen TKIs in early trials). Histologic transformation to SCLC occurs in ~5–10% — and those patients benefit from platinum/etoposide. For those without an identified targetable resistance mechanism, platinum-based chemotherapy remains standard. Amivantamab (Rybrevant) also has an approved indication in EGFR exon 20 insertion tumors — a different mutation type, so check if any atypical mutations are present. I can share the resistance algorithm from the latest NCCN guidelines if helpful."

## 4. IMPORTANT SAFETY INFORMATION (ISI) — ABBREVIATED

**CONTRAINDICATIONS**: None.

**WARNINGS AND PRECAUTIONS**: Interstitial lung disease (ILD)/pneumonitis (3.3%; fatal 0.6%): withhold and permanently discontinue in Grade 3/4. QTc prolongation: monitor ECG; withhold if QTc >500ms. Left ventricular dysfunction: assess LVEF; withhold if significant decrease. Keratitis: refer to ophthalmology if symptoms occur. Embryo-fetal toxicity: advise females of reproductive potential to use effective contraception.

**MOST COMMON ADVERSE REACTIONS (≥20%)**: Diarrhea, rash, paronychia, dry skin, stomatitis, fatigue, decreased appetite.

**DRUG INTERACTIONS**: Avoid strong CYP3A inducers (rifampin etc.); if unavoidable, increase Tagrisso to 160mg. QT-prolonging agents: monitor ECG.

**USE IN SPECIFIC POPULATIONS**: Embryo-fetal toxicity; verify pregnancy status before initiating. Males with female partners of reproductive potential: effective contraception during treatment and 4 months after final dose.
