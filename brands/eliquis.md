# Eliquis (apixaban) — Brand Intelligence

**Generic Name:** Apixaban  
**Brand:** Eliquis  
**Company:** Bristol-Myers Squibb (BMS) + Pfizer (co-development/co-promotion)  
**Drug Class:** Direct oral anticoagulant (DOAC); direct Factor Xa inhibitor  
**Mechanism of Action:** Selectively and reversibly inhibits free and clot-bound Factor Xa in the coagulation cascade, blocking thrombin generation and thrombus formation without requiring antithrombin as a cofactor  
**FDA Approval:** December 2012 (stroke prevention in AF); March 2014 (VTE treatment/prevention)  
**Formulation:** 2.5 mg and 5 mg oral tablets; BID dosing  

---

## Approved Indications (US)

1. **Nonvalvular Atrial Fibrillation (NVAF):** Reduce risk of stroke and systemic embolism
2. **DVT Treatment:** Treatment of deep vein thrombosis (DVT)
3. **PE Treatment:** Treatment of pulmonary embolism (PE)
4. **VTE Prophylaxis (post-surgery):** Prevention of DVT/PE after hip or knee replacement surgery
5. **Recurrent VTE Prevention:** Reduce risk of recurrent DVT and PE after initial treatment

## Clinical Efficacy

### ARISTOTLE Trial (AF stroke prevention — flagship study)
- **Design:** Apixaban 5 mg BID vs warfarin (INR 2–3) in 18,201 NVAF patients
- **Primary endpoint:** Stroke or systemic embolism
- **Results:**
  - Stroke/SE: **1.27%/yr vs 1.60%/yr** (HR 0.79, p<0.001 for non-inferiority; p=0.01 for superiority)
  - **21% relative risk reduction** in stroke/SE vs warfarin
  - **Major bleeding:** 2.13%/yr vs 3.09%/yr (**HR 0.69, 31% reduction**, p<0.001)
  - **ICH:** 0.33%/yr vs 0.80%/yr (**HR 0.42, 58% reduction**, p<0.001)
  - **All-cause mortality:** 3.52%/yr vs 3.94%/yr (HR 0.89, p=0.047)
- **Conclusion:** Apixaban superior to warfarin on stroke prevention, major bleeding, ICH, and mortality

### AMPLIFY Trial (acute VTE treatment)
- **Design:** Apixaban (10 mg BID × 7 days → 5 mg BID × 6 months) vs enoxaparin/warfarin
- **Recurrent VTE or VTE-related death:** 2.3% vs 2.7% (relative risk 0.84, non-inferior)
- **Major bleeding:** **0.6% vs 1.8%** (relative risk 0.31, p<0.001) — 69% bleeding reduction
- Apixaban non-inferior for efficacy and superior for safety; enabled monotherapy without heparin bridge

### AMPLIFY-EXT Trial (extended VTE prevention)
- **Design:** Apixaban 2.5 mg or 5 mg BID vs placebo for 12 months after completing 6–12 months treatment
- **Recurrent VTE/VTE-related death:**
  - 2.5 mg: 3.8% vs 11.6% (placebo) — **67% reduction**
  - 5.0 mg: 4.2% vs 11.6% — **64% reduction**
- Both doses significantly reduced recurrent VTE without significantly increasing major bleeding vs placebo

### ADVANCE-3 Trial (VTE prophylaxis, hip replacement)
- Apixaban 2.5 mg BID vs enoxaparin 40 mg QD
- **Total VTE/VTE-related death:** 1.4% vs 3.9% (relative risk 0.36, p<0.001)
- **Major bleeding:** 0.82% vs 0.86% (similar)

## Safety Profile

### Common Adverse Events
| AE | Frequency |
|---|---|
| Minor/nuisance bleeding (gum, epistaxis, bruising) | 5–10% |
| Nausea | 3–5% |
| Anemia | 1–3% |

### Major Bleeding Profile
- **Annual major bleeding rate (ARISTOTLE):** 2.13% — lower than warfarin (3.09%)
- **ICH rate:** 0.33%/yr — significantly lower than warfarin; clinically important differentiation
- **GI bleeding:** Slight increase vs warfarin in some analyses; lower than dabigatran and rivaroxaban
- **Reversal agent:** Andexxa (andexanet alfa) — FDA approved 2018; reverses anti-Xa activity within minutes

### Black Box Warning
- **Premature discontinuation increases stroke risk:** Premature discontinuation increases risk of thrombotic events; bridge to another anticoagulant if stopping for reasons other than pathological bleeding
- **Epidural/spinal hematoma:** Risk with neuraxial anesthesia or spinal puncture; monitor for neurological impairment

### Drug Interactions
- **Strong dual CYP3A4/P-gp inhibitors** (e.g., ketoconazole): Reduce dose by 50% or avoid
- **Strong dual CYP3A4/P-gp inducers** (e.g., rifampin, carbamazepine): Avoid — significantly reduces apixaban exposure
- **Antiplatelet agents/NSAIDs:** Increased bleeding risk with combination

### Dose Adjustments
- **NVAF dose reduction to 2.5 mg BID:** ≥2 of: age ≥80, weight ≤60 kg, serum creatinine ≥1.5 mg/dL
- **Renal impairment:** No adjustment required for CrCl ≥25 mL/min; limited data CrCl <25
- **Dialysis/ESRD:** Not recommended (not studied)

## Key HCP Messages

1. **Superior to warfarin on three endpoints simultaneously:** ARISTOTLE uniquely demonstrated Eliquis superior to warfarin for stroke prevention, major bleeding, and all-cause mortality in NVAF patients — no other DOAC achieved all three in a single study.

2. **58% lower intracranial hemorrhage vs warfarin:** ICH (0.33% vs 0.80%/yr) is the most feared bleeding complication in anticoagulated patients; the magnitude of ICH reduction with Eliquis provides critical safety reassurance for high-risk patients.

3. **Treatment and extended prevention in one drug:** Eliquis enables acute VTE treatment (10 mg BID × 7 days) followed by long-term management (5 mg BID) and extended prevention (2.5 mg BID) — a single-agent continuum without heparin bridging (AMPLIFY).

4. **Reversal is available when you need it:** Andexxa provides rapid, specific reversal of anti-Xa activity for life-threatening bleeding or urgent surgery — addressing a historical barrier to DOAC adoption.

5. **Simple dose-adjustment algorithm:** Only two criteria-driven dose adjustment (2.5 mg BID for ≥2 of: age ≥80, weight ≤60 kg, creatinine ≥1.5 mg/dL) reduces prescribing complexity compared to renal-based dosing of competitors.

## Patient Profile

### Ideal AF Patient
- NVAF with CHA₂DS₂-VASc score ≥2 (men) or ≥3 (women)
- Warfarin candidate who desires simplicity (no INR monitoring)
- High bleeding risk concern (ICH history, elderly — Eliquis lowest ICH of DOACs)
- Labile INR history on warfarin (TTR <65%)

### Ideal VTE Patient
- Acute DVT/PE requiring anticoagulation
- Prefers oral-only regimen without initial heparin injection (AMPLIFY monotherapy)
- Extended prevention needed (cancer patients: consider LMWH or edoxaban; Eliquis for non-cancer extended VTE)

### Key Exclusions
- Mechanical heart valves (warfarin required)
- Moderate-to-severe mitral stenosis (warfarin required)
- ESRD on dialysis (limited data; consider warfarin)
- Active significant bleeding

## Market Position

### Sales Performance
- **2023 Global Revenue:** ~$12 billion USD (BMS share ~$6B; Pfizer share ~$6B)
- **2022 Revenue:** ~$11.8 billion USD
- **Growth:** Slowing as patent cliffs approach (US patent expiry 2026–2028)
- **Market share (DOAC, US):** ~50% of dispensed DOAC prescriptions

### Payer/Access Dynamics
- Preferred or Tier 2 status on most major US commercial formularies
- Medicare Part D: Strong coverage; price negotiations under IRA (Eliquis selected for IRA drug price negotiation 2026)
- **IRA negotiation impact:** CMS negotiated price ~$280/month vs ~$590 WAC; effective 2026
- **Generic entry:** First generic expected ~2026–2028; significant revenue erosion anticipated

### Key Pressure Points
- IRA drug price negotiation — largest single revenue risk
- Generic/authorized generic entry 2026+
- Oral Factor XIa inhibitors in late-stage trials (milvexian, asundexian) — potential successor class with better bleeding profiles
