## 1. Approved Indications (US FDA)

### 1.1 Ozempic (semaglutide 0.5mg, 1mg, 2mg injection)

**Full Indication Text (US PI, effective 2023):**

> Ozempic (semaglutide) injection is indicated:
> - As an adjunct to diet and exercise to improve glycemic control in adults with type 2 diabetes mellitus.
> - To reduce the risk of major adverse cardiovascular events (cardiovascular death, non-fatal myocardial infarction, and non-fatal stroke) in adults with type 2 diabetes mellitus and established cardiovascular disease.

**Limitations of Use:**
- Ozempic has not been studied in patients with a history of pancreatitis. Consider other antidiabetic therapies in patients with a history of pancreatitis.
- Ozempic is not indicated for use in patients with type 1 diabetes mellitus.
- Ozempic has not been studied in combination with prandial insulin.
- Ozempic is not a substitute for insulin in insulin-requiring patients.

**FDA Approval Date:** December 5, 2017 (initial); 2mg dose approved March 2022

### 1.2 Wegovy (semaglutide 2.4mg injection) — Same Molecule, Distinct Brand

**Full Indication Text:**
> Wegovy (semaglutide) injection is indicated:
> - As an adjunct to a reduced calorie diet and increased physical activity for chronic weight management in adults with an initial body mass index (BMI) of:
>   - 30 kg/m² or greater (obesity), OR
>   - 27 kg/m² or greater (overweight) in the presence of at least one weight-related comorbid condition (e.g., hypertension, type 2 diabetes, or dyslipidemia)
> - To reduce the risk of serious cardiovascular events (cardiovascular death, non-fatal myocardial infarction, or non-fatal stroke) in adults with established cardiovascular disease and either obesity or overweight.

**FDA Approval Date:** June 4, 2021 (weight management); August 8, 2024 (CV indication added via SELECT data)

### 1.3 Rybelsus (semaglutide oral tablets) — 3mg, 7mg, 14mg

**Indication:** Adjunct to diet and exercise to improve glycemic control in adults with T2D
**FDA Approval Date:** September 20, 2019

| Phase | Dose | Duration | Purpose |
|-------|------|----------|---------|

| Dose 1 | 0.5mg QW | ≥4 weeks | Initial therapeutic dose |

| Maximum dose | 2.0mg QW | Maintenance | Maximum approved dose (2022) |

### 2.2 Wegovy Dosing Escalation

| Week | Dose |
|------|------|
| 1–4 | 0.25mg QW |
| 5–8 | 0.5mg QW |
| 9–12 | 1.0mg QW |
| 13–16 | 1.7mg QW |
| ≥17 | 2.4mg QW (maintenance) |

**Missed dose instructions:** If ≤5 days missed — administer as soon as possible. If >5 days — skip and resume on next scheduled day.

### 2.3 Administration
- Subcutaneous injection: abdomen, thigh, or upper arm
- Rotate injection sites within the same region
- Administer any time of day, with or without meals
- FlexTouch pen: single-patient use; 1.5 mL prefilled pen
- Do NOT share pens between patients (even if needle is changed)
- Store: refrigerated 2–8°C; after first use up to 56 days at room temperature ≤30°C

## 3. Contraindications

1. **Personal or family history of medullary thyroid carcinoma (MTC)**
2. **Patients with Multiple Endocrine Neoplasia syndrome type 2 (MEN 2)**
3. **Known hypersensitivity to semaglutide or any excipient** — serious reactions including anaphylaxis and angioedema reported
4. **Pregnancy** — may cause fetal harm; discontinue at least 2 months before planned pregnancy

## 4. Warnings and Precautions

### 4.1 Black Box Warning — Thyroid C-Cell Tumors

> **WARNING: RISK OF THYROID C-CELL TUMORS**
> Semaglutide causes thyroid C-cell tumors at clinically relevant exposures in both genders of rats and mice. It is unknown whether Ozempic/Wegovy causes thyroid C-cell tumors, including medullary thyroid carcinoma (MTC), in humans, as the human relevance of semaglutide-induced rodent thyroid C-cell tumors has not been determined.
> Ozempic/Wegovy is contraindicated in patients with a personal or family history of MTC or in patients with MEN 2. Counsel patients regarding the potential risk of MTC and the symptoms of thyroid tumors (mass in the neck, dysphagia, dyspnea, persistent hoarseness). Routine serum calcitonin monitoring is of uncertain value.

**Clinical Guidance:**
- Rodent data at 8× (rat) and 5× (mouse) clinical exposure
- No confirmed MTC cases in semaglutide clinical trial program (>100,000 patient-years exposure)
- Post-marketing surveillance ongoing through Novo Nordisk pharmacovigilance

### 4.2 Pancreatitis
- Acute pancreatitis observed; cases reported including fatal hemorrhagic/necrotizing pancreatitis
- Discontinue promptly if suspected; do not restart if confirmed
- No evidence of excess pancreatitis in SELECT (HR 0.97, 95% CI 0.68–1.39) or SUSTAIN-6

### 4.3 Diabetic Retinopathy Complications (T2D patients)
- SUSTAIN-6: Increased rate of diabetic retinopathy complications (HR 1.76, 95% CI 1.11–2.78)
- Mechanism: Rapid HbA1c reduction → early worsening (similar phenomenon seen with insulin intensification)
- Monitor patients with pre-existing diabetic retinopathy
- Risk appears concentrated in first year; long-term risk not elevated

### 4.4 Acute Kidney Injury
- GI AEs (nausea, vomiting, diarrhea) → dehydration → AKI reports
- Use with caution in patients with renal impairment
- Adequate hydration counseling required, particularly during dose escalation
- Monitor renal function in patients reporting severe GI reactions

### 4.5 Hypoglycemia
- Low risk as monotherapy (glucose-dependent mechanism)
- Significant risk when combined with insulin secretagogues (sulfonylureas) or basal insulin
- Consider dose reduction of concomitant insulin secretagogue or insulin when initiating
- Patient education on hypoglycemia symptoms required when used with SU or insulin

### 4.6 Hypersensitivity Reactions
- Anaphylaxis and angioedema reported post-marketing
- Discontinue immediately and treat appropriately
- Do not re-administer to patients with known hypersensitivity to semaglutide

### 4.7 Heart Rate Increase
- Mean increases of 3–4 bpm observed; persisted over time
- Clinical significance unknown; monitor patients with known cardiac conditions
- Consider discontinuation if sustained clinically relevant increases observed

### 4.8 Gallbladder and Biliary Disease
- Increased gallbladder disease: cholelithiasis and cholecystitis
- Clinical trial incidence: ~1.9% (Ozempic) vs 1.3% (placebo); ~2.6% (Wegovy) vs 1.4% (placebo)
- GLP-1 class effect related to reduced gallbladder motility

## 6. Drug Interactions

| Interacting Drug/Class | Mechanism | Clinical Management |
|-----------------------|-----------|---------------------|
| Oral medications (all) | Delayed gastric emptying may alter absorption | Monitor particularly for narrow TI drugs |
| Warfarin / VKAs | Delayed absorption → INR variability | Monitor INR more frequently when initiating |
| Sulfonylureas | Pharmacodynamic potentiation | Reduce SU dose; monitor glucose |
| Basal/prandial insulin | Pharmacodynamic potentiation | Reduce insulin dose; monitor glucose |
| Cyclosporine (oral) | Gastric emptying delay reduces Cmax ~50% | Use alternate route if possible |
| Levothyroxine | Delayed absorption | Take on empty stomach, monitor TSH |

**No significant interactions with:** statins, ACE inhibitors, ARBs, beta-blockers, calcium channel blockers, metformin (can be combined freely)

## 7. Special Populations

### 7.1 Renal Impairment
- No dose adjustment required for any degree of renal impairment, including ESRD
- Pharmacokinetics not meaningfully altered by renal status
- FLOW trial enrolled eGFR 25–75: semaglutide well-tolerated and effective
- GI-mediated dehydration risk: counsel patients with CKD to maintain hydration

### 7.2 Hepatic Impairment
- No dose adjustment required for mild, moderate, or severe hepatic impairment
- Semaglutide is not renally or hepatically cleared (proteolytic metabolism)

### 7.3 Pediatric Patients
- Ozempic: Not approved in pediatric patients
- Wegovy: **FDA approved December 2022 for adolescents ≥12 years** with obesity (BMI ≥95th percentile)
- STEP TEENS trial: -16.1% weight loss vs -0.6% placebo at 68 weeks

### 7.4 Geriatric Patients (≥65 years)
- No dose adjustment required
- SUSTAIN-6: Mean age 65; SELECT included patients ≥45; consistent benefit in older subgroups
- Greater attention to GI tolerance, dehydration risk, hypoglycemia co-risk with SU/insulin

### 7.5 Pregnancy and Lactation
- **Pregnancy Category X equivalent (Contraindicated)**
- Animal data: adverse fetal outcomes at sub-clinical exposures; embryofetal toxicity
- Discontinue ≥2 months before planned pregnancy (half-life ~7 days → 10 half-lives = ~70 days washout)
- Lactation: Unknown if excreted in breast milk; potential risk to nursing infant; not recommended
