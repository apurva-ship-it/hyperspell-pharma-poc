## 1. Brand Vision

Eliquis aspires to remain the world's most trusted oral anticoagulant — delivering superior stroke prevention with the best-in-class bleeding safety profile across every patient who requires anticoagulation therapy. As the DOAC market leader with approximately 50% US market share, the Eliquis strategy in 2025–2028 centers on maximizing value through LOE (loss of exclusivity), defending against generic entry, and establishing a lasting clinical and patient-trust legacy that extends beyond patent expiration.

**Long-Term Vision Statement (Internal):**
"To be the anticoagulant that both patients and physicians choose first — not because it's prescribed, but because the evidence for safety and outcomes is undeniable."

## 2. Brand Positioning

### 2.1 Core Positioning Statement
For adults requiring anticoagulation therapy who need both effective clot prevention and manageable bleeding risk, Eliquis is the Factor Xa inhibitor that uniquely combines superior stroke reduction with the lowest major bleeding rate among approved DOACs — because preventing strokes should never mean accepting dangerous bleeds.

## 3. Strategic Imperatives (FY2025–2027)

### Imperative 1: Maximize Revenue Through the LOE Window
Generic apixaban entry is expected 2026–2028 (Teva, Sandoz, others in ANDA pipeline). Every commercial dollar counts. Priority: Volume defense via formulary access, specialty expansion, and persistence programs.

### Imperative 2: Consolidate DOAC Market Leadership Ahead of Generic Competition
Eliquis holds ~50% DOAC market share in the US. The strategy is to cement prescriber habit, patient loyalty, and payer relationships so that when generics arrive, brand Eliquis retains the residual branded segment (price-insensitive patients, complex cases, physician brand loyalty).

### Imperative 3: Expand VTE Utilization — Underpenetrated Indication
Warfarin still holds ~20–25% of NVAF patients, but the DVT/PE treatment indication is significantly underpenetrated vs. NVAF. Internal medicine, emergency medicine, and hospitalist activation for acute VTE management represents a meaningful volume opportunity.

### Imperative 4: Strengthen the Cancer-Associated Thrombosis (CAT) Narrative
Post-ADAM-VTE and CARAVAGGIO trials, apixaban has strong evidence in cancer-associated VTE. Oncology-adjacent prescribers (hematology, oncology NPs) are an emerging and growing segment.

### Imperative 5: Counter Xarelto's Once-Daily Convenience Narrative
Rivaroxaban (Xarelto) continues to drive share with "once daily" messaging. BID dosing is Eliquis's primary perceived disadvantage. Counter-strategy: emphasize bleeding safety superiority, especially ICH — which matters more than dosing frequency for stroke prevention patients.

## 4. SWOT Analysis

### Strengths
- Largest DOAC by market share (~50% US); ~$12B global revenue (2023)
- Strongest safety profile: lowest major bleeding, lowest ICH of any DOAC in AF
- Mortality benefit: HR 0.89 (ARISTOTLE) — no other DOAC showed all-cause mortality reduction vs. warfarin
- Reversal agent available: Andexxa (andexanet alfa) — FDA approved 2018
- Broad indications: NVAF, DVT/PE acute treatment, extended VTE prevention, post-surgical prophylaxis
- Strong brand loyalty among specialists (EP, cardiology, hematology)
- BMS + Pfizer co-promotion = largest co-promotion sales force in DOAC category

### Weaknesses
- Twice-daily dosing vs. Xarelto once-daily — adherence perception disadvantage
- IRA drug price negotiation: Eliquis selected for 2026 negotiated price — price ceiling impact ~30–40% WAC reduction
- Generic entry expected 2026–2028; ANDA filers include Teva, Mylan, Sandoz
- No direct head-to-head vs. rivaroxaban or dabigatran RCT (only observational comparisons)
- Renal elimination partial (~27%) — requires dose reduction monitoring
- Factor Xa inhibitors lack universally available reversal (Andexxa limited formulary)

### Opportunities
- IRA negotiation: Lower net price may paradoxically improve Medicare formulary access post-2026
- Cancer-associated thrombosis: Underpenetrated; CARAVAGGIO/ADAM-VTE evidence base growing
- Heart failure: Emerging evidence; AF+HF population highly relevant
- Extended VTE prevention: AMPLIFY-EXT 2.5mg dose significantly underprescribed
- International growth: EU, Japan, Brazil — DOAC conversion from warfarin ongoing
- Digital adherence programs: BID compliance tools; connected pill cap technology

### Threats
- Generic apixaban entry (Teva, Sandoz, others): Estimated 80%+ volume erosion within 12–18 months of generic launch
- IRA price negotiation impact: ~30–40% WAC reduction for Medicare Part D (2026)
- Xarelto + generic rivaroxaban: Bayer filing supplemental EINSTEIN outcomes data; rivaroxaban generic already launched internationally
- Pradaxa (dabigatran) generic available in US since 2022: Price competition at payer level
- Warfarin persistence: ~20–25% of AF patients still on warfarin; generic cost advantage
- Abelacimab and other emerging anticoagulants (Factor XI inhibitors): Phase 3 — could displace all Factor Xa inhibitors if ICH risk is eliminated

## 6. Campaign Pillars (2025–2026)

### Pillar 1: "The Safest Stroke Preventer" — Bleeding Safety Leadership
Target: Cardiologists, electrophysiologists, internists
Core message: Eliquis reduces strokes AND bleeds — the only DOAC to show both stroke superiority and major bleed reduction vs. warfarin in NVAF

### Pillar 2: "From Clot to Clear" — VTE Management Breadth
Target: Hospitalists, emergency medicine, hematology, oncology
Core message: Eliquis offers a complete VTE pathway — acute treatment + extended prevention in a single oral agent

### Pillar 3: "Protect What Matters Most — the Brain" — ICH Focus
Target: Neurologists, stroke specialists, AF patient communities
Core message: Eliquis reduces intracranial hemorrhage by 58% vs. warfarin — the most feared complication of anticoagulation

### Pillar 4: "Trusted Through Transition" — LOE / Generic Readiness
Target: Payers, pharmacy benefit managers, patient advocacy groups
Core message: Years of safety experience; reversal agent available; continuation of brand for complex patients
