## 1. Sales Call Preparation Guide

### Pre-Call Planning
Before every IBRANCE sales call, the representative should complete the following:

**Target Identification and Prioritization:**
1. Review IQVIA NPA data for target prescriber — current IBRANCE TRx share, trend vs prior quarter
2. Check CRM (Veeva) for last call notes, sample history, objections raised
3. Review prescriber's institution type: academic vs community; IDN pathway constraints
4. Identify current CDK4/6 inhibitor mix: IBRANCE Champion, Balanced Prescriber, Ribociclib Preferrer, or Verzenio Preferrer (see segmentation framework)
5. Note any recent payer/formulary changes affecting the practice

**Call Objectives by Segment:**
| Prescriber Segment | Call Objective | Key Message to Lead | Supporting VA |
|------------------|---------------|--------------------|-|
| IBRANCE Champion | Reinforce; provide new RWE data; build PA network | RWE OS/PFS consistency | KM slide; real-world study |
| Balanced Prescriber | Tip share; address OS objection proactively | Monitoring simplicity + RWE | QTc comparison; safety profile |
| Ribociclib Preferrer | Targeted OS counterdetail; QTc burden messaging | QTc monitoring burden | ECG requirement callout; febrile neutropenia 1.4% |
| Verzenio Preferrer | 1L metastatic IBRANCE benefit; diarrhea comparison | PALOMA-2 CBR 84.9%; diarrhea <5% | PALOMA-2 safety table |
| Low Volume | Identification: who is the CDK4/6 decision maker? | Class awareness; IBRANCE as standard | Disease state + IBRANCE overview |

### IBRANCE Call Model (STAR Framework)

**S — Situation:** Open with a patient scenario relevant to the physician's practice
*Example: "Dr. Singh, you mentioned last time you have several postmenopausal HR+/HER2- patients newly diagnosed with MBC coming in. I wanted to revisit how IBRANCE could be the right CDK4/6 choice for those patients."*

**T — Trial Data:** Present key data point (1–2 slides maximum in a focused call)
*Example: "In PALOMA-2, IBRANCE + letrozole extended median PFS to 24.8 months — a 10.3-month improvement over letrozole alone, HR 0.58, p<0.0001. And look at the 24-month PFS rate: 49.4% of patients were still progression-free at 2 years."*

**A — Address:** Proactively surface and address the key objection in your segment
*Example (for Ribociclib Preferrer): "I know the OS discussion is front-of-mind. Here's how I'd contextualize that..."*
[Deliver OS Battle Card response — see Section 4]

**R — Resolution:** Close to action
- Commitment to prescribe IBRANCE for an identified next patient type
- Invitation to attend IBRANCE speaker program or CME event
- Agreement to review patient support materials for current IBRANCE patients
- Commitment to discuss with NP/PA who manages CDK4/6 monitoring

## 2. Disease State and Brand Mastery Checklist

### Disease State Mastery (Rep must be able to address all of the following)

**HR+/HER2- MBC Basics:**
- [ ] Define HR+ (ER/PR IHC ≥1%) and HER2- (IHC 0/1+; IHC 2+ with FISH negative)
- [ ] Explain CDK4/6 cell cycle pathway in simple terms (cyclin D1 → CDK4/6 → RB phosphorylation → S-phase)
- [ ] Describe palbociclib MOA: competitive ATP binding at CDK4/6 active site → G1 arrest
- [ ] Articulate WHY CDK4/6 + endocrine therapy works better than endocrine alone (synergistic growth arrest)
- [ ] Explain ESR1 mutations: what they are, when they develop, why they matter (elacestrant setting)
- [ ] Describe the metastatic treatment algorithm: 1L CDK4/6 + AI → 2L CDK4/6 + fulvestrant (ESR1-WT) or elacestrant (ESR1-mutant)

**IBRANCE Efficacy Knowledge:**
- [ ] Recite PALOMA-2 primary endpoint: PFS 24.8 vs 14.5 months, HR 0.58, p<0.0001
- [ ] Recite PALOMA-3 primary endpoint: PFS 9.5 vs 4.6 months, HR 0.46, p<0.0001
- [ ] Explain PALOMA-2 OS: 53.9 vs 51.2 months; HR 0.956; p=0.81 — NOT statistically significant
- [ ] Explain PALLAS and PENELOPE-B negative results (adjuvant setting; do not promote in adjuvant)
- [ ] Know clinical benefit rate (CBR): 84.9% PALOMA-2; 67.0% PALOMA-3
- [ ] Know ORR data: 55.3% (PALOMA-2); 19.1% (PALOMA-3)

**IBRANCE Safety Knowledge:**
- [ ] Grade 3/4 neutropenia: 66.4% (PALOMA-2); 62.1% (PALOMA-3)
- [ ] Febrile neutropenia: 1.4% (PALOMA-2) — must always pair with above
- [ ] CBC monitoring schedule: pre-treatment, Day 1 each cycle, Day 15 Cycles 1–2
- [ ] Dose levels: 125mg → 100mg → 75mg
- [ ] No QTc monitoring required; no Black Box Warning; no REMS
- [ ] ILD/pneumonitis: rare (<1%); permanently discontinue Grade 3–4

**Competitive Knowledge:**
- [ ] Kisqali OS: MONALEESA-2 HR 0.76, p=0.004; MONALEESA-7 OS HR 0.76, p=0.009
- [ ] Kisqali QTc: mandatory ECG at baseline, Day 14 C1, Day 14 C2, annually
- [ ] Kisqali hepatotoxicity: Grade 3 ALT/AST ~10% vs IBRANCE ~3%
- [ ] Verzenio monarchE: adjuvant, iDFS HR 0.72 (early BC — NOT metastatic)
- [ ] Verzenio diarrhea: Grade 3 ~13% vs IBRANCE <5%
- [ ] Verzenio: continuous BID dosing; no 21/7 schedule

## 3. Account Management — Territorial Planning

### Territory Segmentation Protocol

**Tier 1 Accounts: High-Priority Academic Medical Centers (AMC)**
- Characteristics: >20 CDK4/6 starts/month; thought leader presence; oncology fellowship training
- Call frequency: 1x/month minimum; MSL engagement recommended for OS scientific discussions
- Rep role: Brand navigation; patient support coordination; speaker program recruitment
- Tools: Scientific data package, RWE publications, speaker bureau roster

**Tier 2 Accounts: Community Oncology — High Volume**
- Characteristics: 8–20 CDK4/6 starts/month; often in US Oncology or ION network
- Call frequency: 2x/month
- Rep role: PA support, co-pay card enrollment, 21/7 education, nurse/PA engagement
- Tools: Patient support materials, co-pay card enrollment, CBC monitoring protocol

**Tier 3 Accounts: Community Oncology — Mid Volume**
- Characteristics: 3–7 CDK4/6 starts/month; independent or small group practice
- Call frequency: 1x/month
- Rep role: Access support, patient support enrollment, objection management
- Tools: Simplified clinical message; RxPathways enrollment guides

**Tier 4 Accounts: Low Volume / GYN / Internal Medicine**
- Characteristics: <3 CDK4/6 starts/month; referral management
- Call frequency: Quarterly
- Rep role: Brand awareness; referral to oncologist; disease state education
- Tools: Simplified brand overview; patient brochures

## 4. Objection Scripts (Field Reference)

### OS Objection Script (Full Conversation Guide)

**Setup:** HCP says: "MONALEESA-2 showed ribociclib gives patients over a year more survival. I'm using Kisqali now."

**Rep Response — Step by Step:**

*Step 1 — Validate:*
"Dr. Chen, the MONALEESA-2 OS data are meaningful, and I understand why they influenced your decision. An HR of 0.76 and p=0.004 is a significant result."

*Step 2 — Contextualize with PALOMA-2 OS:*
"What I'd ask you to consider is that PALOMA-2's OS analysis was reviewed at a time when fewer than 40% of OS events had occurred. The median OS difference — 53.9 vs 51.2 months — is 2.7 months. The question for your individual patient is: does a 2.7-month median OS difference, in the context of active second-line therapies afterward, translate to a meaningful change in patient selection for you?"

*Step 3 — Raise monitoring burden:*
"And there's a practical dimension: every ribociclib patient needs a baseline ECG, a Day 14 Cycle 1 ECG, a Day 14 Cycle 2 ECG — at minimum. How is that working in your practice? Are your NPs coordinating those? Is that creating scheduling delays for treatment starts?"

*Step 4 — Real-world data:*
"We also have real-world data from Flatiron Health showing that palbociclib patients in community oncology achieve outcomes consistent with the PALOMA trial population. The OS difference between CDK4/6 agents in real-world practice is not established."

*Step 5 — Close:*
"For patients where monitoring simplicity matters, where your staff can execute the CBC protocol without adding cardiac monitoring, and where you want the agent with the most clinical experience behind it — that's the IBRANCE patient profile. Is there a specific patient type where you'd consider IBRANCE as your choice?"

### Adjuvant Objection Script

**Setup:** HCP says: "All my high-risk early BC patients are starting on Verzenio now for adjuvant use. I'm becoming an abemaciclib practice."

**Rep Response:**
"The monarchE data are impressive for early-stage patients, and abemaciclib earning that adjuvant indication is an important advance. What I want to make sure we're aligned on is that IBRANCE is not indicated in the adjuvant setting — and we don't compete there. PALLAS and PENELOPE-B were negative, and IBRANCE's value is entirely in the advanced/metastatic setting.

What I'm curious about is: when those adjuvant Verzenio patients eventually relapse, which treatment will you choose for them in the metastatic setting? Many oncologists don't continue the same CDK4/6 agent at metastatic recurrence. IBRANCE + AI as first-line metastatic therapy — or IBRANCE + fulvestrant second-line — is a natural choice for those patients. The PALOMA-2 PFS of 24.8 months is the deepest validation we have for 1L CDK4/6 in MBC.

Can we talk about what your relapsed early BC patients look like when they come in for metastatic treatment?"

## 6. Coaching Guide for District Managers

### Monthly Rep Coaching Protocol

**Coaching Observation Framework (RICE Model):**
- **R — Relationship:** Does the rep build genuine rapport before selling? Observed behaviors: physician body language, greeting, closing interaction
- **I — IBRANCE Knowledge:** Can the rep deliver PALOMA-2 PFS, febrile neutropenia, and OS objection handling accurately without hesitation?
- **C — Competitive Accuracy:** Does the rep contextualize the OS objection without disparaging competitors? Are ribociclib PI citations accurate?
- **E — Execution:** Does the rep use approved materials only? Deliver ISI/fair balance appropriately? Close to a specific commitment?
