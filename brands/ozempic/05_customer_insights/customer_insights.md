### 1.2 HCP Attitudinal Segments

**Segment 1: "CV Champions" (18% of prescribers, 35% of TRx volume)**
- Profile: Cardiologists, high-volume endocrinologists with T2D+CVD focus
- Behavior: Prescribe Ozempic specifically for CV risk reduction; familiar with SUSTAIN-6
- Motivator: Proven outcomes data; guideline-concordant care
- Message resonance: MACE reduction, CV death HR 0.73
- Channel: MSL engagement, peer-to-peer KOL programs, journal advertising

**Segment 2: "Metabolic Optimizers" (24% of prescribers, 30% of TRx volume)**
- Profile: Endocrinologists, obesity medicine specialists
- Behavior: Weight loss + HbA1c reduction as dual goals; may alternate between Ozempic and Mounjaro
- Motivator: Maximum efficacy; access to outcomes data; pipeline interest
- Message resonance: SUSTAIN-7 head-to-head, FLOW renal data, STEP-HFpEF
- Challenge: Tirzepatide weight loss data compelling for this segment; risk of switching
- Channel: Conference presence, advisory boards, clinical education

**Segment 3: "Convenience-First PCPs" (38% of prescribers, 25% of TRx volume)**
- Profile: PCPs, NPs, PAs in high-volume practice; limited endocrine specialty access
- Behavior: Prescribe GLP-1 RA reactively, often patient-requested; value simplicity
- Motivator: Easy dosing, manageable AE profile, access/cost confidence
- Message resonance: FlexTouch pen ease, supply reliability, NovoCare program
- Challenge: May default to Trulicity due to familiarity; persuadable on outcomes
- Channel: Sales rep visits, digital HCP platforms (Doceree, Veeva Engage), CME

**Segment 4: "Evidence Skeptics / Inertia Prescribers" (12% of prescribers, 6% of TRx volume)**
- Profile: PCPs who resist GLP-1 RA adoption; concerns about cost, GI AEs, injectables
- Behavior: Primarily metformin + SU + DPP-4; refer out for complex T2D
- Motivator: Guideline alignment (ADA 2025 GLP-1 RA preferred after metformin in T2D+CVD)
- Message resonance: Guideline position, patient success stories, access/affordability
- Channel: CME, POD rep visits, digital education

**Segment 5: "Renal Advocates" (8% of prescribers, emerging, growing)**
- Profile: Nephrologists and advanced practice NPs in nephrology
- Behavior: New prescribers of Ozempic following FLOW data (2024); frequently co-manage with endocrinologist
- Motivator: Organ protection; eGFR preservation; CKD composite reduction
- Message resonance: FLOW data (24% CKD composite reduction; eGFR slope benefit)
- Channel: Nephrology society sponsorships, dedicated nephrology MSLs, JASN/CJASN publications

## 2. Patient Journey Map

### 2.1 T2D Patient Journey: Uncontrolled Glycemia + CV Risk

**Stage 1 — Undiagnosed / Pre-diagnosis**
- Profile: Overweight/obese, often family history T2D; HbA1c 5.7–6.4% (prediabetes)
- Touchpoints: Annual wellness visits, pharmacy screenings, consumer advertising (Ozempic brand)
- Key opportunity: Early engagement, prediabetes education; Novo Nordisk does not currently promote to prediabetes
- Barrier: Asymptomatic; no urgency to seek treatment

**Stage 2 — Newly Diagnosed T2D (HbA1c 6.5–8%)**
- Initial treatment: Metformin (ADA preferred first-line)
- GLP-1 consideration triggers: BMI ≥30, any CVD risk, ASCVD equivalent (CKD, HF)
- Key HCP decision point: Step 1 to GLP-1 vs. step through DPP-4i, SGLT2i, or SU
- Ozempic differentiation: ADA 2025 guidelines — GLP-1 RA recommended after metformin if ASCVD or high CV risk
- Patient concern: Injection fear (~42% cite injections as barrier; Novo Nordisk needs tracker, 2024)

**Stage 3 — Treatment Initiation (Ozempic Start)**
- Key moment: First injection; GI tolerance in weeks 1–8
- Dropout risk: 25% of patients discontinue in first 3 months due to GI AEs
- Support needed: Dose escalation counseling, nausea management tips, injection technique
- Ozempic Care program enrollment critical at this stage
- HCP touchpoint: Follow-up call at 4 weeks for GI check-in

**Stage 4 — Early Response (Months 1–6)**
- Typical outcomes: HbA1c begins declining at 4 weeks; weight loss visible at 6–12 weeks
- Psychological driver: Weight loss is often the primary motivator for adherence (even in T2D)
- Adherence rate at 6 months: ~62%
- Key risk: Insurance/PA denial → abandonment (~18% of new Rx do not fill due to PA rejection)
- Action: Coach prescribers on documenting PA justification; PA Navigator program

**Stage 5 — Long-Term Management (6 months+)**
- Goal: Maintain HbA1c <7%, sustain weight loss, monitor CV status
- Adherence at 12 months: ~52% (vs. metformin ~72%)
- Compliance barrier: GI AE persistence, cost, forgetting weekly injection
- Re-engagement touchpoints: Annual HbA1c review, Ozempic Care digital reminders
- Platform engagement: MyOzempic app (reminder, tracker) — 380,000 active users (2025 est.)
