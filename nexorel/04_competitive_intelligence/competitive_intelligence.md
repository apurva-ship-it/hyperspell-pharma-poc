## Competitor Deep Dives

### 1. Tagrisso (osimertinib) — AstraZeneca
**Mechanism:** 3rd-generation irreversible EGFR TKI (mutant-selective)
**Status:** Market leader, >10 years on market

**Clinical Profile:**
- FLAURA trial: mPFS 18.9 months vs. 10.2 months (1G TKI); HR 0.46
- ADAURA trial: adjuvant indication in Stage IB–IIIA resected NSCLC (DFS benefit 83% vs. 73% at 4yr)
- CNS PFS: 15.2 months in patients with CNS metastases (FLAURA CNS analysis)
- Grade 3+ adverse events: rash 11%, diarrhea 9%, interstitial lung disease 3.3%

**Key Strengths vs. Nexorel:**
- Adjuvant indication — captures patients before metastatic stage (Nexorel has no adjuvant data yet)
- 10+ years of real-world OS data — payer and physician comfort
- LAURA trial: Tagrisso in unresectable Stage III (new indication Feb 2026 — significant expansion)
- Massive market penetration — 68% share creates prescriber habit/inertia

**Key Weaknesses vs. Nexorel:**
- CNS PFS inferior to Nexorel (14.1 months vs. 21.4 months in CNS-M+ population)
- Higher rash rate (11% Grade 3+ vs. 3% for Nexorel)
- WAC: $22,000/month — similar pricing, no cost differentiation
- No superiority data in brain-active disease

**AstraZeneca Strategy Watch:**
- Tagrisso + savolitinib combination (MET amplification) — Phase III SAVANNAH data Q4 2026
- LAURA trial expansion to community setting — significant MSL investment planned
- AZ investing heavily in companion diagnostics partnership with Guardant Health

### 2. Lazertinib + Amivantamab (Rybrevant) — Janssen/J&J
**Mechanism:** Lazertinib (3G EGFR TKI) + amivantamab (EGFR/MET bispecific antibody)
**Status:** Combo approved 1L EGFR+ (MARIPOSA trial, Nov 2024)

**Clinical Profile:**
- MARIPOSA: Lazertinib + amivantamab vs. osimertinib; mPFS 23.7 vs. 16.6 months (HR 0.70) — impressive
- OS data immature but trending positive
- Key limitation: IV + oral combination; infusion-related reactions 67% (mostly Gr 1–2); VTE risk 11%
- Grade 3+ adverse events: 75% (significantly higher than monotherapy TKIs)

**Competitive Threat Level: HIGH**
- Superior PFS data vs. Tagrisso — creates a new efficacy benchmark
- Challenge to Nexorel: how to position CNS PFS benefit vs. overall PFS advantage of combo
- Positioning: Nexorel is the "tolerability + CNS" option vs. Janssen's "max efficacy, accept toxicity" approach

**Janssen Strategy Watch:**
- Expanding to exon 20 insertions (PAPILLON data) — different patient population
- Subcutaneous amivantamab formulation in Phase III — if approved, removes IV burden, significantly increases threat
- High-touch patient support program with infusion center partnerships

---

### 3. Aumolertinib (Ameile) — Hansoh Pharma
**Mechanism:** 3rd-generation EGFR TKI
**Status:** Approved in China (2020); US NDA filed Q1 2026; FDA decision expected Q1 2027

**Clinical Profile:**
- AENEAS trial: mPFS 19.3 months vs. 9.9 months vs. gefitinib (Chinese population)
- Limited CNS data — brain mets sub-group not primary endpoint
- Favorable rash profile (Grade 3+: 4%)
- WAC in China: significantly lower (~$3,000/month equivalent)

**Competitive Threat Level: MEDIUM (2026), HIGH (2027+)**
- If FDA approved at cost-competitive pricing, creates significant payer pressure on Nexorel and Tagrisso
- Will likely be positioned as value option — watch for formulary displacement

---

### 4. Erlotinib / Gefitinib / Afatinib — Generic/Boehringer Ingelheim
**Status:** Generic erlotinib and gefitinib widely available; afatinib (Gilotrif) branded

**Market Role:**
- Primarily used in cost-sensitive settings or by physicians not updated on 3G TKI data
- Afatinib retains niche in uncommon EGFR mutations (exon 18, compound mutations)
- Combined share declining: 15% → 11% over past 2 years as 3G TKI awareness grows

**Nexorel Action:** Not a primary competitive priority; opportunity to convert afatinib users with uncommon mutations through medical education.
