## 1. Approved Indications (US FDA)

### Full Indication Text (US Prescribing Information, current)

> **Eliquis (apixaban) tablets** is indicated:
>
> **(1) To reduce the risk of stroke and systemic embolism in patients with nonvalvular atrial fibrillation (NVAF).**
>
> **(2) For the treatment of deep vein thrombosis (DVT).**
>
> **(3) For the treatment of pulmonary embolism (PE).**
>
> **(4) To reduce the risk of recurrent DVT and PE following initial therapy.**
>
> **(5) For the prophylaxis of DVT, which may lead to PE, in patients who have undergone hip replacement surgery or knee replacement surgery.**

**FDA Approval Dates:**
- December 28, 2012 — NVAF stroke prevention (ARISTOTLE basis)
- August 8, 2014 — DVT/PE treatment and extended VTE prevention (AMPLIFY + AMPLIFY-EXT basis)
- March 14, 2014 — VTE prophylaxis post hip/knee replacement (ADVANCE-1/2/3 basis)

**Important Limitations of Use:**
- Eliquis is not recommended in patients with severe hepatic impairment
- Eliquis is not recommended in patients with prosthetic heart valves (safety not established in RE-ALIGN analog context)
- No clinical trial data in patients with CrCl <15 mL/min or on dialysis; use with caution
- Not studied in patients with significant mitral stenosis (valvular AF) — not indicated for this population

## 2. Dosing and Administration

### 2.1 Dosing by Indication

| Indication | Dose | Duration |
|-----------|------|---------|
| NVAF stroke prevention (standard) | 5mg twice daily | Indefinite (ongoing) |
| NVAF dose reduction (≥2 of 3 criteria*) | 2.5mg twice daily | Indefinite |
| DVT/PE treatment — initial 7 days | 10mg twice daily | 7 days |
| DVT/PE treatment — after day 7 | 5mg twice daily | ≥6 months |
| Extended VTE prevention (after 6 months anticoag) | 2.5mg twice daily | Up to 12 months additional |
| Hip replacement prophylaxis | 2.5mg twice daily | 35 days |
| Knee replacement prophylaxis | 2.5mg twice daily | 12 days |

*Dose reduction criteria (≥2 of the following 3): Age ≥80 years; Body weight ≤60 kg; Serum creatinine ≥1.5 mg/dL

### 2.2 Dose Reduction Criteria Application (ARISTOTLE Validation)

The 3-criterion dose reduction rule was validated in ARISTOTLE — 831 of 18,201 patients received 2.5mg BID. In this subgroup:
- Similar stroke reduction benefit maintained vs. warfarin
- Major bleeding rate consistent with full-dose cohort
- Validated specifically for patients meeting ≥2 of 3 criteria simultaneously

**Clinical Reminder:** Creatinine ≥1.5 mg/dL alone is NOT sufficient for dose reduction. Must meet ≥2 criteria.

### 2.3 Conversion from Other Anticoagulants

| From | To Eliquis | Timing |
|------|-----------|--------|
| Warfarin (VKA) | Discontinue warfarin; start Eliquis when INR <2.0 | Do NOT overlap |
| UFH infusion | Start Eliquis at time of next scheduled dose or when infusion stops | |
| LMWH | Start Eliquis at time of next scheduled LMWH dose | |
| From Eliquis to warfarin | Continue Eliquis ≥2 days after starting warfarin; measure INR before next Eliquis dose | INR monitoring during transition |
| From Eliquis to parenteral | Start parenteral 0–2 hours before next scheduled Eliquis dose | |

### 2.4 Administration
- Administer with or without food
- If a dose is missed: take as soon as possible on the same day; resume twice-daily the following day; do not double up
- Tablets can be crushed and suspended in water, apple juice, or applesauce within 4 hours (for patients unable to swallow)
- Nasogastric tube administration: suspend in 60mL of water or D5W; flush tube after administration

## 3. Contraindications

1. **Active pathological bleeding** — the primary absolute contraindication
2. **Severe hypersensitivity reaction to apixaban** (anaphylaxis or severe allergic reaction)

*(Note: Unlike warfarin, there is no contraindication for pregnancy [though not recommended]; no absolute INR-based contraindication; fewer absolute contraindications than VKAs)*

## 4. Warnings and Precautions

### 4.1 Black Box Warning — Premature Discontinuation and Thrombotic Risk

> **WARNING: PREMATURE DISCONTINUATION INCREASES THE RISK OF THROMBOTIC EVENTS; SPINAL/EPIDURAL HEMATOMA**
>
> **Premature Discontinuation of Eliquis:**
> Premature discontinuation of any oral anticoagulant, including Eliquis, increases the risk of thrombotic events. If anticoagulation with Eliquis must be discontinued for reasons other than pathological bleeding or completion of a course of therapy, consider coverage with another anticoagulant.
>
> **Spinal/Epidural Anesthesia or Puncture:**
> Patients treated with Eliquis who are undergoing spinal/epidural anesthesia or puncture are at risk of developing epidural or spinal hematomas which can result in long-term or permanent paralysis. The risk of these events may be increased by the use of post-operative indwelling epidural catheters or concomitant use of drugs affecting hemostasis (NSAIDs, platelet inhibitors, other anticoagulants), or by traumatic or repeated epidural or spinal puncture. Monitor patients frequently for signs and symptoms of neurological impairment. If neurological compromise is noted, urgent treatment is necessary.

### 4.2 Increased Risk of Bleeding
- Risk of serious and potentially fatal bleeding
- DO NOT administer to patients with active pathological hemorrhage
- Concomitant aspirin, NSAIDs, other anticoagulants increase risk significantly
- Promptly evaluate and manage patients with signs of blood loss
- Andexxa available for reversal in life-threatening or uncontrolled bleeding

### 4.3 Prosthetic Heart Valves
- Not recommended in patients with prosthetic heart valves
- Evidence from RE-ALIGN (dabigatran study) with mechanical valves showed harm; extrapolated to class
- No specific Eliquis trial in prosthetic valve context

### 4.4 Acute PE with Hemodynamic Instability
- Not recommended for initial treatment of acute PE in patients with hemodynamically unstable PE
- These patients may need thrombolysis or embolectomy
- Ensure patient is hemodynamically stable before initiating Eliquis for PE

### 4.5 Patients with Renal Impairment
- No dose adjustment required for mild/moderate renal impairment
- Dose reduction based on 3 criteria (age/weight/creatinine) — not purely on eGFR
- Limited data for CrCl <25 mL/min; use with caution in severe CKD
- Dialysis: No data; Eliquis is not dialyzable (high protein binding)

### 4.6 Hepatic Impairment
- Mild: No dose adjustment needed
- Moderate: Not recommended (limited data; coagulation factor synthesis may be impaired — unpredictable anticoagulant effect)
- Severe: Contraindicated

### 4.7 Pregnant/Nursing Patients
- No adequate data in pregnancy; expected to cross placenta (low MW); use only if benefit outweighs risk
- Fetal/neonatal hemorrhage risk
- Discontinue breastfeeding or discontinue Eliquis

## 6. Drug Interactions

### 6.1 Strong CYP3A4 + P-gp Inhibitors (AVOID or Dose-Reduce)

| Drug Class | Examples | Effect | Recommendation |
|-----------|---------|--------|----------------|
| Antifungals (systemic) | Ketoconazole, itraconazole, posaconazole | Apixaban AUC ↑ 2× | Avoid concomitant use |
| HIV protease inhibitors | Ritonavir | Apixaban exposure ↑ | Avoid or reduce dose by 50% |
| Antimicrobials | Clarithromycin (strong P-gp) | Moderate increase | Use with caution |

**Dose reduction when DUAL inhibitors used:** Reduce apixaban dose to 2.5mg BID (or avoid) if on strong dual CYP3A4 + P-gp inhibitors and currently on 5mg BID

### 6.2 Strong CYP3A4 + P-gp Inducers (AVOID)

| Drug Class | Examples | Effect | Recommendation |
|-----------|---------|--------|----------------|
| Anticonvulsants | Rifampin, carbamazepine, phenytoin | Apixaban AUC ↓ 54% | AVOID — substantially reduced efficacy |
| St. John's Wort | Herbal supplement | ↓ Exposure | AVOID |
| Glucocorticoids | Dexamethasone | Modest induction | Caution |

### 6.3 Pharmacodynamic Interactions

| Drug | Interaction | Management |
|------|-------------|------------|
| Antiplatelet agents (aspirin, clopidogrel) | Additive bleeding risk | Use lowest effective antiplatelet dose; monitor |
| NSAIDs (ibuprofen, naproxen) | Increased GI bleed risk | Avoid chronic NSAID use; counsel patients |
| SSRIs/SNRIs | Antiplatelet effect adds bleeding | Use with caution; monitor for bleeding signs |
| Other anticoagulants | Pharmacodynamic addition | Avoid concurrent use; bridging only |

### 6.4 Non-Interacting Drugs (Cleared for Concurrent Use)
- Statins (all): No clinically significant interaction
- Beta-blockers: No interaction
- ACE inhibitors / ARBs: No interaction
- Metformin / antidiabetics: No interaction
- Digoxin: No significant interaction
- Proton pump inhibitors: No interaction

## 7. Special Populations

### 7.1 Renal Impairment

| CrCl | Indication | Dose |
|------|-----------|------|
| ≥25 mL/min | NVAF | Standard (5mg BID or 2.5mg BID if ≥2 criteria) |
| <25 mL/min | NVAF | Limited data; use with caution |
| All | DVT/PE | Standard dosing; no adjustment for renal alone |
| Dialysis | Any | Not recommended (no data; non-dialyzable) |
| ESRD | Any | 2024 study data: some use reported; label update pending |

### 7.2 Hepatic Impairment

| Severity | Recommendation |
|---------|----------------|
| Mild (Child-Pugh A) | No dose adjustment |
| Moderate (Child-Pugh B) | Use with caution; not recommended in most guidelines |
| Severe (Child-Pugh C) | Contraindicated |

### 7.3 Elderly (≥75 years)
- ARISTOTLE included many patients >75; benefit consistent
- 2.5mg dose may apply for dose-reduction criteria
- Higher fall risk: discuss with patient — ICH risk on anticoagulant vs. stroke risk
- AHA/ACC 2023: Eliquis preferred DOAC in elderly due to ICH profile

### 7.4 Pediatric Patients
- Not approved in pediatric patients
- Pediatric DOAC program (off-label cancer-associated VTE; limited data)

### 7.5 Pregnancy and Lactation
- Not recommended during pregnancy (no adequate human data)
- Excretion in breast milk: Unknown — recommend against use while nursing

## 9. Reversal Agent — Andexxa (Andexanet Alfa)

**Indication:** Reversal of anticoagulant effect of Factor Xa inhibitors (apixaban, rivaroxaban) in life-threatening or uncontrolled bleeding

**Dosing:**
- Low-dose regimen (apixaban ≤5mg last dose; ≥8h before reversal): Bolus 400mg IV + infusion 4mg/min × 120 min
- High-dose regimen (apixaban >5mg or unknown dose; <8h before reversal): Bolus 800mg IV + infusion 8mg/min × 120 min

**Key Data (ANNEXA-4):** 82% of patients achieved hemostatic efficacy; anti-Xa activity reduced 92%
**Limitations:** 30-day thromboembolic events ~10% (anticoagulation removed); restart anticoagulation as soon as clinically appropriate
