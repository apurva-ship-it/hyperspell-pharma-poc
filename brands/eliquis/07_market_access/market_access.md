## 3. Prior Authorization (PA) Criteria

### 3.1 Commercial PA (Where Required)

**Standard Requirements (plans that require PA):**
1. Diagnosis confirmation: NVAF (ICD-10 I48.xx), DVT (I80.xx), PE (I26.xx), post-orthopedic (Z96.641/Z96.651)
2. Indication-specific: AF patients must document CHA₂DS₂-VASc score ≥2 (men) or ≥3 (women) — or physician attestation of indication
3. VTE: Objective diagnosis confirmation (imaging report)
4. Step therapy (MedImpact and some smaller plans): Must document trial of lower-cost anticoagulant (usually generic dabigatran or warfarin) OR document why not appropriate
5. Renal function: Eliquis does NOT require renal function documentation for AF indication (unlike Pradaxa)

### 3.2 PA Success Rates (BMS Internal, 2024)

| PA Outcome | Rate |
|-----------|------|
| Initial approval | 91% |
| Approval after first appeal | 96% |
| Denial after full appeal | 4% |

**Eliquis PA is significantly less burdensome than GLP-1 RAs** — most NVAF and VTE patients have straightforward qualifying diagnoses that satisfy commercial PA criteria immediately.

## 5. Patient Assistance Programs

### 5.1 Eliquis Co-Pay Card (Commercial)
- **Eligibility:** Commercially insured patients (not government programs)
- **Benefit:** $0 co-pay up to $3,600/year ($300/month cap on benefit)
- **Restrictions:** Not valid for Medicare, Medicaid, CHIP, TRICARE, or federal/state programs
- **Enrollment:** Online at Eliquis.com; automatic activation at pharmacy
- **Active cardholders (2025 est.):** ~2.1 million

### 5.2 BMS/Pfizer Patient Assistance Foundation
- **Eligibility:** Uninsured or underinsured patients; income-based (≤250% FPL for free drug)
- **Benefit:** Free Eliquis for qualifying patients
- **Annual re-enrollment** required

### 5.3 Medicare Patients — Access Programs
- **Medicare Extra Help (LIS):** Federal low-income subsidy; Eliquis co-pay reduced to $0–9.85
- **State Pharmaceutical Assistance Programs (SPAPs):** Available in 14 states (NY, PA, NJ, CT, DE, ME, MD, MA, VT, RI, IL, IN, WI, MO)
- **BMS Patient Assistance:** Income-qualified Medicare patients may receive free drug
- **IRA 2026 Impact:** Lower MFP reduces patient OOP significantly; OOP catastrophic cap ($2,000 annual max) further limits patient burden

### 5.4 Peri-Procedural Management Support
- Free physician consultation service for peri-procedural Eliquis management
- Online decision support tool: eliquis-hcp.com/periprocedural
- Addresses: When to hold, when to restart, bridging decisions

## 7. LOE / Generic Entry Contingency Planning

### 7.1 Scenarios and Response

**Scenario A: Generic entry 2026 (settlement with Teva)**
- Authorized generic launch by BMS/Pfizer to capture generic revenue
- Branded Eliquis maintained for complex patients (CAT, elderly, dose-reduction subgroup)
- Formulary: Some plans shift to generic preferred; maintain branded preferred where possible

**Scenario B: Generic entry 2027 (litigation conclusion)**
- Same strategy; slightly longer window for branded revenue optimization
- Invest in patient loyalty (persistence tools, support programs) during 2026

**Scenario C: Authorized Generic Strategy**
- BMS/Pfizer launch own authorized generic (apixaban AG) at 80% of branded WAC
- Capture generic market share vs. competing ANDAs
- Maintain ~$2–3B in combined branded + AG revenue post-LOE
