# Tagrisso (osimertinib) — Brand Intelligence

**Generic Name:** Osimertinib  
**Brand:** Tagrisso  
**Company:** AstraZeneca  
**Drug Class:** Third-generation EGFR tyrosine kinase inhibitor (TKI); irreversible  
**Mechanism of Action:** Selectively and irreversibly inhibits EGFR with sensitizing mutations (Ex19del, L858R) and the T790M resistance mutation; crosses the blood-brain barrier; spares wild-type EGFR (reduced skin/GI toxicity vs earlier TKIs)  
**FDA Approval:** November 2015 (accelerated, T790M+ 2L); April 2018 (1L EGFR+ NSCLC); December 2020 (adjuvant EGFR+ NSCLC post-resection)  
**Formulation:** 40 mg and 80 mg oral tablets; once daily  

---

## Approved Indications (US)

1. **First-line metastatic NSCLC** — Adults with EGFR exon 19 deletions or exon 21 L858R mutations (FLAURA)
2. **Second-line+ metastatic NSCLC** — Adults with T790M mutation-positive NSCLC following prior EGFR TKI (AURA3)
3. **Adjuvant NSCLC** — Adults with EGFR Ex19del or L858R following complete tumor resection (ADAURA)

---

## Clinical Efficacy

### FLAURA (1L metastatic EGFR+ NSCLC — primary approval driver)
- **Design:** Osimertinib 80 mg QD vs gefitinib or erlotinib, EGFR Ex19del/L858R, treatment-naïve
- **Primary endpoint:** Investigator-assessed PFS
- **PFS:** **18.9 mo vs 10.2 mo** (HR 0.46, p<0.0001) — nearly double PFS
- **OS (final analysis):** **38.6 mo vs 31.8 mo** (HR 0.80, p=0.046) — statistically significant OS benefit
- **CNS PFS:** Not reached vs 11.7 mo (HR 0.48) — 52% reduction in CNS progression risk
- **ORR:** 80% vs 76% (similar); DoR 17.2 mo vs 8.5 mo
- **CNS-M subgroup OS:** 15.2 mo improvement (stronger benefit in brain mets patients)

### FLAURA2 (1L osimertinib + chemotherapy — 2023)
- **Design:** Osimertinib + pemetrexed/platinum vs osimertinib alone
- **PFS:** **25.5 mo vs 16.7 mo** (HR 0.62, p<0.001) — significant PFS improvement with combo
- **CNS PFS:** HR 0.75 (favors combo)
- **OS:** Immature; interim favors combo
- Trade-off: Increased toxicity with chemotherapy addition; patient selection required

### AURA3 (2L T790M+ — accelerated approval basis)
- **Design:** Osimertinib vs platinum/pemetrexed in T790M+ post-1st gen TKI progression
- **PFS:** **10.1 mo vs 4.4 mo** (HR 0.30, p<0.001) — 70% reduction in progression risk
- **CNS ORR (T790M+, brain mets):** 70% vs 31%
- **ORR:** 71% vs 31%

### ADAURA (adjuvant, post-resection — landmark OS data 2023)
- **Design:** Osimertinib 80 mg QD × 3 years vs placebo after complete resection (Stage IB–IIIA, EGFR Ex19del/L858R)
- **DFS (Stage II–IIIA):** **65.8% vs 26.2% at 5 years** (HR 0.23, p<0.0001) — 77% reduction in disease recurrence
- **OS (2023 update):** **88% vs 78% at 5 years** (HR 0.49, p<0.0001) — **51% reduction in death**
- **CNS DFS:** 98% vs 85% at 5 years (HR 0.24) — profound CNS protection
- First adjuvant TKI to demonstrate statistically significant OS benefit in early NSCLC

## Safety Profile

### Common Adverse Events
| AE | Frequency (all grade) |
|---|---|
| Diarrhea | 42–58% |
| Rash/acne-like dermatitis | 25–41% |
| Dry skin | 23–32% |
| Nail changes/paronychia | 22–35% |
| Stomatitis | 15–20% |
| Fatigue | 20–28% |
| Decreased appetite | 15–18% |

### Grade 3/4 Adverse Events (low rates — key advantage over earlier TKIs)
| AE | Grade 3/4 Rate |
|---|---|
| Diarrhea | 1–2% |
| Rash | 1% |
| Pneumonitis | 3–4% (rare but important) |
| QTc prolongation | 1–2% |
| Cardiomyopathy/LVEF decrease | ~3% |
| Venous thromboembolic events | ~1% |

### Key Safety Warnings
- **Interstitial Lung Disease (ILD)/Pneumonitis:** 3.3% overall; 0.7% fatal; withhold/permanently discontinue based on severity; monitor for new/worsening respiratory symptoms
- **QTc Prolongation:** Monitor ECG; avoid with strong QTc-prolonging agents
- **Cardiomyopathy:** Monitor LVEF at baseline; withhold for symptomatic CHF or LVEF decline ≥10% below LLN
- **Embryo-fetal toxicity:** Highly teratogenic; contraception required

### Black Box Warning
None

### Compared to 1st/2nd Gen EGFR TKIs
- **Significantly less** Grade 3 rash, diarrhea, hepatotoxicity vs erlotinib/gefitinib
- Wild-type EGFR sparing = major GI and skin tolerability advantage
- **No Grade 3/4 hepatotoxicity** in major trials (vs 5–10% with earlier TKIs)

## CNS Penetration — Key Differentiation

### BBB Penetration Data
- Osimertinib CSF/plasma ratio: **~2.5%** — measurable CNS exposure
- First/second-gen EGFR TKIs: CSF concentrations near or below quantifiable limits
- **BLOOM study (leptomeningeal mets):** ORR 62% in T790M+ CNS-only progression

### Practical CNS Impact
- **FLAURA:** Median CNS PFS not reached (osi) vs 11.7 mo (comparator)
- **ADAURA:** 98% vs 85% free of CNS recurrence at 5 years
- **Clinical implication:** Tagrisso can delay/prevent brain metastases — reducing need for WBRT (and its cognitive sequelae)

## Competitive Landscape

### EGFR TKI Generations
| Generation | Agents | Limitations |
|---|---|---|
| 1st Gen | Gefitinib (Iressa), Erlotinib (Tarceva) | T790M resistance ~9–13 months; poor CNS penetration |
| 2nd Gen | Afatinib (Gilotrif), Dacomitinib (Vizimpro) | Pan-HER; higher toxicity; T790M cross-resistance; modest CNS |
| **3rd Gen** | **Osimertinib (Tagrisso)** | T790M+; best CNS; best tolerability; OS data |
| Emerging | Lazertinib (Rybrevant combo), Aumolertinib, Furmonertinib | Competing 3rd gen agents; combo strategies |

### Lazertinib + Amivantamab (MARIPOSA — key competitive threat)
- **MARIPOSA trial:** Lazertinib + amivantamab (EGFR+MET bispecific) vs osimertinib in 1L
- **PFS:** **23.7 mo vs 16.6 mo** (HR 0.70, p<0.001) — statistically significant advantage
- Trade-off: Higher toxicity (infusion reactions, VTE, paronychia, hypomagnesemia)
- **OS data:** Immature; Tagrisso has mature OS advantage from FLAURA
- **Practical challenge:** IV+oral combo vs oral-only; tolerability concerns limit real-world uptake

### Aumolertinib (China/global development)
- AENEAS trial: Aumolertinib vs gefitinib (1L); superior PFS
- Limited head-to-head vs osimertinib; primarily China market currently

### Tagrisso's Defense
- **Mature OS data (FLAURA OS 38.6 mo):** No competitor 3rd-gen TKI has OS benefit data
- **ADAURA OS (51% reduction in death):** Unprecedented adjuvant OS in NSCLC
- **Oral monotherapy convenience** vs IV+oral combos
- **Long-term real-world data:** >8 years in clinical use; established safety understanding

## Key HCP Messages

1. **The only EGFR TKI with proven OS benefit in 1L metastatic NSCLC:** FLAURA demonstrated a statistically significant OS of 38.6 months vs 31.8 months (HR 0.80) — the only 3rd-generation TKI with mature OS data in the first-line setting.

2. **51% reduction in death in the adjuvant setting — ADAURA:** Osimertinib is the first and only adjuvant EGFR TKI to demonstrate significant OS benefit (HR 0.49; 88% vs 78% 5-year survival) after complete resection of Stage IB–IIIA NSCLC.

3. **Profound CNS protection across settings:** Superior BBB penetration enables 98% freedom from CNS recurrence at 5 years in ADAURA, and extended CNS PFS in metastatic disease — delaying or eliminating the need for cranial radiation.

4. **Favorable tolerability enables continuous treatment:** Wild-type EGFR sparing results in significantly lower Grade 3 rash, diarrhea, and hepatotoxicity vs earlier-generation TKIs, supporting dose intensity and quality of life on long-term therapy.

5. **T790M coverage provides a safety net:** For patients who progressed on 1st/2nd gen EGFR TKIs with T790M resistance, osimertinib delivers 71% ORR and 10.1-month PFS — over double the chemotherapy comparator (AURA3).

## Patient Profile

### Ideal 1L Patient
- Metastatic NSCLC confirmed EGFR Ex19del or L858R by validated assay
- Performance status ECOG 0–2
- CNS metastases present or at high risk (Asian ethnicity, young, adenocarcinoma, never-smoker)
- Patient who values oral convenience and tolerability
- Consideration for FLAURA2 combo: High disease burden, multiple metastatic sites

### Ideal Adjuvant Patient
- Completely resected NSCLC (R0), Stage IB–IIIA
- EGFR Ex19del or L858R confirmed by tumor testing
- Adequate recovery from surgery/adjuvant chemotherapy
- No active ILD

### Key Biomarker Testing
- **EGFR mutation testing mandatory:** Tissue NGS or liquid biopsy (ctDNA); Ex19del, L858R, T790M
- **No PD-L1 required** for EGFR+ patients (IO monotherapy not standard in EGFR+ NSCLC)
