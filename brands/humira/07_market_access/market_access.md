## 2. Payer Landscape Overview

### Commercial Insurance
- **Coverage Rate**: >97% of commercial lives covered for HUMIRA for at least one indication
- **Formulary Position**: Specialty; typically Tier 4–5; PA required universally
- **Biosimilar Substitution Policies (Key PBM Plans)**:
  - CVS Caremark / Aetna: Removed HUMIRA from formulary for some plans (2024); biosimilar preferred
  - Express Scripts / Cigna: Complex dual-pathway; HUMIRA with higher rebate vs. biosimilar; member may be able to request HUMIRA at higher cost share
  - OptumRx / UHC: Similar dual-tier structure; biosimilar mandated in some plans; HUMIRA available with PA
  - BCBS plans: Vary by state; some maintain HUMIRA preferred; some require biosimilar step therapy

### Medicare Part D
- HUMIRA dispensed through specialty pharmacy as self-injectable; covered under Part D
- **Formulary**: Specialty tier (Tier 5) in most Part D plans; protected class (immunosuppressants for transplant patients have protected status, but adalimumab IMIDs do not have same protection)
- **Patient OOP**: Under IRA 2025 OOP cap: $2,000 annual OOP maximum for Part D beneficiaries — significant patient protection
- **Low-Income Subsidy (LIS)**: Full benefit patients: minimal OOP; partial LIS patients: reduced but meaningful contribution

### Medicaid
- Covered in most state programs; prior authorization universal
- Supplemental rebates negotiated by states; net pricing further reduced for Medicaid
- Managed Medicaid plans may prefer biosimilar with lower net cost; HUMIRA access varies
- Pediatric Medicaid (CHIP): JIA, pediatric CD/UC covered

### VA/DoD
- HUMIRA on VA National Formulary for RA, AS, CD, UC, and other indications
- Federal Ceiling Price (FCP): 24% below WAC ($5,600/carton approximately)
- VA prescribers generally follow Class Therapeutic Agreement (CTA); adalimumab biosimilars may be preferred depending on VA/PBM contract

## 4. Prior Authorization Criteria (Typical Commercial Plan)

### Rheumatoid Arthritis
- [ ] Diagnosis of moderate-to-severe RA (DAS28 >3.2 or HAQ-DI documentation)
- [ ] Failure of at least one conventional synthetic DMARD (methotrexate preferred): documented inadequate response after ≥3 months at therapeutic dose
- [ ] Negative TB screening (TST or IGRA result on file; within 12 months)
- [ ] Negative HBV serology (or confirmation of antiviral prophylaxis)
- [ ] Prescriber: Rheumatologist or specialist with rheumatology expertise
- [ ] Duration of initial approval: 6 months; continuation requires clinical response documentation

### Plaque Psoriasis
- [ ] Diagnosis of moderate-to-severe plaque psoriasis (BSA ≥10% or DLQI >10 or involvement of special areas: hands, feet, face, genitalia)
- [ ] Failure of at least one conventional systemic therapy (MTX, cyclosporine, acitretin) OR phototherapy (UVB/PUVA)
- [ ] TB and HBV screening
- [ ] Prescriber: Dermatologist

### Crohn's Disease / Ulcerative Colitis
- [ ] Diagnosis of moderate-to-severe CD (CDAI >220) or UC (Mayo Score ≥6)
- [ ] CD: Failure of immunomodulator (AZA, 6-MP, or MTX) and/or corticosteroid-refractory disease
- [ ] UC: Failure of 5-aminosalicylate (for CD, may not be required) and immunomodulator
- [ ] TB and HBV screening
- [ ] Prescriber: Gastroenterologist

### Hidradenitis Suppurativa
- [ ] Diagnosis of moderate-to-severe HS (Hurley Stage II or III, or documented HiSCR-related severity)
- [ ] Failure of tetracycline-class antibiotics (doxycycline 100 mg BID × ≥90 days)
- [ ] Surgical/procedural evaluation documented (I&D or excision attempted or evaluated)
- [ ] Prescriber: Dermatologist or specialist familiar with HS management

## 6. Patient Assistance Programs

### myAbbVie Assist — Commercial Co-Pay Program
- **Eligibility**: Commercially insured patients; income-dependent (typically <600% FPL or uncapped depending on current program terms); US residency; valid prescription
- **Benefit**: Patient pays $0 per prescription fill for eligible patients
- **Maximum Annual Benefit**: Currently $15,000–$25,000 per year (varies by program year; see current program details at myabbvieassist.com)
- **Duration**: Renewed annually; no lifetime limit
- **Enrollment**: Online, by phone (1-800-4HUMIRA), or via specialty pharmacy. HCP office can enroll on patient's behalf
- **Nurse Ambassador**: Enrolled patients receive outreach calls at 2, 6, and 12 weeks; injection training available

### AbbVie Patient Assistance Foundation (APAF)
- **Eligibility**: Uninsured or underinsured patients; gross household income below program thresholds
- **Benefit**: Free HUMIRA provided at no charge
- **Application**: Submitted by healthcare provider; income and insurance documentation required
- **Processing**: 1–2 weeks standard; bridge supply available in urgent situations
- **Renewal**: Annual recertification

### Medicare Part D Extra Help (Low-Income Subsidy)
- **Resource**: Social Security Administration application; connects eligible Medicare patients with reduced Part D premiums and cost-sharing
- **HUMIRA Coverage**: Under IRA 2025 $2,000 annual OOP cap, all Medicare Part D patients have meaningful financial protection

### Specialty Pharmacy Support
- HUMIRA distributed exclusively through specialty pharmacy networks (Accredo, CVS Specialty, Walgreens Specialty, etc.)
- SP handles insurance verification, PA coordination, refill management, cold chain delivery
- myAbbVie Assist co-pay cards applied at SP level for commercially insured patients

| Country | Reimbursement Status | HUMIRA Position | Biosimilar Competition | Net Price vs. WAC |
|---------|---------------------|----------------|----------------------|-------------------|
| Germany | Fully reimbursed | Established; biosimilars since 2018 | 8+ biosimilars; dominant market share | ~30% WAC (heavy biosimilar substitution) |
| France | Fully reimbursed | Established; biosimilar substitution since 2020 | 6+ biosimilars | ~28% WAC |

| Canada | Provincial reimbursement; biosimilars preferred | Provinces mandating biosimilar entry | 4+ biosimilars | ~35% WAC |

**EU Biosimilar Penetration Context**: In Germany and UK, adalimumab biosimilar market share >85% of adalimumab prescriptions; HUMIRA originator retains only specialty/patient preference segment. This trajectory foreshadows US market evolution post-2025.
