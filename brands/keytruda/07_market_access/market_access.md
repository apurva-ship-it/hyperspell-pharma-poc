## 2. Payer Landscape Overview

### Commercial Insurance
- **Coverage Rate**: ~94% of commercial lives covered for at least one pembrolizumab indication
- **Formulary Status**: Typically preferred specialty; some plans with non-preferred requiring PA
- **PA Requirements**: Required for all indications; median PA approval time: 6.2 days (range 1–21 days)
- **Step Therapy**: Rare for NSCLC PD-L1 ≥50% (NCCN 1A protects); more common for lower PD-L1 strata and non-tumor-agnostic indications

### Medicare Part B (Physician-Administered)
- **Coverage**: Covered under Medicare Part B as physician-administered drug (J9271)
- **Reimbursement Rate**: ASP + 6% in physician office setting; ASP + 6% in hospital outpatient (HOPD) / 340B accounts may be lower
- **Patient OOP**: 20% coinsurance after deductible; with Medigap supplement = minimal patient cost
- **MAC (Medicare Administrative Contractor)**: LCD policies vary; align with approved indication and ICD-10 coding

### Medicare Part D
- **Note**: QLEX SC, when dispensed for home administration, may shift to Part D — significant formulary and tier implications
- **Coverage gap**: Inflation Reduction Act 2025 smoothed gap; reduced patient OOP exposure under Part D

### Medicaid
- **Coverage**: Federal Best Price rebates apply; states generally cover oncology injectables
- **Supplemental Rebates**: Many states negotiate additional rebates; PDL placement may be restricted
- **Managed Medicaid**: PAs required; formulary decisions vary by MCO; Merck account managers work with MCO medical directors

### VA/DoD
- **Coverage**: KEYTRUDA on VA National Formulary for multiple indications
- **Federal Ceiling Price**: ~24% below WAC; standard for VA procurement
- **Utilization**: ~3% of total US KEYTRUDA volume

## 4. Prior Authorization Criteria (Typical Commercial Requirements)

The following criteria typically apply across major commercial payers. Specific criteria vary by payer.

### 1L NSCLC (PD-L1 ≥50%, monotherapy)
- [ ] Diagnosis of Stage IIIB/IV non-small cell lung cancer
- [ ] Documentation of tumor PD-L1 TPS ≥50% by 22C3 pharmDx (FDA-approved CDx)
- [ ] No activating EGFR mutation or ALK rearrangement (required per label)
- [ ] ECOG performance status 0–1
- [ ] No prior systemic anti-cancer therapy for metastatic disease
- [ ] Prescriber specialty: Medical oncologist

### MSI-H/dMMR Tumor-Agnostic Indication
- [ ] Pathology confirmation of tumor MSI-H or dMMR status by FDA-approved test (Foundation One CDx, PCR, or IHC)
- [ ] Prior therapy (≥1 line); no satisfactory alternative treatment option documented
- [ ] Any solid tumor diagnosis (organ-agnostic)

### Early-Stage TNBC (Neoadjuvant/Adjuvant)
- [ ] Diagnosis of stage II–III TNBC (ER<1%, PR<1%, HER2 negative by IHC or ISH)
- [ ] Initiation of neoadjuvant chemotherapy (paclitaxel + carboplatin regimen)
- [ ] Pathology confirmation of TNBC status

## 6. Patient Assistance Programs

### KEYTRUDA Cost Support Program (Commercial)
- **Eligibility**: Commercially insured patients; income thresholds apply (typically ≤$150,000/yr household income; verification required)
- **Benefit**: Reduces patient co-pay to $0 per infusion/dose
- **Annual Cap**: Up to $25,000 per year per patient (higher caps for some indications)
- **Enrollment**: Online at MerckHelps.com or via 1-888-KEY-INFO; enrollment typically within 24–48 hours
- **Ongoing**: Patient must re-enroll annually; income verification renewed yearly

### Merck Patient Assistance Program (MAP)
- **Eligibility**: Uninsured or underinsured patients; income below 600% of federal poverty level; US residency
- **Benefit**: Free KEYTRUDA provided by Merck at no cost to patient
- **Coverage**: All FDA-approved indications
- **Application**: Completed by healthcare provider on patient's behalf; 1–2 week processing time
- **Bridge Program**: Short-term supply (up to 90 days) while MAP application under review

### Medicare Part B Assistance
- **IQVIA Oncology Patient Support**: Partners with third-party foundations for Medicare patient co-insurance assistance
- **Cancer Care Fund**: Income-eligible Medicare patients; covers 20% coinsurance
- **State Pharmaceutical Assistance Programs (SPAPs)**: Vary by state; available in 36 states
