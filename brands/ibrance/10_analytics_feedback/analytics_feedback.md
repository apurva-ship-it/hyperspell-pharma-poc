## 4. Net Promoter Score (NPS)

### HCP NPS (Quarterly Survey, n=250 Oncologists Who Have Used IBRANCE in Prior 90 Days)

| Quarter | Promoters (9–10) | Passives (7–8) | Detractors (0–6) | NPS |
|---------|-----------------|----------------|-----------------|-----|
| Q4 2022 | 42% | 38% | 20% | +22 |
| Q1 2023 | 40% | 37% | 23% | +17 |
| Q2 2023 | 39% | 38% | 23% | +16 |
| Q3 2023 | 38% | 37% | 25% | +13 |
| Q4 2023 | 37% | 36% | 27% | +10 |
| Q1 2024 | 36% | 36% | 28% | +8 |

**NPS Trend:** IBRANCE HCP NPS has declined from +22 (Q4 2022) to +8 (Q1 2024). An NPS below +10 signals active promoter erosion. Key drivers of detractor responses:
1. "No OS data" (cited by 61% of detractors)
2. "Patient neutropenia management burden" (cited by 32%)
3. "Prefer ribociclib's newer data" (cited by 28%)

### Patient NPS (Pfizer RxPathways Survey, n=450 Current IBRANCE Patients)
| Quarter | Promoters | Passives | Detractors | NPS |
|---------|----------|---------|-----------|-----|
| Q2 2023 | 58% | 26% | 16% | +42 |
| Q4 2023 | 56% | 27% | 17% | +39 |
| Q1 2024 | 55% | 27% | 18% | +37 |

**Patient NPS remains strong** at +37–42, driven by treatment efficacy perception, Pfizer support program satisfaction, and the 21/7 schedule. Patient NPS is significantly more stable than HCP NPS — reflecting the gap between physician concern (OS data) and patient experience (living well on therapy).

## 8. Forecasting and Outlook

### 2024–2027 Revenue Forecast (Scenario Analysis)
| Scenario | 2024 | 2025 | 2026 | 2027 (LOE year) | Assumptions |
|---------|------|------|------|----------------|------------|
| Base Case | $4.05B | $3.60B | $3.10B | $2.00B | Continued share erosion; no new indication; generic 2027 |
| Upside Case | $4.15B | $3.85B | $3.50B | $2.50B | INAVO120 approval drives palbociclib combination; share stabilizes |
| Downside Case | $3.85B | $3.20B | $2.60B | $1.50B | Accelerated Kisqali preference; payer formulary shifts |

**Key Forecast Risk Factors:**
- Generic entry timing (2027 assumed; early generic settlement would accelerate decline)
- NATALEE ribociclib adjuvant approval (HR 0.748; could further shift prescriber preference)
- INAVO120 inavolisib NDA approval timeline (upside for palbociclib franchise)
- Medicare Part D negotiations under IRA (potential price reduction for high-revenue drugs)
- Biosimilar/generic ribociclib timeline (would reduce competitive pressure if Kisqali loses exclusivity)
