## 2. MLR-Approved Key Messages (7 Core)

### Key Message 1: Long-Term Survival Benefit
**Claim**: "In previously untreated NSCLC patients with PD-L1 TPS ≥50%, KEYTRUDA demonstrated a 5-year OS rate of 31.9% vs. 16.3% with chemotherapy (KEYNOTE-024; HR 0.62; 95% CI 0.48–0.81)."

**Supporting Data**:
- KEYNOTE-024 5-year OS update (Reck et al., ASCO 2022)
- Median OS: 26.3 months (pembro) vs. 13.4 months (chemo)
- Landmark analysis at 5 years: HR for OS = 0.62
- Context: 5-year OS of 31.9% is 2–3× higher than historical chemotherapy benchmarks in advanced NSCLC (<12%)

**Approved Usage**: Sales calls (Tier 1–2 oncologists), congress materials, speaker programs
**Not Approved For**: DTC (mortality claim restriction), first visit/introductory call without establishing data context

---

### Key Message 2: Early-Stage Treatment Paradigm Shift
**Claim**: "In high-risk, early-stage TNBC, KEYTRUDA significantly improved pCR rates (64.8% vs 51.2%; p<0.001) and 5-year EFS (81.3% vs 72.3%; HR 0.63; p<0.001) vs. neoadjuvant chemotherapy alone (KEYNOTE-522)."

**Supporting Data**:
- KEYNOTE-522 final analysis (Schmid et al., NEJM 2022)
- Both pCR and EFS co-primary endpoints met
- EFS benefit observed regardless of pCR status — important for patients who don't achieve pCR
- First immunotherapy to improve outcomes in early-stage TNBC

**Approved Usage**: Breast oncology/medical oncology calls, breast cancer congresses, CME presentations
**Key Objection Handler**: If asked about pCR vs. OS — "The 5-year OS data (86.6% vs 81.7%; HR 0.66) further validates pCR as a surrogate with clinically meaningful OS improvement"

### Key Message 3: Tumor-Agnostic First-to-Market Position
**Claim**: "KEYTRUDA is the first and only FDA-approved treatment for adult and pediatric patients with MSI-H or dMMR unresectable or metastatic solid tumors that have progressed after prior treatment and have no satisfactory alternative treatment options — regardless of tumor type."

**Supporting Data**:
- FDA approval: May 2017 (MSI-H/dMMR); June 2020 (TMB-H ≥10 mut/Mb)
- KEYNOTE-158 ORR: 36% across 10 tumor types (MSI-H); 29% in TMB-H
- Median DOR: Not reached in MSI-H cohort at time of analysis
- 22C3 pharmDx not required for tumor-agnostic indication; MSI-H/dMMR testing via FDA-approved assays (Foundation One CDx, Promega MSI panel, IHC)

**Approved Usage**: All HCP calls, especially for GI, GYN, and miscellaneous oncology
**Differentiation**: Only PD-1 inhibitor with tumor-agnostic approval for both MSI-H/dMMR AND TMB-H

### Key Message 4: Combination Regimen Breadth
**Claim**: "KEYTRUDA-based combinations have demonstrated statistically significant OS improvement across non-squamous NSCLC, squamous NSCLC, gastric/GEJ, esophageal, TNBC, and urothelial cancers — providing HCPs the most versatile combination immunotherapy platform available."

**Supporting Data**:

- Sq NSCLC: KEYNOTE-407 OS HR 0.71 (17.2 vs 11.6 months)
- Gastric/GEJ: KEYNOTE-811 ORR 74.4% vs 51.9%
- Esophageal: KEYNOTE-590 OS HR 0.73
- TNBC: KEYNOTE-522 pCR 64.8% and EFS HR 0.63
- Urothelial: EV-302/KEYNOTE-A39 (enfortumab vedotin + pembro) — ORR 67.7%, OS HR 0.47

**Approved Usage**: Tumor board presentations, combination therapy discussions, congress symposia

### Key Message 5: QLEX SC — Innovation in Delivery
**Claim**: "KEYTRUDA QLEX SC (pembrolizumab 70 mg/1.75 mL) offers a 3–5-minute subcutaneous administration option with the same pembrolizumab molecule — providing a clinically equivalent alternative to IV infusion that reduces infusion chair time and improves access across care settings."

**Supporting Data**:
- QLEX SC FDA approval: September 2025
- PK bridging study: Geometric mean ratio of AUC within 0.80–1.25 equivalence bounds
- Same safety profile as IV pembrolizumab in SC bridging study
- Administration time: 3–5 minutes vs. 30 minutes (IV infusion)
- Pen device: Single-use, prefilled autoinjector; no needle required by patient (nurse-administered or self-administered per HCP discretion)
- Halozyme ENHANZE technology: rHuPH20 hyaluronidase co-formulation enables SC delivery

**Approved Usage**: All oncology calls; infusion center/administration discussions; practice efficiency conversations

### Key Message 6: Proven Safety and Manageable irAE Profile
**Claim**: "The majority of immune-mediated adverse reactions associated with KEYTRUDA are manageable with established protocols, and most patients do not require permanent treatment discontinuation — with a permanent discontinuation rate due to AEs of approximately 7–12% across pivotal trials."

**Supporting Data**:
- KEYNOTE-024 treatment discontinuation due to AE: 7.1%
- irAE algorithm: ESMO/ASCO/SITC irAE management guidelines; Merck irAE toolkit
- Grade 3–4 irAE overall incidence: 10–15% across indications
- Most common manageable irAEs: hypothyroidism (11%), rash (16%), fatigue (32%)
- Fatal irAE rate: <1% in controlled trials

**Approved Usage**: All oncology calls; safety discussions; irAE management education programs
**Important**: Always contextualize with boxed warning; not to be presented in isolation from risk information

## 4. Common Objections and MLR-Approved Responses

### Objection 1: "The irAE profile worries me, especially for my older patients."
**Response**: "That's a very valid consideration. In the pivotal trials, the permanent discontinuation rate due to irAEs was approximately 7–12%. The majority of irAEs are manageable with established protocols — corticosteroids for most cases, hormone replacement for endocrinopathies. For your elderly patients, the KEYNOTE-024 enrolled patients up to 82 years, and there was no significant difference in the benefit-risk profile by age in the subgroup analyses. We also have dedicated irAE management tools I can leave with your team. Would it be helpful if our MSL reviewed the irAE algorithm with your nursing staff?"

### Objection 2: "Cemiplimab shows similar survival data in PD-L1 high NSCLC. Why should I use KEYTRUDA?"
**Response**: "Both agents have demonstrated OS benefit in PD-L1 ≥50% NSCLC, which validates the class. For KEYTRUDA, we have 5-year OS follow-up data showing 31.9% of patients alive at 5 years from KEYNOTE-024 — one of the longest follow-ups available. We also have the combination data for PD-L1 <50% patients, the tumor-agnostic indication for MSI-H/dMMR, QLEX SC formulation, and patient support infrastructure. It's a holistic package your patients benefit from from diagnosis through long-term survivorship."

### Objection 3: "I'm waiting for a biosimilar — it'll be much cheaper."
**Response**: "We understand cost is important. The pembrolizumab IV core patent expires in 2028 — at which point biosimilars may enter the market. However, KEYTRUDA QLEX SC (the subcutaneous formulation) has patent protection extending to approximately 2041. For patients on QLEX SC today, they'll have consistent access to the same molecule through a protected formulation. Also, our co-pay support program ensures eligible commercially insured patients pay $0 today — biosimilar pricing for patients may not be substantially different once rebates and access programs are accounted for."

### Objection 4: "KEYTRUDA doesn't have an approval for Stage III NSCLC consolidation — that patient would get Imfinzi."
**Response**: "You're absolutely right that KEYTRUDA does not have an FDA approval for Stage III unresectable NSCLC consolidation after chemoradiation — that is Imfinzi's space. For those patients, if they progress after consolidation durvalumab or are in stage IV disease, KEYTRUDA-based combinations are the established 1L standard per NCCN. Additionally, we have an ongoing trial in this setting — KN-867 — and we look forward to sharing data as it matures."

### Objection 5: "PD-L1 testing in my community lab takes too long and results vary."
**Response**: "Testing access is critical. Merck has a partnership with Quest Diagnostics and LabCorp for 22C3 pharmDx testing with a 5–7 business day turnaround. For expedited cases, companion diagnostic labs can run 3-day service. We also support your pathology department with educational materials on specimen preparation. For your MSI-H/dMMR patients with no PD-L1 result, the tumor-agnostic indication doesn't require 22C3 — MMR IHC or PCR testing is sufficient."

### Objection 6: "My patient had a prior autoimmune condition — I'm hesitant to use a checkpoint inhibitor."
**Response**: "Patients with pre-existing autoimmune conditions were largely excluded from registration trials, so we rely on real-world data here. Published cohort studies (Leonardi et al., JCO 2018; Richter et al., CCR 2020) in patients with controlled autoimmune disease show manageable irAE rates — though higher than in the general population. The key is close monitoring and proactive corticosteroid use. I'd recommend a case-by-case risk-benefit discussion with the patient, and our MSL can review the most current evidence with you."

### Objection 7: "The Q3W dosing means too many clinic visits. My patient has transportation barriers."
**Response**: "We hear this frequently, and it's exactly why KEYTRUDA QLEX SC is such an important advance. With Q3W dosing, that's 17 visits per year — a real burden. However, KEYTRUDA IV also has a Q6W 400 mg dose option — approved and supported by PK modeling — which reduces visits to approximately 8–9 per year. And with QLEX SC, administration takes 3–5 minutes and may eventually enable home nursing or even self-administration protocols at select sites. We can work with your team to explore the SC option for new patients."
