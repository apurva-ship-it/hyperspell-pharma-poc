## 2. MLR-Approved Key Messages (7 Core)

### Key Message 1: Most Clinically Proven TNF Inhibitor
**Claim**: "HUMIRA is backed by more than 1 million patient-years of real-world safety and efficacy data — the largest post-market safety surveillance dataset for any biologic medicine — providing unparalleled confidence in long-term outcomes."

**Supporting Data**:
- AbbVie internal long-term safety database; >23,000 patients enrolled; >1 million patient-years
- Long-term RA registry studies: CORRONA; NDB; BSRBR
- No significant increase in malignancy vs. RA background rate in long-term registries
- Published in Ann Rheum Dis, JAMA, Arthritis & Rheumatology over 20-year period

**Approved Usage**: HCP calls (all specialties); medical education symposia; reimbursement discussions
**Compliance Note**: Do not claim "safest" biologic; claim is data volume and experience, not absolute safety superiority

## 3. Objection Handlers (7 Key Objections)

### Objection 1: "My payer now requires the biosimilar — I can't justify prescribing HUMIRA."
**Response**: "I understand that payer dynamics are creating real pressure. For patients who are stable on HUMIRA and doing well, a conversation about medical necessity for continuity is worth having — many payers have exceptions for established, stable patients. For new starts, let me share what I know about the co-pay program: myAbbVie Assist means eligible patients may have the same out-of-pocket cost on HUMIRA as on a biosimilar. It's worth running a benefits investigation before assuming the switch is necessary."

---

### Objection 2: "Skyrizi has much higher PASI 90/100 rates — why would I use HUMIRA for psoriasis?"
**Response**: "You're right that PASI 90/100 rates are higher with IL-23 agents in clinical trials, and for patients with severe, extensive psoriasis where complete clearance is the goal, Skyrizi is an excellent option — it's why AbbVie developed it. For moderate psoriasis patients or those who need their first biologic with a well-established long-term safety record, HUMIRA remains a validated choice. Also, in patients with comorbid psoriatic arthritis, HUMIRA's structural damage protection data may be relevant alongside skin clearance."

---

### Objection 3: "I'm worried about the malignancy and infection black box. My patient has a history of cancer."
**Response**: "This is an important conversation that deserves nuance. In large long-term registry studies, the malignancy rate with HUMIRA is not significantly elevated above the background rate for RA and IBD populations themselves — both diseases carry inherent malignancy risk. For a patient with prior cancer, the decision requires a case-by-case risk-benefit analysis — timing since cancer treatment, cancer type, and remission status all factor in. Our MSL can provide a literature summary on TNF inhibitor use in cancer survivors to support your clinical decision."

---

### Objection 4: "My IBD patient has inadequate response on HUMIRA — what should I do?"
**Response**: "First, we should assess whether it's primary failure (never responded) or secondary failure (lost response). For secondary failure, a therapeutic drug monitoring step is valuable — if adalimumab levels are low due to anti-drug antibodies, optimizing MTX co-therapy or dose intensification (40 mg weekly) may help. If there's true primary failure with adequate drug levels, stepping up to ustekinumab, vedolizumab, or — within AbbVie's portfolio — RINVOQ or SKYRIZI for CD/UC is supported by evidence. AbbVie's portfolio means we can support that transition."

---

### Objection 5: "My patient is pregnant — what are the risks?"
**Response**: "This is an area where HUMIRA actually has an advantage: the OTIS Autoimmune Diseases in Pregnancy registry has tracked >2,000 pregnancies in patients on adalimumab. The data shows no significant increase in major birth defects vs. disease-matched controls. Active RA or IBD in pregnancy itself carries meaningful fetal risks, so controlling maternal disease is essential. The main consideration is neonatal exposure — adalimumab crosses the placenta in third trimester, so live vaccines should be deferred in infants exposed in utero until 4–6 months. Biosimilars lack this depth of pregnancy safety data — HUMIRA's 20+ year dataset is genuinely differentiated here."

---

### Objection 6: "My patient is afraid of the injection — they don't want to self-inject."
**Response**: "This is one of the most common barriers I hear, and it's exactly why myAbbVie Assist offers in-home nurse training visits in select markets. The citrate-free formulation has significantly reduced the stinging and burning that made earlier formulations uncomfortable. Between the pen device and the auto-injector, most patients find a comfortable option. We also have video training guides and a 24/7 support line. The first injection is the hardest — if we can get your patient to that first dose with confidence, adherence tends to follow. Can I arrange a nurse training visit for your next new start?"

---

### Objection 7: "I just had a patient experience a serious infection on HUMIRA — I'm reconsidering my use."
**Response**: "I'm sorry to hear that — those cases are always difficult and deserve careful review. HUMIRA does carry a real risk of serious infection, which is why pre-treatment TB and HBV screening, and patient education about early infection signs, are so important. In the large long-term safety database, serious infection rates are approximately 4.7 per 100 patient-years — elevated vs. background, but manageable with appropriate screening and monitoring. I'd like to make sure your practice has our current infection screening checklist and irAE patient wallet card. And if it would be helpful, our MSL can meet with your team to review the full safety monitoring protocol."
