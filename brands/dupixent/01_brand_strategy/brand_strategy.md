## 1. BRAND VISION

Dupixent aspires to be the defining biologic of type 2 inflammation — the first and only treatment that addresses the shared biological driver across atopic dermatitis, asthma, chronic rhinosinusitis with nasal polyps, eosinophilic esophagitis, prurigo nodularis, COPD with type 2 inflammation, and alopecia areata. The vision is to transform how medicine understands, diagnoses, and treats the spectrum of type 2 inflammatory disease — elevating millions of patients from cycle of suffering into sustained, meaningful disease control without the immunosuppression trade-offs of existing options.

---

## 2. BRAND POSITIONING STATEMENT

**For patients with inadequately controlled type 2 inflammatory disease** who have suffered from the limitations of conventional therapies and the risks of non-specific immunosuppressants, **Dupixent (dupilumab)** is the **first IL-4/IL-13 dual-pathway biologic** that delivers **durable, broad-spectrum disease control** across the full spectrum of type 2 conditions, **without a Black Box Warning, without TB screening requirements, and with a pediatric dosing program extending to 6 months of age** — enabling clinicians to treat earlier, treat confidently, and treat across comorbid disease states simultaneously.

## 3. STRATEGIC IMPERATIVES (2026–2028)

### SI-1: Defend and Grow the Atopic Dermatitis Franchise
- Maintain leadership in moderate-to-severe AD against IL-13-selective competitors (tralokinumab/lebrikizumab) and JAK inhibitors (upadacitinib/abrocitinib)
- Accelerate pediatric adoption (6 months–5 years); expand newborn-family messaging
- Drive real-world evidence (RWE) publication cadence to reinforce long-term safety advantage over JAK inhibitors with Black Box Warnings

### SI-2: Establish Dupixent as Standard of Care in Asthma Type 2
- Grow share in eosinophilic asthma (eos ≥300 cells/μL) where QUEST data shows -65.8% exacerbation reduction
- Differentiate against anti-IL-5 agents (mepolizumab, benralizumab) with IL-4/IL-13 dual-axis MOA and comorbidity treatment value (AD + asthma simultaneous coverage)
- Target OCS-dependent patients via VENTURE data: 48% vs 25% achieved complete OCS elimination

### SI-3: Build the COPD Beachhead
- Launch market development campaign for COPD with type 2 inflammation (eos ≥300) following BOREAS approval
- Educate pulmonologists on biomarker testing (blood eosinophil count) as standard of care
- Differentiate from ICS/LABA/LAMA combinations with exacerbation reduction data (-30%, BOREAS)

### SI-4: Penetrate Specialist Markets (CRSwNP, EoE, PN, AA)
- ENT/otolaryngology: SINUS-52 nasal polyp score reduction (-2.06 vs -0.28 placebo) as primary proof point
- Gastroenterology: EoE histologic remission and symptom data to drive gastroenterologist prescribing
- Dermatology: Leverage prurigo nodularis and alopecia areata approvals to deepen dermatologist relationship
- Cross-specialty: Develop comorbidity bundling messages (e.g., AD + asthma patients)

### SI-5: Protect the Franchise Through Long-Cycle Pipeline and RWE
- Support regulatory submissions for new indications (bullous pemphigoid, chronic spontaneous urticaria, pediatric EoE)
- Generate RWE datasets in >50,000 patients annually to reinforce safety and durability claims
- Invest in head-to-head study design vs JAK inhibitors to cement long-term safety differentiation

## 6. CAMPAIGN PILLARS

### Pillar 1: "One Biology. Seven Conditions."
Core brand unifying concept — dupilumab targets the single IL-4Rα receptor shared across all type 2 inflammatory diseases. Messaging: "Why treat one condition when you can treat the biology driving all of them?"

### Pillar 2: "Confidence Without Compromise"
Safety differentiation — No BBW, no immunosuppression, no mandatory screening — enabling prescribing without the risk management burden of JAK inhibitors or cyclosporine.

### Pillar 3: "From Infancy to Adulthood"
Pediatric leadership — only biologic approved from 6 months of age in AD; demonstrates investment in the most vulnerable patients; drives early brand loyalty with pediatric allergists and dermatologists.

### Pillar 4: "The Long Game"
Durability and real-world outcomes — 5-year safety and efficacy data; LIBERTY program long-term extensions; position dupilumab as a sustainable long-term therapy, not a short-cycle treatment.

### Pillar 5: "Built for the Real World"
RWE and chart audit campaigns; registry studies showing consistent outcomes across diverse patient populations; address payer and KOL skepticism with real-world data aligned to controlled trial results.
