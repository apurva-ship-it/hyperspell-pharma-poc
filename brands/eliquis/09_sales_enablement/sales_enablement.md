## 1. Sales Call Framework

### 1.1 The SCORE Call Model (Eliquis-Specific)

**S — Set the Scene (Pre-call, 2 min)**
Review before every call:
- HCP's Eliquis TRx volume (NVAF + VTE separately)
- Xarelto TRx trend in territory (switching risk)
- Any open PA issues or access complaints logged in CRM
- Patient profile mix: High-ICH-risk (elderly)? Cancer VTE? New AF diagnosis rate?

**C — Connect with Clinical Need (1–2 min)**
Lead with a clinical question tied to the HCP's practice:
- "I know you've been seeing more patients with AF and CKD — how are you approaching anticoagulation in those patients now that we have better data on apixaban's renal profile?"
- "I've been hearing from cardiologists that the once-daily conversation with patients about Xarelto is coming up more. How are you thinking about that?"

**O — Own the Data (4–6 min)**
Deliver no more than 2 key messages per call:
- Primary message: Safety differentiation (ICH data) OR efficacy superiority (stroke HR 0.79) — match to HCP segment
- Approved sales aid only (ELQ-US-2025-SA-003)
- For cardiologists/EPs: ICH data is the opener — "58% lower brain bleeds"
- For PCPs: Access + safety — "No monitoring, no food restrictions, and the safest bleed profile"
- For hematologists: AMPLIFY + CARAVAGGIO VTE data

**R — Respond to Objections (2 min)**
Have objection responses ready (see Section 4); limit to 2 objections per call; do not argue.

**E — Execute and Establish Next Steps (1 min)**
- Commit request: "Would you consider starting your next high-risk AF patient — say, over 75 with a history of falls — on Eliquis given the ICH profile?"
- Leave one material only
- Document everything in Veeva within 2 hours

## 2. Call Guide by HCP Segment

### 2.1 Electrophysiologist (EP) Call Guide

**Business Objective:** Maintain Eliquis as the default anticoagulant for AF ablation candidates and new AF diagnoses from EP; leverage mortality benefit for high-risk patients.

**Opening Probe:**
"When you're anticoagulating before and after an ablation, do you have a preference between Eliquis and other DOACs, or does it depend on the plan?"

**Key Message 1 (Efficacy):** ARISTOTLE stroke superiority — "Eliquis is the only DOAC that beat warfarin on stroke reduction in the primary efficacy endpoint."
**Key Message 2 (Safety):** ARISTOTLE ICH — "58% lower brain bleed with Eliquis vs. warfarin — the most feared complication of AF anticoagulation."

**EMANATE trial note (cardioversion):** Eliquis can be continued without interruption for cardioversion — reassuring for EP workflow.

**Leave-Behind:** ARISTOTLE efficacy reprint + ICH comparison card

**Frequency:** Tier 1 EP (>25 AF ablations/quarter): Monthly | Others: Bi-monthly

---

### 2.2 Cardiologist Call Guide

**Business Objective:** Ensure all newly diagnosed NVAF patients in cardiology are started on Eliquis; prevent Xarelto substitution for "convenience."

**Opening Probe:**
"When you have a new AF patient, what's your first thought on anticoagulation choice — and does the patient's profile change that decision?"

**Key Message 1 (ICH):** "For elderly patients — your 70s and 80s who have AF — the 58% lower ICH rate with Eliquis is potentially life-saving. Xarelto shows 33% ICH reduction vs. warfarin; Eliquis is 58%."
**Key Message 2 (Mortality):** "Eliquis is the only oral Factor Xa inhibitor to reduce all-cause mortality vs. warfarin — 11% reduction in ARISTOTLE. That's a unique clinical finding."

**Objection Prep:** Xarelto once-daily (see Objection 1 in Section 4)

---

### 2.3 Primary Care Physician Call Guide

**Business Objective:** Improve Eliquis initiation rate for new AF diagnoses in PCP offices; reduce reflexive Xarelto prescribing driven by QD convenience.

**Opening Probe:**
"In your practice, when a patient gets flagged with AF on a routine EKG or Holter monitor, how do you handle the anticoagulation conversation — start yourself or refer?"

**Key Message 1 (Access):** "No PA required at CVS/Caremark and Express Scripts for your AF patients on Eliquis — it's Tier 2 preferred with no step therapy. Here's a one-page formulary quick guide for your zip code."
**Key Message 2 (Safety):** "No special diet, no monitoring, and the lowest ICH profile of any DOAC. Eliquis is as close to hands-off for the patient as anticoagulation gets."

**Leave-Behind:** Formulary access guide + patient savings card information

---

### 2.4 Hematologist / Oncologist Call Guide

**Business Objective:** Establish Eliquis as the standard of care for cancer-associated VTE; grow VTE indication beyond AF.

**Opening Probe:**
"How are you managing anticoagulation in your VTE patients with active cancer? Are you still using LMWH, or have you made the switch to oral therapy?"

**Key Message (CARAVAGGIO):** "CARAVAGGIO showed apixaban is non-inferior to dalteparin for CAT — with similar bleeding. Oral tablet vs. daily subcutaneous injection. Most patients strongly prefer the oral option. NCCN guidelines now list apixaban as a Category 1 recommendation for CAT."

**GI Cancer Caveat:** "For luminal GI cancers — gastric, colorectal — there's a numerically higher GI bleeding rate with any DOAC vs. LMWH. For these patients, shared decision-making is important. For most other histologies, apixaban is well-tolerated and preferable."

---

### 2.5 Hospitalist / Emergency Medicine Call Guide

**Business Objective:** Capture acute VTE treatment starts in the hospital before discharge — hospitalists write the first prescription.

**Opening Probe:**
"When you diagnose a DVT or submassive PE in your ED, what's your discharge anticoagulation plan? Do you start them on something in-house?"

**Key Message (AMPLIFY):** "Eliquis is the only DOAC that's truly oral-only for acute DVT and PE — no LMWH bridge needed for most patients. 10mg BID × 7 days, then 5mg BID. AMPLIFY showed 69% lower major bleeding vs. enoxaparin + warfarin — the standard approach."

**Low-Risk PE Discharge (Hestia criteria):** "For hemodynamically stable PE patients who meet Hestia criteria, same-day discharge with Eliquis is supported by evidence. Patient goes home the same day on Eliquis — no hospital day, no PICC for heparin."

## 3. Territory Planning Framework

### 3.1 Target Customer Grid

**Tier 1 — Platinum Accounts** (Top 20%; 65% of volume)
- Criteria: ≥25 Eliquis TRx/quarter (NVAF) OR EP practice with >30 AF ablations/quarter
- Call frequency: Bi-weekly (cardiologist/EP); Monthly (PCP)
- Activities: Advisory board nominations, tandem MSL + clinical rep calls, speaker bureau recruitment
- Goal: Defend share; prevent Xarelto switch; increase VTE indication TRx

**Tier 2 — Gold Accounts** (Next 30%; 28% of volume)
- Criteria: 10–24 Eliquis TRx/quarter; engaged prescribers
- Frequency: Monthly
- Activities: Standard clinical call; access support; clinical education events
- Goal: Increase NVAF initiation rate; cross-sell VTE indication

**Tier 3 — Silver Accounts** (Bottom 50%; 7% of volume)
- Criteria: <10 TRx/quarter; limited engagement
- Frequency: Quarterly (rep); Digital/CME complement
- Goal: AHA/ACC guideline education; awareness of access improvements

### 3.2 New Business Development

**Priority 1 — Convert Warfarin Patients**
~2.5 million AF patients in US still on warfarin. Focus on PCPs with high warfarin volume. Message: AHA/ACC 2023 guidelines recommend DOAC over warfarin for all eligible NVAF patients.

**Priority 2 — VTE Indication Expansion (Hospitalists, ED)**
DVT/PE patients are often prescribed anticoagulants at discharge — hospitalists and ED physicians are the first-touch prescribers. AMPLIFY + oral-only messaging is highly compelling.

**Priority 3 — Cancer-Associated VTE (Hematology/Oncology)**
CARAVAGGIO data is the door-opener. Estimated 180,000 new cancer-associated VTE cases per year in US. Currently underpenetrated for Eliquis.

## 6. Coaching Guide — District Manager Framework

### 6.1 Coaching Model — INSIGHT

**I — Identify the pattern:** Review call recordings and Veeva notes — where is the rep losing momentum?
**N — Name the behavior:** Specific, observable (not "you're not good with objections"; "when you answered the once-daily objection, you moved straight to data before empathizing")
**S — Show the alternative:** Role-play the ideal version together
**I — Invite the rep's perspective:** "How did that feel different?" "What would you do differently?"
**G — Get commitment:** "What will you do differently in your next Tier 1 cardiology call?"
**H — Hold accountable at next observation:** Follow-up at next ride-along

### 6.2 Common Coaching Focus Areas (2025)

**Issue 1: Reps lead with "Eliquis is once daily" — wrong claim**
"This is a compliance issue and an accuracy issue. Eliquis is TWICE daily. Some reps have been confused by the Xarelto QD conversation. Correct this immediately. Coach: Role-play the BID dosing framing as a positive (predictable 12-hour coverage)."

**Issue 2: Reps not addressing fall-risk objection confidently**
"The 295 falls/year data is powerful but often delivered poorly. Coach: Practice the exact framing — 'The data shows a patient would need to fall 295 times a year for fall risk to outweigh stroke prevention benefit. That's not our population.'"

**Issue 3: VTE indication underutilized in internal medicine/hospitalist accounts**
"AMPLIFY data is underused. Assign a dedicated 'VTE call' to top hospitalist accounts; separate from AF message. Debrief: Was AMPLIFY presented? Was oral-only messaging used?"
