## 1. Brand Vision

Ozempic aspires to be the definitive cardiometabolic therapy for people living with type 2 diabetes — delivering superior glycemic control, meaningful weight reduction, and proven cardiovascular protection through a single, convenient once-weekly injection. The broader semaglutide franchise (Ozempic + Wegovy + Rybelsus) positions Novo Nordisk as the undisputed leader in GLP-1 based medicine, addressing the intersecting epidemics of diabetes, obesity, and cardiovascular disease.

**Long-Term Vision Statement (Internal):**
"To prevent the first heart attack for every patient with T2D at cardiovascular risk — and to make obesity a treated disease, not a personal failing."

## 2. Brand Positioning

### 2.1 Core Positioning Statement
For adults with type 2 diabetes who need more than glycemic control, Ozempic is the once-weekly GLP-1 receptor agonist that provides superior HbA1c reduction, clinically meaningful weight loss, and proven reduction in major adverse cardiovascular events — because treating the whole cardiometabolic patient matters more than targeting blood sugar alone.

| Pillar | Claim Anchor | Key Data |

## 3. Strategic Imperatives (FY2025–2027)

### Imperative 1: Defend Ozempic's Position in T2D Against Tirzepatide Encroachment
Mounjaro/Zepbound (tirzepatide) has captured significant new-to-brand share with superior weight loss data (~20% vs ~14.9% for Wegovy). Counter-strategy centers on:
- Emphasizing Ozempic's longer cardiovascular outcomes evidence base (SUSTAIN-6, 10+ years post-approval safety)
- Reinforcing the FLOW renal outcomes data as a differentiator (tirzepatide renal outcomes data immature)
- Maintaining formulary preference wherever possible through robust payer relationships

### Imperative 2: Expand the Semaglutide Franchise Narrative to "Cardiometabolic Continuum"
Bridge Ozempic (T2D + CV) and Wegovy (obesity + CV) under a unified scientific story. Leverage SELECT trial data (HR 0.80 MACE reduction in obesity without T2D) to broaden HCP awareness that GLP-1 RA benefit extends beyond blood sugar.

### Imperative 3: Accelerate FLOW Trial Penetration in Nephrology and Endocrinology
The FLOW trial (published 2024) demonstrated a 24% reduction in a composite CKD endpoint with semaglutide. Nephrology is an underpenetrated specialty. Activate dedicated nephrology MSL deployment and pursue CKD labeling update.

### Imperative 4: Resolve Supply Constraints and Rebuild Access Confidence
2022–2024 supply shortages damaged HCP confidence and drove trial of competitors. Prioritize messaging around restored supply reliability, patient support program access, and pen device availability.

### Imperative 5: Defend and Expand Formulary Access in Anticipation of Generic / Biosimilar Pressure
No generic semaglutide is currently approved, but oral and injectable formulations from competitors are crowding formulary space. Lock in preferred/exclusive formulary positions ahead of potential LOE through outcomes-based contracts.

## 4. SWOT Analysis

### Strengths
- Unmatched cardiovascular outcomes data (SUSTAIN-6, SELECT, FLOW)
- Highest-revenue GLP-1 RA globally (~$13.9B Ozempic + ~$4.5B Wegovy in 2023)
- Strong HCP familiarity and brand equity; 7+ years of post-approval experience
- Once-weekly dosing with intuitive FlexTouch pen
- Broad indication set spanning T2D, CV risk reduction, obesity (Wegovy)
- Pipeline extensions: oral semaglutide (Rybelsus), semaglutide CVI (SOUL trial results awaited)
- FLOW trial provides emerging renal differentiation

### Weaknesses
- GI tolerability burden: nausea 44%, diarrhea 30%, vomiting 24% (Wegovy dose)
- Black Box Warning: thyroid C-cell tumors (rodent data; MTC/MEN2 contraindication limits patient pool)
- Supply constraint legacy (2022–2024) damaged HCP trust in Novo Nordisk
- Price/access barriers: WAC ~$935/month; PA requirements at most PBMs
- Injection barrier vs. oral options (Rybelsus partially addresses but lower efficacy)
- No head-to-head data vs. tirzepatide (SURPASS-SEMA trials show tirzepatide superiority on weight)

### Opportunities
- GLP-1 RA market projected to exceed $100B globally by 2030
- CKD indication expansion via FLOW labeling update (submitted 2024)
- Heart failure with preserved ejection fraction (HFpEF): STEP-HFpEF data positive
- SELECT data driving Wegovy adoption beyond T2D prescribers into cardiology
- Emerging markets: China, India, Brazil — significant unmet T2D burden
- Combination therapy: semaglutide + amycretin (Novo Nordisk pipeline)
- Non-alcoholic steatohepatitis (MASH): ESSENCE trial (resmetirom comparator context)

### Threats
- Tirzepatide (Mounjaro/Zepbound): superior weight loss, growing outcomes data (SURMOUNT-MMO)
- Oral GLP-1s: oral semaglutide competitors (Orforglipron — Eli Lilly) entering market 2025–2026
- IRA drug price negotiation: semaglutide not yet selected but at risk post-2026
- Biosimilar semaglutide: Biocon, Sandoz developing; timeline 2028–2030
- Compounded semaglutide: FDA crackdown ongoing, but gray market persists
- Payer step therapy restricting to lower-cost agents first

## 6. Campaign Pillars (2025–2026)

### Pillar 1: "Beyond A1c" — Cardiovascular First
Target: Cardiologists, high-CV-risk endocrinologists, PCPs with T2D+CVD patients
Core message: Ozempic is the only GLP-1 RA proven to reduce CV death in T2D with established CVD
Evidence: SUSTAIN-6 (CV death HR 0.73, MACE HR 0.74)

### Pillar 2: "The Metabolic Multiplier" — Weight + Glucose + CV
Target: Endocrinologists, obesity medicine specialists
Core message: Simultaneous improvements across the cardiometabolic triad
Evidence: SUSTAIN-7 (weight -6.5 kg, HbA1c -1.8%), FLOW (renal)

### Pillar 3: "Proven. Trusted. Here to Stay." — HCP Confidence Rebuild
Target: PCPs, NPs, PAs who experienced supply issues
Core message: Supply restored, patient support enhanced, Ozempic is the proven standard

### Pillar 4: "Now in Every Formulary" — Access and Affordability
Target: Office managers, payer decision-makers, patient advocacy
Core message: Expanded access via formulary wins + NovoCare savings program
