## 1. CORE KEY MESSAGES (MLR-APPROVED)

### KM-1: Dual Pathway. Proven Results.
**Message**: "DUPIXENT is the only biologic that blocks both IL-4 AND IL-13 — the two master regulators of type 2 inflammation — delivering proven, sustained skin clearance without a Black Box Warning."

**Supporting Data**:
- SOLO 1: IGA 0/1 achieved in **38%** of dupilumab patients vs **10%** placebo (p<0.0001)
- SOLO 2: IGA 0/1 achieved in **36%** vs **8%** placebo (p<0.0001)
- CHRONOS: EASI-75 in **64%** with DUPIXENT + TCS vs **22%** TCS alone at 52 weeks (p<0.0001)
- No Black Box Warning; approved since 2017 with >30,000 patients in the clinical database

**Approved for**: Dermatology field force, specialty reps, digital HCP channels
**Indication**: Moderate-to-severe atopic dermatitis

---

### KM-2: Exceptional Exacerbation Reduction in High-Eosinophil Asthma
**Message**: "In patients with blood eosinophils ≥300 cells/μL, DUPIXENT reduced annualized exacerbation rates by **65.8%** vs placebo — significantly improving FEV1 and asthma control in the QUEST trial."

**Supporting Data**:
- QUEST (N=1,902): Annualized exacerbation rate reduction: **-65.8%** (eos ≥300) vs placebo (p<0.001)
- FEV1 improvement from baseline: **+0.32L** (eos ≥300) vs +0.09L placebo
- ACQ-5 improvement: **-0.87** vs -0.46 (eos ≥300)
- VENTURE: Complete OCS elimination achieved in **48%** vs **25%** placebo (p<0.001)

**Approved for**: Respiratory/allergy field force, KOL medical education
**Indication**: Moderate-to-severe asthma (eos ≥300, or OCS-dependent)

**Message**: "DUPIXENT is the only biologic approved for atopic dermatitis in patients as young as **6 months** — with a weight-based dosing program validated in the LIBERTY AD INFANT trial, delivering **53%** EASI-75 response vs **11%** placebo."

**Approved for**: Pediatric dermatology, pediatric allergy/immunology field force
**Indication**: Moderate-to-severe AD (pediatric)

### KM-4: Breakthrough COPD Data — A New Standard for Type 2 COPD
**Message**: "In the landmark BOREAS trial, DUPIXENT reduced moderate-to-severe COPD exacerbations by **30%** vs placebo in patients with blood eosinophils ≥300 cells/μL — the first biologic to demonstrate significant exacerbation reduction in COPD."

**Approved for**: Pulmonology field force, COPD specialty reps

## 3. OBJECTION HANDLERS (MLR-APPROVED RESPONSES)

### Objection 1: "Rinvoq/upadacitinib has better efficacy numbers at 16 weeks."
**Approved Response**:
"The 16-week EASI numbers for upadacitinib are real — and worth discussing with patients. What's equally important to discuss is the risk profile. Upadacitinib carries a Black Box Warning for serious infections, malignancy, MACE, thrombosis, and mortality. Dupixent has no Black Box Warning, no mandatory TB or lab screening, and over 5 years of safety data showing a consistent safety profile. For a patient who may be on this biologic for decades, the long-term safety story matters as much as the 16-week EASI score. What does your patient's long-term risk profile look like?"

**Data references**: JADE COMPARE 16wk; SOLO 1&2; CHRONOS 52wk; JAK inhibitor USPI BBW section

---

### Objection 2: "Conjunctivitis is a real problem — my patients complain about their eyes."
**Approved Response**:
"Conjunctivitis is one of the most common adverse events in AD patients — and it's real. In clinical trials, we observed it in approximately 10–11% of patients with Q2W dosing, versus 2% placebo. The good news is that most cases are mild-to-moderate, respond well to topical ophthalmic treatments such as lubricating drops or topical antihistamines, and rarely require discontinuation of therapy. In other Dupixent indications like asthma and COPD, the rate is only 2–3% — suggesting it's related to the inflammatory milieu in AD specifically. Managing expectations upfront and having a simple ophthalmologic protocol in place helps patients navigate this effectively."

**Data references**: SOLO 1&2 AE tables; QUEST AE tables; clinical management guidance document

**Approved Response**:

What I can offer is the data foundation behind the approval: BOREAS enrolled 939 patients, was a rigorous Phase 3 randomized trial, and showed statistically significant exacerbation reduction with a p-value of 0.0005. The safety profile was consistent with what we've seen across Dupixent's 9-year track record in other indications. For your highest-burden COPD patients — those with repeated hospitalizations, blood eos ≥300 — the risk-benefit discussion is well supported by these data. I'm happy to share the full publication or arrange a KOL conversation."

### Objection 6: "Lebrikizumab seems similar and might be cheaper."
**Approved Response**:
"Lebrikizumab and Dupixent have similar 16-week IGA 0/1 data in adults with AD — that's accurate. The key differences are: first, lebrikizumab only blocks IL-13; Dupixent blocks both IL-4 AND IL-13, targeting the biology more completely. Second, lebrikizumab has no approvals beyond AD — so if your patient has asthma, CRSwNP, or EoE, Dupixent is the only option that can address all simultaneously. Third, Dupixent's formulary position — check [payer name] specifically, because on many commercial plans, Dupixent remains the preferred biologic. I can pull that up right now."

---

### Objection 7: "I worry about injecting an infant with a biologic."
**Approved Response**:
"That concern comes from a place of wanting to protect your youngest patients — and it reflects excellent clinical judgment. What I can share is that the LIBERTY AD INFANT trial specifically enrolled infants from 6 months old and showed EASI-75 in 53% vs 11% placebo — with no new safety signals beyond what we see in adults. Dupixent is not an immunosuppressant; it doesn't suppress T-cell or B-cell function broadly. It specifically blocks the IL-4/IL-13 pathway that drives type 2 inflammation. The safety follow-up in the pediatric program is ongoing, and I'd be happy to share the full pediatric publication. For your most severely affected infants — where cyclosporine or wet wrapping have failed — the data really do support it."

## 5. IMPORTANT SAFETY INFORMATION (ISI) — ABBREVIATED

**CONTRAINDICATIONS**: DUPIXENT is contraindicated in patients with known hypersensitivity to dupilumab or excipients.

**WARNINGS AND PRECAUTIONS**: Hypersensitivity reactions (including anaphylaxis) have been reported. Conjunctivitis and keratitis have been reported; patients should promptly report new or worsening eye symptoms. Treat pre-existing helminth infections before initiating. Live vaccines should be avoided.

**MOST COMMON ADVERSE REACTIONS**: Injection site reactions, conjunctivitis, blepharitis, oral herpes, eye pruritus, and other eye disorders.
