# HCP Segmentation & Targeting Guide — Nexorel
**Brand:** Nexorel | **Cycle:** H1 2026 | **Territory Planning Document**
**Prepared by:** Sales Operations & Analytics, Aether Oncology

---

## Segmentation Framework

Nexorel uses a **4-dimensional segmentation model** to prioritize HCPs:

1. **Volume** — Total EGFR+ NSCLC patients treated (Rx volume, claims data)
2. **Adoptability** — Current Nexorel prescribing vs. potential; share of voice opportunity
3. **Influence** — Peer influence score (thought leader index, publication count, speaking activity)
4. **Access** — Field force accessibility (office call policies, gatekeeping)

---

## Tier Definitions

### Tier 1A — Strategic Priority (Top 150 HCPs nationally)
**Profile:** High-volume thoracic/medical oncologists at major academic cancer centers and large regional oncology groups. Current Nexorel adopters OR high-potential converts.

**Targeting Parameters:**
- ≥25 EGFR+ NSCLC new patient starts/quarter
- Located at NCI-designated cancer centers OR large community networks (e.g., US Oncology, OneOncology)
- Nexorel share <40% (conversion opportunity) OR >40% (defend and grow)

**Call Frequency:** 1:1 MSL visit monthly + Sales rep biweekly
**Key Programs:** National Advisory Board, NEXUS-1 data presentations, investigator meetings
**Sample Accounts:**
- MD Anderson Cancer Center (Houston, TX) — 8 target HCPs
- Memorial Sloan Kettering (New York, NY) — 6 target HCPs
- Dana-Farber Cancer Institute (Boston, MA) — 5 target HCPs
- Mayo Clinic (Rochester, MN) — 4 target HCPs
- UCSF Helen Diller (San Francisco, CA) — 4 target HCPs

---

### Tier 1B — High Potential Community (300 HCPs)
**Profile:** High-volume community oncologists at practice groups with 10–25 EGFR+ patients/quarter. Strong prescribing habits but more susceptible to formulary guidance.

**Targeting Parameters:**
- 10–24 EGFR+ NSCLC new patient starts/quarter
- Community oncology practice (not academic)
- Current Tagrisso-dominant prescribers with access challenges to Nexorel

**Call Frequency:** Sales rep weekly + MSL quarterly + Digital touchpoints monthly
**Key Programs:** Regional speaker programs, lunch-and-learns, clinical case forums
**Critical Payer Overlap:** Identify Tier 1B HCPs in markets where Nexorel has payer step edits — prioritize market access pull-through conversations

---

### Tier 2 — Growth Segment (1,400 HCPs)
**Profile:** General medical oncologists treating lung cancer patients among broader solid tumor practice. Lower volume but collectively significant.

**Targeting Parameters:**
- 2–9 EGFR+ NSCLC new patient starts/quarter
- Mix of community and small regional centers
- Often rely on academic center recommendations; peer influence highly effective

**Call Frequency:** Sales rep monthly + Digital biweekly
**Key Programs:** Digital symposia, email nurture programs, journal rep/clinical resource
**Message Priority:** Simplified messaging — focus on CNS differentiation and Navigator program, not deep data

---

### Tier 3 — Digital/Remote Engagement (4,000+ HCPs)
**Profile:** Low-volume general oncologists, hospitalists, and pulmonologists occasionally encountering NSCLC patients.

**Targeting Parameters:**
- <2 EGFR+ NSCLC patients/quarter
- Limited in-person access; primarily digital engagement
- Pulmonologists: biomarker testing education focus

**Channel:** Email, approved digital platforms (Veeva Engage, Doceree), medical journal digital ads
**Message Priority:** EGFR testing importance, referral to thoracic oncologist, awareness of Nexorel Navigator

## Call Planning Guidance

### Field Force Structure
- **RSMs (Regional Sales Managers):** 8 regions, each managing 10–12 reps
- **Sales Representatives:** 84 total; territory size ~100–150 target HCPs
- **MSLs (Medical Science Liaisons):** 38 total; 1 MSL per 4–5 Tier 1A/1B accounts
- **Inside Sales/Digital Reps:** 22 total; covering Tier 3 remotely

### Call Objectives by Tier

**Tier 1A Objective:** Establish Nexorel as preferred 1L choice for all CNS-active EGFR+ patients; drive investigator engagement; secure NEXUS-RWE enrollment.

**Tier 1B Objective:** Convert Tagrisso-loyal community oncologists; pull through formulary wins; drive Navigator program enrollment.

**Tier 2 Objective:** Build awareness of Nexorel CNS differentiation; ensure reflex brain MRI at diagnosis; promote Navigator for patient support.

**Tier 3 Objective:** EGFR testing education; appropriate referral behavior; brand familiarity for when patients ask about Nexorel.

## Targeting Exclusions

- HCPs under active OIG/compliance investigation — remove from targeting list
- HCPs who have opted out of pharmaceutical sales visits (flag in Veeva)
- HCPs with <1 NSCLC patient in past 12 months (move to digital-only or deprioritize)
- Pediatric oncologists (Nexorel not indicated in patients <18)

---

## Competitive Conversion Targeting

**Priority Conversion: Tagrisso-dominant HCPs with CNS-M+ patient panel**
- Identified via claims data: HCPs prescribing Tagrisso in patients with ICD-10 codes suggesting CNS metastases
- ~380 HCPs nationally fit this profile
- Message: "For your patients where the brain is already involved, the data now gives you a better option."
- Tactic: CNS-focused peer-to-peer discussion led by Dr. Worthington or Dr. Mehta (regional)

**Priority Conversion: Lazertinib/amivantamab users**
- ~140 HCPs currently using Lazertinib + amivantamab combination
- Segment into: a) using for all patients (target with tolerability + CNS messaging) vs. b) using for high-risk patients only (less urgent conversion target)
