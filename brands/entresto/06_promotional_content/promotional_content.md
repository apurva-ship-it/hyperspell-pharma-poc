## 1. MLR-Approved Core Key Messages

The following messages have been reviewed and approved by the Medical, Legal, and Regulatory (MLR) committee. All claims must be used exactly as written. Data support and references are included.

### KEY MESSAGE 1: Superior Mortality Reduction vs. ACE Inhibitor

**Headline:** "Entresto delivered a 20% relative reduction in cardiovascular death vs. enalapril in PARADIGM-HF."

**Full approved claim:**
> In the PARADIGM-HF trial (N=8,442; HFrEF, LVEF≤40%), Entresto (sacubitril/valsartan) significantly reduced the risk of cardiovascular death by 20% compared with enalapril [HR 0.80; 95% CI, 0.71–0.89; p<0.0001].

**Supporting data:**
- PARADIGM-HF: McMurray et al., NEJM 2014;371(11):993–1004
- CV death: Entresto 13.3% vs. enalapril 16.5%
- Absolute risk reduction: 3.2% for CV death
- NNT (for primary composite CV death/HF hosp): 21 over 27 months

**Visual aid anchor:** "PARADIGM-HF: The Mortality Curve" — Kaplan-Meier separation begins at ~3 months and widens throughout follow-up.

**Usage context:** Cardiologists; HF specialists; primary care with HFrEF patients; in-office professional detailing; journal advertising

---

### KEY MESSAGE 2: Reduced Heart Failure Hospitalizations

**Headline:** "Entresto cut HF hospitalizations by 21% vs. enalapril — fewer hospitalizations means more life lived at home."

**Full approved claim:**
> In PARADIGM-HF, Entresto reduced the risk of first heart failure hospitalization by 21% compared with enalapril [HR 0.79; 95% CI, 0.71–0.89; p<0.0001].

**Supporting data:**
- HF hospitalization: 12.8% (Entresto) vs. 15.6% (enalapril)
- Total HF hospitalizations (first + recurrent): reduced by 32%
- 8-week rehospitalization in PIONEER-HF: 8.0% vs. 13.8% (p=0.011)

**Visual aid anchor:** "Hospitalizations — Total Event Burden" showing first + recurrent event rate comparison.

**Usage context:** Hospitalists; accountable care organizations; hospital quality officers; value-based care discussions

---

### KEY MESSAGE 3: In-Hospital Initiation — Start Before Discharge

**Headline:** "Starting Entresto in-hospital — before discharge — reduced NT-proBNP by 47% vs. 25% for enalapril."

**Full approved claim:**
> In the PIONEER-HF trial (N=881; HFrEF patients stabilized following acute decompensated HF), in-hospital initiation of Entresto achieved a 47% time-averaged reduction in NT-proBNP vs. 25% for enalapril [ratio 0.71; 95% CI, 0.63–0.81; p<0.001].

**Supporting data:**
- PIONEER-HF: Velazquez et al., NEJM 2019;380:539–548
- NT-proBNP reduction: −46.7% (Entresto) vs. −25.3% (enalapril)
- Rehospitalization at 8 weeks: 8.0% vs. 13.8% (Entresto vs. enalapril; p=0.011)

**Usage context:** Hospital-based cardiologists; hospitalists; HF nurses; in-patient quality improvement programs

---

### KEY MESSAGE 4: Guideline-Preferred ARNI Therapy

**Headline:** "2022 ACC/AHA/HFSA HF Guidelines recommend Entresto as preferred over ACE-I or ARB — a Class I recommendation."

**Full approved claim:**
> Per the 2022 AHA/ACC/HFSA Heart Failure Guideline, sacubitril/valsartan (Entresto) is recommended as a first-line neurohormonal therapy in patients with HFrEF, with a Class I, Level of Evidence B-R recommendation, preferred over ACE inhibitor or ARB therapy for reducing the risk of mortality and morbidity.

**Source:** Heidenreich PA, et al. 2022 AHA/ACC/HFSA Guideline for the Management of Heart Failure. *J Am Coll Cardiol.* 2022;79(17):e263–e421. Section 7.4, recommendation COR I LOE B-R.

**Visual aid anchor:** "2022 Guideline Algorithm — HFrEF Foundational Therapy" showing Entresto as the preferred ARNI at the top of the neurohormonal pillar.

**Usage context:** PCPs; resistor cardiologists; academic/teaching discussions; formulary committee presentations

### KEY MESSAGE 5: Broad Label — Beyond Reduced EF

**Headline:** "The 2021 FDA label update means Entresto benefits extend to patients with LVEF below normal — not just ≤40%."

**Full approved claim:**
> Following the 2021 FDA labeling update, Entresto is indicated to reduce the risk of cardiovascular death and hospitalization for heart failure in adult patients with chronic HF. Benefits are most clearly evident in patients with LVEF below normal. This expansion was supported by PARAGON-HF data, particularly in patients with LVEF <57% and in women.

**Supporting data:**
- PARAGON-HF subgroup (LVEF below median <57%): HR 0.78 (95% CI 0.64–0.95) — statistically significant
- PARAGON-HF women: HR 0.73 (95% CI 0.59–0.90) — statistically significant
- Indication covers HFmrEF (LVEF 41–50%) with elevated NT-proBNP

**Usage context:** General cardiologists managing HFmrEF/HFpEF patients; broad HF education programs

---

### KEY MESSAGE 6: Tolerability Advantage over ACE Inhibitors

**Headline:** "Less cough, fewer discontinuations — Entresto is better tolerated than enalapril in PARADIGM-HF."

**Full approved claim:**
> In PARADIGM-HF, Entresto had a significantly lower rate of cough compared with enalapril (11.3% vs. 14.3%), and a lower rate of treatment discontinuation due to adverse events (10.7% vs. 12.3%).

**Supporting data:**
- Cough: 11.3% (Entresto) vs. 14.3% (enalapril); statistically significant
- Discontinuation due to AE: 10.7% vs. 12.3%
- Hyperkalemia >6.0 mEq/L: 4.3% (Entresto) vs. 5.6% (enalapril)
- Renal impairment: 3.3% vs. 4.1%

**Usage context:** Addressing tolerability concerns; ACE-I cough patients; elderly patient discussions

---

### KEY MESSAGE 7: All-Cause Mortality Reduction

**Headline:** "Entresto reduced all-cause mortality by 16% vs. enalapril — meaning 1 life saved for every ~31 patients treated."

**Full approved claim:**
> In PARADIGM-HF, Entresto significantly reduced all-cause mortality by 16% compared with enalapril [HR 0.84; 95% CI, 0.76–0.92; p<0.0001]. All-cause mortality was 17.0% (Entresto) vs. 19.8% (enalapril).

**Supporting data:**
- All-cause death: 17.0% vs. 19.8%
- NNT for all-cause mortality: ~35 over 27 months
- Consistent across age, sex, and geographic subgroups

**Usage context:** High-impact conversations with cardiologists; hospital quality rounds; KOL presentations

## 3. Objection Handlers (MLR-Approved Responses)

### Objection 1: "My patient is doing fine on lisinopril."

**Approved Response:**
> "I understand — lisinopril is a proven therapy. But PARADIGM-HF showed that compared to enalapril — the same class of drug — Entresto reduced cardiovascular death by 20% and HF hospitalizations by 21%. Stability on lisinopril doesn't mean the patient is protected from future cardiovascular events. The 2022 ACC/AHA/HFSA guidelines now prefer ARNI over ACE inhibitor as first-line therapy (Class I, Level B-R). For an HFrEF patient with LVEF ≤40%, Entresto offers meaningful additional protection beyond what lisinopril provides."

**Leave-behind:** PARADIGM-HF reprint + 2022 guideline summary card

---

### Objection 2: "The 36-hour washout is inconvenient and risky."

**Approved Response:**
> "The 36-hour washout is a straightforward, well-established protocol — stop the ACE inhibitor, wait 36 hours, then start Entresto. The risk of angioedema with concurrent use is real but the washout essentially eliminates it. Thousands of physicians have successfully transitioned patients using this protocol. The TITRATION study showed 76% of patients tolerant to the target dose at 12 weeks. We have a simple transition guide and a nurse educator line — Entresto Together — to support you and your patient through the switch."

**Leave-behind:** ACE-I to ARNI transition protocol card

---

### Objection 3: "I'm concerned about hypotension, especially in my elderly patients."

**Approved Response:**
> "Hypotension is real — 14% vs. 9.2% in PARADIGM-HF. But in practice, the key is the starting dose: for elderly patients or those on high-dose diuretics, begin at 24/26 mg BID — the lowest dose — and titrate slowly. PARADIGM-HF showed consistent benefit in patients ≥75 years (HR 0.83). The benefit in preventing CV death and hospitalizations outweighs the manageable side effect profile. We also recommend reducing diuretic dose before initiation in volume-depleted patients. Our case-based titration guide walks through exactly how to manage these patients."

**Leave-behind:** Titration and dose adjustment guide for elderly/renal patients

---

### Objection 4: "We already put everyone on empagliflozin — isn't that enough?"

**Approved Response:**
> "Empagliflozin is excellent — and your patients should stay on it. But Entresto and empagliflozin work through completely different mechanisms. SGLT2 inhibition addresses metabolic and volume factors. Entresto addresses neurohormonal remodeling through dual NEP inhibition and AT1 blockade — the pathophysiologic driver of disease progression. The 2022 guidelines recommend both as complementary Class I therapies. The EMPEROR-Reduced benefit was observed on TOP of background RAS therapy. These are synergistic therapies, not alternatives."

**Leave-behind:** GDMT 4-pillar framework card showing Entresto + SGLT2i + beta-blocker + MRA as the standard of care

---

### Objection 5: "I can't get it approved through insurance."

**Approved Response:**
> "We understand access challenges are real. Entresto Together provides comprehensive support — our PA navigation team has a >78% first-round appeal success rate. For step therapy situations, we have a clinical summary document showing that ACE-I/ARB-first requirements contradict ACC/AHA/HFSA 2022 guidelines, and our medical affairs team can provide supporting letters. For cost concerns: the PAP provides free medication for eligible patients (income ≤400% FPL). We have a dedicated hub services team — one call handles PA, appeal, copay card, and PAP enrollment."

**Leave-behind:** Entresto Together hub services card; PA appeal template

---

### Objection 6: "What about angioedema risk?"

**Approved Response:**
> "Angioedema risk is an important consideration. Entresto is contraindicated in patients with prior ACE-I–related angioedema — those are appropriately excluded. For other patients, the rate in PARADIGM-HF was 0.45% vs. 0.24% for enalapril — a small absolute difference. Compare that to the NNT of 21 — for every 21 patients treated, you prevent one cardiovascular death or HF hospitalization. The risk-benefit strongly favors Entresto for appropriate patients. Standard screening: confirm no prior ACE-I angioedema history before initiating."

**Leave-behind:** Angioedema screening checklist

---

### Objection 7: "PARAGON-HF failed — why would I use this in HFpEF patients?"

**Approved Response:**
> "PARAGON-HF's primary endpoint was not statistically significant at the prespecified alpha level (HR 0.87, p=0.059). However, the 2021 FDA label update was based on the significant benefit in patients with LVEF below normal — specifically those with LVEF <57% (HR 0.78, p<0.05) and women (HR 0.73, p<0.05). The label specifically says benefits are 'most clearly evident in patients with LVEF below normal.' For HFmrEF patients with elevated NT-proBNP and LVEF between 40–57%, there is a labeled indication and supporting subgroup evidence. The indication does not extend to LVEF ≥60%."

**Leave-behind:** 2021 label update summary; PARAGON-HF subgroup data card

## 5. Compliance and Regulatory Guidelines for Promotional Use

- All promotional materials must carry the full Indication and Important Safety Information (ISI) or direction to prescribing information
- The Boxed Warning (Fetal Toxicity) must appear prominently in all branded direct-to-HCP materials
- Off-label claims are prohibited; HFpEF (LVEF ≥60%) messaging is not permitted
- Efficacy claims must always be paired with relevant safety information
- PARADIGM-HF claims must include active comparator context (vs. enalapril, not vs. placebo)
- Guideline citations must specify source, year, and classification level (Class I, Level B-R)
