# IBRANCE (Palbociclib) — Regulatory & Prescribing Information Reference
**Brand:** IBRANCE (palbociclib) | **Company:** Pfizer Inc. | **Document Version:** 2024
**NDA:** 207103 (capsules) / 212436 (tablets) | **FDA Approval:** February 3, 2015 (accelerated); full approval 2017

---

## 1. Approved Indications (US FDA Label)

### Full Approved Indication Text
IBRANCE is indicated for the treatment of adults with hormone receptor-positive (HR+), human epidermal growth factor receptor 2-negative (HER2-) advanced or metastatic breast cancer in combination with:
- An aromatase inhibitor as initial endocrine-based therapy in postmenopausal women or in men; or
- Fulvestrant as initial endocrine-based therapy in women with disease progression following endocrine therapy.

**Male Breast Cancer Addendum (April 2019, expanded 2021):** Use of IBRANCE in men is based on data from male patients enrolled in clinical trials and a pharmacokinetic study that demonstrated similar exposure in men as in women.

**Key Regulatory Milestones:**
| Date | Regulatory Event |

| February 3, 2015 | FDA Accelerated Approval (palbociclib + letrozole, 1L); based on PALOMA-1 PFS |

**EU/EMA Status:** Authorized as IBRANCE via centralized procedure; indications aligned with FDA label for HR+/HER2- locally advanced/metastatic BC in combination with AI or fulvestrant.

## 2. Dosing and Administration

### Recommended Dose
- **Starting dose:** 125 mg orally once daily (QD) for 21 consecutive days, followed by 7 days off treatment (28-day cycle = 21/7 schedule)
- **Alternative formulations:** Available as capsules (75mg, 100mg, 125mg) and tablets (75mg, 100mg, 125mg)
- **Administration:** Take with food to maximize absorption (AUC increased ~21% with high-fat meal; bioavailability ~46% fed)
- **Swallow whole:** Do not crush, chew, or open capsules; tablets can be taken whole only

### Dose Modification Schedule

#### Dose Levels for Adverse Reaction Management
| Dose Level | Capsule/Tablet Dose |
|-----------|-------------------|
| Recommended starting dose | 125 mg/day |
| Dose level -1 | 100 mg/day |
| Dose level -2 | 75 mg/day |
| Below 75 mg/day | Discontinue IBRANCE |

#### Hematologic Toxicity Dose Modifications (NCI CTCAE v5.0)
| Grade | Timing | Action |
|-------|--------|--------|
| Grade 1 or 2 | Any | No dose modification required |
| Grade 3 (ANC 500–<1000/mm³) — first occurrence | Day 1 of cycle | Withhold until ANC ≥1000/mm³; resume at same dose |
| Grade 3 (ANC 500–<1000/mm³) — recurrent | Day 1 | Withhold; resume at next lower dose level |
| Grade 3 + fever ≥38.5°C or Grade 4 ANC (<500/mm³) | Any | Withhold; resume at next lower dose; consider G-CSF |
| Grade 4 ANC (<500/mm³) — recurrent | Any | Withhold; resume at next lower dose or discontinue |

#### Non-Hematologic Toxicity
| Toxicity | Grade | Action |
|---------|-------|--------|
| Pulmonary embolism (PE) | Grade 3-4 | Withhold; resume at next lower dose after recovery |
| ILD/pneumonitis | Grade 3-4 | Permanently discontinue |
| Any other toxicity | Grade 3, intolerable Grade 2 | Withhold; resume at same or lower dose |
| Any other toxicity | Grade 4 | Permanently discontinue |

### Partner Drug Dosing
| Combination Partner | Dose | Schedule |
|-------------------|------|----------|
| Letrozole | 2.5 mg orally QD | Continuous |
| Anastrozole | 1 mg orally QD | Continuous |
| Fulvestrant | 500 mg IM | Day 1, 15, 29, then monthly |
| LHRH agonist (premenopausal women/men) | Per product labeling | Per product labeling |

## 4. Warnings and Precautions

### 4.1 Myelosuppression
- **Neutropenia** is the most common serious adverse reaction associated with IBRANCE.
- Grade 3/4 neutropenia occurred in **62–66% of patients** in the PALOMA trials.
- Febrile neutropenia occurred in 1.4–2.3% of patients — notably low given severity of neutrophil nadirs.
- **CBC monitoring required:** Before starting, at the beginning of each cycle, on Day 15 of first 2 cycles, and as clinically indicated.
- Do NOT initiate next cycle until ANC ≥1,000/mm³, platelet count ≥50,000/mm³, and resolution of non-hematologic toxicities to Grade ≤1.
- Grade 3/4 leukopenia: ~25%; Grade 3/4 anemia: ~5%; Grade 3/4 thrombocytopenia: ~2%

### 4.2 Infections
- Incidence of infections: 59% vs 43% (PALOMA-2, palbociclib vs placebo arms)
- Serious infections: 6% vs 3%
- Monitor for signs/symptoms of infection; treat promptly.

### 4.3 Interstitial Lung Disease (ILD) / Pneumonitis
- Severe, life-threatening, or fatal cases of ILD/pneumonitis have been reported.
- Incidence in clinical trials: <1% (Grade 3-4)
- Post-marketing reports include fatal cases.
- Monitor for new/worsening respiratory symptoms.
- Permanently discontinue for Grade 3-4 ILD/pneumonitis.

### 4.4 Embryo-Fetal Toxicity
- Can cause fetal harm based on MOA and animal studies.
- Advise females of reproductive potential to use effective contraception during treatment and for at least 3 weeks after final dose.
- Advise males with female partners of reproductive potential to use effective contraception during treatment and for 3 months after final dose.
- Verify pregnancy status before initiation in females of reproductive potential.

### 4.5 No Black Box Warning
IBRANCE does **NOT** carry a Black Box Warning — an important tolerability distinction versus some oncology agents and a key commercial talking point.

## 6. Drug Interactions

### CYP3A Interactions
Palbociclib is a CYP3A substrate (major pathway). Strong CYP3A inhibitors significantly increase palbociclib exposure; strong inducers significantly decrease it.

| Interaction Category | Example Agents | Effect on Palbociclib | Recommendation |
|---------------------|---------------|----------------------|----------------|
| Strong CYP3A inhibitors | Clarithromycin, ketoconazole, itraconazole, voriconazole, ritonavir, grapefruit/grapefruit juice | AUC ↑87% (clarithromycin) | Avoid; if unavoidable, reduce palbociclib dose to 75mg |
| Moderate CYP3A inhibitors | Fluconazole, erythromycin, verapamil, diltiazem | AUC ↑30–50% | Monitor closely; dose modification if not tolerated |
| Strong CYP3A inducers | Rifampin, phenytoin, carbamazepine, St. John's Wort | AUC ↓85% (rifampin) | Avoid concomitant use |
| Sensitive CYP3A substrates (narrow TI) | Midazolam, triazolam | Palbociclib inhibits CYP3A | Consider dose reduction of substrate |

### pH-Modifying Agents
- Proton pump inhibitors (PPIs): Not clinically significant based on simulated data
- Acid-reducing agents: Minimal impact; IBRANCE can be administered regardless of gastric pH

## 7. Special Populations

### Hepatic Impairment
| Hepatic Impairment Category | Dose Recommendation |
|-----------------------------|-------------------|
| Mild (Child-Pugh A) | No dose adjustment |
| Moderate (Child-Pugh B) | No dose adjustment (PK study: similar AUC) |
| Severe (Child-Pugh C) | Reduce starting dose to 75mg |

### Renal Impairment
| Renal Function | Dose Recommendation |
|---------------|-------------------|
| Mild-moderate (CrCl 30–89 mL/min) | No dose adjustment |
| Severe (CrCl <30 mL/min) | No dose adjustment (limited data; monitor closely) |
| End-stage renal disease (ESRD) | Not studied; use caution |

### Pediatric Population
- Not approved for use in pediatric patients
- Safety and efficacy in patients <18 years not established

### Geriatric Population
- No significant differences in safety/efficacy observed in patients ≥65 years in PALOMA trials
- Subgroup analysis of PALOMA-2: PFS benefit maintained in age ≥65 subgroup (28.2 vs 14.5 months)
- Standard dose and monitoring apply; dose modification per standard toxicity criteria

### Pregnancy and Lactation
- **Pregnancy Category:** No formal category; fetal harm expected based on MOA
- Advise females of reproductive potential: avoid pregnancy; use contraception ≥3 weeks after last dose
- **Lactation:** Avoid breastfeeding during treatment and for 3 weeks after last dose; no data on presence in human milk
- **Males:** Use effective contraception during treatment and 3 months after last dose; advise regarding sperm banking if fertility preservation desired

### Male Breast Cancer
- Pharmacokinetics in male BC patients similar to female patients
- No dedicated dose adjustment for men
- Combine with LHRH agonist + AI in men (standard endocrine backbone)

### No QTc Monitoring Required
Unlike ribociclib (Kisqali), IBRANCE does **NOT** require baseline or on-treatment QTc monitoring. This simplifies clinical management and reduces patient burden.
