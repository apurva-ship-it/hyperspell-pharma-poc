## 1. PURPOSE AND SCOPE

This document provides translation-ready core messaging, regional adaptation guidelines, and an approved terminology glossary for DUPIXENT (dupilumab) global brand teams. All translated materials must undergo country-specific Medical/Legal/Regulatory (MLR) review before external use. The source language for all brand communications is **American English (en-US)**.

## 3. REGIONAL CONSIDERATIONS

### 3.1 United States (en-US) — Source Market
- **Regulatory reference**: FDA-approved Prescribing Information (PI) / USPI
- **Key differentiators emphasized**: No BBW; pediatric down to 6 months; 7 indications
- **Cultural context**: Direct data communication preferred; outcome-oriented ("clear skin," "fewer attacks"); patient empowerment messaging resonates
- **Approved indications**: All 7 (AD, Asthma, CRSwNP, EoE, PN, COPD, AA)
- **Copay/access messaging**: MyWay program heavily integrated into all HCP messaging

### 3.2 European Union (Multi-language) — EMA Approved
- **Regulatory reference**: European SmPC (Summary of Product Characteristics) — may differ from US PI
- **Languages required**: French (FR), German (DE), Spanish (ES), Italian (IT), Polish (PL), Dutch (NL), Portuguese (PT), Swedish (SV), and others
- **Approved indications (EU)**: AD (adult, adolescent, peds 6mo+), Asthma (≥12yr some markets, ≥6yr expanding), CRSwNP, EoE, PN, AA — COPD EU approval pending/in process
- **Key adaptation notes**:
  - No DTC advertising permitted in EU (prohibition on direct-to-consumer advertising for prescription medicines)
  - HCP materials must reference SmPC not US PI; data must come from EU label
  - NICE (UK) has separate BLUETEQ criteria; German G-BA assessment shapes market access language
  - "Boxed Warning" equivalent in EU = "Special warnings and precautions for use" in SmPC Section 4.4

| Country | Key HTA Body | Formulary Status | Access Notes |
|---|---|---|---|
| Germany | G-BA / AMNOG | In formulary | AMNOG benefit assessment required; "Erheblicher Zusatznutzen" (significant added benefit) status for AD |
| France | HAS | Reimbursed | Avis favorable; restricted to moderate-to-severe AD with specialist prescription |
| UK (NHSE) | NICE / NHSE | NICE approved | BLUETEQ criteria apply; specialist initiation required |
| Italy | AIFA | SSN listed | Restricted prescribing (specialists only); AIFA DG |
| Spain | AEMPS/regional | Listed (varies) | Regional autonomy; some regions more restrictive |
| Netherlands | ZIN | Reimbursed | Conditional; restricted to dermatologists |

### 3.3 Japan — PMDA / MHLW
- **Regulatory reference**: Japanese approved Package Insert (添付文書)
- **Approved indications (Japan)**: AD (adult/pediatric), Asthma, CRSwNP — EoE, PN, COPD pending or recently approved
- **Language**: Japanese (JA) — do not machine translate; use medically certified translation with local pharma expertise
- **Key adaptation notes**:
  - Japanese labeling format differs significantly from US/EU; separate product name and structure
  - NHI price revision process: annual price revisions may affect messaging
  - Cultural communication: understatement preferred; avoid superlative claims; outcome language should be precise and data-anchored
  - Kanji/katakana for scientific terms: IL-4/IL-13 typically written in Latin alphabet (アイエル4/アイエル13 in phonetic contexts)
  - HCP communication highly regulated; no competitive claims without head-to-head data
- **Sanofi Japan partner**: Local entity handles field force and promotional materials; global provides core platform

### 3.4 Emerging Markets (EM) — China, Brazil, India, GCC, SE Asia
- **China (NMPA approved)**: Dupilumab approved by NMPA; sold as "达必妥" (Dabi Tu); marketed via Sanofi China affiliate
  - Indication: AD (adults); asthma approval in process
  - Language: Simplified Chinese (zh-CN); traditional Chinese for Taiwan/HK (zh-TW)
  - Key cultural adaptation: Strong emphasis on "safety without systemic immunosuppression" — Chinese HCPs and patients are concerned about infection risk with immunosuppressants
  - Biomarker testing infrastructure for asthma/COPD is developing; eosinophil count messaging needs basic education scaffolding
- **Brazil (ANVISA approved)**: AD approved; asthma under review
  - Language: Brazilian Portuguese (pt-BR) — NOT European Portuguese
  - Access: SUS (public system) coverage negotiation ongoing; private insurance more accessible
  - "Dupixent" brand name consistent; generic name "dupilumabe" in pt-BR
- **India**: Not yet approved; compassionate use/early access programs in place at select AIIMS centers
  - Language: English primary for HCP communications; Hindi/regional languages for patient-facing
- **GCC (Saudi Arabia, UAE, Kuwait)**: SFDA/MOH approved in key countries; AD primary indication
  - Arabic language required for patient-facing; HCP materials often in English
  - Cultural sensitivity: skin disease carries social stigma; messaging that normalizes AD and normalizes biologic use is critical

## 4. APPROVED TERMINOLOGY GLOSSARY

### 4.1 Scientific Terms — English Approved Equivalents

| Preferred Term | Avoid (Do Not Use) | Rationale |
|---|---|---|
| "Dupilumab" | "Dupixent" in scientific context | Use INN in scientific/regulatory documents; brand name in promotional |
| "IL-4Rα" | "IL-4 receptor" alone | Precision: it is specifically the alpha subunit |
| "Dual IL-4/IL-13 inhibition" | "Dual cytokine blocker" | Technically precise; "blocker" too informal for medical contexts |
| "Type 2 inflammation" | "Allergic inflammation" | "Type 2" is the approved scientific umbrella; "allergic" is narrower |
| "Blood eosinophil count" | "Eosinophil level" | Standard clinical laboratory terminology |
| "Moderate-to-severe atopic dermatitis" | "Severe eczema" | Regulatory-approved disease descriptor; "eczema" OK for patient-facing |
| "Annualized exacerbation rate" | "Flare rate" | AER is the validated clinical endpoint term |
| "Prior authorization" (US) | "Preauthorization," "pre-auth" | PA is standard; note: other markets use different terms |
