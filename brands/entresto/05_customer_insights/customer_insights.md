## 1. HCP Segmentation

### 1.1 Segmentation Framework

HCP prescribers of heart failure therapy are segmented across three primary dimensions:
1. **Specialty** (cardiologist, HF specialist, internist/hospitalist, PCP, NP/PA)
2. **Prescribing Volume** (high/medium/low)
3. **Entresto Adoption Status** (Champion, Adopter, Resistor, Non-prescriber)

### 1.2 Specialty-Level Prescribing Profiles

| Specialty | % of HF Patients Managed | Entresto Rx Share | Priority Level |
|----------|--------------------------|------------------|----------------|
| HF Specialists / Advanced HF Centers | 8% of HF pts | 52% ARNI adoption | Tier 1 — Defend |
| General Cardiologists | 34% of HF pts | 28% ARNI adoption | Tier 1 — Grow |
| Internists / Hospitalists | 21% of HF pts | 14% ARNI adoption | Tier 2 — Convert |
| PCPs (Family Medicine / Internal Medicine) | 30% of HF pts | 9% ARNI adoption | Tier 2 — Activate |
| NPs / PAs (HF clinic setting) | 7% of HF pts | 18% ARNI adoption | Tier 2 — Educate |

**Key insight:** General cardiologists manage the largest addressable segment with room to grow. PCPs manage 30% of HF patients with only 9% ARNI adoption — the highest-volume, lowest-penetration opportunity.

### 1.3 Adoption Segment Definitions and Characteristics

#### Champions (~15% of prescribers, ~48% of volume)
- Characteristics: HF specialist training; familiar with PARADIGM-HF data; routinely initiates ARNI in newly diagnosed and ACE-I/ARB-experienced patients; educates colleagues
- Needs: Access/PA support tools; RWE data for difficult payer cases; continuing medical education on HFmrEF/HFpEF label; peer-to-peer speaker opportunities
- Engagement: Speaker bureau; KOL advisory boards; early data access; clinical trial investigator relationships

#### Adopters (~30% of prescribers, ~35% of volume)
- Characteristics: Uses Entresto for "clear-cut" HFrEF patients; may still prefer ACE-I for elderly/frail patients; understands guideline recommendation but not fully converting existing patients
- Needs: Reassurance on tolerability in elderly/renal-impaired patients; practical titration guidance; case-based education on gradual conversion
- Engagement: Rep visits with case studies; lunch-and-learn programs; clinical decision support tools; CHAMP-HF registry data

#### Resistors (~25% of prescribers, ~12% of volume)
- Characteristics: Skeptical of cost-benefit; defer to "lisinopril works fine"; concerns about hypotension or washout complexity; often older, community-based cardiologists or high-volume PCPs
- Needs: Address cost concerns (Entresto Together PAP); simplify washout protocol explanation; demonstrate NNT=21 in tangible patient terms; show total cost of care
- Engagement: 1:1 rep discussions with objection handling; CME on GDMT optimization; pharmacoeconomic evidence; peer influence from champions

#### Non-Prescribers (~30% of prescribers, ~5% of volume)
- Characteristics: PCPs/internists who refer all HF patients to cardiology; minimal HF-specific education; do not consider themselves "HF prescribers"
- Needs: Guideline awareness; clear algorithm for when to initiate ARNI; confidence in managing stable HFrEF patients without specialist referral; PAP and hub support
- Engagement: Digital programs; professional society partnerships; EHR-integrated clinical decision support

## 2. Patient Journey Mapping

### 2.1 HFrEF Patient Journey

**Stage 1: Pre-Diagnosis (Symptom Onset)**
- Duration: Typically 6–24 months before HF diagnosis
- Symptoms: Exertional dyspnea, fatigue, leg swelling, orthopnea
- Patient mindset: "It must be age/deconditioning"
- HCP contact: PCP visits for non-specific complaints; often misattributed initially
- Key gap: Delayed recognition; echocardiogram not ordered promptly
- Brand opportunity: Disease awareness; NT-proBNP screening partnerships

**Stage 2: Diagnosis**
- Event: Echocardiogram reveals LVEF ≤40%; BNP/NT-proBNP elevated
- Patient mindset: Fear, uncertainty; "Am I going to die?"; often shock at diagnosis
- HCP action: Initiated on diuretics, beta-blocker, ACE-I or ARB; typically NOT started on ARNI at diagnosis despite guidelines
- Key gap: Only 14% of de novo HF patients initiated on ARNI at first prescription event (CHAMP-HF Registry 2022)
- Brand opportunity: "Start strong" — in-hospital or early post-diagnosis ARNI initiation

**Stage 3: Early Stabilization (0–6 months)**
- Patient mindset: Adjusting to diagnosis; managing new medications; motivated to comply
- HCP action: Dose titration; repeat echo; NT-proBNP monitoring; referral to HF clinic if not already there
- Key gap: Titration inertia — many patients stuck at starting ACE-I dose; ARNI not introduced
- Brand opportunity: GDMT optimization campaign; target cardiologists reviewing 3-month echo results

**Stage 4: Stable Chronic HF (6 months–years)**
- Patient mindset: Adapted to diagnosis; daily routine; may minimize symptoms; adherence fatigue risk
- HCP action: 6-monthly monitoring; device therapy evaluation; ACE-I/ARB optimization; ARNI conversion window
- Key gap: 61% persistence at 90 days drops to 47% at 12 months (specialty pharmacy data); conversions from ACE-I not happening
- Brand opportunity: Adherence programs; nurse educator support; 90-day dispense at specialty pharmacy

**Stage 5: Worsening / Decompensation**
- Event: Acute decompensated HF — hospitalization required
- Patient mindset: Fear; "I'm not getting better"; hospitalization is traumatic anchor event
- HCP action: IV diuresis; hemodynamic stabilization; medication review
- Key gap: PIONEER-HF data shows in-hospital ARNI initiation reduces 8-week rehospitalization by 42% (8% vs. 13.8%), yet only 28% of eligible patients receive ARNI at discharge
- Brand opportunity: Hospital Pathways program; pharmacist activation; discharge GDMT checklist

**Stage 6: Advanced HF / End Stage**
- LVAD, transplant evaluation, or palliative care
- Entresto may continue unless hemodynamic compromise precludes use
- Brand role: Guideline-recommended through advanced stages; delay to advanced therapy

## 3. Unmet Needs Analysis

### 3.1 Unmet Needs — HCP Level

| Unmet Need | Prevalence | Impact |
|-----------|-----------|--------|
| Inability to quickly identify ACE-I/ARB patients eligible for ARNI upgrade | Very High | Large conversion gap |
| Complexity of ACE-I washout protocol limiting transition | High | Delays or avoidance of ARNI |
| Uncertainty about ARNI use in frail/elderly with low BP | High | Underprescribing in highest-risk group |
| Lack of RWE on long-term ARNI outcomes | Moderate | Resistor physicians require RWE |
| Limited NT-proBNP monitoring infrastructure in community settings | Moderate | HFmrEF/HFpEF label underutilized |
| Administrative burden of prior authorizations | Very High | ~22% PA denial rate; rep time diverted |

### 3.2 Unmet Needs — Patient Level

| Unmet Need | % of Patients Reporting | Source |
|-----------|------------------------|--------|
| "I don't know why my heart failure medications were changed" | 62% | Entresto Patient Survey 2023 |
| Difficulty affording HF medications (out-of-pocket burden) | 44% | CHAMP-HF Registry patient survey |
| Missed doses due to side effects (dizziness, fatigue) | 31% | Specialty pharmacy adherence data |
| Fear of medication changes after feeling "stable" | 58% | Qualitative research 2023 |
| Lack of understanding of HF progression risk | 71% | Health literacy assessment |
| Poor access to HF specialist (rural/underserved) | 38% | AHA access equity study |

### 4.2 Adherence Driver Analysis (Multivariate Regression, Internal Data 2023)

**Positive predictors of 12-month persistence:**
1. Specialty pharmacy enrollment (OR 1.82; p<0.001)
2. Entresto Together PAP enrollment (OR 2.14; p<0.001)
3. Initial 90-day supply (vs. 30-day) (OR 1.61; p<0.01)
4. Active HF clinic follow-up (OR 1.73; p<0.001)
5. HCP proactively discussed side effects at initiation (OR 1.44; p<0.05)

**Negative predictors:**
1. Age ≥75 (OR 0.71; p<0.05) — frailty/polypharmacy concerns
2. Medicaid coverage (OR 0.64; p<0.001) — cost barriers
3. No HF specialist involvement (OR 0.68; p<0.001)
4. Rural geography (OR 0.73; p<0.05)
5. Prior hypotension event within 3 months of initiation (OR 0.52; p<0.001)

## 5. Prescriber Behavior Insights

### 5.1 Key Prescribing Decision Drivers (HCP Survey, N=412 US Cardiologists/PCPs, Q3 2023)

**For cardiologists who prescribe Entresto:**
1. "Guideline-driven decision" — 78% cite ACC/AHA 2022 Class I recommendation
2. "Clinical trial evidence from PARADIGM-HF" — 71% cite as primary evidence
3. "Superior outcomes vs. ACE-I" — 64% cite CV death reduction
4. "Fewer HF hospitalizations = fewer readmissions I have to manage" — 58%
5. "Patient with rising NT-proBNP despite ACE-I" — 51% as trigger event

**For cardiologists who do NOT prescribe Entresto:**
1. "Patient already stable on lisinopril" — 67%
2. "Cost/insurance concerns" — 54%
3. "Worried about hypotension, especially in elderly" — 49%
4. "The 36-hour washout makes the switch inconvenient" — 41%
5. "I refer HF patients to specialists for medication optimization" — 38%

## 6. Multicultural Patient Insights

### 6.1 Black Patients with HFrEF

- Higher HF incidence at younger age vs. White patients (median diagnosis age 58 vs. 67)
- Higher LVEF depression at diagnosis; higher rehospitalization rates
- Historical underrepresentation in PARADIGM-HF (only ~5% Black patients); some hesitancy among physicians regarding generalizability
- ARNI adoption in Black HFrEF patients estimated at 16% — below overall average
- Action: Targeted physician education on PARADIGM-HF racial subgroup data; multicultural patient education materials; community health partnerships

### 6.2 Hispanic Patients

- Growing HF burden; DM co-prevalence higher (57% vs. 43% overall)
- Language barriers affecting medication counseling
- Action: Spanish-language patient materials; bilingual HCP education; telehealth access programs

### 6.3 Women with HF

- PARAGON-HF: Women with HFpEF showed HR 0.73 (0.59–0.90) — significant benefit supporting label expansion
- HFrEF: PARADIGM-HF subgroup HR 0.78 (0.64–0.95) — consistent benefit
- Action: Women-specific HF messaging; PARAGON-HF gender subgroup data for HFmrEF/HFpEF initiations
