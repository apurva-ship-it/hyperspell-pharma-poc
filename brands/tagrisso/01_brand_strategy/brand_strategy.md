## 1. BRAND VISION

Tagrisso aspires to be the standard of care at every stage of EGFR-mutant NSCLC — from resection to relapse — redefining long-term survival expectations for the most molecularly defined patient population in lung cancer. The vision is to eradicate EGFR-mutant NSCLC as a death sentence: through adjuvant cure-intent therapy, first-line metastatic control, and biomarker-guided precision at disease progression. As the first-in-class third-generation EGFR TKI with proven OS benefit, CNS penetration superiority, and an expanding adjuvant indication, Tagrisso will define precision oncology for the next decade.

---

## 2. BRAND POSITIONING STATEMENT

**For oncologists treating patients with EGFR-mutant NSCLC** — whether in the resected early-stage or metastatic setting — **Tagrisso (osimertinib)** is the **only third-generation EGFR TKI** that delivers **proven overall survival benefit, superior CNS protection, and a tolerable safety profile** across all lines and stages, **without the skin and GI toxicity burden of first- and second-generation EGFR inhibitors** — enabling physicians to offer patients not just longer remissions, but meaningfully longer lives.

## 3. STRATEGIC IMPERATIVES (2026–2028)

### SI-1: Fortify the First-Line Metastatic Standard of Care
- Maintain Tagrisso as the unambiguous first-line standard of care for EGFR Ex19del/L858R metastatic NSCLC against lazertinib+amivantamab (MARIPOSA), aumolertinib, and other 3rd-gen TKIs
- Leverage FLAURA OS data (HR 0.80; mOS 38.6 vs 31.8 months) — only approved agent with mature OS survival data at first-line
- Defend against MARIPOSA (HR 0.70) through tolerability and OS narrative

### SI-2: Maximize the Adjuvant Opportunity — Curative Intent
- Drive adjuvant Tagrisso adoption to standard of care in ALL resected Stage IB–IIIA EGFR-mutant NSCLC (ADAURA)
- Educate thoracic surgeons, surgical oncologists, and oncology teams on ADAURA 5-year DFS (65.8% vs 26.2%; HR 0.23) and OS (HR 0.49) data
- Build biomarker testing (EGFR molecular testing post-resection) into surgical oncology pathways

### SI-3: Own the CNS-Protection Narrative
- Establish Tagrisso as the standard of care for EGFR-mutant patients with brain metastases — CNS PFS not reached vs 11.7 months in FLAURA; AURA3 CNS ORR 70%
- Partner with neuro-oncology to position Tagrisso as the EGFR agent that postpones/prevents brain radiation

### SI-4: Defend Against Emerging Combination Regimens
- Address MARIPOSA and FLAURA2 data head-on: FLAURA2 shows PFS benefit with chemotherapy combination (25.5 vs 16.7 months; HR 0.62) but at cost of significantly increased toxicity
- Develop clinical guidance on patient selection for combination vs Tagrisso monotherapy
- Highlight Tagrisso monotherapy QoL advantage: FLAURA patient-reported outcomes show superior QoL vs platinum doublet comparators

### SI-5: Establish the Biomarker Ecosystem
- Drive universal EGFR testing at diagnosis (tissue NGS + liquid biopsy ctDNA) and at disease progression
- Partner with Foundation Medicine, Guardant Health, Roche cobas — maximize platform-agnostic testing adoption
- Invest in ctDNA monitoring for MRD detection post-adjuvant treatment; support minimal residual disease research

## 4. SWOT ANALYSIS

### STRENGTHS
| Factor | Detail |
|---|---|
| Proven OS in 1L | FLAURA: HR 0.80; 38.6 vs 31.8 month median OS — only 1L EGFR TKI with positive OS |
| CNS penetration | CSF/plasma ratio ~2.5%; superior to all earlier-gen TKIs; CNS PFS not reached (FLAURA) |
| Adjuvant indication | ADAURA: 5yr DFS HR 0.23; OS HR 0.49 — transformational curative-intent indication |
| Tolerability profile | WT-EGFR sparing = less rash, less diarrhea vs erlotinib/afatinib; no BBW |
| T790M resistance coverage | Only approved 2L agent after 1st/2nd gen EGFR TKI progression + T790M mutation |
| Revenue strength | $5.8B (2023); growing adjuvant and 1L indication |
| Brand loyalty | High HCP loyalty in oncology; Tagrisso "the default" in many major cancer centers |

### WEAKNESSES
| Factor | Detail |
|---|---|
| PFS challenge from combinations | MARIPOSA (lazertinib+amivantamab) PFS 23.7 vs 16.6 (Tagrisso) — HR 0.70 |
| Resistance mechanisms | C797S, MET amplification, histologic transformation still occur; limited post-Tagrisso options |
| Biomarker dependency | Only benefits ~15% of NSCLC patients (EGFR-mutant); limited to precision segment |
| Adjuvant treatment duration | 3 years of daily oral therapy — adherence, financial toxicity concerns |
| Cardiotoxicity signal | QTc prolongation (rare); left ventricular dysfunction (<1%); monitoring required |

### OPPORTUNITIES
| Factor | Detail |
|---|---|
| Adjuvant underpenetration | Majority of resected EGFR-mutant patients globally not yet on adjuvant Tagrisso |
| Stage IIIA expansion | Earlier-stage patients; LAURA trial (Stage III unresectable) |
| Neoadjuvant indication | NeoADAURA data maturing — potential new pre-surgical indication |
| Developing markets | China, India, SE Asia — high EGFR mutation prevalence; biosimilar TKI gap |
| Biomarker testing | Liquid biopsy (ctDNA) expansion creates new patient identification opportunities |
| Combination regimen | FLAURA2 chemotherapy combo for high-risk/EGFR complex mutation patients |

### THREATS
| Factor | Detail |
|---|---|
| MARIPOSA combination | Lazertinib + amivantamab (J&J/Janssen): PFS HR 0.70; serious toxicity but growing share |
| Aumolertinib (China) | Haosen Pharmaceuticals — Chinese 3rd-gen TKI; significant China market risk |
| Post-Tagrisso resistance | No approved EGFR agent after Tagrisso progression; amivantamab (C797S), MET inhibitors competing |
| Biosimilar TKI competition | Gefitinib/erlotinib generics in EM markets; some patients inappropriately on 1st-gen agents |
| Patent cliff | Osimertinib core patent expiration horizon ~2030 in key markets; biosimilar risk |

## 6. CAMPAIGN PILLARS

### Pillar 1: "Beyond PFS — The OS Standard"
Tagrisso is the only first-line EGFR TKI with a proven OS advantage (FLAURA HR 0.80). Overall survival — not PFS — is the ultimate clinical endpoint, and Tagrisso has it. Counter PFS-only comparisons from MARIPOSA with OS narrative.

### Pillar 2: "From Surgery to Certainty"
Adjuvant Tagrisso transforms resected EGFR-mutant NSCLC from "watch and wait" to "act and protect." ADAURA 5-year DFS (65.8% vs 26.2%; HR 0.23) and OS HR 0.49 position Tagrisso as the first proven cure-intent biologic for early-stage EGFR NSCLC.

### Pillar 3: "Where Other Treatments Fail — The Brain"
Tagrisso's CNS penetration (CSF/plasma ~2.5%) is clinically validated. In a disease where brain metastases are common and devastating, Tagrisso's CNS protection — CNS PFS not reached in FLAURA — is a defining differentiator.

### Pillar 4: "Precision at Every Turn"
EGFR mutation testing (tissue NGS + ctDNA liquid biopsy) is the gateway to Tagrisso access. AstraZeneca will partner with diagnostic companies and advocate for universal testing protocols — broadening the Tagrisso-eligible patient population.

### Pillar 5: "Living Well, Living Longer"
Quality of life data from FLAURA and ADAURA show patients on Tagrisso maintain function, employment, and independence. Tolerability differentiates Tagrisso from chemotherapy-containing regimens (FLAURA2, MARIPOSA) in patient-centric communication.
