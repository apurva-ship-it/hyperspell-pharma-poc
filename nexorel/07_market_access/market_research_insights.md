# Market Research Insights Report — Nexorel Brand Tracker
**Wave:** Q1 2026 (Wave 5) | **Research Agency:** Synapse Health Analytics
**Sample:** n=312 oncologists (Tier 1–2 prescribers); n=48 payers; n=156 patients (current/past EGFR+ NSCLC therapy)
**Methodology:** Online quantitative survey + 24 qualitative in-depth interviews

---

## Executive Summary

Nexorel continues to gain traction in brand awareness and favorability metrics, with notable progress in CNS differentiation ownership. However, Tagrisso's entrenched position and Lazertinib/amivantamab's strong PFS narrative create a narrowing window for share acceleration. Key action areas: convert CNS awareness into prescribing conviction, address perceived "new drug uncertainty" among community oncologists, and close the payer access gap.

## Brand Funnel Performance

### HCP Metrics (Oncologists, n=312)

| Metric | Nexorel Q1 2026 | Nexorel Q3 2025 | Tagrisso Q1 2026 | Target Q4 2026 |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Unaided Awareness** | 72% | 61% | 98% | 85% |
| **Aided Awareness** | 94% | 88% | 100% | 97% |
| **Trial (ever prescribed)** | 48% | 39% | 96% | 62% |
| **Current Regular Use** | 31% | 22% | 78% | 45% |
| **Brand Preference (EGFR+ 1L)** | 18% | 12% | 64% | 28% |
| **Recommend to Colleagues** | 41% | 30% | 82% | 55% |

**Key Insight:** Awareness to trial conversion rate (94% → 48%) is strong, suggesting that HCPs who hear about Nexorel are curious enough to try. The gap is converting trialists to regular users (48% → 31%). This is a clinical conviction issue, not an awareness issue.

## Message Recall & Attribute Ownership

### Spontaneous Message Association (% mentioning without prompting)

| Message | Nexorel | Tagrisso | Laz+Ami |
|---|---|---|---|
| "Good for brain mets / CNS" | **61%** | 28% | 9% |
| "Well tolerated / low side effects" | **44%** | 31% | 8% |
| "Strong overall PFS" | 18% | 52% | **71%** |
| "Proven / trusted / established" | 9% | **76%** | 21% |
| "Convenient / once daily" | **38%** | 35% | 12% |
| "Good patient support program" | **29%** | 18% | 11% |

**Key Insight:** Nexorel owns "CNS" and is building "tolerability." These are the right attributes — but "proven/trusted" is overwhelmingly Tagrisso's territory. The path to share is not trying to out-trust Tagrisso; it is deepening ownership of CNS as a decision-driving attribute, not just an awareness attribute.

**Action Required:** Current CNS awareness (61%) is not translating to CNS-driven prescribing decisions. Only 31% of oncologists say CNS efficacy is "very important" in their 1L NSCLC decision. Need to elevate the clinical stakes of CNS disease — close to 35% of patients present with brain mets; this should be a routine, not exceptional, consideration.

## Prescribing Decision Drivers

### "What factors most influence your 1L EGFR+ NSCLC prescribing decision?" (Top 3, multi-select)

| Factor | % Selecting (Top 3) |
|---|---|
| Overall PFS/efficacy data | 84% |
| My own clinical experience with the drug | 71% |
| Payer coverage / formulary status | 58% |
| Side effect/tolerability profile | 47% |
| Peer recommendation | 39% |
| CNS activity/intracranial efficacy | **31%** |
| Patient preference | 22% |
| Cost to patient | 19% |
| Duration of market presence | 17% |

**Key Insight:** CNS activity ranks 6th in decision drivers — a significant gap given that Nexorel's primary differentiator is CNS efficacy. Oncologists intellectually know CNS matters but don't yet use it as a primary prescribing filter. Medical education priority: make "does this patient have CNS disease?" the first clinical question, not an afterthought.

## Barriers to Nexorel Adoption

### "What prevents you from prescribing Nexorel more often?" (Top barriers, qualitative themes)

**Barrier 1: "Tagrisso works and I know it" (62% of non-/low-prescribers)**
> "My patients on Tagrisso are doing well. I don't want to experiment with their treatment."

*Response Strategy:* Don't argue with their Tagrisso experience. Identify the CNS sub-population where the risk calculus is different. "For your patients without CNS involvement, Tagrisso is excellent. What about the 35% who already have brain mets?"

**Barrier 2: "Not enough long-term data" (51%)**
> "NEXUS-1 is one trial. I want to see more real-world data before I make it my go-to."

*Response Strategy:* NEXUS-RWE is the answer — accelerate publication of interim data. 18-month MPR and response rate data are compelling. Position the Phase III publication in NEJM as establishing the evidentiary standard.

**Barrier 3: "Insurance/step edit issues" (44%)**
> "My patients have been denied coverage. The prior auth process is exhausting for my staff."

*Response Strategy:* Market access pull-through is the #1 operational priority. In markets with step edits, MSLs and Sales Reps should co-visit to address both scientific and access barriers simultaneously. Navigator prior auth support must be proactively offered.

**Barrier 4: "I haven't had enough guidance on when to choose it" (38%)**
> "I'm not sure I always know which patient is the right fit. The messaging feels like it's for a specific sub-group."

*Response Strategy:* Simplify the selection algorithm. "If your patient has CNS metastases at baseline, Nexorel. If they progress on Tagrisso with CNS involvement, Nexorel. If their CNS is currently clear, both are excellent options — discuss your experience with Nexorel's tolerability advantage."

## Patient Insights

### n=156 current or recent EGFR+ NSCLC patients (74 on Nexorel, 62 on Tagrisso, 20 other)

**Treatment Satisfaction (1–10 scale)**
| Metric | Nexorel (n=74) | Tagrisso (n=62) |
|---|---|---|
| Overall satisfaction | 8.4 | 8.1 |
| Side effect burden | 8.7 | 7.6 |
| Ease of taking medication | 9.1 | 8.9 |
| Confidence in drug effectiveness | 8.2 | 8.7 |
| Support from manufacturer | 8.8 | 7.2 |

**Key Patient Quote — Nexorel:**
> "I feel like Aether actually cares about me as a person, not just a patient. Someone calls me regularly. I know who to call if I have a problem. With my last drug, I felt like I was on my own."

**Key Patient Quote — Tagrisso:**
> "I've been on this for 3 years. I trust it. I know what the side effects feel like. I'm not interested in switching to something new."

**Insight:** Nexorel patients score higher on support but lower on confidence in effectiveness — the "new drug uncertainty" barrier is real. As NEXUS-1 data ages and real-world evidence builds, this gap is expected to close. Navigator program is a genuine competitive advantage on patient support.

## Payer Research Summary (n=48 payers — Medical Directors, P&T committee members)

### Coverage Status
- 48%: Nexorel preferred with no restrictions
- 21%: Nexorel preferred after osimertinib failure (step edit)
- 19%: Nexorel covered, non-preferred
- 8%: Nexorel not covered / formulary exclusion
- 4%: Under review

**Payer Stated Requirements for Preferred Status (non-preferred payers):**
1. Real-world effectiveness data comparable to Tagrisso (71%)
2. Demonstrated cost-effectiveness analysis (54%)
3. Additional head-to-head OS data (48%)
4. Outcomes-based contract option (39%)

**Payer Insight:**
> "The Phase III data is good, but it's one trial. We've seen drugs that look great in RCTs underperform in the real world. Once you have 12–18 months of real-world data showing your trial results hold, we'll have a much easier conversation."

**Action:** Accelerate NEXUS-RWE interim analysis publication. Commission cost-effectiveness analysis (ICER submission planned Q3 2026). Propose outcomes-based contract to top 5 PBMs with CNS progression as primary endpoint.
