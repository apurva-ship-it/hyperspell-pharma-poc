## 1. Competitive Landscape Overview

The US DOAC market generates approximately $20B annually and is highly mature — Eliquis dominates with ~50% share. The competitive dynamic has shifted from DOAC vs. warfarin displacement to three simultaneous threats: (1) DOAC-to-DOAC switching driven by once-daily convenience (Xarelto), (2) IRA price negotiation compressing Eliquis net revenue, and (3) generic apixaban entry anticipated 2026–2028 which will fundamentally restructure the market.

**Trend:** Eliquis market share has been stable to slightly declining since 2023 as Xarelto's once-daily convenience messaging gains traction among busy PCPs.

## 2. Competitor Profiles

### 2.1 Rivaroxaban (Xarelto) — PRIMARY COMPETITOR

**Company:** Bayer (co-promotion with J&J/Janssen)
**Mechanism:** Direct Factor Xa inhibitor
**Dosing:**
- NVAF: 20mg QD with evening meal
- DVT/PE treatment: 15mg BID × 21 days, then 20mg QD
- Extended VTE prevention: 10mg QD (EINSTEIN-CHOICE)
- Post-surgical: 10mg QD

**Key Trial Data:**

| Trial | Population | Result vs. Warfarin |
|-------|-----------|---------------------|
| ROCKET-AF | NVAF | Stroke/SE: Non-inferior (HR 0.88 NS per-protocol); major bleed: No difference vs. warfarin; **ICH HR 0.67** |
| EINSTEIN-DVT/PE | VTE | Non-inferior for recurrent VTE; major bleed similar to warfarin |
| EINSTEIN-CHOICE | Extended VTE | Rivaroxaban 10mg superior to aspirin (72% RRR); similar to 20mg |
| MAGELLAN | Medical VTE prophylaxis | Positive; extended rivaroxaban benefit |
| COMMANDER-HF | HF (no AF) | No benefit vs. placebo — negative trial |

**Xarelto Key Differentiators:**
- **Once-daily dosing (NVAF, extended VTE)** — primary competitive advantage over Eliquis BID
- Broad label: includes NVAF, VTE, post-surgical, peripheral artery disease (VOYAGER PAD), CAD (ATLAS ACS), medical VTE prophylaxis (MAGELLAN)
- Broadest indication set of any DOAC
- Rivaroxaban generic in EU (2024) — Bayer patent cliff in US expected 2024–2025

**Xarelto Vulnerabilities:**
- ROCKET-AF: Higher ICH vs. Eliquis (HR 0.67 for Xarelto vs. warfarin; HR 0.42 for Eliquis — still higher absolute risk)
- No mortality benefit vs. warfarin (ROCKET-AF)
- Must be taken with food for absorption (20mg dose) — adds administration constraint
- Renal-dependent elimination → greater renal impairment concern
- No superiority to warfarin on stroke in ROCKET-AF (only non-inferiority)

### Battle Card — Eliquis vs. Xarelto (NVAF)

| Dimension | Eliquis (Apixaban) | Xarelto (Rivaroxaban) | Eliquis Advantage |
|-----------|-------------------|----------------------|-----------------|
| Stroke reduction vs. warfarin | HR 0.79 (superior) | HR 0.88 (non-inferior) | YES — Eliquis superior on efficacy |

| Reversal | Andexxa | Andexxa | Tied |

### 2.2 Dabigatran (Pradaxa) — Secondary Competitor (Declining)

**Company:** Boehringer Ingelheim
**Mechanism:** Direct thrombin inhibitor (IIa)
**Dosing (NVAF):** 150mg BID (standard); 75mg BID (CrCl 15–30)
**Key Trial:** RE-LY (NVAF): Dabigatran 150mg superior on stroke (HR 0.66); no ICH benefit vs. warfarin? No — ICH HR 0.40 — similar to apixaban

**Competitive Context:**
- **Generic dabigatran available in US (2022):** Teva launched — significant price disruption for dabigatran market
- Pradaxa lost majority of brand market to generic within 12 months of generic entry (70%+ erosion)
- Dabigatran still has GI bleed disadvantage (150mg dose increases GI bleed vs. warfarin)
- Specific reversal agent: Praxbind (idarucizumab) — faster reversal vs. Andexxa; fully available
- Not recommended if CrCl <30 (renal dependence); AF + severe CKD not appropriate
- Eliquis advantage: Lower GI bleed; greater renal flexibility; superior stroke efficacy

**Prescribing Trend:** Dabigatran TRx declining ~15% YoY; minimal competitive threat. Pradaxa legacy brand retreating.

### 2.3 Edoxaban (Savaysa) — Minor Competitor

**Company:** Daiichi Sankyo
**Mechanism:** Direct Factor Xa inhibitor
**Key Trial:** ENGAGE AF-TIMI 48 (NVAF): Edoxaban 60mg non-inferior on stroke; ICH HR 0.47; major bleed HR 0.80
**Limitation:** Requires preceding parenteral therapy for VTE treatment (unlike apixaban oral-only); limited uptake
**Market Position:** Niche; primarily maintained by loyalty pockets; ~4% share

---

### 2.4 Warfarin — Remaining Traditional Competitor

**Current Use in US (2025):** ~7% of NVAF patients; ~9% of VTE patients
**Who remains on warfarin:**
- Patients with mechanical heart valves (DOAC not indicated)
- Patients with antiphospholipid syndrome (warfarin superior per ASTRO-APS type data)
- Patients in access-limited settings (low-cost; no monitoring cost barriers for some)
- Patient preference / physician inertia
- Valvular AF (mitral stenosis)

**Conversion Opportunity:** AHA/ACC guidelines recommend DOACs over warfarin for NVAF in all eligible patients. Approximately 2.5 million AF patients in US remain on warfarin — Eliquis addressable market opportunity for conversion.

**Eliquis vs. Warfarin messaging:** ARISTOTLE is the conversion cornerstone — superior stroke prevention, 58% ICH reduction, no monitoring required.

### 2.5 Generic Apixaban — Critical Emerging Threat (2026–2028)

**Status:** Teva, Sandoz, Mylan (Viatris), Sun Pharma, and others have ANDAs pending
**Expected Launch Window:** Earliest 2026 (patent settlements possible); broad launch 2027–2028
**Impact Assessment:**

| Scenario | Probability | Branded Eliquis Volume Impact |
|---------|-------------|------------------------------|
| Generic launch 2026 (settlement or patent loss) | 35% | 60–70% volume erosion within 18 months |
| Generic launch 2027 (patent litigation) | 45% | 60–70% erosion within 12 months post-launch |
| Generic launch 2028+ (sustained litigation) | 20% | Delayed but same ultimate erosion |

**Brand Defense Strategies:**
1. Formulary positioning before LOE — secure preferred tier for branded Eliquis for complex patients
2. "Authorized generic" consideration (BMS/Pfizer capturing generic revenue)
3. Patient loyalty programs — persistence before LOE reduces switching
4. Clinical education: Branded vs. generic bioequivalence for narrow therapeutic index discussion (FDA 90% BE standard; anticoagulants require careful consideration)
5. Payer outcomes contracts: Tie rebates to outcomes-based metrics

## 3. Emerging Threats — Factor XI Inhibitors (Next-Generation Anticoagulants)

### 3.1 Overview
Factor XI (FXIa) inhibitors represent a potentially superior safety profile vs. current anticoagulants by targeting the amplification arm of coagulation that contributes to thrombus propagation but plays less role in hemostasis — theoretically enabling stroke prevention without bleeding risk.

| Drug | Company | Mechanism | Stage | Key Data |
|------|---------|-----------|-------|---------|
| Abelacimab | Anthos/Daiichi | Anti-FXI antibody | Phase 3 (LILAC-AF) | Phase 2: Zero ICH; 80% lower bleeds vs. rivaroxaban |
| Milvexian | BMS/J&J | Oral FXIa inhibitor | Phase 3 (LIBREXIA-AF) | Phase 2: Dose-dependent VTE prevention; bleeding signal low |
| Osocimab | Bayer | Anti-FXI antibody | Phase 2/3 | Positive pilot data |
| Fesomersen | Ionis | FXI antisense | Phase 3 | VTE prevention: 44% risk reduction |

**Strategic Implication for Eliquis:**
- If Factor XI inhibitors demonstrate superiority in NVAF stroke prevention with near-zero bleeding, this could be a class-level disruption
- Timeline: Phase 3 results expected 2026–2027; approval ~2028–2029
- BMS is co-developing milvexian — potential internal hedge against Eliquis LOE

## 5. IRA Drug Price Negotiation Impact

**Eliquis selected for IRA negotiation — Price effective January 1, 2026**

**Estimated Maximum Fair Price (MFP):** ~$231/month (vs. current WAC ~$619/month for 5mg BID 30-day supply)
**Applies to:** Medicare Part D only (commercial market unaffected by IRA MFP)
**Impact:**

| Metric | Pre-IRA (2025) | Post-IRA (2026) | Change |
|--------|--------------|-----------------|--------|
| Medicare MFP/month | ~$619 WAC | ~$231 MFP | -63% list price |
| Medicare net revenue | ~$4.2B (US) | ~$2.1B est. | -50% |
| Commercial revenue | ~$2.9B | ~$3.1B est. | +7% (formulary wins) |
| Total US Eliquis revenue | ~$7.1B | ~$5.2B est. | -27% |

**Strategic Upside of IRA:** Lower Medicare price improves formulary access — fewer step therapy requirements. Plans that previously imposed high PA burden for Eliquis may reduce restrictions when the MFP is in effect. Net patient access improvement expected.

## 6. Competitive Messages by Situation

**Xarelto once-daily objection:**
"Xarelto's once-daily dosing is appealing — but dosing frequency should be weighed against clinical outcomes. In ARISTOTLE, Eliquis demonstrated superior stroke prevention AND 31% lower major bleeds AND 58% lower intracranial hemorrhage vs. warfarin. Xarelto's ROCKET-AF showed non-inferiority on stroke and no bleeding reduction vs. warfarin. For your atrial fibrillation patient, the most feared outcome is a brain bleed — and Eliquis offers the most protection."

**Generic/cost objection:**
"Generics will be part of the landscape. For your complex patient — elderly with prior GI bleed, fall risk, or CKD — bioequivalence data on a narrow therapeutic index drug matters. The 90% BE window for generics means real variability is possible. Branded Eliquis remains the most studied apixaban formulation with the most real-world data."
