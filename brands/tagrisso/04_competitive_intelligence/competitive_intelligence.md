## 1. COMPETITIVE LANDSCAPE OVERVIEW

Tagrisso operates in the precision oncology space for **EGFR-mutant NSCLC** — representing ~15% of all NSCLC patients in Western populations and ~35–50% in East Asian populations. While the patient population is molecularly defined, competition is intensifying across all lines with the emergence of combination regimens (FLAURA2, MARIPOSA), novel 3rd-gen TKIs in Asian markets (aumolertinib), and next-generation targeted combinations addressing post-Tagrisso resistance.

### 1.1 Competitive Threat Matrix

| Competitor | Drug | Setting | Threat Level | Timeline |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Johnson & Johnson | Lazertinib + Amivantamab (MARIPOSA) | 1L metastatic | **HIGH** | Already launched; growing share |
| Haosen/InventisBio | Aumolertinib | 1L metastatic (China/Global) | HIGH (China), Medium (US/EU) | China dominant; seeking global approval |
| Pfizer | Lorlatinib (ALK; limited overlap) | EGFR atypical (exon 20) | LOW | Different EGFR mutation type |
| Multiple | Erlotinib/Gefitinib generics | 1L in EM markets | Medium (EM only) | Eroding patients who should receive Tagrisso |
| Various | Post-Tagrisso agents (amivantamab, savolitinib, datopotamab-deruxtecan) | 2L post-Tagrisso | Medium | Pipeline competition at resistance |
| AstraZeneca itself | FLAURA2 regimen (Tagrisso + chemo) | Specific 1L subpopulations | Internal competition | Cannibalization of mono |

## 2. COMPETITOR PROFILES

### 2.1 Lazertinib + Amivantamab (RYBREVANT + LAZCLUZE) — Johnson & Johnson
**Products**: 
- Lazertinib (Lazcluze): 3rd-gen EGFR TKI (like osimertinib); CNS penetration comparable
- Amivantamab (Rybrevant): EGFR-MET bispecific antibody (IV); MET pathway coverage adds to EGFR blockade

**Approved Setting**: FDA approved August 2024: 1L metastatic NSCLC (EGFR Ex19del/L858R) — first combination challenge to Tagrisso 1L

**Key Trial — MARIPOSA (Phase 3)**:
| Endpoint | Lazertinib+Amivantamab | Osimertinib (Tagrisso) | HR | p-value |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **PFS (primary)** | **23.7 months** | **16.6 months** | **0.70** | **<0.001** |
| ORR | 86% | 85% | — | NS |
| CNS ORR | 73% | 71% | — | NS |
| OS | Immature | Immature | — | Pending |

**Safety Comparison — MARIPOSA**:
| AE | Lazertinib+Amivantamab | Osimertinib |
|---|---|---|
| Grade ≥3 AEs | **75%** | **27%** |
| Infusion-related reactions | 67% (primarily Grade 1–2; Grade 3+: 3%) | N/A (oral) |
| Venous thromboembolic events | 11% | <1% |
| Neutropenia | 26% | <1% |
| Treatment discontinuation | 10% | 3% |
| Dose reductions | 53% | 10% |

**Dosing**: Lazertinib 240mg QD (oral) + Amivantamab IV (weight-based; ~1,400–1,750mg) Weekly × 4, then Q2W → Q4W

**Commercialization challenges for J&J**:
- IV amivantamab requires infusion center access; adds logistical burden
- RYBREVANT premedication required (antihistamine, corticosteroid, acetaminophen) — increases visit burden
- VTE rate 11% is clinically meaningful; requires prophylactic anticoagulation discussion
- OS data pending — until OS benefit confirmed, many oncologists hesitant to shift from Tagrisso monotherapy
- Subcutaneous amivantamab formulation (amivantamab-vmjw SC) in development to address IV burden

**Battle Card — vs MARIPOSA**:
1. "MARIPOSA shows a PFS advantage — but with 75% Grade ≥3 toxicity vs 27% with Tagrisso. That's a real quality-of-life cost."
2. "FLAURA OS: HR 0.80 in favor of Tagrisso — mature survival data. MARIPOSA OS is still immature. Are you willing to risk your patient's tolerability and quality of life for an unproven survival benefit?"
3. "VTE rate 11% — every patient needs anticoagulation discussion; IV infusion every 2–4 weeks for life."
4. "For the 30–40% of patients with brain metastases — Tagrisso CNS PFS not reached in FLAURA. MARIPOSA CNS ORR 73% vs Tagrisso 71% — essentially equivalent CNS protection at far less systemic toxicity."

### 2.2 Aumolertinib (Aumoline/Furmonertinib) — Haosen/InventisBio
**Setting**: Primarily China; seeking EU/US registration
**Mechanism**: 3rd-gen EGFR TKI (similar to osimertinib); CNS penetration data published

**Key Trial — AENEAS (Phase 3, China)**:
| Endpoint | Aumolertinib 110mg QD | Gefitinib 250mg QD | HR |
|---|---|---|---|
| PFS (primary) | 19.3 months | 9.9 months | 0.46 |
| ORR | 73.8% | 72.1% | — |
| CNS PFS | NR | 10.9 months | 0.32 |

**Threat Assessment**:
- China market: Aumolertinib approved in China; represents significant competition to Tagrisso in Chinese market (~40% of global EGFR NSCLC market)
- US/EU: Limited Phase 3 data outside Asia; would need comprehensive filing for approval
- Head-to-head vs Tagrisso: No direct comparison; numerically similar PFS in respective 1L trials vs 1st-gen TKIs

### 2.3 First- and Second-Generation EGFR TKIs (Gefitinib, Erlotinib, Afatinib, Dacomitinib)
- Generic gefitinib/erlotinib available globally; significant price pressure in EM markets
- **Key issue**: Inappropriate use of 1st/2nd gen TKIs in T790M-naive 1L settings deprives patients of superior Tagrisso outcomes
- Afatinib (Gilotrif/Giotrif, Boehringer Ingelheim): 2nd-gen; approved 1L Ex19del/L858R; ExHALE data show OS benefit in Ex19del vs erlotinib but no head-to-head vs osimertinib
- Dacomitinib (Vizimpro, Pfizer): 2nd-gen; ARCHER 1050 shows OS benefit vs gefitinib but inferior PFS to osimertinib

### 2.4 FLAURA2 Regimen (Internal Competition)
**Concern**: FLAURA2 (osimertinib + platinum-pemetrexed) shows PFS benefit (25.5 vs 16.7 months; HR 0.62) but at cost of significantly higher toxicity (Grade ≥3: 64% vs 27%).

**Patient Selection for FLAURA2 vs Tagrisso Monotherapy**:
| Feature | Favor FLAURA2 (Combo) | Favor Tagrisso Monotherapy |
|---|---|---|
| EGFR mutation type | Complex/atypical EGFR | Classic Ex19del/L858R |
| Co-mutations | STK11, KEAP1, TP53 co-mutations (higher risk) | No co-mutations (lower risk) |
| Disease burden | High disease burden; visceral crisis | Moderate/controlled disease |
| Patient performance status | PS 0 | PS 1–2 |
| CNS disease | Stable CNS mets (after RT) | Active CNS mets (Tagrisso alone may be preferred) |
| Patient preference | Willing to accept toxicity for PFS gain | Quality of life priority |

## 3. HEAD-TO-HEAD TRIAL CONTEXT

### 3.1 FLAURA — Tagrisso vs SoC 1st/2nd Gen TKI (Definitive Head-to-Head)
- Tagrisso PFS: 18.9 vs 10.2 months (HR 0.46; p<0.001) — establishes 1L superiority
- OS: 38.6 vs 31.8 months (HR 0.80; p=0.046) — ONLY 1L EGFR TKI with OS benefit
- Tagrisso rash Grade 3+: 1% vs SOC 10%; demonstrates meaningful tolerability advantage

### 3.2 MARIPOSA — Lazertinib+Amivantamab vs Tagrisso (Key Competitive Context)
- PFS favors combo (HR 0.70) but OS still immature
- No currently approved 1L agent has demonstrated superior OS to Tagrisso
- Tagrisso remains NCCN Category 1, preferred agent for 1L

### 3.3 Network Meta-Analysis Context (No Direct Tagrisso vs Aumolertinib)
Multiple published NMAs comparing osimertinib vs all 3rd-gen TKIs:
- Osimertinib consistently ranks among the best in PFS (HR vs gefitinib: 0.46)
- OS data: only osimertinib has published positive Phase 3 OS data (FLAURA)

## 5. BATTLE CARDS — RAPID REFERENCE

### 5.1 vs MARIPOSA (Lazertinib + Amivantamab) — "The OS + QoL Pivot"

**Objection**: "MARIPOSA showed longer PFS — shouldn't that change my 1L decision?"

**Response**:
1. "The 7-month PFS advantage is real — let's not dismiss it. But here's the context: the toxicity cost is substantial. Grade ≥3 AEs were 75% vs 27% with Tagrisso. One in nine patients discontinued the combination."
2. "OS data for MARIPOSA are still immature. Tagrisso is the only 1L EGFR TKI with a positive, mature OS: 38.6 months vs 31.8 months, HR 0.80."
3. "For your patient with excellent performance status, low disease burden, and a desire to maintain quality of life — Tagrisso monotherapy remains the standard."
4. "For higher-risk patients — STK11 co-mutations, high disease burden — FLAURA2 (Tagrisso + platinum) or the combination might be justified. But that's a specific subset, not everyone."

### 5.2 vs 1st/2nd Gen TKI Inertia — "The Upgrade Argument"

**Objection**: "My patient is doing well on gefitinib — why switch to Tagrisso?"

**Response**:
1. "Gefitinib and erlotinib will fail approximately 100% of patients — resistance develops at median 9–10 months. T790M mutation occurs in ~50% of those patients at progression."
2. "In FLAURA, starting with Tagrisso first-line gave patients a median OS of 38.6 months vs 31.8 months for gefitinib — that's a 6.8-month OS benefit right out of the gate."
3. "And CNS control: Tagrisso CNS PFS not reached in FLAURA — brain metastases are a major source of morbidity. Why wait for the T790M test at progression when you can protect the brain upfront?"
4. "NCCN Category 1 for Tagrisso in 1L — if your patient is treatment-naive and EGFR-positive, the guideline-supported choice is Tagrisso."
