## 1. Revenue Performance — Global and US

### Revenue Trend (Historical and Projected)

| Year | Global Net Revenue | US Net Revenue | YoY Change (US) |
|------|--------------------|----------------|----------------|
| 2022 | $21.2B (peak) | $16.8B | — |
| 2023 | $14.4B | $9.2B | -45.2% (biosimilar entry Jan 2023) |
| 2024 | ~$12.1B | ~$6.8B | -26.1% |
| 2025 (est.) | ~$9.8B | ~$4.2B | -38.2% |
| Q1 2026 Actual | $2.1B | $0.94B | -23.7% vs. Q1 2025 |
| FY2026 Guidance | $8.0–8.8B | $3.6–4.0B | -10 to -14% YoY |

**Revenue Notes**:
- US revenue erosion driven primarily by biosimilar volume loss (market share erosion) AND gross-to-net expansion (deeper rebates to retain remaining formulary positions)
- Ex-US markets declining more slowly; EU down ~12% YoY (biosimilar-first policies now fully implemented in UK, Germany, France); Japan more stable (-6% YoY)
- Non-US markets now represent >55% of total HUMIRA revenue (historically US was 80%)
- AbbVie total immunology revenue (HUMIRA + SKYRIZI + RINVOQ): $22.4B in 2025, growing vs. HUMIRA peak due to SKYRIZI/RINVOQ growth offsetting HUMIRA decline

### HCP NPS (n=400, quarterly, US immunology specialists)

| Quarter | Promoters | Passives | Detractors | NPS Score |
|---------|----------|---------|-----------|-----------|
| Q2 2025 | 57% | 24% | 19% | 38 |

| Q4 2025 | 55% | 26% | 19% | 36 |
| Q1 2026 | 54% | 27% | 19% | 35 |
| FY2026 Target | — | — | — | 45 |

**NPS Decline Analysis**: HUMIRA NPS has trended downward since biosimilar entry; primary detractor drivers:
1. Formulary access restrictions creating friction (52% of detractors)
2. Perception that HUMIRA is "old news" vs. SKYRIZI/RINVOQ (31%)
3. PA burden for new starts (27%)
4. Lower efficacy in IBD vs. newer biologics acknowledged (22%)

**Promoter Drivers** (for retention strategy):
1. HS: HUMIRA-only space; ophthalmology uveitis specialists highly loyal
2. Stable long-term RA/IBD patients ("set it and forget it" prescribers)
3. myAbbVie Assist program experience highly positive
