## 3. Prior Authorization (PA) Criteria and Strategy

### 3.1 Typical Commercial PA Criteria for Ozempic

**Standard Requirements (most commercial plans):**
1. Diagnosis of Type 2 Diabetes Mellitus (ICD-10: E11.xx)
2. HbA1c ≥7.0% (within 3 months)
3. Current or previous use of metformin (unless contraindicated with documentation)
4. BMI documentation (most plans require ≥25 kg/m²)
5. Age ≥18 years

**CV Indication Pathway (faster PA for T2D+CVD):**
- Diagnosis of established ASCVD (ICD-10: I25.xx, I21.xx, I63.xx, etc.) significantly reduces PA burden at most plans
- Express Scripts: CV indication patients receive coverage determination within 24 hours vs. standard 5–7 business days
- Some plans waive step therapy for patients with documented CVD

**Common PA Rejection Reasons:**
- Missing HbA1c documentation (28% of rejections)
- Metformin step not documented (22%)
- Incorrect ICD-10 coding (18%)
- Missing BMI (15%)
- Incomplete provider information (10%)

### 3.2 PA Navigator Program (Novo Nordisk)

**Program Overview:** Dedicated PA support line staffed by trained clinical coordinators
- Phone: 1-800-NOVO-555
- Turnaround: Same-day PA submission support; 48-hour follow-up
- Success rate: 84% initial approval; 91% including first-level appeal
- Volume: ~85,000 PA submissions/month (2025 est.)

**PA Navigator Field Support:**
- Market access liaisons embedded with high-volume practices
- EHR-integrated PA tools (Epic, Cerner, Athena integration)
- Prior auth letter templates available by payer
- Appeal support with clinical evidence packages (SUSTAIN-6 cite-ready)

## 4. Step Therapy Requirements and Navigation

### 4.1 Common Step Therapy Sequences

**Tier 1 (Most Restrictive):** Commercial plans requiring 3-step therapy
1. Metformin (≥90-day trial)
2. SGLT2 inhibitor OR sulfonylurea (≥60-day trial)
3. DPP-4 inhibitor OR second agent
→ Then Ozempic approved

**Tier 2 (Moderate):** Plans requiring 1-step
1. Metformin (≥90-day trial or documented contraindication)
→ Then Ozempic approved

**Tier 3 (CV Access):** Patients with established CVD
- Many plans waive step therapy for patients with T2D + ASCVD
- Requires documentation of ASCVD diagnosis code

**Step Therapy Override Criteria:**
- Prior GI intolerance to metformin (must be documented in chart)
- Renal impairment preventing metformin use (eGFR <30)
- Documented failure of required step therapy agents
- CV indication (bypasses step therapy at many plans)

### 4.2 State Step Therapy Override Laws
As of 2025, 31 states have enacted step therapy reform legislation allowing providers to request expedited overrides. Key states: NY, CA, TX, FL, IL, PA, OH.

## 6. Patient Assistance Programs

### 6.1 Ozempic Savings Offer (Commercial)
- **Eligibility:** Commercially insured patients with Ozempic coverage
- **Benefit:** Pay as little as $25/month co-pay reduction (max $150/month savings)
- **Enrollment:** NovoNordisk.com/OzempicSavings; phone activation
- **Restrictions:** Not valid for Medicare, Medicaid, or federal programs
- **Active cardholders (2025 est.):** ~1.4 million

### 6.2 NovoCare Patient Assistance Program
- **Eligibility:** Uninsured or underinsured patients; income-based (≤400% FPL)
- **Benefit:** Ozempic at no cost or reduced cost; determined by income review
- **Annual application** with income verification
- **Medicare gap patients:** Eligible for NovoCare bridge supply

### 6.3 $99/Month Uninsured Program
- **Eligibility:** No health insurance for Ozempic; not Medicare/Medicaid enrolled
- **Benefit:** Fixed price of $99/month regardless of WAC; no income requirement
- **Process:** Prescription + savings card; no online enrollment required
- **Usage (2025):** ~180,000 patients/month

### 6.4 Ozempic for Medicare Patients — Limited Options
- No co-pay card applicable
- Extra Help / LIS program: May qualify for reduced Part D cost-sharing
- State Pharmaceutical Assistance Programs (SPAPs): Available in 9 states
- NovoCare: Income-based free drug; Medicare patients with income ≤300% FPL may qualify
