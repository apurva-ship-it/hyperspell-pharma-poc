# Jardiance (empagliflozin) — Brand Intelligence

**Generic Name:** Empagliflozin  
**Brand:** Jardiance  
**Company:** Boehringer Ingelheim + Eli Lilly (co-development/co-promotion)  
**Drug Class:** Sodium-glucose cotransporter 2 (SGLT2) inhibitor  
**Mechanism of Action:** Selectively inhibits SGLT2 in the proximal renal tubule → blocks renal glucose reabsorption → increased urinary glucose excretion (glycosuria) → lowers blood glucose. Secondary effects: osmotic diuresis (volume reduction), natriuresis, reduced preload/afterload, decreased uric acid, hemodynamic and metabolic benefits independent of glycemic effect.  
**FDA Approval:** August 2014 (T2D glycemic control); December 2016 (CV mortality reduction); August 2021 (HFrEF); February 2022 (HFpEF — all HF EF ranges); July 2023 (CKD)  
**Formulation:** 10 mg and 25 mg oral tablets; once daily  

---

## Approved Indications (US)

1. **Type 2 Diabetes (T2D):** Glycemic control as adjunct to diet and exercise
2. **Cardiovascular Mortality Reduction (2016):** Reduce risk of CV death in adults with T2D and established CVD
3. **Heart Failure with Reduced EF (HFrEF, 2021):** Reduce risk of CV death and hospitalization for HF in adults with HFrEF
4. **Heart Failure with Preserved EF (HFpEF, 2022):** Reduce risk of CV death and worsening HF in adults with symptomatic chronic HF (EMPEROR-Preserved)
5. **Chronic Kidney Disease (CKD, 2023):** Reduce risk of sustained eGFR decline, ESRD, CV death, and hospitalization for HF in adults with CKD at risk of progression

## Clinical Efficacy

### EMPA-REG OUTCOME (CV outcomes in T2D — landmark trial, 2015)
- **Design:** Empagliflozin 10 mg or 25 mg vs placebo, 7,020 patients with T2D + established CVD; median follow-up 3.1 years
- **Primary endpoint (3-point MACE):** CV death, non-fatal MI, non-fatal stroke
- **3-P MACE:** 10.5% vs 12.1% (**HR 0.86, p=0.04**) — statistically significant
- **CV death:** **3.7% vs 5.9%** (**HR 0.62; 38% relative risk reduction**) — dramatic
- **HF hospitalization:** **2.7% vs 4.1%** (**HR 0.65; 35% reduction**)
- **All-cause mortality:** 5.7% vs 8.3% (**HR 0.68; 32% reduction**)
- **Renal composite (new macroalbuminuria, creatinine doubling, ESRD, renal death):** HR **0.61** — 39% reduction
- **Note:** Stroke and MI: No significant benefit (HR ~1.0); benefit driven primarily by CV death and HF
- First glucose-lowering drug to demonstrate CV mortality reduction (landmark paradigm shift)

### EMPEROR-Reduced (HFrEF — 2020)
- **Design:** Empagliflozin 10 mg vs placebo in 3,730 HFrEF patients (LVEF ≤40%), majority with T2D (~50%) and without T2D (~50%)
- **Primary composite:** CV death or hospitalization for HF
- **Results:** **19.4% vs 24.7%** (**HR 0.75, p<0.001**) — 25% relative risk reduction
- **HF hospitalization:** HR 0.69 (31% reduction)
- **eGFR decline:** Significantly slower in empagliflozin group (secondary endpoint)
- **Benefit consistent** in T2D and non-T2D patients

### EMPEROR-Preserved (HFpEF — 2021, pivotal)
- **Design:** Empagliflozin 10 mg vs placebo, 5,988 patients with HFpEF (LVEF >40%), NYHA II–IV + elevated NT-proBNP
- **Primary composite:** CV death or hospitalization for HF
- **Results:** **13.8% vs 17.1%** (**HR 0.79, p<0.001**) — 21% relative risk reduction
- **First drug ever to demonstrate significant benefit in HFpEF** — no prior drug had succeeded in this difficult population
- **HF hospitalization:** HR 0.73 (27% reduction)
- **CV death:** HR 0.91 (not significant individually)
- **eGFR:** Significantly slower decline with empagliflozin

### EMPA-KIDNEY (CKD — 2022, led to CKD approval)
- **Design:** Empagliflozin 10 mg vs placebo in 6,609 CKD patients (eGFR 20–45 OR eGFR 45–90 + UACR ≥200)
- **Primary composite:** Sustained eGFR decline ≥40%, ESRD, CV death, or renal death
- **Results:** **13.1% vs 16.9%** (**HR 0.72, p<0.001**) — 28% relative risk reduction
- **Hospitalization for any cause:** HR 0.86
- **Benefit extended to eGFR 20–29** — broader than prior SGLT2i CKD trials
- Trial stopped early for efficacy at interim analysis

## Safety Profile

### Common Adverse Events
| AE | Frequency |
|---|---|
| Urinary tract infection (UTI) | 9–18% (women > men) |
| Genital mycotic infections (Candida) | 6–10% women; 3–4% men |
| Nasopharyngitis | 7–8% |
| Polyuria/increased urination | 3–5% |
| Thirst | 3–5% |
| Volume depletion (dizziness, hypotension) | 1–3% |

### Serious Adverse Events
| AE | Notes |
|---|---|
| **DKA (Diabetic Ketoacidosis)** | 0.1–0.2% in T2D; rare; typically precipitated by infection, surgery, severe restriction; hold before procedures |
| **Euglycemic DKA** | Rare but particularly dangerous — glucose may be near-normal; maintain high suspicion |
| **Fournier's Gangrene** | Extremely rare (<0.1%); necrotizing fasciitis of genitalia/perineum; class warning |
| **Bone fractures** | Class signal (mainly canagliflozin); less prominent with empagliflozin |
| Limb amputation | FDA class warning (driven by canagliflozin CANVAS data); less clear with empagliflozin |
| Acute kidney injury | Usually volume-related; generally reversible; hold in acute illness/dehydration |

### Black Box Warning
**None** for Jardiance

### Key Monitoring Parameters
- eGFR and potassium before initiation
- Hold empagliflozin: before major surgery (≥3 days), contrast procedures, prolonged fasting, or severe illness
- Monitor for volume depletion in elderly and those on diuretics
- Genital hygiene counseling at initiation

**CV mortality differentiation:** Jardiance's 38% CV death reduction in EMPA-REG remains a unique data point; Farxiga (DECLARE) did not show significant CV mortality reduction. This drives cardiology preference for Jardiance.

**CKD breadth:** EMPA-KIDNEY enrolled lower eGFR (20–45) than DAPA-CKD (≥25 + required albuminuria), potentially broader applicability.

### Combination Strategies
- **Jardiance + GLP-1 RA (Ozempic/Mounjaro):** Complementary mechanisms; different AE profiles; growing in cardiometabolic bundled prescribing
- **Jardiance + Entresto in HF:** SGLT2i + ARNI = two pillars of four-pillar GDMT for HFrEF; additive outcome benefits
- **Jardiance in CKD + ACE-I/ARB:** Synergistic renoprotection (RAAS + SGLT2 inhibition)

## Key HCP Messages

1. **First glucose-lowering drug to reduce cardiovascular mortality:** EMPA-REG OUTCOME demonstrated a 38% reduction in CV death with empagliflozin in T2D + established CVD — the first antihyperglycemic drug ever to show this effect, reshaping how cardiovascular benefit is considered in diabetes pharmacotherapy.

2. **Proven in both HFrEF AND HFpEF — the full heart failure spectrum:** Empagliflozin is the only drug to demonstrate benefit in both HFrEF (EMPEROR-Reduced, HR 0.75) and HFpEF (EMPEROR-Preserved, HR 0.79) — enabling consistent prescribing across all EF ranges in heart failure.

3. **First drug to beat HFpEF:** EMPEROR-Preserved was the first cardiovascular outcome trial to demonstrate significant benefit in heart failure with preserved ejection fraction, a condition affecting >50% of HF patients with no previously proven pharmacotherapy.

4. **Kidney protection down to eGFR 20:** EMPA-KIDNEY extended CKD benefit to patients with eGFR as low as 20 mL/min/1.73m² — broader than prior CKD trial entry criteria — with a 28% reduction in the primary composite, including patients both with and without proteinuria.

5. **Mechanism-independent of glycemic control:** The cardiorenal benefits of empagliflozin in HF and CKD trials are consistent in patients without diabetes, confirming effects are not dependent on glucose lowering — supporting use across the cardiorenal population regardless of diabetes status.

## Patient Profile

### Ideal T2D + CVD Patient
- T2D with established atherosclerotic CVD (prior MI, stroke, PAD)
- HbA1c above goal despite metformin
- Cardiology co-management; cardiologist/PCP collaborative prescribing
- eGFR ≥30 (initiation); eGFR ≥20 (continuation for CV/renal benefit)

### Ideal Heart Failure Patient
- Symptomatic HF (NYHA II–IV), any EF (HFrEF ≤40%, HFmrEF 41–49%, HFpEF ≥50%)
- On background GDMT (with/without T2D)
- eGFR ≥20 ml/min
- No prior significant genital infections (counsel at initiation)
- Add as 4th pillar alongside ARNI, beta-blocker, MRA (HFrEF) or standard HFpEF therapy

### Ideal CKD Patient
- CKD Stage 3b–4 (eGFR 20–60) with or without albuminuria
- T2D or non-diabetic CKD (EMPA-KIDNEY included both)
- Already on maximally tolerated ACE-I/ARB
- Risk of eGFR decline, ESRD, or CV events

### Dosing
- Start: 10 mg QD with or without food
- Uptitrate to 25 mg QD for HbA1c benefit (T2D); 10 mg sufficient for CV/renal outcomes
- HF and CKD trials used 10 mg QD dose

## Market Position

### Sales Performance
- **2023 Global Revenue:** ~$7.3 billion USD (combined BI/Lilly)
- **2022 Revenue:** ~$5.9 billion USD (+24% growth)
- Boehringer Ingelheim portion: ~$4.5B (2023); Lilly portion: ~$2.8B
- **Growth drivers:** HFpEF and CKD approvals significantly expanding non-diabetes use
- Cardiologists and nephrologists are new high-value prescribers beyond endocrinologists/PCPs

### Market Share (SGLT2 Class, US)
- **Jardiance:** ~45% of SGLT2 prescriptions by volume (2023)
- **Farxiga:** ~38%
- **Invokana:** ~12% (declining)
- SGLT2 class overall growing ~20% YoY driven by HF and CKD indications

### Payer Access
- Broadly covered in commercial plans for T2D (Tier 2–3)
- Growing formulary coverage for HF and CKD indications; prior auth may require diagnosis documentation
- BI/Lilly patient assistance programs; Jardiance savings card for commercially insured

### Key Threats
- Farxiga (dapagliflozin) with overlapping HF and CKD data — similar clinical profile
- Generic entry: US empagliflozin patents ~2025–2028; multiple patent challenges filed
- New entrant SGLT2 combinations and once-weekly SGLT2i in development (Novo Nordisk)
- GLP-1 + SGLT2 fixed-dose combinations being developed (potential new competitive dynamic)
