## 1. Sales Call Guide

### 1.1 Pre-Call Planning

Before every HCP call, review the following:
1. **Prescribing profile:** Current Entresto NRx, trend vs. prior 3 months, vs. territory average
2. **Segment and persona:** Champion / Adopter / Resistor / Non-prescriber
3. **Payer environment:** What plans does this office's patients typically use? Step therapy issues?
4. **Last call summary:** What objection was raised? What was left behind? What was the agreed follow-up?
5. **Clinical trigger:** Any recent HF hospitalizations in this practice? NT-proBNP testing frequency?
6. **Access needs:** Does this office have the Entresto Together hub number? ePA capability?

### 1.2 Call Opening Framework: The "GDMT Check"

**Effective opening question:**
> "Dr. [Name], when you see an HFrEF patient in your clinic today with LVEF ≤40% who's currently on lisinopril — what's your thought process for considering an ARNI like Entresto?"

This question:
- Surfaces prescribing behavior and barriers without being confrontational
- Opens a clinical conversation (not a transactional pitch)
- Positions Entresto in the context of GDMT optimization

### 1.3 Standard 5-Minute Detail Sequence

**Minute 1 — Patient Context:**
> "Today I want to talk about a patient type you probably see weekly — HFrEF, LVEF ≤40%, currently on an ACE inhibitor, probably NYHA Class II–III. Stable, but at real risk for a hospitalization or worse."

**Minute 2 — Clinical Evidence (PARADIGM-HF):**
> "PARADIGM-HF showed that for exactly this patient, switching to Entresto reduced cardiovascular death by 20% and HF hospitalizations by 21% vs. enalapril. In real terms — NNT of 21 over 27 months. That's one of the most compelling outcomes in modern HF medicine."

**Minute 3 — Guideline Context:**
> "The 2022 ACC/AHA/HFSA guidelines now give ARNI a Class I preferred recommendation — above ACE-I and ARB. This isn't fringe evidence — it's the standard of care."

**Minute 4 — Practical/Access:**
> "On the practical side — the 36-hour washout is straightforward. We have a transition card that walks through the protocol step by step. Entresto Together handles the PA, the appeal if needed, and the copay card — your office doesn't carry that burden."

**Minute 5 — Close:**
> "Who's your next patient where an ARNI conversation makes sense? Can we run a GDMT checklist on your current HFrEF patients together?"

### 1.4 Closing Techniques

| Close Type | When to Use | Script |
|-----------|------------|--------|
| Next patient close | Adopters; willing but not proactive | "Can you think of one patient you're seeing this week who might benefit from the ARNI switch?" |
| Trial patient close | Resistors; skeptical but curious | "Would you be willing to try Entresto in one new HFrEF diagnosis this month — and we'll handle all access support?" |
| Referral close | PCPs/non-prescribers | "For your HFrEF patients already with a cardiologist — have you mentioned Entresto to the specialist? We can provide a referral note template." |
| Data review close | Champions and data-driven HCPs | "I'd love to come back with your practice's ARNI utilization data compared to the national benchmark — when is your next available time?" |

## 2. Objection Scripts (Field-Optimized)

### "The patient is stable on lisinopril"
> "I hear this a lot — and stability is great. But PARADIGM-HF compared Entresto directly to enalapril — the same class as lisinopril — and showed 20% less CV death and 21% fewer hospitalizations. Stability on lisinopril still means that patient has a 13% chance of a CV death in the next 27 months vs. 20% on Entresto. The guidelines now prefer Entresto for exactly this patient. Stability is not optimization."

**Evidence:** PARADIGM-HF HR 0.80 for CV death; 0.79 for HF hospitalization.

### "Too much hypotension risk"
> "It's 14% vs. 9.2% in PARADIGM-HF — that's real but manageable. The key is patient selection and titration: start at 24/26 mg BID for elderly or low BP patients; ensure they're not volume-depleted; reduce the diuretic if needed. The TITRATION study showed 76% of patients reach target dose. And patients who are most hypotension-sensitive — frail elderly with low BP — are also highest risk for HF death. The benefit-risk is favorable even in that population."

### "I can't get it through insurance"
> "That's exactly what Entresto Together is for. One call — one number — and our hub team handles benefits verification, PA submission, step therapy override, and if denied, we appeal. Our first-round appeal success rate is 78%. For uninsured or underinsured patients, the PAP covers up to 100% of cost. I'll leave our hub services card — and I can introduce your office coordinator to our dedicated access specialist for your territory."

### "I put everyone on empagliflozin — isn't that good enough?"
> "Empagliflozin is evidence-based and your patients should stay on it — absolutely. But these work through completely different mechanisms. Entresto targets neurohormonal remodeling; SGLT2i targets metabolic and volume pathways. Both have Class I recommendations in 2022 guidelines. The guidelines say four pillars — Entresto, beta-blocker, MRA, AND SGLT2i. The question isn't SGLT2i or Entresto — it's when you add Entresto to the regimen your patients are already on."

### "The 36-hour washout is complicated"
> "It's actually simpler than it seems: stop the ACE inhibitor, wait 36 hours, start Entresto. We have a one-page transition card designed for exactly this conversation. The complexity of the washout is a one-time event; the benefit is lifelong. I can walk your nurse coordinator through the protocol in 5 minutes — it becomes a seamless workflow."

## 3. Territory Planning Framework

### 3.1 Account Segmentation

Each territory should be segmented into three tiers:

**Tier 1 Accounts (Top 20% by Volume Potential):**
- Criteria: High HFrEF patient volume; cardiology or HF specialist; ≥50 Entresto Rx/month potential
- Call frequency: 2x/month
- Programs: Speaker bureau invitation; advisory board; practice account manager dedicated
- Goal: Maintain champion engagement; close Champions faster on HFmrEF expansion

**Tier 2 Accounts (Middle 30% by Volume Potential):**
- Criteria: Moderate HFrEF volume; general cardiologist or high-volume internist; 15–50 Rx/month potential
- Call frequency: Monthly
- Programs: Lunch-and-learn; case-based CME; GDMT checklist review; PA support
- Goal: Convert Adopters; move Resistors to Adopters; activate 1 additional Rx/month per account

**Tier 3 Accounts (Remaining 50%):**
- Criteria: Lower HFrEF volume; PCPs; NPs/PAs without HF specialty
- Call frequency: Bi-monthly or quarterly; supplement with digital channels
- Programs: HF awareness education; simple algorithm card; Entresto Together referral
- Goal: Disease and guideline awareness; 1 new patient per quarter per account

### 3.2 Territory Planning KPIs

| KPI | Definition | Cadence |
|-----|-----------|---------|
| NRx (new prescriptions) | New Entresto starts by territory | Weekly via IQVIA |
| TRx (total prescriptions) | All Entresto fills in territory | Weekly via IQVIA |
| Market share (ARNI/RAAS) | Entresto NRx ÷ all RAAS NRx in HF | Monthly |
| Call reach | % of Tier 1/2 targets called in period | Monthly |
| Call frequency | Avg calls per target per period | Monthly |
| Program enrollments | Speaker program attendees; CME completions | Monthly |
| PA/access submissions | # of PA submissions through hub | Weekly |
| Access resolutions | # of approved PAs in territory | Weekly |

### 3.3 Territory Prioritization Tools

**GDMT Gap Analysis Tool:**
- Access through Veeva CRM
- Shows each HCP's current ARNI-to-ACE-I prescription ratio
- Identifies accounts with greatest gap vs. national average
- Prioritize accounts with ARNI <15% of RAAS prescribing and high HFrEF volume

**Patient Funnel Analysis:**
- Available quarterly through Novartis field analytics portal
- Shows: Total HFrEF patients managed by each account → % on any therapy → % on ARNI → % on target dose
- Identify where patients are dropping out of GDMT optimization

## 5. Coaching Guide

### 5.1 Field Manager Ride-Along Evaluation Framework

**Call observation rubric (1–5 scale):**

| Dimension | 1 (Below Standard) | 3 (Meeting Standard) | 5 (Exceeding Standard) |
|----------|-------------------|---------------------|----------------------|
| Pre-call planning | No account review | Reviews prescribing profile | Deep account insight; anticipates objections |
| Opening engagement | Product pitch immediately | Clinical question to open | HCP-led clinical conversation from first sentence |
| Data delivery | Reading from detail aid | Discusses key data points fluently | Adapts data to HCP's specific patient context |
| Objection handling | Deflects or apologizes | Uses approved objection response | Resolves objection with evidence; confirms HCP comfort |
| Closing | No ask | Standard call-to-action | Specific, patient-centered commitment secured |
| Access support | Unaware of current PA status | Provides hub card | Proactively connects HCP to hub; knows patient OOP |

### 5.2 Coaching Conversation Framework (POST Model)

| Step | Action |
|------|--------|
| **P** — Prepare | Review CRM notes; identify development area for this ride-along |
| **O** — Observe | Attend call silently; do not interrupt; note specific behaviors |
| **S** — Share feedback | Debrief within 30 minutes; lead with positive, then development area |
| **T** — Target | Agree on one specific behavior to practice in next call |

### 5.3 Common Development Areas and Coaching Plays

| Rep Development Gap | Coaching Play |
|--------------------|---------------|
| Over-reliance on visual aid | Role-play call without visual aid; force conversational data delivery |

| Poor objection confidence | Monthly group role-play sessions; record and review objection scenarios |
| Low program execution | Co-design speaker program calendar; weekly accountability check-in |
