# Dupixent (dupilumab) — Brand Intelligence

**Generic Name:** Dupilumab  
**Brand:** Dupixent  
**Company:** Sanofi + Regeneron (co-development; Regeneron discovered, Sanofi co-promotes)  
**Drug Class:** IL-4Rα monoclonal antibody; type 2 inflammation inhibitor  
**Mechanism of Action:** Blocks the shared IL-4 receptor alpha (IL-4Rα) subunit, simultaneously inhibiting signaling of both **IL-4** and **IL-13** — the key drivers of type 2 (Th2) inflammation underlying atopic dermatitis, asthma, CRS, eosinophilic esophagitis, and related conditions  
**FDA Approval:** March 2017 (first approval — moderate-to-severe AD in adults)  
**Formulation:** 200 mg/1.14 mL and 300 mg/2 mL pre-filled syringes/pens; SQ; various dosing schedules by indication

## Clinical Efficacy

### Atopic Dermatitis — SOLO 1 & SOLO 2 (adults)
- **Design:** Dupilumab 300 mg Q2W vs placebo (monotherapy), 16 weeks
- **IGA 0/1 (clear/almost clear):** 38% (SOLO1) / 36% (SOLO2) vs 10% / 8% placebo
- **EASI-75:** 51% / 44% vs 15% / 12% placebo
- **Peak Pruritus NRS ≥4-point improvement:** 41% / 36% vs 12% / 10%
- Rapid onset: Significant itch improvement within 2 weeks

### AD — CHRONOS Trial (with topical corticosteroids, 52 weeks)
- **IGA 0/1 at week 52:** 40% (Q2W) vs 12% (placebo + TCS)
- **EASI-75 at week 52:** 64% (Q2W) vs 22% (placebo + TCS)
- Sustained efficacy through 52 weeks; no loss of response over time

### AD — LIBERTY AD PEDS (pediatric, 6–11 years)
- **IGA 0/1 at week 16:** 30.4% vs 11.4% (placebo)
- **EASI-75:** 67.2% vs 27.4%
- Consistent efficacy profile vs adult data

### Asthma — QUEST Trial (moderate-to-severe)
- **Design:** Dupilumab 200 or 300 mg Q2W + standard therapy vs placebo
- **Annualized severe exacerbation rate reduction:**
  - All comers: 47.7% (200 mg) and 47.7% (300 mg) reduction vs placebo
  - **Eos ≥300/µL:** 65.8% reduction (300 mg)
- **FEV1 improvement:** +0.32 L (200 mg) vs +0.18 L (placebo) at week 12
- **OCS reduction subgroup (Eos ≥300):** 70.1% annualized exacerbation reduction

### Asthma — VENTURE Trial (OCS-dependent)
- **OCS reduction ≥50%:** 80% (dupilumab) vs 50% (placebo)
- **OCS elimination:** 48% vs 25%
- **Exacerbation rate:** 59% reduction

### CRSwNP — SINUS-24 and SINUS-52
- **NPS (nasal polyp score) reduction:** −2.06 (SINUS-52) vs −0.28 (placebo) (p<0.0001)
- **SNOT-22 improvement:** −19.7 vs −7.4 (placebo)
- **Endoscopic sinus surgery avoidance:** 83% vs 60% with placebo
- **Sense of smell (UPSIT):** Significant improvement

### EoE — MUSE Trial
- **Histologic remission (≤6 eos/hpf):** 59% (300 mg QW) vs 6% (placebo) at 24 weeks
- **Dysphagia improvement:** Significant across multiple endpoints
- **Endoscopic score:** Significant improvement

### COPD — BOREAS Trial (type 2 COPD)
- **Annualized moderate/severe exacerbation rate:** 30% reduction vs placebo in eos ≥300/µL
- First biologic approved for COPD; landmark expansion of type 2 inflammation concept

## Safety Profile

### Common Adverse Events
| AE | Frequency (vs placebo) |
|---|---|
| Injection site reactions | 8–18% vs 3–6% |
| Conjunctivitis | 10–28% (AD) vs 2–11%; lower in asthma |
| Nasopharyngitis | 9–15% |
| Headache | 4–9% |
| Arthralgia | 3–5% |
| Eosinophilia | Transient; mild; clinically significant rarely |

### Conjunctivitis — Key Consideration in AD
- Most common AE specific to dupilumab, particularly in AD patients
- Incidence: 10–28% in AD trials vs 2–11% placebo
- Mechanism: IL-4/IL-13 inhibition may alter conjunctival microbiome
- Management: Ophthalmic artificial tears first-line; topical calcineurin inhibitors for persistent cases; ophthalmology referral if severe
- Notably **lower in asthma, CRS, EoE indications**

### Serious Adverse Events
- No opportunistic infections reported (no immunosuppression of T-cell immunity)
- No increased risk of malignancy
- No black box warnings

### Black Box Warning
**None** — Dupixent has no black box warnings, distinguishing it from:
- JAK inhibitors (BBW: thrombosis, malignancy, CV events, death)
- TNF inhibitors (BBW: serious infections, malignancy)

### Key Safety Advantage
Dupilumab does not broadly suppress the immune system — it selectively targets type 2 inflammation. No TB screening required, no live vaccine restrictions, no serious infection signal.

## Key HCP Messages

1. **Proven efficacy across the type 2 inflammation spectrum:** Dupixent is the only biologic approved across AD, asthma, CRSwNP, EoE, prurigo nodularis, alopecia areata, and COPD — treating the shared underlying IL-4/IL-13 pathway that drives multiple diseases in the same patient.

2. **No black box warning — a critical differentiator:** Unlike JAK inhibitors (which carry BBW for thrombosis, malignancy, and CV events), Dupixent has demonstrated consistent safety across 7+ years of clinical use with no requirement for TB screening, live vaccine restrictions, or immune suppression monitoring.

3. **Rapid and sustained itch relief in AD:** Itch improvement begins within 2 weeks in atopic dermatitis — before skin clearing — addressing the most distressing symptom for patients; sustained efficacy maintained through 3+ years in registry data.

4. **OCS elimination in severe asthma:** In OCS-dependent asthmatic patients (VENTURE), 48% eliminated systemic corticosteroids entirely and 80% achieved ≥50% OCS reduction — addressing significant chronic steroid morbidity.

5. **Pediatric coverage to 6 months:** Dupixent is approved for AD from 6 months of age — the earliest biologic approval in the dermatology space — enabling early intervention during the critical developmental window.

## Patient Profile

### Ideal AD Patient
- Moderate-to-severe AD (IGA ≥3, EASI ≥16, BSA ≥10%)
- Inadequate response to TCS/TCI
- Any age ≥6 months
- Comorbid asthma or allergic conditions (type 2 overlap — strong Dupixent candidate)
- CV risk factors, older age, or infection history (prefer Dupixent over JAK inhibitors)

### Ideal Asthma Patient
- Moderate-to-severe, uncontrolled despite medium/high ICS + LABA
- Blood eosinophils ≥150/µL (or ≥300/µL for maximum benefit) or OCS-dependent
- Comorbid AD, CRS, or EoE ("type 2 patient")

### Ideal CRS Patient
- CRS with nasal polyps, bilateral
- Inadequate response to intranasal corticosteroids
- Prior sinus surgery or surgery candidate
- Comorbid asthma (Samter's triad)
