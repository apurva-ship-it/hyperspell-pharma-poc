## 1. Mechanism of Action (MOA)

### 1.1 Primary Pharmacodynamic Action

Apixaban is a selective, direct, reversible inhibitor of free and clot-bound Factor Xa. Factor Xa sits at the convergence of the intrinsic and extrinsic coagulation pathways — catalyzing the conversion of prothrombin to thrombin, which is the central step in fibrin clot formation. By directly blocking Factor Xa without requiring antithrombin as a cofactor, apixaban provides predictable, dose-dependent anticoagulation.

**Coagulation Cascade Interaction:**

```
Intrinsic pathway     Extrinsic pathway
      ↓                       ↓
   Factor IXa          Factor VIIa/TF
      ↓                       ↓
         Factor X ← ← ← ← ←
              ↓
         Factor Xa ← ← [APIXABAN BLOCKS HERE]
              ↓
         Thrombin
              ↓
           Fibrin → Clot
```

**Key mechanistic advantages vs. warfarin:**
- Direct factor inhibition (no cofactor required)
- Predictable pharmacokinetics → no routine INR monitoring
- Less food/drug interaction burden
- Faster onset and offset of action
- Lower risk of heparin-induced thrombocytopenia (HIT) — no heparin class

## 3. Pivotal Clinical Trial Program

### 3.1 ARISTOTLE — AF Stroke Prevention (Primary Registration Trial)

**Design:** Randomized, double-blind, double-dummy; N=18,201 — largest NVAF trial at time of conduct
**Population:** NVAF or AFL with ≥1 risk factor for stroke; mean age 70; mean CHADS₂ 2.1; 57% prior VKA
**Treatment:** Apixaban 5mg BID (2.5mg BID for dose-reduction criteria, n=831) vs. warfarin (INR 2.0–3.0); median follow-up 1.8 years
**Primary Endpoint:** Stroke (ischemic/hemorrhagic) or systemic embolism

#### Primary and Key Secondary Endpoints

| Endpoint | Apixaban | Warfarin | HR (95% CI) | p-value |
|----------|----------|---------|-------------|---------|
| Stroke / systemic embolism | 1.27%/yr | 1.60%/yr | 0.79 (0.66–0.95) | 0.01 |
| Ischemic stroke | 0.97%/yr | 1.05%/yr | 0.92 (0.74–1.13) | 0.42 |
| Hemorrhagic stroke | 0.24%/yr | 0.47%/yr | 0.51 (0.35–0.75) | <0.001 |
| Major bleeding | 2.13%/yr | 3.09%/yr | 0.69 (0.60–0.80) | <0.001 |
| ICH (intracranial hemorrhage) | 0.33%/yr | 0.80%/yr | 0.42 (0.30–0.58) | <0.001 |
| Fatal bleeding | 0.19%/yr | 0.38%/yr | 0.50 (0.33–0.75) | <0.001 |
| All-cause mortality | 3.52%/yr | 3.94%/yr | 0.89 (0.80–0.99) | 0.047 |
| CV hospitalization | 12.35%/yr | 12.95%/yr | 0.94 (0.88–1.00) | 0.047 |
| TIMI major bleed | 1.7%/yr | 2.3%/yr | 0.74 (0.62–0.88) | <0.001 |

**Key Publication:** Granger CB et al. NEJM 2011;365:981–992.

**Headline Summary:**
- 21% reduction in stroke/SE vs. warfarin (primary endpoint: stroke superiority)
- 31% reduction in major bleeding (safety superiority)
- 58% reduction in ICH (most clinically meaningful safety result)
- 11% reduction in all-cause mortality (only DOAC to show mortality benefit vs. warfarin)
- Warfarin TTR in ARISTOTLE: 66% — typical for controlled trial; better than most real-world settings

### 3.2 AMPLIFY — Acute VTE Treatment

**Design:** Randomized, double-blind; N=5,395
**Population:** Acute objectively confirmed DVT or PE; mean age 57
**Treatment:** Apixaban (10mg BID × 7 days, then 5mg BID) vs. conventional therapy (enoxaparin → warfarin); 6 months
**Primary Efficacy Endpoint:** Recurrent symptomatic VTE or VTE-related death
**Primary Safety Endpoint:** Major bleeding

| Endpoint | Apixaban | Conventional | Result | p-value |
|----------|----------|-------------|--------|---------|
| Recurrent VTE / VTE death | 2.3% | 2.7% | Non-inferior (RR 0.84; 95% CI 0.60–1.18) | <0.001 (NI) |
| Major bleeding | 0.6% | 1.8% | RR 0.31 (0.17–0.55) | <0.001 |
| Clinically relevant non-major bleed | 3.8% | 8.0% | RR 0.48 (0.38–0.60) | <0.001 |
| Major + CRNM bleeding | 4.3% | 9.7% | RR 0.44 (0.36–0.55) | <0.001 |
| All-cause mortality | 1.5% | 1.9% | HR 0.79 (0.53–1.19) | — |

**Key Publication:** Agnelli G et al. NEJM 2013;369:799–808.

**Clinical Implication:**
- Non-inferior efficacy with 69% reduction in major bleeding vs. standard of care
- Oral-only (no parenteral bridging required for most DVT/PE patients) simplifies treatment
- Strong differentiation: AMPLIFY major bleed 0.6% vs. warfarin 1.8%

### 3.3 AMPLIFY-EXT — Extended VTE Prevention

**Design:** Randomized, double-blind; N=2,486
**Population:** Completed 6–12 months of anticoagulation for DVT/PE; at equipoise for continued treatment
**Treatment:** Apixaban 2.5mg BID or 5mg BID vs. placebo; 12 months
**Primary Endpoint:** Recurrent VTE or VTE-related death

| Endpoint | Apixaban 2.5mg | Apixaban 5mg | Placebo | vs. Placebo |
|----------|---------------|-------------|---------|-------------|
| Recurrent VTE / death | 3.8% | 4.2% | 11.6% | 67% reduction (2.5mg) |
| Major bleeding | 0.2% | 0.1% | 0.5% | Lower with apixaban |
| CRNM bleeding | 3.0% | 4.3% | 2.7% | Slight increase |
| Fatal VTE | 0% | 0% | 0.6% | — |

**Key Publication:** Agnelli G et al. NEJM 2013;368:699–708.

**Clinical Implication:**
- 2.5mg BID is the approved extended-use dose (after 6 months of therapeutic anticoagulation)
- Preserves near-full anticoagulant protection with favorable bleeding vs. therapeutic dose
- 67% reduction in recurrent VTE vs. placebo — important for unprovoked VTE, active cancer patients

### 3.4 ADVANCE-1, 2, 3 — Post-Surgical VTE Prophylaxis

**ADVANCE-3 (Hip Replacement — Largest):**
- N=5,407; apixaban 2.5mg BID × 35 days vs. enoxaparin 40mg QD × 35 days
- Primary endpoint: Total VTE (proximal DVT + PE + death) + VTE-related death

| Endpoint | Apixaban 2.5mg BID | Enoxaparin 40mg QD | Result |
|----------|-------------------|-------------------|--------|
| Total VTE / VTE death | 1.4% | 3.9% | RR 0.36 (0.22–0.54); p<0.001 |
| Major VTE | 0.5% | 1.1% | Significant reduction |
| Major bleeding | 0.8% | 0.7% | Non-inferior (no excess) |

**Key Publication:** Lassen MR et al. NEJM 2010;363:2487–2498.

### 3.5 CARAVAGGIO — Cancer-Associated VTE (CAT)

**Design:** Randomized, open-label, non-inferiority; N=1,170
**Population:** Cancer patients with acute DVT or PE; no brain mets exclusion in all comers
**Treatment:** Apixaban 10mg BID × 7d → 5mg BID vs. dalteparin LMWH × 6 months

| Endpoint | Apixaban | Dalteparin | HR (95% CI) | Result |
|----------|----------|-----------|-------------|--------|
| Recurrent VTE | 5.6% | 7.9% | 0.63 (0.37–1.07) | Non-inferior |
| Major bleeding | 3.8% | 4.0% | 0.82 (0.40–1.69) | Non-inferior |
| GI tract major bleeding | 1.9% | 1.3% | — | Numerically higher |

**Key Publication:** Agnelli G et al. NEJM 2020;382:1599–1607.

**Implication:** Apixaban is preferred over LMWH injections in CAT — GI cancer patients require caution (luminal GI tumor may increase GI bleed risk), but for most cancer histologies apixaban is the preferred oral option.

## 4. Real-World Evidence Summary

### 4.1 ORBIT-AF Registry (Real-World AF)

| Comparison | Result |
|-----------|--------|
| Apixaban vs. warfarin (IPTW adjusted) | Stroke HR 0.78; major bleed HR 0.71 |
| Consistency with ARISTOTLE | High |

### 4.2 XALIA-LEX (Real-World DVT)

Apixaban vs. conventional therapy in DVT:
- Recurrent VTE: 1.9% vs. 2.5% (HR 0.76)
- Major bleed: 0.4% vs. 1.3% (HR 0.29)
- Consistent with AMPLIFY in real-world setting

### 4.3 Optum/IQVIA Real-World Studies

Medicare population analysis (N>200,000):
- Apixaban vs. rivaroxaban in AF: ICH reduction HR 0.53 favoring apixaban
- Apixaban vs. warfarin in AF: Stroke HR 0.79; major bleed HR 0.66 (consistent with ARISTOTLE)
