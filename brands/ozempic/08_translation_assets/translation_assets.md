## 1. Purpose and Scope

This document provides core message simplification, regional adaptation guidance, and approved terminology for translating Ozempic brand materials across key global markets. All translated materials must undergo in-country medical-legal-regulatory (MLR) review before use. Core scientific claims are globally consistent; regional adaptations apply to regulatory approval status, reimbursement context, and cultural communication norms.

## 2. Core Messages Simplified for Global Adaptation

### 2.1 Master Message Set (Plain Language)

The following simplified messages represent globally translatable concepts. They do not replace clinical claims and must be paired with region-specific regulatory data.

| # | Clinical Core Message | Plain Language Version | Notes |
|---|----------------------|----------------------|-------|
| 1 | GLP-1 RA reducing HbA1c | "Ozempic helps lower blood sugar in adults with type 2 diabetes" | Universal; diabetes literacy varies |
| 2 | CV outcomes (SUSTAIN-6) | "Ozempic has been shown to reduce the risk of heart attacks and strokes in people with type 2 diabetes and heart disease" | Requires CV indication approval per market |
| 3 | Weight reduction | "Ozempic helps with meaningful weight loss as part of diabetes treatment" | Weight claim restricted in some EMA markets |
| 4 | Once-weekly dosing | "One injection per week — the same day, any time" | Universally applicable |
| 5 | GI tolerability guidance | "Some people feel nauseous when starting Ozempic — this usually improves after a few weeks" | Culturally sensitive framing needed in Japan/Korea |
| 6 | Renal protection (FLOW) | "Ozempic may help protect kidneys in people with diabetes and kidney disease" | Off-label in most markets until label update |
| 7 | FlexTouch pen | "A simple, pre-filled pen designed for ease of use — no mixing, no drawing up" | Device name may vary by region |

## 3. Regional Adaptation — United States

### 3.1 Regulatory Context
- Full CV indication on label (T2D + established CVD) since 2017
- 2mg dose added 2022
- Wegovy CV indication (SELECT) added August 2024
- FLOW label update pending

### 3.2 Communication Priorities
- Lead with CV outcomes for cardiology/endocrinology audiences
- Access and affordability messaging prominent (high WAC concern)
- Direct-to-consumer advertising is permitted and broadly used
- GLP-1 category highly consumer-aware; leverage celebrity/media awareness cautiously per compliance

### 3.3 US-Specific Terminology (Approved)
- "Type 2 diabetes" (not "type II" or "adult-onset diabetes")
- "Established cardiovascular disease" (per PI)
- "Major adverse cardiovascular events (MACE)"
- "Once weekly" (not "weekly" alone — dosing frequency claim)
- "FlexTouch pen" (registered trademark, capitalized)
- "NovoCare" (patient support program, registered)
- "HbA1c" (acceptable; A1c also acceptable in patient-facing materials)

## 4. Regional Adaptation — European Union

### 4.1 Regulatory Context (EMA)
- Ozempic EMA approved: January 2018
- Indication: T2D glycemic control; CV risk reduction in T2D + established CVD (added 2020 via SUSTAIN-6 label update)
- Wegovy EMA approved: January 2022 (obesity); CV indication added 2024
- CHMP assessment confirms CV benefit; renal indication under EMA review

### 4.2 Country-Specific Reimbursement Status

| Country | Ozempic Reimbursement | Criteria |
|---------|----------------------|---------|
| Germany (GKV) | Yes | T2D; AMNOG assessed — additional benefit confirmed for CV |
| France (HAS) | Yes | T2D + ≥1 CV risk factor; HbA1c >8% |
| United Kingdom (NICE) | Yes | T2D + established CVD or high CV risk; HbA1c ≥58 mmol/mol (7.5%) |
| Spain | Yes | T2D; regional authority (CCAA) variation |
| Italy | Yes | AIFA class A; diabetes registries required |
| Netherlands | Yes | Formulary restricted to T2D + CVD |
| Sweden | Yes (TLV assessed) | Moderate restriction |

### 4.3 EU-Specific Communication Norms
- DTC advertising of prescription drugs PROHIBITED across EU (Directive 2001/83/EC)
- All HCP communications must clearly distinguish on-label vs. off-label use
- EMA PI preferred terminology: "established cardiovascular disease" — do NOT use "ASCVD" (US-specific term)
- Glycated hemoglobin expressed as mmol/mol (IFCC) in UK/EU: HbA1c 7% = 53 mmol/mol
- "Heart attack" is acceptable patient-friendly term for MI in EU communications
- "Stroke" acceptable; "non-fatal stroke" preferred in clinical contexts

### 4.4 EU Translation Priorities by Language

| Language | Priority Messages | Cultural Notes |
|----------|-----------------|---------------|
| German | CV outcomes, renal protection, AMNOG data | Scientific precision valued; avoid marketing language |
| French | CV outcomes, glycemic control | HAS evidence package preferred; formal physician tone |
| Spanish | Glycemic efficacy, weight, access | Both Castilian (Spain) and LatAm Spanish — different regulatory contexts |
| Italian | Registry compliance, prescribing criteria | AIFA registry mandatory; include registry onboarding in materials |

## 5. Regional Adaptation — Japan

### 5.1 Regulatory Context (PMDA)
- Ozempic PMDA approved: June 2018 (T2D only; 0.5mg/1mg)
- Wegovy PMDA approved: 2023 (obesity)
- No CV indication added to JP label (SUSTAIN-6 data not submitted for JP label update as of 2025)
- Rybelsus (oral semaglutide): Approved April 2021 (14mg dose; 3mg/7mg for titration)

### 5.2 Japan-Specific Communication Priorities
- **Efficacy focus:** Japan NHI (national health insurance) emphasis on HbA1c control; CV messaging less prominent until label update
- **GI tolerability:** Nausea management is a significant concern; Japanese patients tend to have lower BMI and may experience GI AEs at lower doses — begin at 0.25mg with extended tolerability phase
- **Dose:** 0.5mg and 1mg approved in Japan; 2mg not yet approved by PMDA
- **Body weight context:** Weight loss messaging is culturally sensitive; frame as "metabolic improvement" rather than weight loss in patient materials

## 6. Regional Adaptation — Emerging Markets

### 6.1 China (NMPA)

- Ozempic NMPA approved: August 2021 (T2D); CV indication: 2023
- Wegovy: Filing pending; obesity indication may face regulatory challenges (BMI criteria lower in Chinese guidelines: 28 kg/m² for obesity)
- Distribution: State-owned pharmacy chains; hospital distribution primary channel
- Key regional competitor: Mazdutide (Innovent/Roche — NMPA approved 2024); local preference risk
- Communication priority: T2D epidemic scale in China (>140 million patients); affordability and reimbursement vs. national drug list (NDL) inclusion

**China-Specific Messaging:**
- Lead with glycemic control and HbA1c reduction (CV messaging secondary pending label)
- "国家医保" (National Health Insurance) formulary status highly influential
- Ozempic included in NDL 2023; co-pay ~¥120–200/month
- KOL engagement through Chinese Diabetes Society (CDS) congress critical

### 6.2 India (CDSCO)

- Ozempic CDSCO approved: 2022 (T2D; 0.5mg/1mg; 2mg recently approved)
- Market context: ~77 million T2D patients; strong oral medication preference; injection hesitancy high
- Affordability: WAC INR 4,500/month (~$55); out-of-pocket dominant market
- Key messaging: Glycemic control efficacy; CV outcomes for hospital specialists; cost-effectiveness vs. injectable insulin intensification

### 6.3 Brazil (ANVISA)

- Ozempic ANVISA approved: 2021 (T2D); Wegovy 2023 (obesity)
- Brazilian private market well-developed; public SUS (Sistema Único de Saúde) reimbursement limited
- Obesity epidemic highly prominent; Wegovy demand significant in private market
- Portuguese translation required; Brazilian Portuguese (not European)
- Regulatory: Anvisa requires separate approval from Portugal INFARMED; materials must use BR-Portuguese

## 8. Translation Process and Governance

### 8.1 Required Steps for Any New Translation
1. Source copy finalized and MLR-approved in originating market
2. Professional medical translation (certified translator with pharma background)
3. Back-translation to English by independent translator
4. Reconciliation review by brand/medical
5. In-country MLR review with local regulatory documentation
6. Final approval and version control tagging (e.g., OZM-FR-2025-001)

### 8.2 Do-Not-Translate Elements
- Brand name: Ozempic (registered trademark; do not translate)
- FlexTouch (registered trademark)
- Semaglutide (INN — use as-is across all languages)
- Clinical trial names: SUSTAIN-6, SELECT, FLOW, STEP 1 (alphanumeric names do not require translation)
- p-values, HR values, confidence intervals (numerical — no translation needed)
