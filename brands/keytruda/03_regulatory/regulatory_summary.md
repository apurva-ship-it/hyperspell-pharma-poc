## 2. Approved Indications (Selected — US, as of Q2 2026)

### Melanoma
- Unresectable or metastatic melanoma (adults and pediatric ≥12 years)
- Adjuvant treatment following complete resection of stage IIB, IIC, III, or IV melanoma

### Non-Small Cell Lung Cancer (NSCLC)
- 1L treatment of NSCLC with PD-L1 TPS ≥1% (no EGFR/ALK aberrations): combination with pemetrexed + platinum (non-squamous); combination with carboplatin + paclitaxel/nab-paclitaxel (squamous)
- 1L treatment with PD-L1 TPS ≥50% as single agent (no EGFR/ALK aberrations)
- 2L/3L treatment with PD-L1 TPS ≥1% after platinum-based chemotherapy
- Adjuvant treatment of stage IB (T2a ≥4 cm), II, or IIIA NSCLC following complete resection and platinum-based chemotherapy

### Small Cell Lung Cancer (SCLC)
- 3L+ metastatic SCLC (accelerated approval)

### Head and Neck Squamous Cell Carcinoma (HNSCC)
- 1L, in combination with platinum and 5-FU chemotherapy OR as single agent (PD-L1 CPS ≥1%)
- 2L+ recurrent/metastatic HNSCC with PD-L1 TPS ≥50%

### Classical Hodgkin Lymphoma (cHL)
- Adult and pediatric patients after ≥3 prior lines of therapy (or ≥2 lines for relapsed/refractory after ASCT failure)

### Primary Mediastinal Large B-Cell Lymphoma (PMBCL)
- Adults and pediatric patients after ≥2 prior lines of therapy

### Urothelial Carcinoma
- 1L, in combination with enfortumab vedotin, regardless of PD-L1 expression
- 2L+ locally advanced or metastatic urothelial carcinoma after platinum-containing chemotherapy

### Microsatellite Instability-High or Mismatch Repair Deficient (MSI-H/dMMR) Cancer — Tumor Agnostic
- Unresectable or metastatic solid tumors with MSI-H/dMMR after prior therapy and no satisfactory alternative options

### Tumor Mutational Burden-High (TMB-H) Cancer — Tumor Agnostic
- Unresectable or metastatic solid tumors with TMB-H (≥10 mutations/Mb) after prior therapy and no satisfactory alternative options

### Colorectal Cancer (CRC)
- 1L unresectable or metastatic MSI-H/dMMR CRC (single agent or in combination with chemotherapy per updated labeling)

### Gastric/Gastroesophageal Junction (GEJ) Adenocarcinoma
- 1L with trastuzumab + fluoropyrimidine/platinum, for HER2+ tumors (PD-L1 CPS ≥1%)
- 3L+ with PD-L1 CPS ≥1%

### Esophageal Cancer
- 1L locally advanced/unresectable or metastatic squamous cell carcinoma with PD-L1 CPS ≥10%, in combination with platinum/5-FU

### Cervical Cancer
- 1L persistent, recurrent, or metastatic cervical cancer with CPS ≥1, in combination with chemotherapy ± bevacizumab
- 2L+ cervical cancer with PD-L1 CPS ≥1%

### Hepatocellular Carcinoma (HCC)
- 2L after sorafenib

### Biliary Tract Cancer (BTC)
- 1L advanced BTC in combination with gemcitabine + cisplatin

### Pancreatic Adenocarcinoma
- 1L metastatic MSI-H/dMMR pancreatic cancer (tumor-agnostic coverage)

### Endometrial Carcinoma
- 1L advanced/recurrent with lenvatinib (mismatch repair proficient/MSS tumors)
- 2L+ with lenvatinib after prior platinum therapy
- Adjuvant for dMMR/MSI-H primary advanced endometrial cancer

### Tumor-Agnostic (TMB-H)
- As described above; ≥10 mut/Mb by FDA-approved test

### Triple-Negative Breast Cancer (TNBC)
- 1L locally advanced or metastatic TNBC with PD-L1 CPS ≥10% (pembrolizumab + chemo)
- Neoadjuvant + adjuvant: High-risk, early-stage TNBC (with chemo)

### Renal Cell Carcinoma (RCC)
- 1L advanced RCC in combination with axitinib (KEYNOTE-426)
- 1L advanced RCC in combination with lenvatinib (KEYNOTE-581/Study 307)
- Adjuvant treatment of RCC at intermediate-high or high risk of recurrence after nephrectomy

### Endometrial Carcinoma (dMMR/MSI-H)
- Adjuvant treatment following primary surgical resection

### Mesothelioma
- 1L unresectable advanced/metastatic pleural mesothelioma with platinum/pemetrexed

## 3. Dosing and Administration

### IV Formulation (Standard)
| Population | Dose | Frequency | Duration |
|-----------|------|-----------|----------|
| Adults (fixed dose) | 200 mg | Q3W | Until disease progression or unacceptable toxicity, up to 24 months |
| Adults (fixed dose) | 400 mg | Q6W | Same criteria |
| Pediatric (≥6 months, weight-based) | 2 mg/kg (max 200 mg) | Q3W | Same criteria |
| Adjuvant setting | 200 mg Q3W or 400 mg Q6W | Up to 12 months post-resection | — |

**IV Preparation**: Dilute in 0.9% NaCl or 5% Dextrose to 1–10 mg/mL. Infuse over 30 minutes through 0.2–5 micron in-line filter.

### QLEX SC Formulation (Approved Sep 2025)
| Dose | Volume | Site | Frequency |
|------|--------|------|-----------|
| 70 mg/1.75 mL | Fixed; single-use pen | Abdomen, thigh, upper arm | Q3W |
| Administration time | ~3–5 minutes | Subcutaneous only | — |

**Notes**: SC is not interchangeable on a mg basis with IV; 70 mg SC equivalent to 200 mg IV based on PK bridging data. Do not inject into areas of inflammation, bruising, or skin disease.

## 4. Contraindications

Per current labeling (as of Q2 2026):
- **No absolute contraindications listed in the prescribing information**
- Use with caution in patients with active autoimmune disease, prior organ transplantation, or those who have received allogeneic HSCT (risk of fatal immune-mediated reactions and graft rejection/GVHD)

## 5. Warnings and Precautions

### BLACK BOX WARNING
**Immune-Mediated Adverse Reactions and Organ/Tissue Rejection**
KEYTRUDA is a monoclonal antibody that blocks the PD-1/PD-L1 pathway. Blocking PD-1 increases the risk of immune-mediated adverse reactions, which may be severe or fatal, including:
- Immune-mediated pneumonitis
- Colitis
- Hepatitis
- Endocrinopathies (thyroiditis, hypophysitis, adrenal insufficiency, T1DM)
- Nephritis
- Dermatologic reactions (rash, toxic epidermal necrolysis, Stevens-Johnson syndrome)
- Myocarditis
- Neurological reactions (Guillain-Barré, encephalitis, myasthenic syndrome)
- Solid organ transplant rejection
- Allogeneic HSCT complications

**Organ transplant rejection**: Fatal allogeneic organ transplant rejection has been reported. KEYTRUDA may increase the risk in patients with solid organ transplant.

### Detailed irAE Frequencies

| irAE | All Grades | Grade 3–4 | Median Onset (weeks) | Management |
|------|-----------|-----------|---------------------|------------|
| Immune-mediated pneumonitis | 1–3% | 0.7–1.5% | 9.5 | Hold; corticosteroids; permanently discontinue if severe |
| Immune-mediated colitis | 1–2% | 0.5–1% | 8.5 | Hold; corticosteroids; infliximab if steroid-refractory |
| Immune-mediated hepatitis | 2–4% | 0.5–2% | 8.7 | Hold; corticosteroids; mycophenolate if steroid-refractory |
| Immune-mediated endocrinopathies | 1–6% | 0.1–0.5% | Variable | Hormone replacement; may continue pembrolizumab with stable endocrinopathy |
| Immune-mediated nephritis | 0.5–1% | 0.1–0.3% | 14.6 | Hold; corticosteroids |
| Immune-mediated myocarditis | <1% | <1% | 5–17 | Permanently discontinue; high-dose corticosteroids |
| Immune-mediated dermatitis (severe) | 1–2% | Rare | Variable | Topical steroids; systemic steroids if severe; discontinue for TEN/SJS |

## 7. Drug Interactions

- **Immunosuppressants**: Systemic corticosteroids and other immunosuppressants may attenuate anti-tumor efficacy; use with caution except for treatment of irAEs. Prophylactic/physiologic doses acceptable.
- **Allogeneic Transplant**: See black box — fatal organ transplant rejection documented.
- **Live Vaccines**: Avoid live attenuated vaccines in patients on pembrolizumab; immune response may be compromised.
- **VEGF Inhibitors**: Increased risk of hepatic toxicity when combined with axitinib at pembrolizumab initiation; monitor LFTs closely.
- **CYP450 Interactions**: Not expected — monoclonal antibody; minimal CYP-mediated metabolism.

## 8. Special Populations

### Renal Impairment
- Mild-to-moderate renal impairment (CrCl 30–89 mL/min): No dose adjustment required; pharmacokinetics not meaningfully altered
- Severe renal impairment (CrCl <30 mL/min): Limited data; population PK analysis suggests no dose adjustment needed

### Hepatic Impairment
- Mild hepatic impairment (bilirubin ≤ULN with AST >ULN, or bilirubin 1–1.5× ULN): No dose adjustment required
- Moderate to severe hepatic impairment: Limited data; use with caution and monitor closely

### Pregnancy
- **Category**: Not formally classified; based on MOA, expected to cause fetal harm
- **Warning**: Exposure during pregnancy can cause immune-mediated fetal harm. Advise women of reproductive potential to use contraception during treatment and for ≥4 months after last dose.
- **Lactation**: Unknown if pembrolizumab is excreted in human milk. Advise not to breastfeed during treatment and for ≥4 months after last dose.
- **Fertility**: No animal fertility studies completed; pembrolizumab may impair fertility based on pharmacology

### Pediatric Patients
- Approved for use in pediatric patients ≥6 months (select indications): MSI-H/TMB-H tumors, cHL, PMBCL, melanoma (≥12 years)
- Weight-based dosing: 2 mg/kg Q3W (maximum 200 mg per dose)
- Safety and PK generally consistent with adults

### Geriatric Patients
- No overall difference in safety or efficacy observed in patients ≥65 years compared to younger patients
- Population PK analysis: Age not a significant covariate for pembrolizumab clearance

## 9. REMS Program

As of Q2 2026, pembrolizumab does NOT have a Risk Evaluation and Mitigation Strategy (REMS) requirement.

However, the following Prescriber/Patient Communication Programs are in place:
- **Medication Guide**: Required; distributed to patients at each administration
- **Immune-Mediated Reaction Alert Card**: Provided to patients; instructs when to seek emergency care
- **HCP Education Materials**: irAE recognition and management algorithm; available at KEYTRUDA.com/HCP
