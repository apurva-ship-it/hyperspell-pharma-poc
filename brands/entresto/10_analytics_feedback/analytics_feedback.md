## 2. Brand Tracker

### 2.1 HCP Brand Awareness and Perceptions (US, Q4 2023; N=502 cardiologists/PCPs)

| Metric | Q4 2022 | Q4 2023 | Change | Target 2024 |
|--------|---------|---------|--------|------------|
| Unaided brand awareness (HFrEF) | 88% | 91% | +3 pts | 94% |
| Aided brand awareness | 99% | 99% | stable | Maintain |
| "First drug recalled for HFrEF" | 72% | 78% | +6 pts | 82% |
| Agreement: "Preferred over ACE-I by guidelines" | 64% | 71% | +7 pts | 78% |
| Agreement: "Strong clinical evidence base" | 84% | 87% | +3 pts | 90% |
| Agreement: "Difficult to get approved by insurance" | 71% | 63% | −8 pts | 55% |
| Agreement: "Hypotension limits use in elderly" | 58% | 51% | −7 pts | 44% |
| Intent to prescribe (next 3 months) | 67% | 73% | +6 pts | 79% |

### 2.2 Key Perception Gaps

**Remaining perception barriers:**
1. **Insurance difficulty (63%):** Still high; Entresto Together improvements reducing but not eliminating concern
2. **Hypotension in elderly (51%):** Medical education program on low-dose initiation needed; TITRATION data more prominent
3. **"Lisinopril is adequate" (38%):** Resistor segment still 25% of base; guideline messaging not penetrating this group

**Emerging positive shifts:**
- "Guideline-preferred over ACE-I" perception: +7 pts YoY — 2022 ACC/AHA guideline update is landing
- "Access is manageable" improving — Entresto Together hub impact measurable
- "ARNI + SGLT2i is the standard" awareness: 56% (up from 38% in 2022) — four-pillar GDMT messaging working
