### 1.2 HCP Attitudinal Segments

**Segment 1: "Safety Champions" (22% of prescribers, 38% of TRx volume)**
- Profile: Cardiologists, electrophysiologists, stroke neurologists
- Behavior: Default to Eliquis for AF patients; explicitly cite bleeding safety data
- Motivator: ICH data, mortality benefit, long-term safety confidence
- Message resonance: ARISTOTLE HR 0.42 ICH reduction; all-cause mortality HR 0.89
- Channel: EP society meetings (HRS), AHA/ACC, peer-to-peer KOL programs

**Segment 2: "Efficacy-First Prescribers" (18% of prescribers, 24% of TRx volume)**
- Profile: Cardiologists, internal medicine with high AF patient load
- Behavior: Choose Eliquis primarily on stroke reduction superiority vs. warfarin
- Motivator: ARISTOTLE stroke/SE HR 0.79 (only DOAC with superiority vs. warfarin)
- Message resonance: Stroke superiority; mortality benefit unique to Eliquis
- Challenge: Xarelto messaging on equivalence must be countered clearly

**Segment 3: "Convenience Pragmatists" (31% of prescribers, 22% of TRx volume)**
- Profile: PCPs, hospitalists — high patient volume, limited time per encounter
- Behavior: Often default to Xarelto (once-daily) for ease; prescribe Eliquis when reminded of safety
- Motivator: Simplicity, formulary access, patient adherence
- Message resonance: No food restriction with Eliquis; formulary preferred; OAC adherence tools
- Challenge: Xarelto QD is the perceived ease winner; must educate on safety-over-convenience trade-off

**Segment 4: "VTE Specialists" (12% of prescribers, 10% of TRx volume)**
- Profile: Hematologists, oncologists, vascular medicine
- Behavior: Prescribe Eliquis for VTE management + extended prevention; emerging CAT use
- Motivator: AMPLIFY data; CAT-specific evidence (CARAVAGGIO); no bridging required
- Message resonance: 69% major bleed reduction vs. standard; oral-only (no injections)
- Channel: ASH meetings, ISTH congress, oncology society (ASCO, NCCN guidelines)

**Segment 5: "Orthopedic Proceduralists" (8% of prescribers, 6% of TRx volume)**
- Profile: Orthopedic surgeons, anesthesiologists
- Behavior: Prescribe Eliquis 2.5mg for 12–35 days post-arthroplasty (knee/hip)
- Motivator: ADVANCE trial data; patient convenience vs. daily enoxaparin injections
- Message resonance: ADVANCE-3: 64% VTE reduction vs. enoxaparin; oral; no monitoring

## 2. Patient Journey Map

### 2.1 NVAF Patient Journey

**Stage 1 — Diagnosis: New-Onset Atrial Fibrillation**
- Setting: Emergency department (paroxysmal AF), PCP office (incidental finding on EKG), cardiologist
- Patient emotion: Fear, confusion, shock — "I didn't know I had this"
- HCP decision: CHA₂DS₂-VASc score calculation → anticoagulation decision
- CHA₂DS₂-VASc ≥2 (men) / ≥3 (women): Anticoagulation strongly recommended (AHA/ACC)
- Key moment: First prescription — this is the highest-value moment for brand influence
- Eliquis share of new AF initiations: ~55% (higher than overall share; new-to-DOAC patients)

**Stage 2 — Initiation and Adjustment**
- First 30 days: Highest risk of non-adherence; patient unfamiliar with twice-daily habit
- Common concerns: "Will I bleed?" / "What if I miss a dose?" / "Can I still take aspirin?"
- Drop-off point: ~15% of new NVAF Eliquis patients do not refill after 30-day supply
- Key intervention: BMS/Pfizer patient support hotline; "What Matters AF" patient app
- Aspirin co-use: 28% of AF patients are also on low-dose aspirin — significant interaction risk

**Stage 3 — Chronic Therapy Management (6 months+)**
- Persistence challenge: Unlike short-course VTE, AF anticoagulation is indefinite
- 12-month persistence: ~71% (highest among DOACs in real-world studies)
- Common discontinuation triggers: Patient-initiated stopping ("felt fine"), physician switch at annual review, cost at Medicare gap, bleeding event
- Re-initiation rate after stop: ~38% within 6 months — opportunity for restart education

**Stage 4 — Complex Management Events**
- GI endoscopy: Requires peri-procedural bridging decisions; Eliquis can often be held 24–48h
- Surgery: Hold 48–72h pre-op; restart 24–48h post-op
- Bleeding event: Patient may self-discontinue; physician re-evaluation required
- Cardiac procedure (cardioversion, ablation): Continue Eliquis (no interruption for cardioversion — EMANATE trial)

### 2.2 DVT/PE Patient Journey

**Acute Diagnosis (Hospital or Outpatient)**
- DVT: Usually diagnosed with duplex ultrasound; frequently outpatient
- PE: Diagnosed CT-PA or V/Q; frequently inpatient; may need hemodynamic assessment
- Decision: Anticoagulation initiation within 24 hours of diagnosis

**Eliquis Advantage Moment:** Apixaban allows oral-only treatment (10mg BID × 7d, then 5mg BID) — no IV/SQ heparin bridge required for most patients. This enables home treatment for low-risk PE (Hestia criteria) and outpatient DVT management.

**Treatment Duration Decision (6 months vs. extended)**
- Provoked VTE (surgery, immobility, trauma): 3–6 months, then stop
- Unprovoked VTE: Individualized risk assessment; extended therapy with 2.5mg BID (AMPLIFY-EXT) preferred
- Cancer-associated VTE: Extended treatment standard of care; CARAVAGGIO supports apixaban

## 4. Adherence and Persistence Data

### 4.1 Eliquis Adherence Metrics (US Claims Data, 2024)

| Metric | Eliquis | Xarelto | Dabigatran | Warfarin |
|--------|---------|---------|-----------|---------|
| 3-month persistence (NVAF) | 87% | 85% | 79% | 82% |
| 6-month persistence (NVAF) | 81% | 79% | 73% | 78% |
| 12-month persistence (NVAF) | 71% | 68% | 62% | 70% |
| MPR at 12 months | 0.88 | 0.85 | 0.80 | 0.87 |

**Eliquis leads DOAC class in 12-month persistence** — attributed to:
1. Better tolerated (lower GI bleed = less dose self-reduction)
2. Physician confidence = proactive continuation counseling
3. Patient support programs (What Matters AF app; hotline)
4. Formulary access (preferred at most major plans = lower cost-sharing)

### 4.2 Discontinuation Reasons (Eliquis NVAF patients, N=2,800 chart review, 2024)

1. Patient-initiated stop ("felt well / didn't think needed") — 32%
2. Cost (insurance gap, tier change, Medicare gap) — 24%
3. Physician-initiated switch to competitor (QD preference) — 15%
4. Bleeding event — 12%
5. Procedural hold not restarted — 10%
6. Side effects (GI discomfort, unusual bruising) — 7%

**Key Actionable Insight:** 32% patient-initiated discontinuation is the top driver — patient education on AF being chronic, asymptomatic, and the critical role of anticoagulation must be reinforced. "The stroke you don't feel coming" messaging framework.

## 6. Patient Archetypes

### Archetype 1: "Eleanor" — Elderly AF + Prior TIA (Core Eliquis Patient)
- Female, 79, AF × 5 years, prior TIA 2 years ago, hypertension, weight 55 kg, creatinine 1.6
- Dose: 2.5mg BID (meets 2 of 3 dose-reduction criteria: age ≥80 in 1 year, weight ≤60 kg, creatinine ≥1.5)
- CHA₂DS₂-VASc: 6 — very high stroke risk
- Key concern: Fall risk, bleeding, medication complexity
- Eliquis differentiation: Safest ICH profile; 2.5mg dose validated; no food restriction; once-daily NOT appropriate (age makes bleeding risk with Xarelto higher)

### Archetype 2: "Marcus" — New AF + Moderate CV Risk (Growth Target)
- Male, 62, newly diagnosed paroxysmal AF, hypertension, prediabetes, CHA₂DS₂-VASc 2
- Treatment-naive; first DOAC
- Key concern: "Do I really need a blood thinner if my heart goes back to normal?"
- Eliquis differentiation: Stroke superiority vs. warfarin; easy start (no bridging); flexible timing

### Archetype 3: "Priya" — Cancer-Associated VTE (CAT Segment)
- Female, 58, stage IIIA non-small cell lung cancer, acute PE on CT-PA, on pembrolizumab
- Key concern: GI tolerability; interaction with immunotherapy
- Eliquis differentiation: CARAVAGGIO evidence; oral-only; lower injection burden; no CYP3A4 interaction with most immunotherapy agents
