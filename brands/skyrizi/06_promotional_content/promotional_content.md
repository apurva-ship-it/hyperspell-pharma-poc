## 1. MLR-Approved Core Key Messages

### KEY MESSAGE 1: Superior PASI 90 vs. Ustekinumab (Psoriasis)

**Headline:** "Skyrizi delivered PASI 90 in 75.3% of patients vs. 42.0% for ustekinumab — nearly double the response rate."

**Full approved claim:**
> In the UltIMMa-1 trial (N=506; moderate-to-severe plaque psoriasis), Skyrizi (risankizumab) 150 mg Q12W achieved PASI 90 in 75.3% of patients at week 16 vs. 42.0% for ustekinumab (p<0.001). In the replicate UltIMMa-2 trial (N=491), Skyrizi achieved PASI 90 in 74.8% vs. 47.5% for ustekinumab at week 16 (p<0.001).

**Supporting data:**
- UltIMMa-1: PASI 90 75.3% vs. 42%; PASI 100: 35.9% vs. 12%
- UltIMMa-2: PASI 90 74.8% vs. 47.5%; PASI 100: 30.8% vs. 8%
- Both trials statistically significant vs. ustekinumab AND vs. placebo
- Gordon KB, et al. *Lancet.* 2018;392(10148):650–661

**Usage context:** Dermatologists; academic HCPs considering switching from ustekinumab; prescribers managing moderate-to-severe PsO

---

### KEY MESSAGE 2: Superior PASI 90 vs. Adalimumab (Psoriasis)

**Headline:** "Skyrizi achieved PASI 90 in 72.4% of patients vs. 47.4% for adalimumab — a 25-point superiority."

**Full approved claim:**
> In the IMMvent trial (N=605; moderate-to-severe plaque psoriasis), Skyrizi 150 mg Q12W achieved PASI 90 in 72.4% of patients at week 16 vs. 47.4% for adalimumab (p<0.001).

**Supporting data:**
- PASI 100: 39.9% (Skyrizi) vs. 22.5% (adalimumab)
- sPGA 0/1: 83.7% vs. 60.1%
- Reich K, et al. *Lancet.* 2019;394(10198):576–586

**Usage context:** Prescribers considering switch from adalimumab/biosimilars; dermatologists managing biologic-experienced PsO patients

---

### KEY MESSAGE 3: Complete Skin Clearance — "Nothing is Everything"

**Headline:** "Skyrizi achieved complete skin clearance (PASI 100) in approximately 1 in 3 patients."

**Full approved claim:**
> In UltIMMa-1, Skyrizi achieved PASI 100 (complete skin clearance) in 35.9% of patients at week 16 vs. 12.0% for ustekinumab (p<0.001). At week 52, 56.3% of patients achieved PASI 100 with Skyrizi, reflecting deepening response over time.

**Supporting data:**
- PASI 100 at Wk 16: 35.9% (UltIMMa-1) and 30.8% (UltIMMa-2)
- PASI 100 at Wk 52: 56.3% (UltIMMa-1); consistent in UltIMMa-2
- IMMerge 5-year: 84% PASI 90 maintenance

**Usage context:** Dermatologists; patient-facing education on treatment targets; DLQI 0/1 messaging

---

### KEY MESSAGE 4: Head-to-Head Superiority in Crohn's Disease (SEQUENCE)

**Headline:** "Skyrizi is the only IL-23 inhibitor proven superior to ustekinumab in Crohn's disease — endoscopic remission 31.8% vs. 16.2% (p=0.0003)."

**Full approved claim:**
> In the SEQUENCE trial (N=745; moderate-to-severe Crohn's disease; prior anti-TNF failure), Skyrizi 600 mg IV induction followed by 360 mg SC Q8W maintenance achieved endoscopic remission in 31.8% of patients at week 48 vs. 16.2% for ustekinumab (p=0.0003), representing a statistically significant and clinically meaningful superiority.

**Supporting data:**
- Endoscopic remission: 31.8% vs. 16.2% (p=0.0003)
- Clinical remission (CDAI): 58.6% vs. 39.5%
- Endoscopic response: 46.6% vs. 25.8%
- D'Haens G, et al. *NEJM.* 2024;390(2):114–125

**Usage context:** Gastroenterologists; IBD specialists; prescribers managing post-TNF CD patients

---

### KEY MESSAGE 5: Induction Remission in Crohn's Disease (ADVANCE/MOTIVATE)

**Headline:** "Skyrizi IV induction achieved clinical remission in 45% of CD patients — including those who failed prior biologics."

**Full approved claim:**
> In the ADVANCE induction trial (N=931; moderate-to-severe CD), Skyrizi 600 mg IV at weeks 0, 4, and 8 achieved clinical remission (CDAI <150) in 45.1% of patients at week 12 vs. 24.1% for placebo (p<0.001). Endoscopic response was achieved in 40.3% vs. 12.1% (p<0.001). In the MOTIVATE trial in biologic-experienced patients, clinical remission was achieved in 42.2% vs. 19.6% for placebo (p<0.001).

**Supporting data:**
- ADVANCE: Clinical remission 45.1% vs. 24.1%; Endoscopic response 40.3% vs. 12.1%
- MOTIVATE: Clinical remission 42.2% vs. 19.6% (biologic-experienced)
- Ferrante M, et al. *Lancet.* 2022;399(10340):2031–2046

### KEY MESSAGE 6: Safe Across IBD Spectrum — No IBD Contraindication

**Headline:** "Unlike IL-17 inhibitors, Skyrizi is not only safe in IBD — it's proven effective. One therapy. Four indications."

**Full approved claim:**
> IL-17 inhibitors (secukinumab, ixekizumab) are contraindicated or carry strong safety warnings in patients with inflammatory bowel disease. Skyrizi, as a selective IL-23p19 inhibitor, has no contraindication in IBD; it is approved for the treatment of moderately to severely active Crohn's disease (June 2022) and ulcerative colitis (June 2024), in addition to plaque psoriasis (2019) and psoriatic arthritis (2022).

**Usage context:** Dermatologists/rheumatologists with patients having concurrent or at-risk IBD; cross-specialty education; patients with PsO + IBD

---

### KEY MESSAGE 7: ACR20 Efficacy in Psoriatic Arthritis

**Headline:** "Skyrizi improved joint symptoms in more than half of PsA patients — achieving ACR20 in 57.3% vs. 33.5% for placebo."

**Full approved claim:**
> In the KEEPsAKE-1 trial (N=964; active PsA; prior inadequate response to cDMARD), Skyrizi 150 mg SC Q12W achieved ACR20 in 57.3% of patients at week 24 vs. 33.5% for placebo (p<0.001). In KEEPsAKE-2 (N=444; biologic-experienced), ACR20 was achieved in 51.3% vs. 26.5% for placebo (p<0.001). Additionally, PASI 90 was achieved in 54.2% of patients with skin involvement in KEEPsAKE-1.

**Supporting data:**
- KEEPsAKE-1 ACR50: 33.7% vs. 17.7% (p<0.001)
- MDA: 24.9% vs. 13.2%
- Skin (PASI 90): 54.2% vs. 6.6%
- Mease PJ, et al. *Lancet.* 2021;397(10284):1683–1695

## 2. Objection Handlers (MLR-Approved)

### Objection 1: "I prefer IL-17 inhibitors — they work faster."

**Approved Response:**
> "The speed argument for IL-17 is real — visible response at 2–4 weeks is compelling. But let's look at where patients are at 1 year: Skyrizi achieves 81.5% PASI 90 at week 52 in UltIMMa-1 vs. 50.5% for ustekinumab. The question is: do you want your patient to see initial improvement quickly, or do you want them to have complete clearance at 1 year? Many patients who achieve only PASI 75 at month 1 plateau there. Skyrizi patients continue deepening their response over months. For patients prioritizing sustained total clearance over speed, Skyrizi delivers."

**Leave-behind:** Long-term PASI 90/100 response curves (UltIMMa Wk 52, IMMerge 5-year)

---

### Objection 2: "Guselkumab is the same mechanism — why should I switch?"

**Approved Response:**
> "You're right that both are IL-23p19 inhibitors. The key differences are: First, PASI 100 rates — Skyrizi achieves 35.9% at week 16 vs. guselkumab's 22% in their respective trials (cross-trial comparison with usual caveats). For patients wanting complete clearance, that matters. Second, dosing frequency — Skyrizi Q12W (4 injections/year) vs. guselkumab Q8W (6–7 injections/year). Third, Skyrizi has 4 approved indications including CD and UC — guselkumab does not have IBD approval. For a dermatology patient with IBD concerns, Skyrizi is the only cross-indication IL-23 option."

**Leave-behind:** IL-23 class comparison card (cross-trial — appropriate caveats)

---

### Objection 3: "Ustekinumab biosimilars are much cheaper — I can't justify Skyrizi to my IBD patients."

**Approved Response:**
> "Biosimilar ustekinumab will be significantly cheaper — I understand that's a real consideration. But SEQUENCE showed Skyrizi achieved endoscopic remission in 31.8% of post-TNF CD patients vs. 16.2% for ustekinumab — nearly double the endoscopic remission rate (p=0.0003). Endoscopic remission is associated with surgery avoidance, reduced hospitalizations, and sustained steroid-free remission. We have a health economics model showing that the incremental cost of Skyrizi vs. ustekinumab is partially offset by fewer hospitalizations and surgeries over 3 years. For your most refractory patients — post-TNF failure — Skyrizi's superiority data makes a strong case."

**Leave-behind:** SEQUENCE reprint; HEOR cost-effectiveness model summary

---

### Objection 4: "The IV induction for CD is a barrier — my patients don't want infusions."

**Approved Response:**
> "That's a legitimate practical concern. A few points: First, three IV doses (weeks 0, 4, 8) followed by a convenient SC injection every 8 weeks is a one-time logistical investment for potentially years of remission. Second, many of your CD patients have already had infusions — infliximab, vedolizumab — so they're familiar with the process. Third, we have infusion center support through mySkyrizi and a home health infusion option in some regions. The investment in IV induction delivers significantly higher induction remission rates vs. subcutaneous-only approaches."

**Leave-behind:** mySkyrizi infusion support guide; IV-to-SC transition flowchart

---

### Objection 5: "I'm concerned about infections with any biologic."

**Approved Response:**
> "Infection risk is an important consideration with any immunomodulatory therapy. For Skyrizi: in pooled psoriasis data, serious infection rates were 1.5 per 100 patient-years vs. 2.3 for ustekinumab. There is no Black Box Warning — compared to JAK inhibitors, which carry BBW for serious infections, MACE, and malignancy. For CD patients: ADVANCE showed serious infection rates of 3.5 per 100 PY — manageable with appropriate TB screening and vaccination update before initiation. The IL-23 pathway selectively downregulates Th17 while preserving IL-12-driven Th1 immunity, which theoretically maintains better protection against intracellular pathogens than broader immunosuppression."

**Leave-behind:** Skyrizi safety profile summary; BBW comparison table (vs. JAK, TNF)

---

### Objection 6: "Skyrizi doesn't have structural data for PsA — why choose it over IL-17?"

**Approved Response:**
> "Structural radiographic data is a legitimate consideration for PsA with severe joint involvement and erosive disease. For that specific subset — aggressive, erosive PsA — IL-17 inhibitors have the structural data. However, for the majority of PsA patients who have both skin and joint manifestations, Skyrizi delivers PASI 90 in 54% AND ACR20 in 57% simultaneously. And if your PsA patient also has IBD or is at risk for it — IL-17 inhibitors are off the table entirely. Skyrizi's combined skin-joint efficacy profile with IBD safety makes it the versatile choice for most of your PsA patients."

**Leave-behind:** KEEPsAKE-1 results; Skin + Joint dual efficacy summary card

---

### Objection 7: "UC approval is very new — I'll wait for more data."

**Approved Response:**
> "Completely understandable — it's a new approval. But the INSPIRE/COMMAND program used the same rigorous methodology as the CD program. INSPIRE induction achieved clinical remission in 20.3% vs. 6.2% for placebo, and COMMAND maintenance showed 40.2% clinical remission vs. 21.7% (p<0.001). The safety profile is consistent with the established Skyrizi profile across PsO, PsA, and CD — no new signals emerged in UC trials. And being on the same mechanism as our CD program means you're already familiar with how the molecule behaves. Early prescribers will shape the treatment algorithm for this indication."

**Leave-behind:** INSPIRE/COMMAND data card; UC indication launch materials
