# Skyrizi (Risankizumab) — Brand Strategy
**Brand Owner:** AbbVie (licensed from Boehringer Ingelheim) | **Therapeutic Area:** Immunology | **Document Version:** 2024.1 | **Classification:** Internal Strategic

---

## 1. Brand Vision

Skyrizi is positioned to become the most prescribed selective IL-23 inhibitor across immunology — the engine of AbbVie's post-Humira transition and the centerpiece of a redefined immunology franchise. The brand vision: **"Deliver deep, durable clearance and remission across the full spectrum of immune-mediated inflammatory disease — with a safety profile that opens doors competitors have had to close."**

By 2028, AbbVie projects Skyrizi peak annual revenue exceeding $21 billion — making it one of the most commercially successful biologic therapies in pharmaceutical history. The brand will achieve this through leadership in four indications (Plaque Psoriasis, Psoriatic Arthritis, Crohn's Disease, Ulcerative Colitis) and selective expansion opportunities in additional inflammatory diseases.

## 2. Brand Positioning

### Positioning Statement
For dermatologists, rheumatologists, and gastroenterologists treating patients with moderate-to-severe immune-mediated inflammatory disease, Skyrizi is the only anti-IL-23p19 therapy with a robust, growing clinical evidence base demonstrating PASI 90/100 superiority in psoriasis, ACR50 efficacy in PsA, and endoscopic remission in Crohn's disease — uniquely positioned as safe across the IBD-psoriasis spectrum where IL-17 inhibitors are contraindicated, offering Q12W dosing in dermatology and unmatched head-to-head data vs. ustekinumab in Crohn's disease (SEQUENCE: endoscopic remission 31.8% vs. 16.2%, p=0.0003).

### Brand Essence
**"Clearer. Deeper. Longer."**
- Clearer skin/bowel endoscopy for patients
- Deeper remission responses (PASI 100, endoscopic remission)
- Longer dosing intervals (Q12W in PsO/PsA; Q8W in IBD)

### Brand Personality
- Precision-driven (selective IL-23p19 targeting — not p40; upstream specificity)
- Evidence-forward (multiple head-to-head superiority trials)
- Versatile (four approved indications across dermatology, rheumatology, GI)
- Patient-first (convenience of Q12W dosing; strong safety profile; no BBW)

## 3. Strategic Imperatives (2024–2026)

### SI-1: Dominate the PsO Market — Defend Class Leadership Against IL-17 and IL-23 Competition

**Rationale:** Plaque psoriasis is the most competitive biologics market. IL-17 inhibitors (secukinumab, ixekizumab, bimekizumab) and IL-23 competitors (guselkumab/Tremfya, tildrakizumab/Ilumya) compete for the same patient. Skyrizi has demonstrated PASI 90 rates of 75%+ vs. ustekinumab (74.8%, UltIMMa-2) and Q12W maintenance dosing convenience. The challenge: IL-17 inhibitors offer faster onset; Skyrizi's advantage is long-term deep clearance and safety breadth.

**Tactics:**
- "Beyond PASI 75" education campaign: Drive prescribers to aspire to PASI 90/100 as the target endpoint
- Q12W convenience campaign: 4 injections per year (after 3 loading doses)
- Head-to-head vs. adalimumab messaging: IMMvent PASI 90 72.4% vs. 47.4%
- "IBD-safe IL-23" differentiation vs. IL-17 inhibitors (contraindicated in IBD)

**2024 KPI:** Maintain Skyrizi as the #1 prescribed IL-23 inhibitor in PsO; grow PASI 90/100 response rate awareness to 85% of dermatologists

### SI-2: Capture the PsA Market Against Established Biologics

**Rationale:** Psoriatic Arthritis is a heterogeneous disease with skin, joint, and enthesis involvement. IL-17 inhibitors have joint data (SPIRIT/FUTURE trials); TNF inhibitors are deeply entrenched. Skyrizi demonstrated ACR20 57.3% in KEEPsAKE-1 and 51.3% in KEEPsAKE-2 vs. placebo 33.5%/26.5% — meeting primary endpoints. The skin-joint story is compelling for patients with both manifestations.

**Tactics:**
- Dual manifestation campaign: "One treatment. Clearer skin AND less joint pain."
- Target rheumatologists who manage patients with moderate-to-severe plaque PsO as well as joint disease
- KEEPsAKE complete data dissemination; PASI 90 in PsA patients (skin component)
- ACR50/ACR70 data for enthesitis and dactylitis endpoints

**2024 KPI:** Achieve 18% NRx share in biologic-naive PsA patients; grow rheumatologist engagement by 30%

### SI-3: Establish Skyrizi as Standard-of-Care in Crohn's Disease

**Rationale:** The IBD biologics market is in rapid evolution following the ustekinumab (Stelara/Janssen) patent cliff (US expiry 2023). SEQUENCE trial — the first head-to-head trial of IL-23p19 vs. ustekinumab in CD — demonstrated endoscopic remission of 31.8% vs. 16.2% for Skyrizi (p=0.0003). This superiority data is a historic opportunity to displace ustekinumab as the standard of care in CD.

**Tactics:**
- SEQUENCE head-to-head superiority campaign targeted at GI community
- GI thought leader development: 200 IBD specialist KOLs
- "Post-TNF failure" positioning: Skyrizi as the preferred next-line option after anti-TNF failure
- Ustekinumab LOE (loss of exclusivity) strategy: capture patients switching from branded Stelara

**2024 KPI:** Achieve 35% of new biologic CD prescriptions; 50% share of prescriptions in post-TNF patients

### SI-4: Launch and Grow UC Indication

**Rationale:** Ulcerative Colitis FDA approval granted in 2024 (INSPIRE/COMMAND pivotal data). This opens a new $2.5B+ addressable market. UC indication further solidifies Skyrizi as the only anti-IL-23 therapy approved across the full IBD spectrum (both CD and UC).

**Tactics:**
- Full UC launch team deployment (200 additional GI specialty reps)
- INSPIRE/COMMAND data dissemination through GI societies (DDW, ACG, UEGW)
- UC patient identification program: endoscopist and gastroenterologist targeting
- Differentiation vs. vedolizumab (gut-selective MoA) and tofacitinib (JAK inhibitor, BBW)

**2024 KPI:** Achieve >15% NRx share of biologic UC market within 6 months of launch

### SI-5: Protect and Extend Patent Portfolio and Lifecycle

**Rationale:** Risankizumab's US composition of matter patent extends to ~2031. However, Boehringer Ingelheim retains manufacturing rights and co-commercializes in some ex-US markets. Lifecycle management includes formulation improvements, new indications (HS — hidradenitis suppurativa; atopic dermatitis exploratory), and biosimilar entry defense.

**Tactics:**
- New indication filings: HS Phase III program initiated; AD exploratory Phase II
- Pediatric supplemental NDA for PsO (6-year exclusivity extension)
- Manufacturing process patents and device improvements (auto-injector optimization)
- RWE investment: 20,000-patient cross-indication registry

**2024 KPI:** One new lifecycle indication NDA filing; RWE publication count ≥20

## 4. SWOT Analysis

### Strengths
- **Highest PASI 90/100 rates in class:** UltIMMa PASI 90 75%+ vs. ustekinumab; consistent across two pivotal trials
- **Head-to-head superiority data in CD:** SEQUENCE — endoscopic remission 31.8% vs. 16.2% ustekinumab (p=0.0003) — first ever IL-23p19 superiority over IL-12/23
- **IBD-safe profile:** IL-17 inhibitors contraindicated in IBD; Skyrizi's IL-23 mechanism is safe and effective in IBD — unique competitive moat across indications
- **4 approved indications:** PsO, PsA, CD, UC — broadest indication scope of any IL-23 inhibitor
- **Q12W dosing convenience:** After initial loading doses; reduces injection burden in PsO/PsA
- **No BBW:** Clean label; no black box warning vs. JAK inhibitors (MACE, malignancy BBW) and TNFs (infection, malignancy BBW)
- **AbbVie commercial machine:** >1,500 dermatology/rheumatology/GI reps; established payer access infrastructure; Humira patient transition pathways

### Weaknesses
- **Slower onset vs. IL-17:** Onset of action for IL-17 inhibitors (2–4 weeks to visible response) is faster than IL-23 inhibitors (8–12 weeks to peak); some prescribers prefer speed of IL-17
- **No PsA structural data (radiographic):** IL-17 inhibitors have radiographic inhibition data; Skyrizi PsA label does not include structural modification claim
- **Guselkumab competition:** Tremfya (guselkumab, Janssen) is in the same IL-23p19 class; head-to-head ECLIPSE trial showed guselkumab superiority over secukinumab (IL-17) — direct competitor for dermatology market
- **Biologic-naive vs. experienced differentiation:** Some IL-23 class messaging applies equally to guselkumab — need sharper Skyrizi-specific differentiation
- **IBD market learning curve:** CD/UC are GI specialty markets new to AbbVie's traditional dermatology/rheumatology base; requires new KOL development and market infrastructure

### Opportunities
- **Ustekinumab LOE:** Stelara US patent expiry 2023 — massive patient pool for conversion/retention on IL-23p19 therapy
- **JAK inhibitor headwinds:** FDA BBW for JAK inhibitors (tofacitinib, upadacitinib, baricitinib) creates prescriber hesitancy, pushing interest toward biologics including Skyrizi
- **IL-17 IBD contraindication:** Approximately 25% of PsO patients have comorbid IBD or IBD family history — for this group, IL-17 is contraindicated; Skyrizi is the preferred option
- **Biologic-experienced patients:** Large pool of TNF-experienced patients with inadequate response — ideal second/third-line biologic candidates
- **Pediatric PsO approval opportunity:** High unmet need; 6-year pediatric exclusivity; limited competition in pediatric market
- **New indications:** HS Phase III, AD Phase II — pipeline expansion maintains long-term revenue

### Threats
- **Bimekizumab (Bimzelx, UCB):** Dual IL-17A/F inhibitor with differentiated mechanism; psoriasis data suggests PASI 90/100 competitive with Skyrizi; UC Phase III ongoing
- **Guselkumab (Tremfya, Janssen):** Same mechanism class; ECLIPSE vs. secukinumab creates credibility; recent CD/UC data emerging
- **Upadacitinib (Rinvoq, AbbVie — own competition):** AbbVie's JAK inhibitor active in PsO, PsA, UC, CD — internal cannibalization risk despite different risk profile
- **Biosimilar entry:** IL-23 biosimilar research ongoing; composition patents extend to 2031 in US but earlier in some markets
- **Ustekinumab biosimilars:** Stelara biosimilars (Hadlima, Pyzchiva, etc.) will be extremely low-cost; if prescribers accept biosimilar ustekinumab, may resist switching to branded Skyrizi
- **Payer pressure:** IL-23 inhibitors priced at $25,000–$50,000/year; biosimilar pressure from ustekinumab class will increase formulary negotiation leverage for payers

## 6. Campaign Pillars (2024)

### Pillar 1: "Nothing is Everything" (Dermatology — Patient/HCP)
Brand campaign built around the patient aspiration for complete skin clearance. "Nothing" refers to no visible psoriasis — total skin clearance. Anchored in PASI 100 data. Execution: consumer/DTC television, HCP journal advertising, speaker programs, PASI 100 as benchmark.

### Pillar 2: "SEQUENCE Changes Everything" (IBD/GI)
Campaign anchored in the SEQUENCE head-to-head superiority data (CD vs. ustekinumab). "SEQUENCE Changes Everything" positions Skyrizi as the new standard in CD. Execution: GI congress presence (DDW, ACG); KOL publications; targeted GI rep detailing.

### Pillar 3: "One Body — Four Answers" (Cross-Indication)
Campaign targeting HCPs managing patients with overlapping conditions (PsO + PsA; PsO + IBD). Positions Skyrizi as the versatile choice across the immunology spectrum. Execution: rheumatology-dermatology joint programs; IBD-dermatology crossover CME.

### Pillar 4: "Safe by Design" (Differentiation vs. JAK/IL-17)
Campaign focused on Skyrizi's safety profile: no BBW vs. JAK inhibitors; no IBD contraindication vs. IL-17. Execution: comparative messaging targeting prescribers previously using JAK inhibitors or IL-17 with IBD concerns.
