## 1. Brand Vision

**"In the era of biosimilar competition, HUMIRA remains the trusted, clinically proven anti-TNF foundation — protecting brand equity through patient adherence, portfolio migration to next-generation immunology, and the unmatched real-world experience base of 25+ years."**

HUMIRA faces the defining challenge of its commercial lifecycle: sustaining revenue and patient loyalty in the face of nine-plus US biosimilar competitors following the January 2023 US market entry. The brand strategy must simultaneously defend existing patient relationships, facilitate conversion to SKYRIZI (risankizumab) and RINVOQ (upadacitinib) where clinically appropriate, and sustain HUMIRA as the preferred biosimilar-originator option for payers where value-based contracts create incentives.

## 3. Strategic Imperatives (FY2026–2027)

### Imperative 1: Protect the Core Patient Base Against Biosimilar Erosion
- Payer strategy: Negotiate HUMIRA-as-preferred (originator) contracts with select PBMs/MCOs on basis of rebate structures and total cost of care
- Patient retention: myAbbVie Assist co-pay programs maintain $0 cost for commercially insured patients — neutralize biosimilar cost argument
- Physician retention: Emphasize real-world safety data depth (>1M patient-years); biosimilar real-world data gaps at launch
- Target: Maintain ≥25% of originator adalimumab volume among biosimilar-using payer accounts through 2026

### Imperative 2: Manage Brand-to-Portfolio Migration (HUMIRA → SKYRIZI/RINVOQ)
- Identify HUMIRA patients with inadequate response (PASI 75 non-achievers in psoriasis; ACR50 non-achievers in RA)
- Present SKYRIZI (IL-23 inhibitor) as the next step in psoriasis and PsA
- Present RINVOQ (JAK1 inhibitor) as the next step in RA, atopic dermatitis, UC, CD
- Key message: AbbVie's immunology pipeline delivers the most advanced mechanisms
- Target: 35% of inadequate HUMIRA responders to transition to SKYRIZI/RINVOQ by end of 2026

### Imperative 3: Citrate-Free Formulation as the Preferred Originator Offer
- 80 mg/0.8 mL high-concentration, citrate-free formulation: clinically differentiated from early biosimilar formulations
- Studies show citrate-free reduces injection site burning/pain vs. citrate-containing formulations
- Messaging: "The most comfortable adalimumab injection — because adherence begins with the experience of taking the dose"
- Target: 100% of new HUMIRA starts on citrate-free formulation by Q2 2026

### Imperative 4: Real-World Evidence (RWE) Generation and Deployment
- Commission RWE studies demonstrating HUMIRA long-term safety vs. emerging biosimilar cohorts
- Generate data on HUMIRA performance in special populations (pregnancy, elderly, comorbidities)
- Publish treat-to-target outcomes from myAbbVie Assist adherence program participation
- Target: 5 peer-reviewed RWE publications per year from HUMIRA data assets

### Imperative 5: Precision Payer and Contracting Strategy
- Offer payer value-based contracts tied to ACR50/PASI75/clinical remission outcomes
- Present net cost-of-care analyses: HUMIRA managed adherence programs reduce hospitalizations, ER visits
- Differentiate on formulary by offering originator vs. biosimilar via two-tier contracting strategy
- Target: Maintain preferred status in ≥60% of commercially managed lives through 2026

## 4. SWOT Analysis

### Strengths
- **Indication Breadth**: 10 FDA-approved indications — most of any TNF inhibitor; covers RA, JIA, PsA, AS, nr-AxSpA, PsO, CD, UC, Uveitis, HS
- **Citrate-Free Formulation**: Differentiated patient experience; first high-concentration citrate-free adalimumab in US
- **Real-World Safety Database**: >1 million patient-years; longest post-market surveillance in biologics
- **myAbbVie Assist**: Industry-leading patient support program; co-pay support, nurse ambassadors, refill management
- **Physician Brand Loyalty**: 20+ years of prescribing familiarity; HCPs trained on HUMIRA trust the data
- **Pediatric Approvals**: JIA (≥2 years), pediatric CD (≥6 years), pediatric UC (≥5 years), pediatric uveitis (≥2 years)
- **AbbVie Portfolio Synergy**: SKYRIZI and RINVOQ positioned as next-gen options; AbbVie reps cross-sell entire immunology portfolio

### Weaknesses
- **Biosimilar Erosion**: 9+ US biosimilars launched January 2023+; market share erosion 35–45% from peak
- **Peak Revenue in Rearview**: $21.2B peak (2022) → $14.4B (2023) → ~$9.8B estimated (2025)
- **Mechanism Limitation**: TNF-α inhibition less targeted than IL-23 (SKYRIZI) or JAK1 (RINVOQ) in psoriasis/IBD — some patients need more
- **Malignancy/Infection Black Box**: Ongoing concerns limit use in highest-risk patient populations
- **Subcutaneous Only**: No IV formulation; limited options for patients with self-injection barriers
- **Patent Exhaustion**: Core molecular patents expired; no long-term IP protection

### Opportunities
- **Originator Loyalty Market**: ~40% of payers maintaining HUMIRA as preferred on some formulary tier
- **Uncontrolled Biosimilar Entry Risk**: Early biosimilar real-world data gaps create safety preference opportunity
- **Special Populations**: Pregnancy data (HUMIRA labeled for use in pregnancy per updated labeling); biosimilar pregnancy data absent
- **Emerging Market Expansion**: Latin America, Middle East biosimilar penetration lower; HUMIRA originator market intact
- **Adherence Data Advantage**: myAbbVie Assist program drives superior adherence vs. unmanaged biosimilar patients
- **HS Indication Growth**: Hidradenitis suppurativa — largely underdiagnosed; HUMIRA the only approved biologic; growing awareness

### Threats
- **Biosimilar Price Competition**: Biosimilars priced 50–80% below HUMIRA WAC; PBMs mandating substitution
- **SKYRIZI Cannibalization**: AbbVie's own SKYRIZI (IL-23) outperforms HUMIRA in psoriasis (PASI 90/100 rates); AbbVie itself favors SKYRIZI for new Pso starts
- **RINVOQ Cannibalization**: AbbVie's RINVOQ (JAK1) preferred for RA patients with inadequate TNF response; JAK1 broad indication expansion
- **Interchangeable Biosimilar Designation**: Cyltezo (adalimumab-adbm) FDA-designated interchangeable — pharmacist substitution without HCP approval possible
- **IRA Negotiation Risk**: If adalimumab (small molecule equivalent pricing frameworks applicable) or future biologics subject to negotiation
- **Long-Term Market Shrink**: Class erosion as IL-23, JAK, IL-17 agents capture new patient starts; TNF market share in psoriasis declining 3–5% per year
