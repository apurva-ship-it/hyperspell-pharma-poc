**Headline Message:**
Nexorel demonstrated a 52% reduction in risk of intracranial progression versus osimertinib in patients with CNS metastases at baseline (mPFS: 21.4 vs. 14.1 months; HR 0.48; 95% CI: 0.31–0.74; p<0.001).

**Supporting Messages:**
- In the NEXUS-1 trial, 78% of patients with measurable CNS lesions achieved intracranial partial or complete response with Nexorel
- Nexorel maintains therapeutic CNS concentrations at steady state — brain-to-plasma ratio of 0.82, significantly higher than osimertinib (0.31)
- "Don't wait for CNS progression to act. Nexorel was designed with the blood-brain barrier in mind."

**Data Source:** NEXUS-1 Phase III trial (N=612), CNS sub-group (N=214)
**Publication:** Kimura et al., New England Journal of Medicine, Vol 392, 2025

## Indication & Important Safety Information

### Approved Indication
Nexorel (nexoratinib) is indicated for the first-line treatment of adult patients with metastatic non-small cell lung cancer (NSCLC) whose tumors have epidermal growth factor receptor (EGFR) exon 19 deletions or exon 21 L858R mutations, as detected by an FDA-approved test.

### Important Safety Information (Summary)

**Contraindications:** None established.

**Warnings and Precautions:**
- **Interstitial Lung Disease (ILD)/Pneumonitis:** Occurred in 2.1% of patients; 0.3% fatal. Withhold Nexorel for Grade 2; permanently discontinue for Grade 3–4.
- **QTc Prolongation:** Monitor ECG in patients with risk factors. Avoid concomitant QT-prolonging drugs.
- **Embryo-Fetal Toxicity:** Can cause fetal harm. Advise females of reproductive potential to use effective contraception.
- **Hepatotoxicity:** Monitor liver function tests. Dose modify or discontinue based on severity.

**Most Common Adverse Reactions (≥20%):** Diarrhea (38%), rash (29%), paronychia (24%), fatigue (22%), dry skin (21%).

**Drug Interactions:** Strong CYP3A4 inducers reduce nexoratinib exposure. Avoid concomitant use.

**Full Prescribing Information:** nexorel.com/hcp | 1-800-AETHER-1

## Objection Handlers

**Objection:** "Tagrisso has 10 years of data. I trust what I know."
**Response:** "That trust is well-earned, and Tagrisso is an excellent drug. For your patients without CNS involvement, it's a proven choice. But for your patients presenting with brain mets — 35% of your EGFR+ population — NEXUS-1 showed a 52% reduction in intracranial progression. That's a population where we now have a differentiated option."

**Objection:** "The rash data looks better, but is that really driving my prescribing decision?"
**Response:** "Fair question. Beyond rash numbers — what we saw in NEXUS-1 is that patients on Nexorel stayed on therapy 4.5 months longer on average. Dose reductions were 60% less common. In a disease where you're fighting for every month of control, keeping patients on full-dose therapy matters."

**Objection:** "Lazertinib + amivantamab has a longer PFS. Shouldn't I use that?"
**Response:** "The MARIPOSA PFS data is compelling. The question is patient fit. Seventy-five percent of patients experienced Grade 3+ adverse events on that combination. For your patients who are older, have performance status concerns, or are managing comorbidities — Nexorel gives you best-in-class CNS control without stacking toxicity. Not every patient can handle the combination."
