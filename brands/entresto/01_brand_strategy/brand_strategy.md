# Entresto (Sacubitril/Valsartan) — Brand Strategy
**Brand Owner:** Novartis Pharmaceuticals | **Therapeutic Area:** Heart Failure | **Document Version:** 2024.1 | **Classification:** Internal Strategic

---

## 1. Brand Vision

Entresto aspires to become the universal backbone of heart failure therapy — the foundational neurohormonal agent prescribed at diagnosis and maintained across the full continuum of HF care, regardless of ejection fraction. The overarching brand vision: **"Transform the HF prognosis so that living well with heart failure is the expectation, not the exception."**

By 2027, Novartis aims to position Entresto as the single most-prescribed branded cardiac medication globally, with patient penetration among eligible HFrEF patients exceeding 50% in the US, EU5, and Japan.

---

## 2. Brand Positioning

### Positioning Statement
For cardiologists, heart failure specialists, and internists managing adult patients with HFrEF (LVEF ≤40%) or HF with elevated NT-proBNP, Entresto is the only ARNI that simultaneously inhibits neprilysin and blocks the angiotensin receptor — delivering a 20% relative risk reduction in cardiovascular death vs. enalapril (PARADIGM-HF, HR 0.80, p<0.0001) and a 21% reduction in heart failure hospitalizations, with a proven NNT of 21 over 27 months — unlike ACE inhibitors or ARBs, which address only one neurohormonal axis.

### Brand Essence
**"Dual protection. One tablet. More life."**

### Brand Personality
- Scientifically rigorous and credible (built on landmark Phase III data)
- Progressive (guideline-endorsed, Category I recommendation)
- Reassuring (well-characterized safety profile, tolerable side-effect burden)
- Patient-centered (quality of life improvement, fewer hospitalizations)

## 3. Strategic Imperatives (2024–2026)

### SI-1: Accelerate Conversion from ACE-I/ARB to ARNI in Newly Diagnosed HFrEF
**Rationale:** Despite Class I, Level of Evidence B-R recommendation in ACC/AHA/HFSA 2022 guidelines, only 20–25% of eligible HFrEF patients receive an ARNI. The largest opportunity lies in patients currently managed on lisinopril or other ACE inhibitors who have never been offered guideline-directed therapy optimization.

**Tactics:**
- Deploy HF Navigator program through 1,200 key cardiology accounts
- Launch EMR-integrated clinical decision support alerts for HFrEF patients on ACE-I without ARNI
- Fund 50 institutional Quality Improvement grants targeting GDMT optimization
- Medical education: 30 regional symposia, 6 national congress presence

**2024 KPI:** Increase new ARNI starts among ACE-I/ARB-experienced patients by 18% YoY

### SI-2: Defend and Grow the In-Hospital Initiation Franchise
**Rationale:** PIONEER-HF demonstrated that in-hospital initiation of Entresto (vs. enalapril) achieved a 47% reduction in NT-proBNP at 8 weeks vs. 25% for enalapril (p<0.001). In-hospital initiation creates an early-adherence anchor and drives 30-day refill rates above 78%.

**Tactics:**
- Hospital HF Pathways program in 400 Magnet-certified hospitals
- Partner with AHA's Target: HF initiative
- Pharmacist activation programs in 200 top hospital systems
- Nurse practitioner and PA CME focused on in-hospital ARNI initiation protocol

**2024 KPI:** Achieve ARNI in-hospital initiation in 35% of eligible acute HF admissions at partner institutions

### SI-3: Expand Label Utilization — HFmrEF/HFpEF and Pediatric Populations
**Rationale:** 2021 FDA label expansion (broader HF label for patients with elevated NT-proBNP regardless of EF) opens a significant new population. Pediatric label (≥1 year, systemic LV dysfunction) creates a unique unmet-need story with no competitive alternative.

**Tactics:**
- Disease education campaign on HFmrEF/HFpEF targeting general cardiologists
- Pediatric HF specialist engagement program (120 pediatric cardiology centers)
- NT-proBNP testing initiative to identify eligible patients with mid-range EF
- Real-world evidence generation: 5,000-patient registry in broadened label population

**2024 KPI:** 12% growth in prescriptions from non-HFrEF label; pediatric NRx +25%

### SI-4: Optimize Market Access and Reduce Payer Friction
**Rationale:** Step therapy requirements (mandating ACE-I/ARB trial before ARNI) affect ~60% of commercial plans and ~40% of Medicare Part D plans. PA denial rates average 22%. Each denied PA represents ~$2,400 lost revenue and patient delay.

**Tactics:**
- Deploy 45-person payer access team for formulary negotiation
- Launch ePA (electronic prior authorization) integration with 8 major EHR platforms
- Publish total cost of care model: ARNI reduces 30-day readmissions by 16% vs. SOC
- Entresto Together patient support program: copay cards, PAP, hub services

**2024 KPI:** Reduce average PA turnaround time from 4.2 to 2.5 days; increase formulary Tier 2 placement from 44% to 55% of covered lives

### SI-5: Sustain Scientific Leadership Through RWE and Post-Marketing Data
**Rationale:** With generic sacubitril/valsartan not anticipated until ~2026 in the US, the brand has a window to build an impenetrable evidence fortress through RWE, HEOR, and post-marketing studies that generics cannot replicate.

**Tactics:**
- ARNI-REAL registry: 10,000-patient prospective cohort (US + EU)
- HEOR publication strategy: 12 manuscripts/year targeting JAMA, JACC, Circulation
- Health technology assessment dossiers for EU5 QALY-based payer submissions
- Investigator-initiated study grants: $15M/year

**2024 KPI:** 15 peer-reviewed RWE publications; HEOR model accepted in 4/5 EU HTA submissions

## 4. SWOT Analysis

### Strengths
- **Landmark clinical evidence:** PARADIGM-HF (8,442 patients, median 27 months) demonstrated statistically significant superiority over enalapril across all primary and secondary endpoints
- **Guideline dominance:** ACC/AHA/HFSA 2022 Class I recommendation — preferred over ACE-I/ARB; listed in all major international HF guidelines (ESC, CCS, JHFS)
- **Broad label:** HFrEF + elevated NT-proBNP label + pediatric indication = largest addressable ARNI population
- **Novel MOA:** Only ARNI — dual inhibition of neprilysin and AT1 receptor; no generic equivalent mechanism
- **Commercial infrastructure:** Global Novartis cardiovascular franchise; established payer relationships; 800-person US CV sales force
- **Revenue base:** ~$3.8B global revenue in 2023 providing reinvestment capacity

### Weaknesses
- **Penetration gap:** Only 20–25% of eligible HFrEF patients currently on therapy; significant unrealized market
- **36-hour ACE-I washout requirement:** Creates a practical barrier to conversion; increases risk of perceived complexity vs. simple dose switch
- **Angioedema BBW and ACE-I contraindication:** Limits conversion from patients with ACE-I–induced angioedema history; requires careful screening
- **Price sensitivity:** WAC ~$700/month creates access barriers; generic ACE-I cost <$5/month creates stark cost comparison narrative
- **Hypotension risk:** 14% incidence (vs. 9.2% for enalapril) in PARADIGM-HF; limits uptake in frail/elderly patients
- **PARAGON-HF miss:** Failure to demonstrate significant benefit in HFpEF primary analysis (HR 0.87, p=0.06) leaves HFpEF story incomplete

### Opportunities
- **Massive undertreated population:** ~5.7M HF patients in US; ~2M HFrEF patients eligible; ~1.5M not yet on ARNI
- **HFmrEF expansion:** 2021 label update for elevated NT-proBNP patients unlocks ~800,000 additional US patients
- **SGLT2i synergy:** Entresto + dapagliflozin/empagliflozin combination now standard GDMT; positions ARNI as a companion to SGLT2i rather than competitor
- **In-hospital initiation momentum:** Joint Commission, AHA quality metrics now tracking GDMT at discharge
- **Pediatric market exclusivity:** No generic ARNI competition in pediatrics; strong unmet need story
- **Digital health integration:** Remote patient monitoring + NT-proBNP trending can identify patients ready for ARNI initiation

### Threats
- **ACE inhibitor genericization narrative:** Health systems increasingly emphasize cost-per-outcome; ARNI cost-effectiveness requires active communication
- **Generic entry timeline:** Novartis IP expiry ~2025–2026 in some markets; generic sacubitril/valsartan would erode branded revenue
- **SGLT2i ascendancy:** Empagliflozin and dapagliflozin have mortality data and are generic/near-generic in some markets; may receive disproportionate attention in prescribing decisions
- **Payer step therapy:** 60% of commercial plans require ACE-I/ARB failure before ARNI; creates treatment delay and administrative burden
- **Label confusion:** HFpEF failure in PARAGON-HF creates prescriber hesitancy in mid-range EF patients despite 2021 label expansion
- **Pipeline competition:** Other neurohormonal agents (ARNIs with different profiles, omecamtiv mecarbil) in development

## 6. Campaign Pillars (2024)

### Pillar 1: "The Evidence Is Clear" (Professional)
Scientific credibility campaign anchored in PARADIGM-HF data. Targets cardiologists and HF specialists. Key message: Entresto reduces CV death by 20% vs. enalapril — a magnitude of benefit rarely achieved in CV outcomes trials. Execution: peer-reviewed journal advertising, conference sponsorship, medical education grants.

### Pillar 2: "Start Strong" (In-Hospital Initiation)
Campaign promoting PIONEER-HF evidence for in-hospital initiation. Targets hospitalists, cardiologists, and HF nurses managing acute HF admissions. Key message: Starting Entresto before discharge reduces NT-proBNP by 47% — setting patients up for better long-term outcomes. Execution: hospital education programs, nurse pathway tools, discharge checklist resources.

### Pillar 3: "Every Patient Deserves GDMT" (Access and Equity)
Campaign addressing ARNI access gaps — particularly in underserved populations and among patients managed by non-specialists. Targets PCPs, NPs, PAs managing HF patients. Key message: With Entresto Together, access barriers can be overcome. Execution: copay support messaging, digital PA tools, multicultural patient education materials.

### Pillar 4: "Beyond the Ejection Fraction" (Broad Label)
Campaign educating on the 2021 label expansion and NT-proBNP-guided patient selection. Targets general cardiologists and internists. Key message: If NT-proBNP is elevated, the conversation about Entresto should begin. Execution: NT-proBNP testing initiative partnerships, digital CME, electronic clinical decision support.

## 8. Global Strategic Alignment

The global brand strategy is anchored in three overarching themes consistent across all markets:

1. **Scientific Leadership:** PARADIGM-HF remains the most-cited HF outcomes trial since SOLVD. Novartis will invest in extending this evidence base through RWE, HEOR, and investigator-sponsored studies.
2. **Access Excellence:** Market access strategies are market-specific but unified by the principle that no eligible patient should be denied ARNI therapy due to cost or administrative barriers.
3. **Ecosystem Engagement:** Entresto is positioned as the neurohormonal cornerstone of a four-pillar GDMT regimen (ARNI + beta-blocker + MRA + SGLT2i), actively co-promoting alongside the broader HF treatment ecosystem rather than competing with it.
