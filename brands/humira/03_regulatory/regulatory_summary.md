## 2. Full Approved Indications (US, as of Q2 2026)

### 1. Rheumatoid Arthritis (RA)
Reducing signs/symptoms, inducing major clinical response, inhibiting structural damage, and improving physical function in adult patients with moderately to severely active RA. Can be used alone or in combination with methotrexate (MTX) or other non-biologic DMARDs.

### 2. Juvenile Idiopathic Arthritis (JIA)
Reducing signs/symptoms of moderately to severely active polyarticular JIA in patients 2 years and older. Can be used alone or in combination with MTX.

### 3. Psoriatic Arthritis (PsA)
Reducing signs/symptoms, inhibiting structural damage progression, and improving physical function in adult patients with active psoriatic arthritis. Can be used alone or in combination with non-biologic DMARDs.

### 4. Ankylosing Spondylitis (AS)
Reducing signs/symptoms in adult patients with active ankylosing spondylitis.

### 5. Non-Radiographic Axial Spondyloarthritis (nr-AxSpA)
Reducing signs/symptoms in adult patients with active non-radiographic axial spondyloarthritis with objective signs of inflammation, who have had an inadequate response to or are intolerant of NSAIDs.

### 6. Plaque Psoriasis (PsO)
Treatment of adult patients with moderate-to-severe chronic plaque psoriasis who are candidates for systemic therapy or phototherapy, and when other systemic therapies are medically less appropriate.

### 7. Crohn's Disease (CD)
- **Adults**: Reducing signs/symptoms and inducing/maintaining clinical remission in adult patients with moderately to severely active CD who have had inadequate response to conventional therapy.
- **Pediatric (≥6 years)**: Reducing signs/symptoms and inducing/maintaining clinical remission in pediatric patients with moderately to severely active CD.

### 8. Ulcerative Colitis (UC)
- **Adults**: Inducing and sustaining clinical remission in adult patients with moderately to severely active UC who have had inadequate response to immunosuppressants.
- **Pediatric (≥5 years)**: Inducing and sustaining clinical remission in pediatric patients.

### 9. Uveitis
Treatment of non-infectious intermediate, posterior, and panuveitis in adults and pediatric patients (≥2 years).

### 10. Hidradenitis Suppurativa (HS)
Treatment of moderate-to-severe hidradenitis suppurativa in adult patients. HUMIRA is the first and only FDA-approved biologic for HS.

## 3. Dosing and Administration

### Rheumatoid Arthritis
| Regimen | Dose | Frequency |
|---------|------|-----------|
| Monotherapy or combination (no MTX loading) | 40 mg | Every other week (EOW) |
| Some patients not on MTX | 40 mg | Weekly may be considered |
| Subcutaneous injection | Abdomen or thigh preferred | Rotate sites; avoid irritated skin |

### Juvenile Idiopathic Arthritis
| Weight | Dose | Frequency |
|--------|------|-----------|
| 10 kg to <15 kg | 10 mg | EOW |
| 15 kg to <30 kg | 20 mg | EOW |
| ≥30 kg | 40 mg | EOW |

### Psoriatic Arthritis and Ankylosing Spondylitis
- 40 mg SC EOW; may increase to 40 mg weekly if inadequate response in some patients (not explicitly labeled; practice-based)

### Plaque Psoriasis
- **Loading**: 80 mg SC on Day 1
- **Maintenance**: 40 mg SC EOW starting Week 1 (Day 8)
- Some patients: May consider 40 mg weekly for non-responders (off-label practice)

### Crohn's Disease
- **Adult Induction**: 160 mg SC Day 1 (or 80 mg/day over 2 days), then 80 mg at Week 2
- **Adult Maintenance**: 40 mg SC EOW starting Week 4
- **Pediatric**: Weight-based induction (≥40 kg: 160/80 mg; <40 kg: 80/40 mg); maintenance 20 mg or 40 mg EOW per weight

### Ulcerative Colitis
- **Induction**: 160 mg SC on Day 1, 80 mg on Day 15
- **Maintenance**: 40 mg SC EOW starting Week 8; may increase to 40 mg weekly if inadequate response

### Hidradenitis Suppurativa
- **Loading**: 160 mg SC on Day 1 (single or split 80 mg × 2 consecutive days)
- **Week 2**: 80 mg SC
- **Maintenance**: 40 mg SC weekly starting Week 4
- **Note**: Weekly dosing for HS — higher frequency than other indications

### Uveitis
- **Loading**: 80 mg SC on Day 1
- **Maintenance**: 40 mg SC EOW starting Week 1
- **Pediatric (≥2 years)**: Weight-based; ≥30 kg: adult dosing; <30 kg: 20 mg EOW

## 4. Contraindications

- **Serious Infections**: Do not use HUMIRA in patients with active infections, including chronic or localized infections, until the infection is resolved or being adequately treated.
- **No absolute contraindications** formally listed in labeling, but the following are strong precautions treated as contraindications in practice:
  - Active tuberculosis (untreated or inadequately treated)
  - Active bacterial/fungal/viral infections of moderate-to-severe severity
  - Severe hypersensitivity reaction to adalimumab or any component of the formulation

## 5. Warnings and Precautions

### BLACK BOX WARNINGS

**Warning 1: Serious Infections**
Patients treated with HUMIRA are at increased risk for developing serious infections that may lead to hospitalization or death. These infections include:
- Active tuberculosis (including reactivation of latent TB)
- Invasive fungal infections (histoplasmosis, coccidioidomycosis, candidiasis, aspergillosis, blastomycosis, pneumocystosis)
- Bacterial infections (including Legionella and Listeria)
- Viral infections

**Pre-treatment screening required**:
- Tuberculosis: Tuberculin skin test (TST/PPD) OR Interferon Gamma Release Assay (IGRA; QuantiFERON-TB Gold or T-SPOT.TB)
- Hepatitis B: HBsAg, anti-HBs, anti-HBc total before initiating therapy
- If latent TB positive: Complete 9-month course of isoniazid (INH) before starting HUMIRA (or 4-month rifampin alternative)
- HBV carriers: Consult hepatology; anti-viral prophylaxis often required

**Warning 2: Malignancies**
Malignancies, some fatal, have been reported in patients receiving TNF blockers including HUMIRA:
- Lymphoma (including rare post-transplant lymphoproliferative disorder): Risk may be increased vs. general population; however, RA itself carries elevated lymphoma risk
- Leukemia: Rare cases reported; causality unclear
- Hepatosplenic T-cell lymphoma (HSTCL): Very rare but often fatal; primarily in adolescents and young adults with IBD on concomitant azathioprine or 6-mercaptopurine
- Non-melanoma skin cancers (NMSC): Squamous cell and basal cell carcinoma risk slightly elevated; regular skin examinations recommended
- Melanoma: Cases reported; unclear causality
- Solid tumors: No significant increase observed in large observational studies

## 6. Full Warnings & Precautions (Additional)

| Warning/Precaution | Description |
|-------------------|------------|
| Hepatitis B Reactivation | HBV carriers must be monitored; antiviral prophylaxis required in select patients; rare cases of fatal HBV reactivation |
| Demyelinating Disease | New onset or exacerbation of CNS demyelinating disease (MS, Guillain-Barré) reported; exercise caution; do not use in patients with MS |
| Hematological Reactions | Rare cases of pancytopenia, aplastic anemia; advise patients to seek medical attention for persistent fever, bruising, bleeding |
| Heart Failure | Worsening or new-onset heart failure reported; exercise caution in patients with CHF Class III–IV; avoid use in severe CHF |
| Lupus-Like Syndrome | Drug-induced lupus: rare; clinical features include arthritis, rash, serositis; ANA/anti-dsDNA elevation; usually resolves with discontinuation |
| Hypersensitivity Reactions | Anaphylaxis and serious allergic reactions reported; medications for hypersensitivity treatment should be available |
| Live Vaccines | Avoid live vaccines during HUMIRA treatment; 3–6 month washout period after stopping HUMIRA before live vaccines recommended |
| Surgery | Consider risk of infection; may need to hold therapy perioperatively (typically 1–2 dosing intervals before major surgery) |
| Psoriasis | New onset or worsening paradoxical psoriasis/psoriasiform lesions reported during TNF therapy |

## 8. Drug Interactions

| Drug/Class | Interaction | Recommendation |
|-----------|------------|---------------|
| Abatacept | Increased serious infections and no added efficacy; do NOT combine | Contraindicated combination |
| Anakinra (IL-1Ra) | Increased risk of serious infections and neutropenia | Avoid combination |
| Live vaccines | Immune response may be impaired; transmission of infection | Avoid during HUMIRA treatment |
| Methotrexate | Reduces anti-adalimumab antibody formation; additive efficacy | Standard combination in RA/PsA; monitor LFTs |
| Azathioprine / 6-MP | Risk of HSTCL in IBD patients (young/adolescent); | Caution in young IBD patients; inform of HSTCL risk |
| CYP450 enzymes | Adalimumab may affect CYP1A2, CYP2C9 via IL-6 normalization | Monitor warfarin, theophylline levels in patients on these drugs |
| Other biologics | Additive immunosuppression; serious infection risk | Avoid biologic-to-biologic combinations without washout |

## 9. Special Populations

### Pregnancy
- **Category**: Based on mechanism, adalimumab can cross the placenta (third trimester); fetal exposure documented
- **Post-market data**: OTIS (Organization of Teratology Information Specialists) cohort: No increased rate of major birth defects vs. disease-matched controls in RA or IBD patients
- **Recommendation**: Benefits of treating active maternal disease (RA, IBD) generally outweigh risks; IBD flare in pregnancy carries independent fetal risk
- **Neonatal precaution**: Adalimumab detectable in neonates up to 4 months post-delivery; live vaccines (e.g., rotavirus) should be deferred for 4–6 months after birth in infants exposed in utero

### Renal Impairment
- No dedicated renal impairment studies; adalimumab is metabolically catabolized; renal excretion minimal
- No dose adjustment required based on population PK data

### Hepatic Impairment
- No formal hepatic impairment studies; no dose adjustment recommended
- Monitor LFTs particularly with concomitant MTX

### Geriatric Patients
- Higher frequency of serious infections in elderly (≥65 years); greater caution warranted
- No pharmacokinetic differences by age; no dose adjustment required

### Pediatric Patients (By Indication)

| Ulcerative Colitis | 5 years |
| Plaque Psoriasis | 4 years |
| Uveitis | 2 years |
| HS | 12 years |

## 10. REMS Program

HUMIRA does NOT currently require a Risk Evaluation and Mitigation Strategy (REMS) from FDA.

**Required Patient Communication Programs**:
- **Medication Guide**: Required at each dispensing; covers serious infection, malignancy, and TB risks
- **Patient Wallet Card**: Provided to all patients; instructs to inform HCPs and emergency responders of HUMIRA use before surgery, travel to endemic fungal infection areas, or at signs of infection
