## 3. PRIOR AUTHORIZATION CRITERIA

### 3.1 Standard PA Criteria — Atopic Dermatitis (Commercial, Majority of Plans)
1. **Diagnosis**: ICD-10 L20.x (Atopic dermatitis) confirmed by board-certified dermatologist or allergist
2. **Disease severity**: Documentation of moderate-to-severe AD (IGA ≥3 OR EASI ≥16 OR BSA ≥10%)
3. **Age criteria**: Patient meets approved age group (≥6 months)
4. **Conventional therapy failure**: Documented failure, intolerance, or contraindication to ≥2 topical therapies (typically TCS Class I/II + TCI or PDE4i)
5. **Prescriber type**: Prescription by dermatologist, allergist/immunologist, or specialist (some plans exclude PCP)
6. **Renewal criteria**: Clinical documentation of response (IGA improvement ≥1 grade, EASI improvement ≥50%, or HCP attestation of meaningful benefit)

### 3.2 Standard PA Criteria — Asthma (Commercial)
1. **Diagnosis**: ICD-10 J45.x (Asthma, moderate-to-severe) confirmed by pulmonologist or allergist
2. **Phenotype documentation**: Blood eosinophil count ≥300 cells/μL (result within 12 months) OR OCS-dependent severe asthma
3. **Controller therapy requirement**: Patient on ≥2 controller medications including ICS + LABA at medium-to-high dose
4. **Exacerbation history**: ≥2 severe exacerbations in past 12 months requiring systemic steroids, or OCS-dependent
5. **Prescriber**: Pulmonologist, allergist/immunologist, or specialist

### 3.3 Standard PA Criteria — COPD (Emerging; Most Plans)
1. **Diagnosis**: ICD-10 J44.x (COPD) confirmed by pulmonologist
2. **Biomarker**: Blood eosinophil count ≥300 cells/μL documented within 12 months (critical new requirement)
3. **Existing therapy**: On LAMA + LABA + ICS (triple therapy) with documented inadequate control
4. **Exacerbation history**: ≥2 moderate or ≥1 severe exacerbation in past 12 months
5. **Smoking status**: Current or ex-smoker with ≥10 pack-year history
6. **Prescriber**: Pulmonologist only (many plans; some allow specialist)

## 4. STEP THERAPY REQUIREMENTS

### 4.1 AD Step Therapy by Plan Type
| Plan Type | Step Therapy Requirements |
|---|---|
| Most commercial plans | ≥2 topical therapies failed (TCS + 1 other); some require documented TCI failure |
| United Healthcare | Failure of medium-to-high potency TCS + 1 of: TCI, crisaborole, or wet wrapping × 3 months |
| Medicaid (select states) | Cyclosporine (where available) or methotrexate failure required prior to biologics |
| Medicare Part D | Varies; some plans require TCS + TCI + 1 systemic agent |

### 4.2 Streamlining Step Therapy — Practice Tools
- **AD Step Therapy Checklist** (available in MyWay portal): guides documentation of prior conventional therapy failures
- **DUPIXENT MyWay Case Manager**: handles payer-specific step therapy documentation on behalf of practice
- **Exception Process**: State step therapy exception laws (21+ states have laws) allow exceptions for patients where conventional therapy is medically contraindicated or inappropriate

## 8. PAYER VALUE STORY — HEALTH ECONOMIC EVIDENCE

### 8.1 Cost-Effectiveness Data
- **QALY analysis (AD)**: Cost per QALY gained vs conventional therapy: ~$65,000–$85,000 (US); within accepted WTP thresholds
- **OCS elimination (asthma)**: Elimination of systemic steroid use saves ~$8,000/patient/year in COPD/osteoporosis/fracture costs (published HE model, 2022)
- **Sinus surgery avoidance (CRSwNP)**: SINUS-52 data: 36% absolute reduction in sinus surgery; average sinus surgery cost ~$8,000–$15,000 (outpatient) to ~$25,000 (inpatient); net cost offset partially offsetting biologic cost
- **COPD hospitalization reduction**: BOREAS -30% exacerbation reduction; COPD hospitalization avg cost ~$22,000/admission; 1–2 hospitalizations avoided/year per patient provides significant offset

### 8.2 Budget Impact Model (BIM)
Available for payer presentation: 100,000 member plan, 5-year model
- Demonstrates net cost impact of Dupixent formulary placement vs step-therapy only
- Shows downstream healthcare utilization savings (ER visits, hospitalizations, surgeries, OCS complications)
