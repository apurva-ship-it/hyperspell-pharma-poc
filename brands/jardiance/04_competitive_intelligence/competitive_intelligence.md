## 1. Competitive Landscape Overview

The SGLT2 inhibitor class now spans three therapeutic areas: type 2 diabetes, heart failure, and chronic kidney disease. Three major agents compete: empagliflozin (JARDIANCE), dapagliflozin (Farxiga), and canagliflozin (Invokana, with generic). JARDIANCE holds the strongest overall clinical evidence position, with differentiated CV mortality benefit and the broadest HF + CKD approvals.

**Trend:** JARDIANCE maintains dominant SGLT2 market share but Farxiga is growing faster on the strength of HFrEF, HFpEF (DELIVER), and CKD (DAPA-CKD) approvals. Invokana continues to decline due to amputation BBW and generic competition.

## 2. Competitor Profiles

### Competitor 1: Farxiga (Dapagliflozin) — AstraZeneca

#### Product Overview
| Attribute | Details |
|-----------|---------|
| Generic name | Dapagliflozin |
| Company | AstraZeneca |
| Mechanism | SGLT2 inhibitor (IC50 ~1.1nM; more potent SGLT2 inhibitor than empagliflozin in vitro) |
| Dose | 10mg QD (HF, CKD); 5mg/10mg QD (T2D) |
| Formulation | Tablets: 5mg, 10mg |
| Approved indications | T2D glycemic; HFrEF (DAPA-HF); HFpEF (DELIVER); CKD (DAPA-CKD); Type 1 diabetes (EU) |

#### Key Farxiga Clinical Data
| Trial | Setting | Primary Endpoint | HR | p-value | Notes |
|-------|---------|-----------------|-----|---------|-------|
| DECLARE-TIMI 58 | T2D + CVD/CVD risk factors | 3P-MACE / CV death + HF hosp | 3P-MACE HR 0.93 (NS); **CV death + HF hosp HR 0.83** | NS / **p=0.005** | **No CV mortality benefit** (unlike JARDIANCE) |
| DAPA-HF | HFrEF (LVEF <40%) | CV death + HF worsening | **HR 0.74** | **p<0.001** | Comparable to EMPEROR-Reduced (HR 0.75) |
| DELIVER | HFpEF (LVEF >40%) | CV death + HF worsening | **HR 0.82** | **p<0.001** | Later than EMPEROR-Preserved (HR 0.79); slightly weaker HR |
| DAPA-CKD | CKD (eGFR 25–75) + T2D or non-T2D | eGFR decline + ESRD + CV/renal death | **HR 0.61** | **p<0.001** | Minimum eGFR 25 vs JARDIANCE eGFR 20 |
| DAPA-HF T1D | HFrEF in T1D | HF events | Benefit observed | — | T1D label not available in US |

#### Farxiga Strengths vs JARDIANCE
- **DAPA-CKD HR 0.61:** Numerically stronger primary composite HR than EMPA-KIDNEY (0.72) — important in nephrologist discussions
- **DAPA-HF and DELIVER:** Two separate dedicated HF trials; data package comparable depth to EMPEROR
- **T1D label (EU, Canada):** Approved in T1D in several markets; Farxiga captures T1D population that JARDIANCE does not address
- **Earlier HF approvals:** DAPA-HF approved August 2020 (same month as EMPEROR-Reduced); DELIVER 2022

#### Farxiga Weaknesses vs JARDIANCE
- **No CV mortality benefit:** DECLARE-TIMI 58 did NOT show significant CV mortality benefit (HR 0.98, NS); JARDIANCE CV death HR 0.62 is a unique and powerful differentiator
- **Minimum eGFR 25 (DAPA-CKD):** Cannot be used (per study criteria) in eGFR 20–25; JARDIANCE's EMPA-KIDNEY included eGFR ≥20 — broader CKD population
- **No T2D+CVD CV mortality label:** DECLARE did not have same CVD-enriched population as EMPA-REG; JARDIANCE has specific CV mortality reduction indication in T2D+CVD
- **Higher amputation risk signal in some analyses** (less significant than canagliflozin but tracked post-CANVAS): JARDIANCE has no amputation signal
- **DELIVER primary HR 0.82 vs EMPEROR-Preserved HR 0.79:** Numerically less robust HFpEF benefit

#### Jardiance + GLP-1 Combination Strategy
ADA/EASD 2023 guidelines recommend considering BOTH SGLT2 inhibitor AND GLP-1 RA in high-CV-risk T2D patients. JARDIANCE should be positioned as the **essential base** of cardiometabolic therapy, with GLP-1 RA added for additional weight loss, glycemic control, and potential ASCVD risk reduction.

## 4. Battle Cards

### Battle Card A: JARDIANCE vs Farxiga — CV Mortality

**Objection:** "Farxiga is just as good as JARDIANCE and the data packages are similar. Why should I use JARDIANCE?"

**Response Framework:**
1. **Acknowledge parity in HF:** "You're right that in heart failure, DAPA-HF and EMPEROR-Reduced show comparable results (HR 0.74 vs 0.75). The HF data are genuinely similar."
2. **CV mortality differentiator:** "Where JARDIANCE stands alone is cardiovascular mortality. In EMPA-REG OUTCOME, JARDIANCE reduced CV death by 38% (HR 0.62, p<0.001). In DECLARE-TIMI 58, Farxiga showed no significant CV mortality benefit (HR 0.98, p=0.78). This is not a subtle difference — JARDIANCE has a dedicated FDA-approved indication for CV mortality reduction in T2D+CVD. Farxiga does NOT."
3. **CKD breadth:** "In CKD, JARDIANCE's EMPA-KIDNEY included patients with eGFR as low as 20. DAPA-CKD had a minimum eGFR of 25. For your most severe CKD patients, JARDIANCE has the evidence and the label."
4. **Close:** "For your high-CV-risk T2D patients, or patients where reducing the risk of dying from heart disease is the priority, JARDIANCE is the only SGLT2 inhibitor with proven, FDA-labeled CV mortality benefit."

---

### Battle Card B: JARDIANCE vs Canagliflozin/Generic — Amputation

**Objection:** "My pharmacy benefit now covers generic canagliflozin at a lower co-pay. Can I switch to that?"

**Response Framework:**
1. **Safety concern:** "Generic canagliflozin carries the same Black Box Warning that Invokana carries — a doubling of lower extremity amputation risk (HR ~2x in CANVAS). For any patient with peripheral artery disease, neuropathy, prior foot ulcers, or any amputation risk, that's a serious safety concern that has no equivalent with JARDIANCE."
2. **No BBW:** "JARDIANCE has no Black Box Warning. No amputation signal has been detected in any JARDIANCE trial — EMPA-REG, EMPEROR-Reduced, EMPEROR-Preserved, EMPA-KIDNEY — across over 25,000 patients."
3. **CV mortality:** "Canagliflozin has never demonstrated significant CV mortality benefit. JARDIANCE has FDA-approved CV mortality reduction in T2D+CVD — a meaningfully better cardiovascular profile."
4. **HEOR context:** "When you factor in the cost of managing a lower extremity amputation — hospitalization, rehabilitation, prosthetics, long-term disability — the cost savings from generic canagliflozin are quickly eroded by the downstream consequences of that amputation risk."

---

### Battle Card C: HFpEF — Establishing JARDIANCE Leadership

**Objection:** "I've never really treated HFpEF with anything. What does JARDIANCE actually do in HFpEF?"

**Response Framework:**
1. **Historic context:** "Until EMPEROR-Preserved was published in 2021, there was no drug approved for HFpEF. It was a condition we managed symptoms but couldn't modify the course of disease. JARDIANCE changed that."
2. **EMPEROR-Preserved data:** "In 5,988 HFpEF patients across the full EF range >40%, JARDIANCE + standard of care reduced the primary composite of CV death and HF hospitalization by 21% (HR 0.79, p<0.001). Hospitalization for HF specifically was reduced by 29%."
3. **Practical patient impact:** "That means for every 100 HFpEF patients you treat with JARDIANCE for 26 months, roughly 3–4 fewer patients will be hospitalized for heart failure or die from CV causes."
4. **Guideline endorsement:** "ACC/AHA 2022 HF guidelines now give JARDIANCE a Class 2a recommendation in HFpEF — the first pharmacological agent to reach any guideline recommendation in HFpEF."
5. **Ease of initiation:** "10mg once daily, no QTc monitoring, no thyroid concerns. Start it at the first visit."
