**Key Insight:** CV mortality benefit (EMPA-REG HR 0.62) and safety profile (no BBW) are the top two JARDIANCE differentiators. Cost/formulary access is canagliflozin's only competitive advantage.

### Specialty-Specific Decision Drivers

#### Cardiologists
- **Primary driver:** CV mortality reduction (EMPA-REG) and HF data (EMPEROR)
- **Barrier to initiation:** "JARDIANCE is a diabetes drug — my job is the heart, not the pancreas"
- **Emerging shift:** 2022 ACC/AHA guidelines give SGLT2 inhibitors Class 1 and 2a recommendations in HF; now cardiologists increasingly initiate JARDIANCE independent of endocrinology
- **Key message need:** "JARDIANCE is a cardiovascular drug with glucose-lowering as a secondary benefit"

#### Nephrologists
- **Primary driver:** eGFR protection; EMPA-KIDNEY 28% RRR in progression
- **Barrier to initiation:** Historic concern about initiating SGLT2 with low eGFR (now resolved by label changes and EMPA-KIDNEY eGFR ≥20 data)
- **Competitor concern:** DAPA-CKD HR 0.61 (numerically better than EMPA-KIDNEY 0.72) — nephrologists may cite this
- **Key message need:** EMPA-KIDNEY eGFR ≥20 inclusion; broader eligible CKD population than Farxiga

#### Primary Care Physicians
- **Primary driver:** Simplicity, guideline compliance, patient co-pay card
- **Barrier:** UTI/genital mycotic infection counseling complexity; DKA sick-day rules
- **Key message need:** Co-pay card enrollment; ADA guideline context; safety management protocol
- **DTC influence:** JARDIANCE DTC advertising drives patient-initiated conversations in PCP offices

## 3. Patient Journey

### T2D + Cardiovascular Disease Patient Journey
```
T2D Diagnosis (average age 56)
    ↓
Initial therapy: Metformin ± lifestyle
    ↓ [~2–5 years]
CV Risk Assessment:
    - Established CVD (prior MI/stroke/ACS) → HIGH CV risk
    - Multiple CV risk factors without established CVD → HIGH CV risk
    ↓
Treatment Intensification Discussion:
    - ADA guideline: If T2D + established CVD → SGLT2 inhibitor (or GLP-1 RA) with CV mortality benefit
    - JARDIANCE: the ONLY SGLT2 with CV mortality benefit (EMPA-REG HR 0.62)
    ↓
JARDIANCE Initiation (10mg QD):
    - Glycemic benefit: -0.6% HbA1c
    - Weight loss: ~2–3 kg
    - BP reduction: ~3–4 mmHg systolic
    - CV protection begins within 3 months (EMPA-REG early separation of KM curves)
    ↓ [Ongoing — years of treatment]
Long-term outcomes:
    - CV death risk reduced 38%
    - HF hospitalization risk reduced 35%
    - Renal protection ongoing (eGFR decline slowed)
```

### Heart Failure Patient Journey (HFpEF)
```
New-Onset Dyspnea / Exertional Limitation in Patient >65 years
    ↓
Echocardiogram: LVEF >50%; BNP elevated
    ↓
HFpEF Diagnosis (NYHA Class II–III)
    ↓
Prior Treatment Path (pre-JARDIANCE era):
    - Diuretics for congestion
    - Treat underlying comorbidities (hypertension, diabetes, AFib)
    - No disease-modifying therapy available → No approved drugs for HFpEF
    ↓
Post-EMPEROR-Preserved era (2022):
    - JARDIANCE 10mg QD added to standard of care
    - Primary composite CV death/HF hospitalization ↓21% (HR 0.79)
    - HF hospitalizations ↓29%
    - KCCQ quality of life improved
    - **First and only FDA-approved drug for HFpEF**
    ↓
Patient maintained on JARDIANCE + diuretics ± ARNI (sacubitril/valsartan in selected HFpEF subgroups)
```

### CKD Patient Journey
```
CKD Detected (eGFR 20–60, ± Proteinuria)
    ↓
Nephrology Referral at eGFR <45
    ↓
Current Standard: RAAS blockade (ACEi/ARB); BP control; protein restriction
    ↓
EMPA-KIDNEY Data (2023):
    - Add JARDIANCE 10mg QD on top of standard of care
    - Primary composite (progression + CV death) ↓28% (HR 0.72)
    - ESRD + sustained ≥40% eGFR decline ↓29%
    - Benefit in eGFR 20–45 AND in non-diabetic CKD
    ↓
Ongoing monitoring: eGFR, UACR, potassium (with ACEi/ARB)
    - Initial eGFR dip (~5%) upon JARDIANCE initiation is expected and reversible
    - Long-term trajectory: eGFR preservation vs. faster decline without JARDIANCE
```

## 6. Market Research: Attitudinal Insights

### Focus Group Findings — Cardiologists (2023, n=28 cardiologists)

**On JARDIANCE for HFpEF:**
- "EMPEROR-Preserved was genuinely surprising. Nothing had ever worked in HFpEF before. I've started putting all my HFpEF patients on JARDIANCE."
- "The non-diabetic data is crucial. I don't prescribe antidiabetics. But JARDIANCE is a heart drug that happens to lower glucose."
- "I was initially nervous about starting 'a diabetes drug' in my patients without diabetes. After reading the trial data, I get it now — it's a hemodynamic drug."
- "The ACC guidelines giving it a 2a recommendation in HFpEF sealed it for me. That's my permission to prescribe it."

**On JARDIANCE for HFrEF:**
- "DAPA-HF and EMPEROR-Reduced are essentially the same result. I was already using JARDIANCE, so I haven't switched to Farxiga."
- "The CV mortality benefit in EMPA-REG makes JARDIANCE my default SGLT2. Farxiga doesn't have that."

**On Competitive Differentiation:**
- "The Farxiga DAPA-CKD HR is 0.61 — better than EMPA-KIDNEY. My nephrology colleagues cite this."
- "Invokana? I wouldn't touch it with a ten-foot pole. The amputation thing settled that."

### Focus Group Findings — Nephrologists (2023, n=22 nephrologists)

**On JARDIANCE:**
- "EMPA-KIDNEY going down to eGFR 20 was a big deal. I have patients in the 20–30 range that I now feel I can treat."
- "The HR in DAPA-CKD was 0.61, and EMPA-KIDNEY was 0.72. Farxiga's number looks better on paper. But JARDIANCE includes my sicker patients."
- "We've been starving for a drug that actually prevents dialysis. Now we have two — JARDIANCE and Farxiga."
- "Educating patients to hold it before procedures is the hardest part. We've built a protocol into our pre-procedure checklist."
