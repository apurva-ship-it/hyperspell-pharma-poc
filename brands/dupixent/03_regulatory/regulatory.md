## 1. APPROVED INDICATIONS — FULL LABEL TEXT (US FDA)

### 1.1 Atopic Dermatitis
DUPIXENT is indicated for the treatment of **moderate-to-severe atopic dermatitis** in patients 6 months of age and older whose disease is not adequately controlled with topical therapies or when those therapies are not advisable. DUPIXENT can be used with or without topical corticosteroids.

### 1.2 Asthma
DUPIXENT is indicated as an **add-on maintenance treatment** of patients with **moderate-to-severe asthma aged 6 years and older with an eosinophilic phenotype** or with oral corticosteroid-dependent asthma.

*Limitation of use: Not for relief of acute bronchospasm or status asthmaticus.*

### 1.3 Chronic Rhinosinusitis with Nasal Polyps (CRSwNP)
DUPIXENT is indicated as an **add-on maintenance treatment** in adult patients with **inadequately controlled chronic rhinosinusitis with nasal polyps (CRSwNP)**.

### 1.4 Eosinophilic Esophagitis (EoE)
DUPIXENT is indicated for the treatment of **eosinophilic esophagitis (EoE)** in patients 1 year of age and older weighing at least 15 kg.

### 1.5 Prurigo Nodularis (PN)
DUPIXENT is indicated for the treatment of **prurigo nodularis** in adults.

### 1.6 Chronic Obstructive Pulmonary Disease (COPD)
DUPIXENT is indicated as an **add-on maintenance treatment** of adults with **inadequately controlled chronic obstructive pulmonary disease (COPD) with an eosinophilic phenotype** (blood eosinophil count ≥300 cells/μL).

### 1.7 Alopecia Areata
DUPIXENT is indicated for the treatment of **alopecia areata** in adults and pediatric patients 12 years of age and older with refractory, moderate-to-severe disease.

### 2.4 Administration Instructions
- Administer by subcutaneous injection into the abdomen (except 2-inch area around navel), thigh, or upper arm (if by caregiver)
- Rotate injection sites; do not inject into skin that is tender, bruised, red, hard, scarred, or affected by psoriasis or rash
- Allow prefilled syringe/pen to reach room temperature (30–45 minutes) before injection
- Available as 200 mg/1.14 mL or 300 mg/2 mL prefilled syringe or SYRIS autoinjector
- Missed dose: administer as soon as possible; then resume scheduled dose from date of last injection

## 3. CONTRAINDICATIONS

**Known hypersensitivity** to dupilumab or any of its excipients.
*(No additional contraindications — notably no cardiac, hepatic, renal, or hematologic contraindications)*

## 4. WARNINGS AND PRECAUTIONS

### 4.1 Hypersensitivity Reactions
- Rare cases of anaphylaxis, serum sickness, and serum sickness-like reactions have been reported
- If serious or hypersensitivity reaction occurs, discontinue DUPIXENT and institute appropriate therapy
- Anaphylaxis reported in <0.1% of patients in clinical trials

### 4.2 Conjunctivitis and Keratitis
- **Conjunctivitis** is the most common adverse event of special interest
- Higher incidence in AD patients: 10–28% (dupilumab) vs 2–4% (placebo) — dose and indication dependent
- Lower incidence in asthma: ~2–3%; minimal in CRSwNP, COPD, and other indications
- Most cases are mild-to-moderate; respond to topical ophthalmic treatments
- Keratitis reported rarely; patients should be advised to seek ophthalmologic evaluation if symptoms develop
- No serious vision loss reported in clinical trial program

### 4.3 Eosinophilia
- Treatment-emergent blood eosinophilia (>500 cells/μL) reported, particularly in patients with underlying asthma
- Rare cases of eosinophilic pneumonia reported; monitor patients with unexplained pulmonary symptoms
- Dupilumab should not be discontinued abruptly in asthma patients without medical supervision

### 4.4 Parasitic (Helminth) Infections
- IL-4 and IL-13 are involved in host defense against certain parasitic infections
- Treat patients with pre-existing helminth infections before initiating dupilumab
- If patients become infected while on dupilumab and do not respond to anthelmintic treatment, discontinue until infection resolves

### 4.5 Vaccinations
- Avoid use of live vaccines while on dupilumab treatment
- All age-appropriate vaccines according to current immunization guidelines should be administered prior to initiation
- Non-live vaccines administered during dupilumab treatment demonstrated adequate immune response

### **NO BLACK BOX WARNING** — Dupilumab does NOT carry a BBW for serious infections, malignancies, thromboembolism, or cardiovascular events.

## 6. DRUG INTERACTIONS

### 6.1 Formal Drug Interactions
| Drug/Class | Interaction | Clinical Recommendation |
|---|---|---|
| Live vaccines | Potential risk of infection | Avoid concurrent use |
| CYP450 substrates with narrow therapeutic index | IL-4/IL-13 blockade may normalize CYP450 enzyme levels previously altered by inflammation; potential changes in substrate levels | Monitor levels of narrow TI drugs (e.g., warfarin, cyclosporine) at initiation or discontinuation |

### 6.2 Notable Absence of Interactions
- **No CYP450 enzyme induction or inhibition** by dupilumab itself (it is a biologic, not small molecule)
- **No drug-drug interactions** with standard asthma controllers (ICS, LABA, LAMA, montelukast)
- **No interactions** with common dermatologic agents, oral antihistamines, or topical corticosteroids
- **No interaction** with EGFR inhibitors, TKIs, or oncology regimens (relevant for COPD patients with concurrent malignancy)

## 7. USE IN SPECIAL POPULATIONS

### 7.1 Renal Impairment
- Population PK analysis shows no clinically significant effect of mild-to-moderate renal impairment on dupilumab PK
- **No dose adjustment required** for mild, moderate, or severe renal impairment
- Limited data in severe renal impairment; use with caution

### 7.2 Hepatic Impairment
- Population PK analysis shows no significant effect of mild-to-moderate hepatic impairment
- **No dose adjustment required** for any degree of hepatic impairment
- Dupilumab is an IgG4 antibody; not metabolized by hepatic CYP450 system

### 7.3 Pediatric Patients
- Approved and validated in infants as young as **6 months** (atopic dermatitis)
- Pharmacokinetic data support weight-based dosing; exposure parameters (AUC, Cmax) comparable to adults
- No pediatric-specific safety signals beyond those observed in adults
- Long-term growth and development data ongoing in LIBERTY AD INFANT long-term extension

### 7.4 Geriatric Patients
- Population PK analysis shows no clinically significant age effect on dupilumab exposure (18–80+ years)
- **No dose adjustment required** based on age alone
- Efficacy and safety consistent across age subgroups; limited data in patients >80 years

### 7.5 Pregnancy
- **Pregnancy Category**: No FDA letter category assigned (post-2015 labeling format)
- Human IgG antibodies are known to cross the placental barrier; dupilumab could theoretically be transmitted to fetus
- Available data from pharmacovigilance and pregnancy registries are insufficient to establish risk
- Animal studies: no evidence of fetal harm at exposures up to 10× human dose
- **DUPIXENT Pregnancy Registry** (1-877-311-8972): healthcare providers should enroll patients who become pregnant
- Risk-benefit discussion recommended; consider discontinuation during pregnancy unless benefit clearly outweighs risk

### 7.6 Lactation
- Unknown whether dupilumab is excreted in human breast milk
- Human IgG is present in breast milk at low levels; clinical relevance unknown
- Developmental and health benefits of breastfeeding should be considered along with maternal need
