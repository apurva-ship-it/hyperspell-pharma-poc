## 1. Competitive Landscape Overview

Skyrizi competes across four distinct therapeutic areas, each with its own competitive dynamics. The immunology biologics market is among the most competitive in pharmaceuticals. Skyrizi's competitive moat derives from three structural advantages:

1. **Selective IL-23p19 mechanism** — Superior clinical efficacy vs. ustekinumab (IL-12/23) and TNF inhibitors
2. **No IBD contraindication** — Unlike IL-17 inhibitors; unique cross-indication safety profile
3. **No Black Box Warning** — Unlike JAK inhibitors; prescribers and payers increasingly concerned about JAK safety signals

**Battle Card vs. Tremfya:**

- No head-to-head Skyrizi vs. guselkumab trial; rely on cross-trial comparisons cautiously
- PASI 100 ("Nothing is Everything") is the narrative anchor — complete clearance matters to patients and dermatologists

## 5. Competitive Battle Cards (Quick Reference)

### vs. Ustekinumab (Stelara / biosimilars)
> "Ustekinumab is a proven therapy — but SEQUENCE showed risankizumab achieved more than double the endoscopic remission rate in Crohn's disease (31.8% vs. 16.2%, p=0.0003). And in psoriasis, UltIMMa-1/2 showed 75% vs. 42–47% PASI 90. Ustekinumab is the gold standard — Skyrizi is what surpasses it."

### vs. IL-17 inhibitors in PsO+IBD patient
> "Secukinumab and ixekizumab are contraindicated in IBD. For your psoriasis patient with concurrent Crohn's, personal IBD history, or family history — there is only one IL-23 pathway option that is also approved and effective in IBD: Skyrizi."

### vs. JAK inhibitors (upadacitinib)
> "Upadacitinib achieves high remission rates — but with a Black Box Warning for MACE, serious infections, and malignancy. For patients who are older, have cardiovascular risk factors, or are smokers, many physicians and payers are hesitant on JAK inhibitors. Skyrizi has no BBW — and head-to-head data showing superiority over ustekinumab."

### vs. Bimekizumab (IL-17A/F)
> "Bimekizumab has impressive early clearance data. But IBD safety with dual IL-17A/F inhibition is not established — and for your patients with psoriasis and GI comorbidities, that question matters. Skyrizi is the only IL-23 inhibitor approved across the full IBD spectrum."
