## 3. PRIOR AUTHORIZATION CRITERIA

### 3.1 Standard PA Criteria — 1L Metastatic NSCLC

1. **Diagnosis**: ICD-10 C34.xx (NSCLC, specified site) or C34.90 (NSCLC, unspecified) confirmed by biopsy/pathology
2. **Histology**: Non-small cell lung cancer (adenocarcinoma, squamous, large cell — adenocarcinoma most common for EGFR)
3. **EGFR Mutation**: Positive result for EGFR Ex19del or L858R on FDA-approved companion diagnostic test (must specify test name and result)
4. **Line of therapy**: First-line treatment (no prior systemic therapy for advanced/metastatic disease)
5. **Prescriber**: Board-certified medical oncologist or hematologist/oncologist
6. **NCCN alignment**: Documentation consistent with NCCN Category 1 recommendation
7. **Performance status**: ECOG 0–2 (most plans; PS 3 may require additional justification)

### 3.2 Standard PA Criteria — Adjuvant Post-Resection

1. **Diagnosis**: Resected NSCLC (Stage IB–IIIA); documentation of complete (R0) resection
2. **EGFR Mutation**: Positive for EGFR Ex19del or L858R on FDA-approved test (on resection specimen)
3. **Timing**: Must initiate within 10–26 weeks post-resection (per ADAURA eligibility criteria)
4. **No evidence of disease**: Documentation that imaging shows no residual/recurrent disease at adjuvant initiation
5. **Prescriber**: Medical oncologist or board-certified oncologist

### 3.3 Standard PA Criteria — 2L T790M+

1. **Diagnosis**: Advanced/metastatic NSCLC with confirmed EGFR T790M mutation (at disease progression)
2. **Prior therapy**: Documentation of prior EGFR TKI treatment (1st or 2nd generation) AND disease progression
3. **T790M Test**: Positive result from FDA-approved CDx for T790M (Guardant360 CDx liquid or cobas tissue)
4. **Prescriber**: Medical oncologist

### 3.4 PA Approval Rates (Tagrisso, FY2025)

| Indication | First-Pass Approval | Average Time to Approval | Appeal Success |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1L Metastatic | **93%** | 2–3 business days | 89% |
| Adjuvant | **84%** | 3–5 business days | 82% |
| 2L T790M+ | **91%** | 2–3 business days | 87% |

**Key insight**: Tagrisso has among the highest first-pass PA approval rates in oncology — driven by clear CDx requirement (molecular test documentation is often the only missing element in denials) and strong NCCN Category 1 alignment.

**Most Common PA Denial Reasons**:
1. Missing CDx test result documentation (44% of denials)
2. Prescriber not verified as oncologist (15%)
3. Incomplete staging/pathology documentation (21%)
4. Performance status documentation missing (12%)
5. Other administrative issues (8%)

## 4. BIOMARKER TESTING — MARKET ACCESS ANGLE

### 4.1 CDx Testing Coverage
All FDA-approved companion diagnostic tests for Tagrisso are covered under the Category B IDE or routine clinical use:
- FoundationOne CDx: Covered by Medicare (MAAA-specific codes); commercial coverage >90%
- cobas EGFR v2: Covered by Medicare and most commercial plans
- Guardant360 CDx (liquid biopsy): Medicare coverage via MolDX LCD; commercial varies (70–80% coverage)

### 4.2 Testing Coverage Barriers
| Barrier | Impact | Mitigation |
|---|---|---|
| Liquid biopsy (ctDNA) commercial coverage | ~20% of commercial plans without coverage | Tissue testing as alternative; AZ advocacy for ctDNA coverage |
| Insufficient tissue — repeat biopsy cost | Rebiopsy adds $3,000–$8,000 cost + 1–2 week delay | Liquid biopsy as complementary first-line test |
| Testing in community settings | Variable lab access; some community oncologists rely on send-out labs | AZ partnership with Foundation Medicine for community access |

## 8. PAYER VALUE STORY

### 8.1 OS Benefit — Payer-Focused Framing
- Tagrisso is the only 1L EGFR TKI with proven OS benefit — payers recognize that OS benefit corresponds to reduced downstream costs (fewer hospitalizations, supportive care regimens, hospice costs from early mortality)
- 5-year OS in ADAURA (adjuvant): 88% vs 78% — 10pp absolute OS improvement with a clearly defined 3-year treatment course

### 8.2 Adjuvant Budget Impact Considerations
- 3-year fixed treatment duration (ADAURA eligibility: 3 years maximum) — payers appreciate finite course vs indefinite maintenance
- Recurrence prevention: Average cost of recurrence management (chemo + subsequent targeted therapies + hospitalization) estimated $300,000–$500,000 per patient over 2–3 years
- Adjuvant Tagrisso net cost ~$150,000–$170,000/year × 3 years = ~$450,000–$510,000 vs cost of managing metastatic recurrence
