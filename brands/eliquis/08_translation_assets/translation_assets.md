## 1. Purpose and Scope

This document provides globally consistent core messages, regional regulatory adaptation guidance, and an approved terminology glossary for Eliquis across key markets. All market-specific materials require in-country MLR review. The BMS/Pfizer co-promotion structure means translation and regulatory submission responsibilities are divided by territory — BMS leads US/EU; Pfizer leads Japan/Asia/EM. Confirm territorial responsibility before initiating translation projects.

## 4. Regional Adaptation — European Union

### 4.1 Regulatory Context (EMA)

**EMA-Approved Indications:**
1. Prevention of stroke and systemic embolism in adult patients with NVAF
2. Treatment of DVT and PE, and prevention of recurrent DVT and PE in adults
3. Prevention of VTE in adults after elective hip or knee replacement surgery
4. Prevention of recurrent DVT and PE in adults

**Apixaban EMA approval:** May 2011 (VTE prophylaxis post-surgery); December 2012 (NVAF); August 2014 (VTE treatment)

**Key EU Regulatory Differences vs. US:**
- DTC advertising PROHIBITED across EU (Directive 2001/83/EC) — all consumer communication must be non-promotional
- EU label uses CHADS₂ and CHA₂DS₂-VASc references differently — refer to EMA SmPC
- Reversal agent: Andexxa received EMA conditional approval 2019; not universally stocked across EU
- EU dose reduction criteria identical to US: ≥2 of: age ≥80, weight ≤60kg, creatinine ≥1.5

## 5. Regional Adaptation — Japan

### 5.1 Regulatory Context (PMDA)

**Approved Indications (PMDA):**
- Prevention of stroke and systemic embolism in NVAF: 5mg BID (2.5mg BID if dose-reduction criteria)
- DVT/PE treatment: 10mg BID × 7d, then 5mg BID
- Post-surgical VTE prophylaxis (hip/knee): 2.5mg BID
- Extended VTE prevention: 2.5mg BID

**PMDA Approval:** July 2012 (NVAF); 2014 (VTE); all 4 indications approved

### 5.2 Japan-Specific Communication Notes
- Warfarin still used in ~30% of AF patients in Japan (vs. ~7% in US); conversion opportunity significant
- Japanese cardiologists are evidence-driven; KOL thought leader engagement critical
- J-RHYTHM Registry: Japanese AF patients on warfarin have lower TTR (~60%); DOAC benefit larger
- Dose: 2.5mg dose reduction criteria application in Japan — elderly Japanese patients often meet ≥2 criteria
- PMDA label uses identical dose-reduction criteria to US (age ≥80, weight ≤60kg, creatinine ≥1.5)
- Pfizer Japan handles promotion; BMS provides medical affairs support

## 6. Regional Adaptation — Emerging Markets

### 6.1 China (NMPA)

- Eliquis NMPA approved: 2013 (NVAF); 2014 (VTE); all indications approved
- Market context: ~9M patients with AF in China; warfarin dominates hospital formulary
- Distribution: Hospital formulary critical; community pharmacy secondary
- NMPA label: Consistent with global label; dose reduction criteria apply
- Key message: AF stroke prevention; direct data comparison vs. warfarin (ARISTOTLE); no INR monitoring (cost savings in China where INR monitoring is logistically challenging in rural areas)
- NDL inclusion: Eliquis included in National Drug List (NDL) 2023; significantly improved access
- Regional messaging: Emphasize total cost of care (Eliquis WAC vs. warfarin + INR monitoring + bleed event costs in Chinese hospital settings)

### 6.2 India (CDSCO)

- Eliquis CDSCO approved: 2012 (NVAF); 2014 (VTE)
- Market: Growing AF awareness; rheumatic heart disease (valvular) historically dominant — but NVAF population growing
- Price: INR 3,800/month (~$46); predominantly private out-of-pocket market
- Key message: Safety over warfarin (INR monitoring unreliable in many Indian settings); ICH reduction especially relevant in elderly Indian population
- Important: India has high rates of valvular AF (rheumatic mitral stenosis) — Eliquis NOT indicated for valvular AF; ensure clear communication of NVAF restriction

### 6.3 Brazil (ANVISA)

- Eliquis ANVISA approved: 2012 (all indications)
- Market: Growing private market; SUS (public) does not reimburse DOACs (warfarin on SUS)
- Price: BRL 900/month (~$180); private market only
- Portuguese-Brazil required; materials cannot use European Portuguese (Portugal uses different regulatory framework — INFARMED)
- Key message for Brazil: Physicians are research-literate; ARISTOTLE data resonates; "sem monitorização do INR" (no INR monitoring) is a patient convenience differentiator vs. warfarin in urban private practices

### 8.2 Non-Translatable Elements
- Brand name: Eliquis (registered trademark globally; do not translate)
- Apixaban (INN — invariant)
- Clinical trial names: ARISTOTLE, AMPLIFY, AMPLIFY-EXT, ADVANCE — do not translate
- ANDEXXA / Andexanet alfa — trademark; INN invariant
- Numeric data: p-values, HRs, confidence intervals — numeric, no translation
- ICD-10 codes: Universal; no translation needed

### 8.3 Quality Standards for Medical Translation
- All translations require certified medical translator with cardiology/anticoagulation background
- Back-translation required for promotional materials (not required for internal reference documents)
- In-country clinical reviewer sign-off required before MLR
- Version control: Assign market-specific document code (e.g., ELQ-DE-2025-KM-001)
- Shelf-life: MLR approval expires maximum 24 months; renew with label updates
