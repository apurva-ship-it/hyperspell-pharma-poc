# Entresto (Sacubitril/Valsartan) — Regulatory & Prescribing Information Reference
**Brand Owner:** Novartis Pharmaceuticals | **Document Version:** 2024.1 | **Classification:** Internal Regulatory Affairs | **Last PI Review:** March 2024

---

## 1. Approved Indication (US FDA Full Label Text)

### Current Approved Indication (Post-2021 Label Update)

Entresto is indicated to reduce the risk of cardiovascular death and hospitalization for heart failure in adult patients with chronic heart failure. Benefits are most clearly evident in patients with left ventricular ejection fraction (LVEF) below normal. In patients with LVEF ≥60%, effectiveness has not been established.

### Previous Indication (Pre-2021)
Entresto was indicated to reduce the risk of cardiovascular death and hospitalization for heart failure in patients with chronic heart failure (New York Heart Association [NYHA] Class II–IV) and reduced ejection fraction. It is usually administered in conjunction with other heart failure therapies, in place of an angiotensin-converting enzyme (ACE) inhibitor or other angiotensin II receptor blocker (ARB).

### Pediatric Indication (2019 Approval)
Entresto is indicated to reduce the risk of death and hospitalization in pediatric patients 1 year and older with symptomatic heart failure with systemic left ventricular systolic dysfunction.

### EU Approved Indication (EMA — European Medicines Agency)
Entresto is indicated in adult patients for treatment of symptomatic chronic heart failure with reduced ejection fraction. In adult patients who are NYHA class II–IV with LVEF ≤40%, Entresto is a substitution for an ACE-I or ARB.
*(EU label does not include the broader LVEF wording as of 2024; EMA review of broader indication ongoing)*

## 2. Dosing and Administration

### 2.1 Adult Dosing

**Starting Dose:**
- Standard initiation: 49/51 mg (sacubitril/valsartan) BID
- For patients not currently taking an ACE-I or ARB, or taking low-dose ACE-I/ARB: start at 24/26 mg BID
- For patients transitioning from ACE-I: mandatory 36-hour washout before first dose

**Target Dose:**
- 97/103 mg BID (double the starting standard dose)
- Titrate to target as tolerated, typically over 2–4 weeks
- Most patients reach target dose within 3–6 months

**Titration Schedule (recommended):**
| Week | Dose |
|------|------|
| 1–2 | 24/26 mg BID (if low-dose ACE-I/ARB) or 49/51 mg BID |
| 3–4 | Double dose if tolerated (assess BP, renal function, K+) |
| 5–6+ | 97/103 mg BID (target) |

**Dose Adjustment for Renal Impairment:**
- eGFR ≥30 mL/min/1.73m²: No dose adjustment required
- eGFR <30 mL/min/1.73m²: Starting dose 24/26 mg BID recommended; not studied below this threshold
- Dialysis patients: Use not recommended (insufficient data)

**Dose Adjustment for Hepatic Impairment:**
- Mild (Child-Pugh A): No adjustment
- Moderate (Child-Pugh B): 24/26 mg BID starting dose; use caution
- Severe (Child-Pugh C): Use not recommended

**Dose Adjustment for Elderly (≥75 years):**
- No mandatory adjustment; however, increased hypotension risk warrants starting at 24/26 mg BID

### 2.2 Pediatric Dosing

**Children ≥1 year:**

For patients <40 kg body weight (oral suspension):

| Body Weight | Starting Dose | Target Dose |
|-------------|--------------|-------------|
| 4.5 to <8 kg | 1.6 mg/kg BID | 2.3 mg/kg BID |
| 8 to <18 kg | 1.6 mg/kg BID | 3.1 mg/kg BID |
| 18 to <40 kg | 1.4 mg/kg BID | 2.7 mg/kg BID |

For patients ≥40 kg body weight (tablets): Same titration as adults starting at 49/51 mg BID; target 97/103 mg BID.

**Pediatric formulation:** 4 mg/mL oral suspension (sacubitril/valsartan ratio maintained)

### 2.3 Administration Notes
- Take with or without food
- Tablets should be swallowed whole; do not crush, cut, or chew
- Oral suspension: shake well before each use; use within 15 days of preparation
- If a dose is missed: take as soon as possible unless within 6 hours of next dose; then skip and resume schedule
- Store tablets at 25°C (77°F); excursions permitted to 15–30°C

## 3. Contraindications

1. **Concomitant use with ACE inhibitors:** Do not administer within 36 hours of switching from or to an ACE inhibitor. Concomitant use with ACE inhibitors is contraindicated due to risk of angioedema.

2. **History of angioedema related to previous ACE-I or ARB therapy:** Patients who have experienced angioedema with ACE-I therapy are at increased risk for angioedema with Entresto.

3. **Hypersensitivity:** Known hypersensitivity (e.g., anaphylaxis, angioedema) to any component of Entresto.

4. **Concomitant use with aliskiren in patients with diabetes:** Concomitant use with aliskiren-containing products in patients with diabetes mellitus is contraindicated. *(Also avoid in patients with renal impairment regardless of diabetes status.)*

## 4. Boxed Warning (Black Box Warning)

```
WARNING: FETAL TOXICITY

When pregnancy is detected, discontinue Entresto as soon as possible.
Drugs that act directly on the renin-angiotensin system can cause
injury and death to the developing fetus.
```

**Mechanism:** Both sacubitril and valsartan act on the renin-angiotensin system. Drugs of this class — ACE inhibitors and ARBs — are teratogenic and cause oligohydramnios, neonatal skull hypoplasia, anuria, hypotension, renal failure, and death. The valsartan component of Entresto confers the same fetal toxicity risk.

**Management:** Counsel patients of reproductive potential about contraception. If pregnancy is confirmed, discontinue immediately and consider alternative therapy. Neonatal monitoring required if exposure occurred in second or third trimester.

## 5. Warnings and Precautions

### 5.1 Angioedema
- Entresto can cause angioedema (including of face, lips, tongue, glottis, larynx)
- Risk is increased in patients with prior ACE-I–associated angioedema (absolute contraindication in this setting)
- Black patients have historically higher rates of ACE-I–induced angioedema; same caution applies
- Incidence in PARADIGM-HF: 0.45% (Entresto) vs. 0.24% (enalapril)
- Management: Discontinue immediately; provide emergency supportive care; do not rechallenge

### 5.2 Hypotension
- Symptomatic hypotension: 14.0% (Entresto) vs. 9.2% (enalapril) in PARADIGM-HF
- Risk is higher in volume-depleted patients, patients on diuretics, high-dose antihypertensives, or at low baseline SBP
- Before initiating: Correct volume depletion; assess baseline BP
- If hypotension occurs: Reduce diuretic dose, adjust concomitant antihypertensives; consider temporarily reducing Entresto dose

### 5.3 Hyperkalemia
- Risk increased in patients with renal impairment, diabetes, hypoaldosteronism, or on potassium supplements/potassium-sparing diuretics
- PARADIGM-HF: Hyperkalemia (K+ >5.5 mEq/L) 16.1% vs. 17.3%; serious hyperkalemia (>6.0) 4.3% vs. 5.6%
- Monitor serum potassium periodically; dose reduction or discontinuation if K+ >5.5 mEq/L persists

### 5.4 Impaired Renal Function
- Entresto may cause decreased renal function, acute renal failure, renal artery stenosis
- Monitor serum creatinine and eGFR periodically
- Patients with bilateral renal artery stenosis are at particular risk
- If significant renal function decline: Reduce dose or discontinue

### 5.5 NEP Inhibition and Amyloid-Beta Clearance (Theoretical CNS Concern)
- Neprilysin participates in degradation of amyloid-beta peptide in the brain
- Theoretical concern: Sustained NEP inhibition could impair amyloid-beta clearance and increase Alzheimer's risk
- Long-term evidence from PARADIGM-HF (27 months) and RWE studies to date have not demonstrated an increased rate of cognitive impairment or dementia vs. ACE-I
- Ongoing pharmacovigilance tracking; no label requirement for CNS monitoring at this time

### 5.6 BNP as Monitoring Biomarker
- NEP inhibits BNP degradation, leading to elevated BNP levels; these do NOT reflect clinical worsening
- Use NT-proBNP (not a neprilysin substrate) for biomarker-guided monitoring in patients on Entresto

## 7. Drug Interactions

### 7.1 Contraindicated Combinations
| Drug/Class | Reason | Management |
|-----------|--------|-----------|
| ACE inhibitors | Risk of angioedema (dual RAS blockade) | 36-hour washout required; do not co-administer |
| Aliskiren (patients with diabetes) | Excessive RAS blockade; hypotension, hyperkalemia, renal impairment | Contraindicated |

### 7.2 Clinically Significant Interactions
| Drug/Class | Interaction | Recommendation |
|-----------|-------------|----------------|
| NSAIDs / COX-2 inhibitors | Reduced antihypertensive effect; increased renal impairment risk | Monitor renal function; avoid if possible |
| Potassium-sparing diuretics (spironolactone, eplerenone) | Hyperkalemia | Monitor K+ closely |
| Potassium supplements | Hyperkalemia | Monitor K+ |
| Lithium | Elevated lithium levels (reduced renal clearance) | Monitor lithium levels; avoid if possible |
| Phosphodiesterase-5 inhibitors (sildenafil) | Additive hypotension | Use with caution; monitor BP |
| Organic anion transporters (OAT1/3) substrates | Sacubitril may inhibit OAT1/3; increased exposure to substrates | Monitor for toxicity |
| Statins (OATP1B1/1B3 substrates: rosuvastatin, atorvastatin) | Increased statin exposure via inhibition of OATP1B1/3 | Rosuvastatin max dose 40 mg; simvastatin max 20 mg |
| Metformin | Theoretical: OAT2 inhibition may reduce metformin renal elimination | Monitor glucose and signs of metformin accumulation |

### 7.3 CYP Interactions
- Sacubitril/LBQ657 and valsartan are not metabolized by CYP450 enzymes
- Minimal CYP interaction risk for typical CYP-inhibiting/inducing drugs

## 8. Special Populations

### 8.1 Pregnancy (Category D/X)
- BBW: Fetal toxicity. Discontinue immediately upon pregnancy confirmation.
- No adequate controlled studies in pregnant women; animal reproduction studies not applicable given known teratogenicity of RAS-acting drugs
- Register pregnant patients exposed to Entresto with the Entresto Pregnancy Registry (1-888-XXX-XXXX)

### 8.2 Lactation
- No data on presence in human milk; potential for serious adverse reactions in nursing infant
- Discontinue drug or discontinue nursing, considering importance of drug to mother

### 8.3 Females and Males of Reproductive Potential
- Counsel on effective contraception during therapy
- No evidence of impaired fertility in animal studies

### 8.4 Pediatric Patients
- Safety and efficacy established in patients ≥1 year of age (PANORAMA-HF)
- Not studied in neonates (<1 year); safety unknown
- Age-appropriate oral suspension available

### 8.5 Geriatric Patients (≥65 years)
- No overall differences in safety or effectiveness vs. younger patients
- Greater sensitivity to hypotension; consider starting at lower dose (24/26 mg BID)
- Higher prevalence of renal impairment in elderly; monitor eGFR

### 8.6 Patients with Renal Impairment
- See Section 2.2 above for dosing
- Not recommended in patients on dialysis (insufficient data)
- Sacubitril metabolites are renally cleared; accumulation occurs with severe renal impairment

### 8.7 Patients with Hepatic Impairment
- Mild: No adjustment
- Moderate: Start at 24/26 mg BID
- Severe (Child-Pugh C): Not recommended

### 8.8 Race/Ethnicity
- Efficacy consistent across racial subgroups in PARADIGM-HF
- Black patients: Historically higher angioedema risk with ACE-I (risk applies to Entresto via bradykinin pathway); increased monitoring recommended
- No dose adjustment required by race
