# JARDIANCE (Empagliflozin) — Brand Strategy
**Brand:** JARDIANCE (empagliflozin) | **Company:** Boehringer Ingelheim + Eli Lilly and Company Alliance | **Document Version:** 2024 Annual Planning
**Therapeutic Area:** Cardiometabolic — T2D, Heart Failure (HFrEF/HFpEF), CKD | **Mechanism:** SGLT2 Inhibitor

---

## 1. Brand Vision

JARDIANCE's brand vision is to be **the leading cardiometabolic medicine** that reduces cardiovascular mortality, hospitalizations for heart failure, and kidney disease progression — across the broadest range of patients, regardless of diabetes status — establishing empagliflozin as the foundational treatment in the cardiovascular-renal-metabolic (CRM) continuum.

**Brand Promise:** "JARDIANCE: The only SGLT2 inhibitor proven to reduce cardiovascular death and hospitalization for heart failure across heart failure with reduced and preserved ejection fraction — while extending its benefits to chronic kidney disease."

## 2. Brand Positioning

### Positioning Statement
For cardiologists, nephrologists, and endocrinologists treating patients with T2D, heart failure, or CKD, JARDIANCE (empagliflozin 10mg/25mg) is the SGLT2 inhibitor with the **most comprehensive evidence base** across cardiometabolic disease — spanning CV mortality reduction in T2D+CVD (EMPA-REG OUTCOME: CV death HR 0.62), heart failure hospitalization reduction in HFrEF (EMPEROR-Reduced: HR 0.75) and HFpEF (EMPEROR-Preserved: HR 0.79, **first drug proven effective in HFpEF**), and renal protection in CKD (EMPA-KIDNEY: HR 0.72) — enabling physicians to treat the whole patient with one agent.

## 3. Strategic Imperatives (2024–2026)

### SI-1: Defend and Grow the Heart Failure Franchise
JARDIANCE was the first drug approved for HFpEF — a condition affecting ~3 million Americans with virtually no pharmacological treatments previously. The EMPEROR-Preserved approval (2022) created an entirely new treatment category. Strategic priority is to establish JARDIANCE as the **standard of care for ALL heart failure patients**, driving class-leading HF prescribing.

**Tactics:**
- Medical Affairs engagement with Heart Failure Society of America (HFSA) and ACC/AHA guideline committees
- Deploy EMPEROR-Preserved data prominently in HF congress (HFSA, ACC, ESC Heart Failure)
- KOL identification among HFpEF specialists; speaker programs in cardiology settings
- EHR-integrated clinical decision support tools prompting JARDIANCE for HFpEF patients

### SI-2: Maximize CKD Indication Launch (EMPA-KIDNEY — 2023)
The 2023 CKD approval (including eGFR ≥20 — broader than dapagliflozin's DAPA-CKD) opens the nephrology channel. Approximately 37 million Americans have CKD; CDK4/6 class penetration in nephrology remains low.

**Tactics:**
- Dedicated nephrology sales force; hire 150 additional nephrology-focused reps
- Nephrologist speaker programs emphasizing EMPA-KIDNEY eGFR 20–45 population
- Establish JARDIANCE on VA/DoD nephrology formularies
- Patient advocacy engagement with American Kidney Fund, National Kidney Foundation
- Deploy KDIGO 2024 guideline updates incorporating JARDIANCE in CKD messaging

### SI-3: Consolidate CV Mortality Position vs Farxiga
Dapagliflozin (Farxiga) did NOT demonstrate CV mortality benefit in DECLARE-TIMI 58 (3P-MACE HR 0.93, NS for CV death). JARDIANCE's CV death HR 0.62 in EMPA-REG is a unique and defensible differentiator. Strategic goal: ensure CV mortality benefit is the primary differentiator in cardiologist targeting.

**Tactics:**
- Head-to-head comparative messaging: "JARDIANCE is the only SGLT2 inhibitor to reduce cardiovascular death by 38% in patients with T2D+established CVD"
- Deploy EMPA-REG CV death data prominently at cardiology congresses (ACC, AHA)
- Partner with ACC on NCDR (National Cardiovascular Data Registry) registry research

### SI-4: Expand Non-Diabetes HF/CKD Indications Utilization
EMPEROR trials enrolled ~50% non-diabetic patients, with equal HF benefits regardless of diabetes status. EMPA-KIDNEY also enrolled non-diabetic CKD patients. This creates the opportunity to position JARDIANCE beyond diabetes specialists into cardiology and nephrology for patients without T2D.

**Tactics:**
- Cardiologist-specific messaging emphasizing glucose-independent benefit
- "You don't need to treat the sugar — you treat the heart" messaging framework
- EHR alerts for HF/CKD patients not on SGLT2 inhibitor
- Oncology analogy: cardio-renal protection, not just glucose lowering

### SI-5: Defend Against Generic/Biosimilar and Competitive Threats
JARDIANCE patent protection extends through approximately 2025–2026 for empagliflozin. Boehringer Ingelheim/Lilly defend IP vigorously. Additionally, canagliflozin (Invokana) generics have entered the market; defend JARDIANCE's premium position based on CV mortality and HF data superiority.

**Tactics:**
- Health economic dossiers demonstrating JARDIANCE superiority to canagliflozin (no amputation signal; positive CV mortality)
- Market access programs ensuring JARDIANCE preferred tier placement vs generic canagliflozin
- LOE planning: value-based programs; long-term patient benefit messaging

## 4. SWOT Analysis

### Strengths
- **Pioneer CV mortality benefit:** EMPA-REG OUTCOME (2015, NEJM) — first antidiabetic drug to show CV mortality reduction (HR 0.62); landmark regulatory achievement
- **HFpEF first-mover advantage:** EMPEROR-Preserved (2021) — first drug approved for HFpEF; transformational for ~3 million US patients
- **Broadest CKD population:** EMPA-KIDNEY (eGFR ≥20 – broader than dapagliflozin's ≥25 in DAPA-CKD)
- **All-EF heart failure:** Only SGLT2 inhibitor with dedicated approvals across HFrEF and HFpEF
- **Revenue momentum:** 2023 revenue ~$7.3B combined (BI+Lilly split), +24% YoY; fastest-growing major cardiovascular brand
- **~45% SGLT2 US market share**; growing from dominant position
- **Glucose-independent benefits:** Resonates strongly with cardiologists and nephrologists
- **Strong guideline endorsement:** ACC/AHA HF guidelines Class I; ADA/EASD T2D/CKD guidelines preferred SGLT2 agent
- **No Black Box Warning**; lower risk profile vs. canagliflozin (no amputation signal)

### Weaknesses
- UTI rate 9–18%: patient counseling burden; competitive talking point for metformin or DPP-4 combinations
- Genital mycotic infections: 6–10%; higher in women; patient education required
- DKA risk: rare (0.1–0.2%) but potentially life-threatening; requires patient education on sick-day rules and perioperative hold
- Hold before surgery, invasive procedures, fasting — requires coordination with surgical teams and anesthesiology
- Cost/affordability: specialty pricing creates adherence barriers for uninsured/underinsured
- Complexity of four approved indications — risk of prescriber confusion about appropriate patient selection

### Opportunities
- Heart failure with preserved EF (HFpEF): Under-treated (~3 million US patients; JARDIANCE first approved therapy)
- CKD market: Nephrology channel significantly under-penetrated by SGLT2 class; 37 million US CKD patients
- Broader cardiometabolic risk reduction: JARDIANCE could become standard preventive therapy in CV-risk patients with or without T2D
- Pipeline: Empagliflozin combinations (e.g., empagliflozin/linagliptin fixed-dose combination GLYXAMBI) and once-weekly formulations
- International markets: Growing CRM awareness in Asia-Pacific and LatAm
- Type 1 diabetes: Exploratory (empagliflozin shows promise as T1D adjunct in EU — check current label)
- Post-ACS patients: Ongoing trials (EMPACT-MI: empagliflozin in acute MI patients)

### Threats
- Farxiga (dapagliflozin): DAPA-HF (HFrEF HR 0.74), DELIVER (HFpEF HR 0.82), DAPA-CKD (HR 0.61), T1D label — competitive data package
- Generic dapagliflozin (Farxiga patent ~2025): price competition entering SGLT2 class
- Generic canagliflozin: Price pressure; Invokana generics already available
- Class-level safety concerns (Fournier's gangrene, rare DKA): may suppress prescribing in some practices
- GLP-1 receptor agonists (semaglutide/Ozempic, tirzepatide/Mounjaro): competing for T2D priority positioning; semaglutide SELECT trial (CV benefit in non-diabetic obese); potential displacement for T2D+CVD positioning
- Medicare IRA negotiation: High revenue ($7B+) makes JARDIANCE a candidate for CMS price negotiation under IRA
- Perioperative management complexity: hospital formulary gatekeepers may limit inpatient use

## 6. Campaign Pillars

### Pillar A: "The Heart Failure Revolution"
Lead with EMPEROR-Preserved as the defining clinical breakthrough — the first and only drug proven to reduce cardiovascular death or hospitalization for heart failure in patients with HFpEF. Position JARDIANCE as having changed the paradigm of HFpEF management.

### Pillar B: "CV Mortality — Unique in Class"
Reinforce EMPA-REG OUTCOME CV death HR 0.62 (38% reduction) as the SGLT2 differentiator versus Farxiga. "Not all SGLT2 inhibitors are the same — only JARDIANCE has proven CV mortality benefit."

### Pillar C: "Beyond Glucose — The Cardiometabolic Medicine"
Frame empagliflozin's glucose-independent hemodynamic and renal benefits as the core MOA for HF/CKD. Target cardiologists and nephrologists who think of JARDIANCE as a cardiovascular/renal drug, not merely a diabetes drug.

### Pillar D: "Protect the Kidneys — Broad CKD Coverage"
Emphasize EMPA-KIDNEY: eGFR ≥20 inclusion (lower than DAPA-CKD's ≥25); 28% RRR in primary composite. Target nephrologists in dialysis-prevention and CKD management contexts.
