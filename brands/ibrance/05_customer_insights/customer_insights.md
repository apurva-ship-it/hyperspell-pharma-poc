## 2. Prescriber Behavior Analysis

### Key Driver Research (2023 Pfizer HCP Brand Tracker, n=400 oncologists)
Oncologists were asked to rate CDK4/6 inhibitor selection criteria on a 1–10 scale:

| Selection Criterion | Importance (mean score) | IBRANCE Score | Kisqali Score | Verzenio Score |
|--------------------|------------------------|---------------|---------------|----------------|
| Clinical trial efficacy (PFS) | 9.2 | 8.4 | 8.6 | 8.1 |
| Overall survival data | 8.9 | **5.8** | **9.1** | 7.3 |
| Safety/tolerability profile | 8.7 | 8.5 | 7.8 | 7.1 |
| Ease of administration | 8.1 | 8.2 | 7.4 | 7.6 |
| Monitoring requirements | 7.8 | **9.1** | **6.2** | 8.3 |
| Patient support program | 7.6 | 8.8 | 7.5 | 8.1 |
| Formulary access | 7.4 | 8.3 | 7.8 | 7.9 |
| Clinical experience/familiarity | 7.3 | 9.4 | 7.2 | 7.6 |
| Indication breadth | 6.9 | 7.8 | 7.3 | 8.6 |
| Real-world data availability | 6.8 | 9.0 | 7.1 | 7.3 |

**Key Insight:** IBRANCE scores highest on clinical experience, monitoring simplicity, and patient support. The OS criterion — now the #2 priority — is where IBRANCE is most vulnerable (score 5.8 vs Kisqali 9.1).

### Physician Decision Point: First CDK4/6 Selection (1L)
Primary reasons for 1L CDK4/6 inhibitor choice (2024 survey, n=220):
- "Most evidence" (includes OS data): 34% → favors Kisqali
- "Patient familiarity / I know how to manage it": 28% → strongly favors IBRANCE
- "No cardiac monitoring required": 18% → favors IBRANCE or Verzenio
- "Formulary/prior authorization ease": 12% → varies by payer
- "Patient's specific disease characteristics": 8% — mixed

**Treatment Change Triggers:**
| Trigger | % of Physicians Citing |
|---------|------------------------|
| Disease progression on IBRANCE | 91% |
| Persistent Grade 4 neutropenia with dose holds | 38% |
| Patient intolerance (fatigue, stomatitis) | 22% |
| Formulary formulary restrictions | 19% |
| KOL recommendation at CME/conference | 14% |
| New clinical data publication (e.g., OS trial) | 11% |

## 3. Patient Journey

### Disease Pathway Overview (HR+/HER2- MBC)

```
Breast Cancer Diagnosis
    ↓
Early-Stage Treatment (Surgery + Adjuvant Therapy ± chemo)
    ↓ [Months to years]
Metastatic Recurrence or De Novo Metastatic Diagnosis
    ↓
Biomarker Assessment: HR/HER2/PR status; Ki-67; PIK3CA; ESR1 ctDNA
    ↓
1L Treatment Decision:
    - HR+/HER2-: CDK4/6 inhibitor + AI (palbociclib, ribociclib, or abemaciclib)
    - If premenopausal: + LHRH agonist
    ↓ [Median PFS ~24–28 months on CDK4/6 + AI]
Progression on 1L CDK4/6 + AI
    ↓
Biomarker Testing: ESR1 ctDNA, PIK3CA mutation
    ↓
2L Decision Tree:
    - ESR1 mutant: Elacestrant (Orserdu)
    - PIK3CA/AKT1/PTEN mutant: Capivasertib + fulvestrant OR alpelisib + fulvestrant
    - ESR1 wild-type, no PI3K: IBRANCE + fulvestrant (or alternative CDK4/6 + fulvestrant)
    ↓ [Median PFS ~9.5 months on IBRANCE + fulvestrant]
Later Lines: Chemotherapy, antibody-drug conjugates (sacituzumab, T-DM1 if HER2-low), PARP inhibitors (BRCA)
```

| OS benefit demonstration | Critical | PALOMA-2 OS NS (HR 0.956) | RWE OS generation; contextual framing |

## 6. Market Research: Key Attitudinal Insights

### Focus Group Findings — Community Oncologists (2023, n=32)

**On IBRANCE:**
- "IBRANCE is what I learned. I know how to manage the neutropenia — check CBC, hold if needed, restart. It's second nature."
- "The 7-day off week is great for my patients, actually. They feel like they're getting a break."
- "I worry about the OS question at tumor boards. The academic folks keep citing MONALEESA-2. But in my community, my patients are doing fine."
- "Pfizer's patient support team is excellent. Their hub gets patients started faster than the others."

**On Competitors:**
- "I started using Kisqali for my premenopausal patients after MONALEESA-7. But the EKG requirement is a hassle in my small office."
- "Verzenio's diarrhea is real. I've had three patients discontinue. But if they tolerate it, the data are good."
- "The OS question is becoming a bigger deal. My chair says we should prefer ribociclib. But old habits die hard."

### Key Perception Gap to Address
The gap between IBRANCE's **actual clinical value** (strong PFS, excellent real-world experience, simple monitoring) and **perceived clinical ranking** (downgraded by OS-focused KOLs) is the central brand perception challenge for 2024–2026.
