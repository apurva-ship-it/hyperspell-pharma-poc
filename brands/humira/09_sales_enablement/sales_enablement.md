## 1. Sales Call Guide

### Pre-Call Planning
Before every HUMIRA call in FY2026, complete the following:
- [ ] Review HCP's TRx data: HUMIRA vs. biosimilar vs. SKYRIZI/RINVOQ trends in last 90 days
- [ ] Identify any payer changes in HCP's primary insurance contracts (e.g., new biosimilar mandates)
- [ ] Review myAbbVie Assist enrollment status: number of patients enrolled, any lapsed patients
- [ ] Check for open PA denials or access issues
- [ ] Note the HCP's specialty and primary HUMIRA indication (RA/PsO/IBD/HS/other)
- [ ] Set call objective: Retention? Biosimilar conversion defense? AbbVie portfolio (SKYRIZI/RINVOQ) discussion? HS education?

**FY2026 Call Priority Shift**: Given biosimilar landscape, calls must lead with patient outcomes and support ecosystem — NOT efficacy data alone. Payer access and myAbbVie Assist enrollment are now Tier 1 conversation priorities.

### Call Structure: HUMIRA Retention & Portfolio Consultative Model

#### Stage 1: Opening (2 minutes)
**Goal**: Establish relevance to HCP's current patient challenges, especially around biosimilar navigation

**Script Framework (Biosimilar Era)**:
> "Dr. [Name], I know the biosimilar landscape is creating a lot of questions right now. I wanted to check in on how your patients are being affected — and make sure you have the latest resources for navigating formulary changes and keeping your HUMIRA patients on track."

**Alternative Opening (New HS/Uveitis Patient)**:
> "Dr. [Name], I know HS patients can be among the most challenging to manage — diagnosis delays, prior treatment failures, significant quality of life impact. HUMIRA is the only FDA-approved biologic for HS, and I wanted to make sure your team has the full patient support resources available."

---

#### Stage 2: Probing Questions (3–4 minutes)

**For Rheumatology (RA/PsA/AS focus)**:
1. "Have you had any patients whose payer has moved them to a biosimilar recently? How is that conversation going?"
2. "Are you finding that any of your stable HUMIRA patients are experiencing secondary failure or loss of response?"
3. "What's your typical step-up strategy for a patient who's not achieving DAS28 remission on HUMIRA + MTX?"
4. "Do you use therapeutic drug monitoring (adalimumab levels) to assess secondary failure before switching?"

**For Dermatology (PsO/HS focus)**:
1. "For your psoriasis patients, what response threshold are you targeting — PASI 75 or PASI 90/100?"
2. "How many HS patients are you managing in your practice? Are you finding diagnosis timing is improving?"
3. "Have any of your HS patients been denied coverage or required step therapy before HUMIRA?"
4. "What's your experience been with the citrate-free formulation — are patients noticing the difference?"

**For Gastroenterology (CD/UC focus)**:
1. "For your moderate-to-severe CD patients, what's your typical biologic sequencing? Where does HUMIRA fit?"
2. "Are you seeing payer pressure to use a specific biologic first-line in IBD?"
3. "For your UC patients on HUMIRA, what percentage are achieving mucosal healing?"
4. "Have any of your IBD patients been denied HUMIRA and required to try a biosimilar first?"

---

#### Stage 3: Presenting (4–5 minutes)

**Core Presenting Themes for FY2026**:

**Theme A — Patient Stability / "Don't Fix What Isn't Broken"**
> "For your patients who are stable on HUMIRA and achieving treatment targets, there's real risk in switching. Nocebo effects alone — just the psychology of switching from a familiar medication to something perceived as 'different' — affect 30% of patients who know they've been switched. We have real-world data showing that patients stable on HUMIRA have different long-term outcomes than patients switched mid-course. The clinical argument for 'stable patient continuity' is strong."

**Theme B — myAbbVie Assist: Neutralize the Cost Argument**
> "When payers push for biosimilar to save patient co-pays, here's what I want your team to know: myAbbVie Assist means eligible commercially insured patients pay $0 for HUMIRA. The cost argument for the biosimilar may simply not hold for your patients. The enrollment process takes 5 minutes — let me show you how and make sure your office manager knows about it."

**Theme C — HS = HUMIRA Only**
> "For your HS patients, there's no choice to make — HUMIRA is the only FDA-approved biologic. PIONEER trial data: HiSCR response 58.9% vs. 27.6% with placebo. For a population with 7–10 years average diagnosis delay and severe QoL impact, we're talking about a transformative treatment. And it's exclusively HUMIRA's space."

**Theme D — AbbVie Portfolio: The Evolution**
> "For patients where HUMIRA isn't delivering enough — the inadequate responders in RA or psoriasis — AbbVie has SKYRIZI and RINVOQ to offer. SELECT-COMPARE showed RINVOQ with significantly higher ACR50 (52% vs. 36%) vs. HUMIRA in RA. In psoriasis, SKYRIZI delivers PASI 90/100 rates well above HUMIRA. AbbVie's commitment to your patients doesn't end at HUMIRA — it's a portfolio approach."

## 3. Top 5 Objection Handling Scripts

### Objection A: "My payer is forcing biosimilar — I can't prescribe HUMIRA."
**Response Script**:
> "Let me help you navigate this. First, for established, stable HUMIRA patients, most payers have a medical necessity/step-edit exception process — and we'll help you write that letter. For new patients, let me walk you through two things: first, myAbbVie Assist means $0 out-of-pocket for commercially insured patients — the patient cost argument for the biosimilar may be weaker than you think. Second, for some payer plans, HUMIRA is actually preferred after factoring in AbbVie's rebate structures. Can I arrange a benefits investigation for your next biologic candidate?"

**Leave behind**: PA exception letter template; myAbbVie Assist enrollment QR code; 1-page payer formulary guide

---

### Objection B: "HUMIRA's efficacy is just not as good as Rinvoq in RA — I'm using Rinvoq first."
**Response Script**:
> "SELECT-COMPARE data is clear — RINVOQ does show higher ACR50 in MTX-IR RA patients, and that's why AbbVie developed it. HUMIRA remains appropriate for patients where JAK inhibitor safety profile requires consideration — specifically, patients with CVD risk factors, prior DVT/PE, active or recent malignancy, or those who are older. FDA JAK class labeling requires risk stratification. For those patients, HUMIRA's long-term safety record — including no MACE signal and no VTE signal — remains differentiating. Let's identify which patients in your practice are appropriate for RINVOQ vs. HUMIRA based on their comorbidity profile."

---

### Objection C: "Skyrizi is better for psoriasis — why would I use HUMIRA?"
**Response Script**:
> "Skyrizi delivers PASI 90 rates in the 85%+ range vs. HUMIRA's 45% — that's a real clinical difference, and for your patients with severe psoriasis where complete clearance is the goal, Skyrizi is the right choice within AbbVie's portfolio. HUMIRA remains appropriate for patients with comorbid psoriatic arthritis where the TNF mechanism offers structural protection alongside skin clearance, for patients on payer step therapy requiring a TNF inhibitor first, and for psoriasis patients who are pregnant where IL-23 data is more limited. Let me make sure your team is clear on which patient profile fits each agent."

---

### Objection D: "I'm worried about the long-term malignancy risk with HUMIRA."
**Response Script**:
> "This is one of the most common concerns, and the long-term data provides important context. In the HUMIRA long-term safety database — over 1 million patient-years — malignancy rates are not significantly elevated above the background rate for RA and IBD patients themselves, who already have higher cancer risk than the general population. There is a modest signal for lymphoma (0.10 per 100 PY vs. 0.06 background), but the absolute risk is very small. For your patients who need disease control, the long-term disability and morbidity of uncontrolled RA or IBD often outweighs this small absolute risk. I can pull the specific registry data for you — our MSL can also provide a deeper review."

---

### Objection E: "My IBD patients aren't achieving remission on HUMIRA — the remission rates seem low."
**Response Script**:
> "The CHARM data shows 36% remission at wk52, and ULTRA2 shows 17% in UC — you're right that these are modest numbers compared to newer agents. For patients who don't achieve remission on HUMIRA, the next step within AbbVie's portfolio is RINVOQ — MOTIVATE/ADVANCE in CD and U-ACHIEVE/U-ACCOMPLISH in UC show remission rates of 35–45%, meaningfully higher. Therapeutic drug monitoring can also identify whether secondary failure is due to anti-drug antibodies — in which case dose intensification or adding MTX may help before switching. Would it be useful to review your non-remission patients and identify which are best suited for RINVOQ escalation?"

## 5. Coaching Guide (Manager / District Manager Use)

### Coaching Framework — Biosimilar Era Selling

**Core Coaching Theme for FY2026**: "In a biosimilar market, reps must be access consultants, not just data presenters. A HUMIRA rep who can't navigate a payer formulary change or enroll a patient in myAbbVie Assist in 5 minutes is leaving patients behind."

**New Rep Development (0–6 months)**:
- Focus: Master 3 core indications (RA, PsO, CD) + myAbbVie Assist enrollment process
- Role-play: Handle biosimilar objection A; demonstrate enrollment portal proficiency
- Red flags: Rep presenting only REVEAL/ARMADA data without addressing biosimilar access realities; not enrolling any patients in myAbbVie Assist
- Milestone: First 10 patients enrolled in myAbbVie Assist by Month 3

**Developing Rep (6–18 months)**:
- Focus: HS indication mastery; AbbVie portfolio navigation; formulary transition letter writing
- Coaching: Field rides on Tier A (defense) and Tier B (HS expansion) accounts
- Red flags: Over-promoting HUMIRA for psoriasis when HCP clearly prefers SKYRIZI; not raising Rinvoq in RA inadequate responder conversations
- Milestone: Achieve 90% myAbbVie Assist enrollment target 2 consecutive quarters

**Senior Rep (18+ months)**:
- Focus: Strategic account management; payer contract impact interpretation; KOL relationship maintenance; AbbVie portfolio territory planning
- Coaching: Annual territory business plan review; HUMIRA lifecycle management integration with SKYRIZI/RINVOQ ramp
- Red flags: Defensive HUMIRA advocacy ignoring portfolio migration opportunities; not leveraging MSL for HSTCL/malignancy safety discussions
- Milestone: Lead one regional advisory event; contribute to team training on biosimilar navigation best practices

### Field Ride Coaching Questions
1. "Your HCP's payer just mandated biosimilar substitution for new starts. Walk me through your response."
2. "A patient comes in after 6 years on HUMIRA saying their insurance switched them to a biosimilar. What do you do?"
3. "Your dermatologist says HUMIRA PASI 75 data isn't relevant anymore because 'everyone uses IL-23.' How do you respond?"
4. "How do you identify a patient in this practice who hasn't been diagnosed with HS yet but might be a candidate?"
5. "Explain the AbbVie portfolio step-up strategy to a new nurse practitioner in this rheumatology practice."
