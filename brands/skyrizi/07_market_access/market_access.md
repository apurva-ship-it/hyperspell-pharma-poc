## 1. Payer Landscape Overview

### 1.1 Market Structure

Skyrizi is a specialty biologic with complex reimbursement dynamics across four indications. The payer landscape varies significantly by indication:
- **Psoriasis (PsO):** Covered as specialty pharmacy (SP) benefit; well-established biologic tier pathways
- **Psoriatic Arthritis (PsA):** SP benefit; often covered alongside methotrexate
- **Crohn's Disease (CD) and Ulcerative Colitis (UC):** Medical benefit (IV induction) AND pharmacy benefit (SC maintenance); complex split-benefit management

### 1.2 Coverage Overview by Indication (2024 US)

| Indication | % Lives Covered | Average PA Requirement | Step Therapy Prevalence |
|-----------|----------------|----------------------|------------------------|
| Plaque PsO | 94% | 87% require PA | 78% require prior biologic/MTX failure |
| PsA | 91% | 82% require PA | 72% require cDMARD failure first |
| Crohn's Disease | 88% | 91% require PA | 85% require anti-TNF failure first |
| Ulcerative Colitis | 76% (new; growing) | 94% require PA | 82% require 5-ASA + corticosteroid + immunomodulator failure |

|----------|-------------|-----------------|

**Key access challenge:** IV induction billed under medical benefit (J-code: J3490 or J3590 — non-specific; seeking dedicated J-code); prior authorization often requires separate medical and pharmacy PA approvals.

## 4. Step Therapy Challenges and Strategies

### 4.1 Psoriasis Step Therapy

**Common payer requirement:** Trial of MTX and/or one prior biologic (typically adalimumab/biosimilar or TNF) before approving Skyrizi.

**Strategic response:**
- "Prefer and skip" advocacy: For moderate-to-severe PsO patients with DLQI ≥10 and BSA ≥10%, MTX is inadequate standard of care per AAD guidelines — document medical necessity for biologic-first approach
- PASI 90/100 target documentation: If prior biologic achieved only PASI 75 but not PASI 90, that constitutes inadequate response under modern treat-to-target standards
- IL-17 contraindication bypass: For patients with concurrent IBD — document IBD diagnosis; IL-17 is contraindicated; step therapy exception warranted for Skyrizi as only appropriate IL-23 therapy

### 4.2 CD Step Therapy

**Payer requirement:** Anti-TNF failure (infliximab and/or adalimumab) before approving Skyrizi.

**Strategic response — Aligned with SEQUENCE:**
- SEQUENCE enrolled post-TNF failure patients → step therapy requirement aligns perfectly with Skyrizi's approved evidence base and clinical indication
- For anti-TNF-naive patients who are biologic-naive: Medical necessity letter citing ADVANCE remission data (45%) and convenience of SC maintenance vs. IV vedolizumab

### 4.3 State Step Therapy Reform

- 27 states have enacted step therapy reform laws as of 2024
- These laws allow physicians to request step therapy exceptions when therapy contradicts clinical guidelines (AAD, ACG, ACR)
- AbbVie Government Affairs team actively pursuing step therapy reform in additional states: TX, FL, GA, OH priority markets

**Published ICER assessment (2019, updated 2022):**
- Cost per QALY vs. MTX: $50,000–$70,000/QALY

**AbbVie HEOR model — PsO total cost of care:**

- Quality-adjusted life years gained: 0.08–0.12 QALY/patient-year vs. ustekinumab

## 7. IBD Market Access Specific Considerations

### 7.1 J-Code and Hospital Billing (IV Induction)

- Risankizumab IV does not yet have a dedicated J-code (as of Q1 2024); billed under J3490/J3590 (not otherwise classified)
- AbbVie submitted HCPCS application for dedicated J-code; decision expected H2 2024
- Dedicated J-code reduces billing ambiguity and denial rates at infusion centers
- Hospital and ASC (ambulatory surgery center) reimbursement: CMS pass-through reimbursement for new drugs pending J-code assignment

### 7.2 IDN Formulary Strategy (CD/UC)

- IBD-focused IDNs: Mass General Brigham, Cleveland Clinic, UCSF, Mayo Clinic, Northwestern, Johns Hopkins — top 10 IBD centers account for ~25% of specialty biologic CD prescriptions
- AbbVie IBD Access team: Dedicated team of 30 IBD-focused market access managers
- Hospital pharmacy committee presentations: SEQUENCE clinical data; IBD pathway integration; cost-effectiveness vs. ustekinumab biosimilars
