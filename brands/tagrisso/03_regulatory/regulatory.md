# TAGRISSO (osimertinib) — Regulatory & Label Reference Dossier
**Classification: INTERNAL — REGULATORY AFFAIRS**
**Last Updated: Q2 2026 | Owner: Global Regulatory Affairs, AstraZeneca Oncology**

---

## 1. APPROVED INDICATIONS — FULL LABEL TEXT (US FDA)

### 1.1 First-Line Metastatic NSCLC
TAGRISSO is indicated for the **first-line treatment of adult patients with metastatic non-small cell lung cancer (NSCLC) whose tumors have epidermal growth factor receptor (EGFR) exon 19 deletions or exon 21 L858R mutations**, as detected by an FDA-approved test.

### 1.2 Second-Line T790M-Positive NSCLC
TAGRISSO is indicated for the treatment of adult patients with **metastatic EGFR T790M mutation-positive NSCLC**, as detected by an FDA-approved test, whose disease has progressed on or after EGFR TKI therapy.

### 1.3 Adjuvant NSCLC — Post-Resection
TAGRISSO is indicated as **adjuvant therapy after tumor resection in adult patients with NSCLC whose tumors have EGFR exon 19 deletions or exon 21 L858R mutations**, as detected by an FDA-approved test.

### 1.4 LAURA Indication (Stage III Unresectable — Pending/Filed 2024)
*Anticipated approval 2025:* TAGRISSO as maintenance therapy after concurrent chemoradiotherapy (CRT) in patients with **unresectable Stage III NSCLC with EGFR Ex19del/L858R mutations** (LAURA trial data submitted for sNDA).

## 3. DOSING AND ADMINISTRATION

### 3.1 Recommended Dose
| Indication | Dose | Frequency | Route | Duration |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1L Metastatic NSCLC | 80 mg | Once daily | Oral (tablet) | Until disease progression or unacceptable toxicity |
| 2L T790M+ Metastatic | 80 mg | Once daily | Oral (tablet) | Until disease progression or unacceptable toxicity |
| Adjuvant post-resection | 80 mg | Once daily | Oral (tablet) | 3 years or until recurrence/unacceptable toxicity |

### 3.2 Dose Reduction Schedule
| Occurrence | Action |
|---|---|
| First dose reduction (if needed) | Reduce to 40 mg once daily |
| Second dose reduction (if needed) | Permanently discontinue |
| Grade 3 AE (no resolution within 3 wks on interruption) | Permanently discontinue |

**Dose Modifications by AE**:

| Adverse Event | Grade/Severity | Action |
|---|---|---|
| ILD/Pneumonitis (any grade) | Any | Withhold; if Grade 2 resolves in ≤3 weeks, resume at 40mg; if Grade 3/4, permanently discontinue |
| QTc >500ms | Grade 3 | Withhold until QTc ≤481ms; resume at 40mg |
| Left ventricular EF decrease | ≥10% absolute decrease; below LLN | Withhold up to 4 weeks; if improved, resume at 40mg |
| Rash Grade 3 | Persistent ≥4 weeks | Withhold; if resolved, resume at 40mg |
| Diarrhea Grade 3+ | Persistent ≥2 days despite antidiarrheal | Withhold; if resolved ≤3 weeks, resume at same or reduced dose |

### 3.3 Administration
- Can be taken with or without food
- Swallow tablet whole; do not crush, split, or chew
- If vomiting occurs after taking dose, do not retake that day; take next dose at scheduled time
- Missed dose: Take as soon as remembered the same day; never double-dose on next day
- **Dispersal for patients unable to swallow**: Disperse in 60 mL water; mix until tablet dispersed (approximately 15 minutes); consume immediately; rinse glass with 120–240 mL water, consume rinse
- **NG tube administration**: Disperse in 15 mL water; administer via syringe; flush with 30 mL water

## 5. WARNINGS AND PRECAUTIONS

### 5.1 Interstitial Lung Disease (ILD)/Pneumonitis
- ILD occurred in 3.3% of patients across clinical trials; fatal in 0.6%
- If ILD/pneumonitis diagnosed, permanently discontinue Tagrisso
- Radiographically assess patients who develop new or worsening pulmonary symptoms
- Differential diagnosis critical: distinguish from pneumonia, lymphangitic spread, progression

### 5.2 QTc Prolongation
- QTc >500ms: 2.1% of patients; QTc 481–500ms: 3.1%
- Avoid Tagrisso in patients with congenital long QT syndrome
- Monitor ECG and electrolytes periodically in patients with HF, electrolyte abnormalities, or on QT-prolonging drugs
- Withhold if QTc >500ms; permanently discontinue if recurrent QTc >500ms with life-threatening arrhythmia

### 5.3 Cardiomyopathy / Left Ventricular Dysfunction
- Decreased LVEF (≥10% absolute decrease below LLN) reported in <1% of patients
- Assess LVEF at baseline and during treatment as clinically indicated
- Withhold if clinically significant deterioration; resume at reduced dose or discontinue

### 5.4 Keratitis
- Keratitis occurred in 0.7% of patients; ulcerative keratitis in 0.4%
- Promptly refer to ophthalmology if eye symptoms develop
- Withhold if Grade ≥3; resume at reduced dose upon resolution

### 5.5 Embryo-Fetal Toxicity
- Osimertinib can cause fetal harm based on mechanism of action and animal data
- **Advise pregnant women and females of reproductive potential of the potential risk**
- Verify pregnancy status in females of reproductive potential prior to initiating Tagrisso
- Advise effective contraception during treatment and for 6 weeks after the final dose (females); males with female partners of reproductive potential: effective contraception during treatment and for 4 months after final dose

## 7. DRUG INTERACTIONS

### 7.1 CYP3A4 Inducers (Decrease Osimertinib Exposure)
| Drug | Interaction | Recommendation |
|---|---|---|
| Strong CYP3A4 inducers (rifampin, carbamazepine, phenytoin, St. John's Wort) | AUC decreased ~78% | Avoid concurrent use; if unavoidable, increase Tagrisso dose to 160mg (monitor closely) |
| Moderate CYP3A4 inducers (efavirenz, modafinil) | AUC decreased ~58% | Avoid if possible |

### 7.2 QT-Prolonging Agents
| Drug Class | Interaction | Recommendation |
|---|---|---|
| Class Ia antiarrhythmics (quinidine, procainamide) | Additive QTc prolongation | Avoid; if unavoidable, ECG monitoring required |
| Class III antiarrhythmics (amiodarone, sotalol) | Additive QTc prolongation | Avoid; monitor ECG |
| Fluoroquinolones, macrolides, antifungals, antiemetics (ondansetron) | Variable QTc risk | Monitor ECG; electrolyte management |

### 7.3 Gastric pH Modifiers
- Tagrisso solubility decreases at higher pH
- Proton pump inhibitors: AUC not significantly affected (adequately soluble at pH 4–5)
- No dose adjustment required for PPIs, H2 blockers, or antacids

## 8. USE IN SPECIAL POPULATIONS

### 8.1 Renal Impairment
| Degree | Creatinine Clearance | Dose Adjustment |
|---|---|---|
| Mild | 60–89 mL/min | No dose adjustment |
| Moderate | 30–59 mL/min | No dose adjustment |
| Severe | 15–29 mL/min | No dose adjustment (limited data; use caution) |
| End-stage renal disease | <15 mL/min | Not recommended (no data) |

### 8.2 Hepatic Impairment
| Degree | Child-Pugh | Dose Adjustment |
|---|---|---|
| Mild | Child-Pugh A | No dose adjustment |
| Moderate | Child-Pugh B | No dose adjustment (based on PK data) |
| Severe | Child-Pugh C | No dose adjustment (limited data; monitor closely) |

### 8.3 Pediatric Patients
- Safety and efficacy in pediatric patients have not been established
- No approved pediatric indication
- Case reports exist for pediatric EGFR-mutant tumors; use off-label in exceptional circumstances only

### 8.4 Geriatric Patients
- No significant age effect on PK up to age 82 (studied in clinical trials)
- No dose adjustment required based on age
- Older patients (≥65 yr): Similar efficacy and AE profile to younger patients; close monitoring for ILD recommended

### 8.5 Pregnancy
- Can cause fetal harm based on mechanism (EGFR inhibition critical in embryogenesis)
- Animal studies: fetal toxicity at ≥0.4× human AUC
- Verify pregnancy status before initiation; advise effective contraception
- TAGRISSO Pregnancy Outcomes Registry — AZ-sponsored; report pregnancies to 1-800-236-9933

### 8.6 Patients with Asian Ancestry
- EGFR mutation prevalence higher (~35–50% of NSCLC) in East Asian patients
- PK similar across racial groups; efficacy consistent in Asian vs non-Asian subgroup analyses
- FLAURA Asian subgroup: PFS HR 0.43 (favoring osimertinib)
