## 1. COMPETITIVE LANDSCAPE OVERVIEW

Dupixent operates in multiple competitive markets across its seven approved indications. The most commercially significant battlegrounds are **Moderate-to-Severe Atopic Dermatitis** (primary revenue driver) and **Severe Asthma** (fast-growing). The COPD indication represents a new market creation opportunity with emerging competitor interest.

### 1.1 Primary Competitive Segments

| Indication | Primary Competitors | Market Dynamics |
|---|---|---|
| Atopic Dermatitis (biologic) | Upadacitinib (Rinvoq), Abrocitinib (Cibinqo), Tralokinumab (Adbry), Lebrikizumab (Ebglyss) | Crowded; JAK inhibitors show higher short-term EASI; IL-13 agents undercut on price |
| Severe Asthma | Mepolizumab (Nucala), Benralizumab (Fasenra), Tezepelumab (Tezspire) | Anti-IL-5/IL-5R strong in eosinophilic; dupilumab uniquely covers non-eosinophilic via IL-4 |
| CRSwNP | Omalizumab (Xolair — off-label), Mepolizumab (Nucala) | Dupilumab has strongest evidence; most competitors lack formal approval |
| COPD (type 2) | Itepekimab (IL-33; Regeneron/Sanofi), Tezepelumab (TSLP; AZ) | Nascent market; dupilumab is first-in-class with FDA approval for COPD |
| EoE | Cendakimab (IL-13; RaQualia), Benralizumab | Limited competition; dupilumab dominant |

## 2. COMPETITOR PROFILES — ATOPIC DERMATITIS

### 2.1 Upadacitinib (Rinvoq) — AbbVie
**Mechanism**: JAK1-selective inhibitor (oral, once daily)
**Approved Indication**: Moderate-to-severe AD (≥12yr US; ≥18yr initial EU)
**Dosing**: 15mg QD (adults); 30mg QD for severe cases

| Efficacy Endpoint (16-week trials vs dupilumab comparator) | Rinvoq 15mg | Rinvoq 30mg | Dupixent 300mg Q2W |
|---|---|---|---|
| EASI-75 | 71.0% | 80.0%* | 51–64% |
| IGA 0/1 | 48.1% | 55.5%* | 36–38% |
| Itch NRS ≥4pt | 52.0% | 60.5%* | 36–41% |

*Measure A & B trials at 16 weeks; dupilumab comparisons at Week 16 in similar populations

**Safety Profile — Critical Differentiators**:

| Safety Item | Rinvoq | Dupixent |
|---|---|---|
| **BLACK BOX WARNING** | YES — Serious infections, mortality, malignancies, MACE, thrombosis | **NO BBW** |
| TB Screening Required | Yes (prior to initiation) | **No** |
| CBC/Chemistry monitoring | Required | Not required |
| MACE risk | Elevated (cardiovascular risk patients) | No signal |
| VTE/DVT/PE risk | Elevated | No signal |
| Malignancy risk | Elevated (lymphoma, NMSC) | No signal |

**Battle Card — Key Differentiation vs Rinvoq**:
- Rinvoq shows numerically higher short-term EASI scores — acknowledge and pivot to long-term durability and safety
- BBW is a meaningful barrier for patients with cardiovascular risk, prior malignancy, or age >65
- Dupixent approved down to 6 months; Rinvoq 12yr minimum
- Pediatric population: significant safety concern with JAK inhibitors in developing immune systems
- Payer: BBW drugs trigger risk management programs; some plans restrict JAK inhibitors

**Revenue (2025E)**: Rinvoq global ~$7.2B total (AD + RA + IBD); AD-specific revenue ~$2.0B estimated

### 2.2 Tralokinumab (Adbry) — LEO Pharma
**Mechanism**: IL-13-selective monoclonal antibody (SC, Q2W/Q4W)
**Approved Indication**: Moderate-to-severe AD (adults)
**Dosing**: 600mg loading, 300mg Q2W (Q4W maintenance after 16wk response)

| Efficacy Endpoint (ECZTRA 1+2, Week 16) | Tralokinumab 300mg Q2W | Placebo |
|---|---|---|
| IGA 0/1 | 15.8% / 22.2% | 7.1% / 10.9% |
| EASI-75 | 25.0% / 33.2% | 8.9% / 11.4% |

*Note: ECZTRA results numerically lower than Dupixent SOLO data — no head-to-head*

**Battle Card vs Adbry**:
- IL-13-only blockade misses IL-4 pathway; limited to non-hematopoietic effects
- No pediatric indication; not approved beyond AD
- Limited physician adoption; formulary position generally Tier 3 or non-preferred vs Dupixent
- Revenue (2025E): ~$500M globally; growing slowly

### 2.3 Lebrikizumab (Ebglyss) — Eli Lilly
**Mechanism**: IL-13-selective monoclonal antibody with enhanced IL-13:IL-4Rα binding disruption
**Approved Indication**: Moderate-to-severe AD (adults; EU approval August 2023; FDA approved 2024)
**Dosing**: 500mg loading, 250mg Q2W (250mg Q4W maintenance option)

| Efficacy Endpoint (ADhere, ADvocate 1&2, Week 16) | Lebrikizumab | Placebo |
|---|---|---|
| IGA 0/1 | ~38–43% | ~13% |
| EASI-75 | ~43–52% | ~17% |
| NRS itch ≥4pt | ~43% | ~12% |

**Battle Card vs Ebglyss**:
- Numerically similar to dupilumab at 16 weeks in AD
- No approval beyond AD; no pediatric data; IL-13 only
- Lilly positioned for competitive pricing; potential formulary challenge
- Dupixent comorbidity story (AD + asthma + CRSwNP) remains unmatched by Ebglyss

### 2.4 Abrocitinib (Cibinqo) — Pfizer
**Mechanism**: JAK1-selective inhibitor (oral, once daily)
**BBW**: Yes — same as upadacitinib

| Efficacy (JADE COMPARE, 16wk) | Cibinqo 200mg | Cibinqo 100mg | Dupilumab 300mg Q2W |
|---|---|---|---|
| EASI-75 | 70% | 61% | 55% |
| IGA 0/1 | 47% | 39% | 36% |

**Battle Card vs Cibinqo**: Identical BBW argument as upadacitinib; thrombocytopenia and VTE risks; no pediatric approval.

## 3. COMPETITOR PROFILES — ASTHMA

### 3.1 Mepolizumab (Nucala) — GSK
**Mechanism**: IL-5 monoclonal antibody; inhibits eosinophil differentiation/survival
**Dosing**: 100mg Q4W SC (adults); 40mg Q4W (6–17yr)

| Key Data (DREAM/MENSA trials) | Mepolizumab 100mg SC | Placebo | vs Dupilumab QUEST |
|---|---|---|---|
| Exacerbation rate reduction | -53% (eos ≥500) | Reference | Dupixent: -65.8% (eos ≥300) |
| FEV1 improvement | +0.10–0.14L | — | Dupixent: +0.32L (eos ≥300) |

**Battle Card vs Nucala**:
- Nucala only addresses IL-5/eosinophil axis; dupilumab also blocks IL-4 driven airway remodeling, IgE production
- Dupixent OCS-elimination superior (VENTURE: 48% vs published mepolizumab data ~32–50%)
- Dupixent approved for comorbid AD + asthma — single biologic for both conditions
- Mepolizumab approved 6yr+ (same as dupilumab) but narrower biomarker threshold (often eos ≥300–500 preferred)

### 3.2 Benralizumab (Fasenra) — AstraZeneca
**Mechanism**: IL-5Rα antibody; ADCC-mediated eosinophil depletion
**Dosing**: 30mg Q4W × 3 doses, then Q8W
**Key differentiator**: Q8W dosing (less frequent); near-complete eosinophil depletion

**Battle Card vs Fasenra**:
- Aggressive eosinophil depletion may be concerning in patients where eosinophils are immune-protective
- No IL-4 pathway coverage; no AD comorbidity benefit
- Q8W convenience advantage; dupixent must counter with broader indication coverage

### 3.3 Tezepelumab (Tezspire) — AstraZeneca/Amgen
**Mechanism**: TSLP (thymic stromal lymphopoietin) inhibitor — upstream of IL-4, IL-5, IL-13
**Dosing**: 210mg Q4W SC
**Key differentiator**: Efficacy across all asthma phenotypes including non-eosinophilic

| Key Data (NAVIGATOR trial) | Tezepelumab | Placebo | vs Dupilumab QUEST |
|---|---|---|---|
| Exacerbation rate reduction (all) | -56% | Reference | Dupixent: -47.7% (all) |
| Exacerbation rate reduction (eos <300) | -47% | Reference | Dupixent: ~-33% (eos <300) |

**Battle Card vs Tezspire**:
- Tezspire is a genuine threat in non-eosinophilic asthma — acknowledge honestly; position dupilumab in eos ≥300 where data is strongest
- Dupixent covers AD + CRSwNP + COPD comorbidities; Tezspire is asthma-only currently
- Dupixent has longer track record, more robust 5yr LTE data

## 6. BATTLE CARDS — RAPID REFERENCE

### 6.1 vs JAK Inhibitors (Upadacitinib/Abrocitinib) — "The BBW Pivot"
**Objection**: "Rinvoq works better — higher EASI scores at 16 weeks"
**Response Framework**:
1. Acknowledge: "The 16-week EASI data for upadacitinib are impressive — those data are real."
2. Pivot: "But the question is: what does 'better' mean to your patient over the long term?"
3. Evidence: "Over 5 years, Dupixent's long-term safety profile remains clean — no BBW, no malignancy signal, no VTE risk"
4. Patient Profile: "For your patient over 40, with cardiovascular risk factors, or who's been on oral steroids — do you really want a BBW in the picture?"
5. Breadth: "And if your patient also has asthma or nasal polyps — Dupixent can treat all three with one injection"

### 6.2 vs IL-13 Agents (Tralokinumab/Lebrikizumab) — "The Breadth Pivot"
**Objection**: "Lebrikizumab is similar efficacy but possibly cheaper"
**Response**: 
1. "Lebrikizumab only blocks IL-13 — half the signal. Dupixent blocks both IL-4 AND IL-13."
2. "If your AD patient also has asthma — lebrikizumab has zero data there. Dupixent has phase 3 asthma data."
3. "Formulary position: Dupixent is preferred on [payer X] — check coverage before writing the prescription."
