# Skyrizi (risankizumab) — Brand Intelligence

**Generic Name:** Risankizumab  
**Brand:** Skyrizi  
**Company:** AbbVie (licensed from Boehringer Ingelheim)  
**Drug Class:** IL-23p19 inhibitor; selective anti-IL-23 monoclonal antibody  
**Mechanism of Action:** Selectively blocks the p19 subunit of **IL-23**, a cytokine central to Th17 and Th22 cell activation. Unlike ustekinumab (anti-IL-12/23p40), risankizumab spares IL-12 — preserving some innate immune function while blocking the IL-23/Th17 pathway driving psoriasis, PsA, and IBD inflammation.  
**FDA Approval:** April 2019 (plaque psoriasis); January 2022 (psoriatic arthritis); June 2022 (Crohn's disease); June 2023 (ulcerative colitis)  
**Formulation:**  
- PsO/PsA: 150 mg/1 mL SQ Q12W (after two 600 mg IV induction doses for IBD)  
- CD/UC: 600 mg IV induction (weeks 0, 4, 8) → 360 mg SQ Q8W maintenance

## Clinical Efficacy

### Plaque Psoriasis — UltIMMa-1 and UltIMMa-2 (vs ustekinumab)
- **Design:** Risankizumab 150 mg Q12W vs ustekinumab 45/90 mg vs placebo
- **PASI 90 at week 16:**
  - UltIMMa-1: **75.3%** (risa) vs 42.0% (uste) vs 4.9% (PBO)
  - UltIMMa-2: **74.8%** (risa) vs 47.5% (uste) vs 2.0% (PBO)
- **PASI 100 (complete clearance):**
  - UltIMMa-1: **35.9%** vs 12.0% (uste)
  - UltIMMa-2: **30.8%** vs 8.0% (uste)
- **IGA 0/1 at week 16:** 87.8% / 83.7% vs 63.0% / 61.6% (uste)
- **Week 52 PASI 90:** 81.9% / 80.6% (risa) — sustained with Q12W dosing

### Psoriasis — IMMvent Trial (vs adalimumab — head-to-head)
- **PASI 90 at week 16:** **72.4%** (risa) vs 47.4% (adalimumab)
- **PASI 100 at week 16:** 39.9% vs 22.8%
- Superior to adalimumab (p<0.0001)

### Psoriatic Arthritis — KEEPsAKE 1 and KEEPsAKE 2
- **ACR20 at week 24:**
  - KEEPsAKE 1 (bio-naïve/inadequate csDMARD): **57.3%** (risa) vs 33.5% (PBO)
  - KEEPsAKE 2 (bio-experienced): **51.3%** vs 26.5%
- **MDA (Minimal Disease Activity):** ~25–30% vs ~10% placebo
- **Enthesitis resolution, dactylitis resolution:** Significant improvements vs placebo
- **Radiographic progression:** Inhibition of joint damage at week 52 (mTSS)

### Crohn's Disease — ADVANCE and MOTIVATE (induction) + FORTIFY (maintenance)
**Induction (ADVANCE — bio-naïve/experienced; MOTIVATE — bio-experienced):**
- **Clinical remission at week 12 (ADVANCE):** **45%** (risa 600 mg IV) vs 25% (PBO; p<0.001)
- **Endoscopic response:** 40% vs 12% (ADVANCE; p<0.001)

**Maintenance (FORTIFY):**
- Induction responders randomized to risa 360 mg SQ Q8W vs placebo
- **Clinical remission at week 52:** **52%** (risa) vs 41% (PBO; p=0.009)
- **Endoscopic remission at week 52:** 47% vs 22% (p<0.001)

### Crohn's Disease — SEQUENCE Trial (vs ustekinumab — head-to-head, 2023)
- **Design:** Risankizumab vs ustekinumab in moderate-to-severe CD; bio-experienced
- **Endoscopic remission at week 48:** **31.8%** (risa) vs **16.2%** (uste) — **significant superiority** (p=0.0003)
- **Clinical remission:** 58.6% vs 39.5%
- First head-to-head biologic CD trial to demonstrate superiority — landmark result

### Ulcerative Colitis — INSPIRE (induction) + COMMAND (maintenance)
**Induction (INSPIRE):**
- **Clinical remission at week 12:** **20.3%** (risa) vs 6.2% (PBO; p<0.0001)
- **Endoscopic improvement:** 35.5% vs 10.3%
- **Histologic-endoscopic mucosal improvement:** 23.8% vs 5.4%

**Maintenance (COMMAND):**
- **Clinical remission at week 52:** **40.2%** (360 mg SQ Q8W) vs **21.7%** (PBO; p<0.0001)
- **Corticosteroid-free remission:** 37.7% vs 19.2%
- **Endoscopic remission:** 31.6% vs 12.7%

## Safety Profile

### Common Adverse Events (Psoriasis)
| AE | Risankizumab | Comparator |
|---|---|---|
| Nasopharyngitis | 13.0% | ~14% |
| Upper respiratory infections | 13.5% | ~11% |
| Headache | 5.2% | ~5% |
| Fatigue | 2.5% | ~3% |
| Injection site reactions | 0.8% | ~2% |
| Tinea infections | 1.2% | ~0.3% (signal) |

### Serious Adverse Events
| AE | Notes |
|---|---|
| Serious infections | 1.4%/year overall; similar to placebo in controlled trials |
| Malignancy | No increased risk observed in controlled or long-term data |
| MACE | No signal in trials; monitoring recommended |
| IBD adverse events (PsO/PsA patients) | No worsening of IBD observed (IL-23 pathway relevant to gut) |

### Black Box Warning
**None** — Skyrizi carries no black box warning.

### Comparison to TNF/IL-12/23 safety
- No TB screening required per label (though clinical judgment advises testing)
- No live vaccine restrictions (generally advised to complete vaccinations before initiation)
- No hepatotoxicity monitoring required
- No lipid panel required (unlike JAK inhibitors)

## Key HCP Messages

1. **PASI 100 (complete skin clearance) in up to 40% of patients:** Risankizumab achieves complete skin clearance (PASI 100) in 36–40% of patients at 16 weeks and up to 56% at 52 weeks — rates unmatched by IL-12/23 or TNF inhibitors.

2. **Head-to-head superiority over ustekinumab in both psoriasis and Crohn's disease:** In UltIMMa (PsO), risankizumab achieved nearly double the PASI 90 rate vs ustekinumab; in SEQUENCE (CD), endoscopic remission was double that of ustekinumab — the only biologic to demonstrate head-to-head superiority in both conditions.

3. **Quarterly dosing after year 1 (Q12W SQ):** Once weekly/biweekly loading is completed, patients move to a once-every-12-weeks subcutaneous injection — among the least frequent dosing schedules in the biologic class, supporting adherence and quality of life.

4. **No black box warning and favorable safety profile:** Skyrizi selectively targets IL-23p19 without broadly suppressing IL-12, preserving some innate immunity; clinical trials show no increased malignancy, no worsening IBD, and lower fungal infection rates vs IL-17 agents.

5. **Proven across dermatology and gastroenterology:** The only anti-IL-23 approved for both psoriatic disease (PsO + PsA) and inflammatory bowel disease (CD + UC), enabling consistent mechanism-based prescribing for patients with overlapping conditions.

## Patient Profile

### Ideal Psoriasis Patient
- Moderate-to-severe plaque psoriasis (BSA ≥10%, DLQI ≥10, or special sites)
- Inadequate response to MTX, phototherapy, or prior biologic
- Comorbid IBD or IBD family history (prefer IL-23 over IL-17)
- Patient who values infrequent (Q12W) dosing for adherence
- Seeking complete skin clearance (PASI 100)

### Ideal Crohn's Disease Patient
- Moderate-to-severe active CD (SES-CD ≥7, CDAI ≥220)
- Prior TNF failure (bio-experienced) or bio-naïve with severe disease
- Preference for IV induction then SQ maintenance (favorable for in-office monitoring)
- Comorbid psoriasis or PsA (treat both with single agent)

### Ideal UC Patient
- Moderate-to-severe UC (Mayo 6–12, endoscopic subscore ≥2)
- Prior failure of 5-ASA, corticosteroids, immunomodulators
- TNF-experienced or naïve
