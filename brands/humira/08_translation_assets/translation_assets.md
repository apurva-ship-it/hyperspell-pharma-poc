## 1. Brand Name Consistency Rules

### Primary Brand Name
- **Global Brand Name**: HUMIRA® (all caps; registered trademark required on first reference in all materials)
- **INN (International Non-proprietary Name)**: adalimumab (lowercase; no trademark)
- **Do NOT use**: Humira (mixed case), "adali" (abbreviation; internal documents only), "anti-TNF" alone (class description, not brand)
- **Biosimilar names**: Never refer to HUMIRA biosimilars by their brand names in promotional materials unless factually necessary for differentiation; use "adalimumab biosimilars" generically
- **AbbVie Portfolio Connection**: When referencing AbbVie immunology portfolio, always list as "HUMIRA (adalimumab), SKYRIZI (risankizumab), RINVOQ (upadacitinib)" — consistent ordering

### Country-Specific Brand Names
| Country/Region | Local Brand Name | Regulatory Authority | Notes |
|---------------|----------------|---------------------|-------|
| USA | HUMIRA | FDA | Reference country |
| EU (EMA) | HUMIRA | EMA | Same brand name retained across EU |
| Japan | ヒュミラ (Hyumira) | PMDA/MHLW | Katakana; AbbVie Japan markets |
| China | 修美乐 (Xiū Měi Lè) | NMPA | Phonetic + aspirational meaning ("beautiful healing") |
| Korea | 휴미라 (Hyumira) | MFDS | Phonetic in Hangul |
| Brazil | HUMIRA | ANVISA | Same name; Portuguese package insert |
| India | HUMIRA | CDSCO | Distributed via AbbVie India/MSD partnership historically |

### Trademark Usage Rules
1. ® required on first mention in every document type (promotional, scientific, educational)
2. Subsequent mentions in same document: HUMIRA without ® acceptable
3. adalimumab should never be capitalized mid-sentence except when starting sentence
4. Company reference: "AbbVie" not "Abbvie" or "ABBVIE"
5. Do not attach emotional brand descriptors (e.g., "revolutionary," "breakthrough") without MLR clearance and substantiation
6. When mentioning biosimilars in HCP materials: use non-derogatory language; factual only

## 2. Core Messages in Simplified Form (Global Adaptation)

### Core Message 1: Proven Long-Term Safety
**Full US Version**: "HUMIRA is backed by more than 1 million patient-years of real-world safety and efficacy data — the largest post-market safety surveillance dataset for any biologic medicine."

**Simplified Global Core**: "HUMIRA has been prescribed to millions of patients worldwide for over 25 years, building one of the largest safety records of any biologic treatment."

**Translation Notes**:
- "Patient-years" (patient-années in French; Patientenjahre in German; 患者年 in Japanese): Requires explanatory footnote in lay materials — "the equivalent of one million patients each treated for one year"
- "Biologic medicine" (médicament biologique in French; Biologikum in German; 生物学的製剤 in Japanese): Technical term; acceptable in HCP materials; for patient materials use "injectable medicine made from living cells"
- "Post-market surveillance" — replace with "monitoring of patients after the medicine was approved" in lay versions
- EU Note: "Largest dataset of any biologic" is a comparative superiority claim; requires substantiation filing under EFPIA Code; use only in markets where this claim is pre-approved
- China Note: NMPA promotional regulations prohibit unsubstantiated superlative claims; use "extensive real-world safety data" without "largest" claim unless locally substantiated

---

### Core Message 2: Broad Indication Coverage
**Full US Version**: "HUMIRA is approved for 10 distinct FDA indications — from rheumatoid arthritis to hidradenitis suppurativa — providing the most comprehensive indication coverage of any TNF inhibitor."

**Simplified Global Core**: "HUMIRA is approved to treat a wide range of conditions — including joint diseases like rheumatoid arthritis, inflammatory bowel conditions, skin diseases like psoriasis, and hidradenitis suppurativa — offering a treatment option for many different patients with immune-related conditions."

**Translation Notes**:
- "10 FDA indications" — replace with locally applicable indication count (e.g., EMA-approved indications may differ; some markets may have 7–9 indications)
- "TNF inhibitor" — retain for HCP materials in all markets; in patient materials replace with "this class of medicines that reduce inflammation by targeting TNF, a protein involved in the inflammatory process"
- "Hidradenitis suppurativa" — requires local language note in many markets; patient-facing: "a painful, long-term skin condition causing lumps under the skin in areas like the armpits or groin"
- Japan: HS indication approved; use Japanese disease term 化膿性汗腺炎 (Kajōsei Kansenēn) in Japanese-language materials
- EM Markets: HS indication may not be locally approved; omit from materials in markets without HS approval; use only locally approved indication list

---

### Core Message 3: Patient-Centered Formulation
**Full US Version**: "HUMIRA's high-concentration, citrate-free formulation reduces injection site pain, with 86% of patients reporting mild or no pain — supporting adherence and patient preference."

**Simplified Global Core**: "HUMIRA's specially formulated injection is designed to be comfortable — most patients report little or no pain, which helps them stay on treatment over the long term."

**Translation Notes**:
- "Citrate-free" — technical term acceptable in all HCP materials globally; for patient materials: "formulated without a preservative that can cause injection stinging"
- "High-concentration" — acceptable globally; context: "means less volume is injected"
- "86% of patients reporting mild or no pain" — specific claim requires source citation (published ISR comparison study); use data only where study has been referenced in submitted promotional materials under local regulations
- EU Note: Patient-directed communications heavily restricted; formulation comfort data allowed in HCP-directed materials with full citation
- Japan: Citrate-free formulation; ISR data should reference Japanese-specific PK/safety studies or bridge to published global data per PMDA guidelines
- Emerging Markets: Focus on practical benefit: "less discomfort helps patients continue their treatment as prescribed"

## 3. Cultural Considerations by Region

### United States
- **Communication Style**: Direct and data-driven; HCPs expect clinical trial citation; patients expect empowerment and shared decision-making
- **Regulatory Framework**: FDA; DDMAC enforces DTC and HCP promotional standards; ISI required on all promotional materials
- **Biosimilar Context**: Biosimilar competition creates need for direct differentiation messaging; HCPs aware of biosimilar economics
- **Patient Advocacy**: Strong patient advocacy in RA (Arthritis Foundation), IBD (CCFA/Crohn's & Colitis Foundation), HS Foundation — sponsorship opportunities
- **Digital**: High digital HCP and patient engagement; e-detailing common; patient communities active on CCFA forums, Reddit (r/Crohns, r/HS, r/rheumatoid)
- **Key Sensitivities**: Avoid "cure" language; avoid disparaging biosimilar language; financial toxicity is a sensitive topic; diversity and inclusion required in imagery
- **DTC Advertising**: Permitted; HUMIRA has run significant DTC investment historically; biosimilar era reduces DTC ROI — budget being redirected to HCP and digital

---

### European Union
- **Communication Style**: Evidence-based, conservative; ESMO/EULAR/ECCO guidelines are authoritative; HCPs reference local practice guidelines
- **Regulatory Framework**: EMA (scientific approval); EFPIA Codes govern promotion; national HTA bodies (G-BA Germany, HAS France, NICE UK, AIFA Italy) determine reimbursement
- **Biosimilar Context**: EU biosimilar penetration >80% in most markets; HUMIRA originator market is very small; strategy is portfolio (SKYRIZI/RINVOQ) rather than HUMIRA defense
- **Key Restrictions**: No DTC advertising; no gifts to HCPs; congress sponsorship heavily regulated; medical education vs. promotion strictly distinguished
- **Language Requirements**: Each EU member state requires materials in local language (German, French, Italian, Spanish, Polish, etc.); translations must be verified by local medical/legal teams
- **GDPR**: Any patient data collected in digital programs must comply with GDPR; explicit opt-in required
- **HTA Nuances**:
  - Germany (AMNOG): Benefit assessment after approval; "added benefit" rating affects price; HUMIRA has "no additional benefit" in most indications vs. comparators due to biosimilar comparison
  - France (HAS): SMR/ASMR ratings; HUMIRA rated "important" (SMR importante) for most indications
  - UK (NICE): HUMIRA retained Technology Appraisal guidance for key indications; NHS biosimilar-first policy effectively displaces HUMIRA in most clinical use

---

### Japan
- **Communication Style**: Formal, relationship-based; detailed; indirect feedback culture; extensive package insert reading before prescribing
- **Regulatory Framework**: PMDA (approval); MHLW (drug pricing 薬価); biannual drug price revisions; market expansion re-examination every 5–7 years for new indications
- **Biosimilar Context**: Japanese adalimumab biosimilars (Juluca, Adalimumab BS) launched from 2017; penetration growing but slower than EU (Japan HCPs historically prefer originators due to efficacy/safety trust)
- **Key Sensitivities**: HCPs value peer relationships and personal trust; MSL/MR relationship building takes years; publications in Japanese journals (e.g., Nihon Rinsho) matter
- **Language**: All promotional and medical materials must be in Japanese; HUMIRA brand name in Katakana: ヒュミラ; adalimumab in Japanese: アダリムマブ (Adarimumab)
- **Patient Communication**: Informed consent culture; patients typically less involved in treatment decisions than US; physicians guide strongly
- **Post-Market Data**: PMDA requires post-marketing surveillance (特定使用成績調査) for new indications; safety data from Japanese patients required for some indications

---

### Emerging Markets (Brazil, India, Middle East, Southeast Asia)

**Brazil**:
- ANVISA regulates promotion; INTERFARMA Code applies
- SUS (public health system) provides HUMIRA for RA/AS/CD/UC at limited access points; private market stronger
- Key specialist societies: SBR (Sociedade Brasileira de Reumatologia), SBAD (dermatology), FBG (gastroenterology)
- Language: Brazilian Portuguese; all materials require local adaptation; European Portuguese content not acceptable
- Biosimilar access: Domestic Brazilian biosimilar (Adalvo, Sirio-libanes formulations) available at substantially lower cost; competes for SUS inclusion

**India**:
- CDSCO regulates; drug promotion code of OPPI (affiliate of IFPMA) applies
- HUMIRA distributed through AbbVie India; strong KOL network at AIIMS, PGI Chandigarh, CMC Vellore
- Price sensitivity very high; HUMIRA at ~60% WAC makes access limited for most patients; patient assistance programs critical
- Language: English widely used in urban HCP communication; regional language patient materials helpful (Hindi, Tamil, Telugu, Marathi)
- Cultural note: Family-centric treatment decisions; involve family in patient counseling materials; Ayurvedic/integrative medicine awareness

**Middle East (GCC)**:
- Ministry of Health (MOH) in Saudi Arabia, UAE, Kuwait drives formulary decisions; tender-based procurement
- Key congress: APLAR (Asia-Pacific League of Associations for Rheumatology); EULAR Middle East CME programs
- Arabic language materials required for Saudi Arabia; English materials widely accepted in UAE physician community
- Biosimilar penetration growing in GCC; Saudi SFDA approving local and international biosimilars

**Southeast Asia**:
- ASEAN harmonization progressing; Singapore HSA, Malaysia NPRA, Thailand FDA as reference agencies
- Singapore: High-income; HUMIRA well-established; biosimilar entry from ~2020; AbbVie maintains strong presence
- Thailand: NLEM (National List of Essential Medicines) drives public formulary; HUMIRA for selected indications
- Indonesia: Major market by volume; BPOM approval; public JKN insurance system covers limited biologic access

## 4. Glossary of Approved Terms

| Approved Term | Definition | Do NOT Use |
|--------------|-----------|-----------|
| TNF-alpha (TNF-α) inhibitor | Class of drugs blocking tumor necrosis factor alpha; reduces inflammation | "Anti-inflammatory shot," "immuno-blocker" (too vague) |
| Adalimumab | INN; generic name for HUMIRA | "adali" (abbreviation), "biologic Humira" |
| Biologic | Medicine derived from living organisms; large-molecule drug | "Drug," "medicine" alone for precision communications |
| Biologic DMARD (bDMARD) | Biologic disease-modifying antirheumatic drug | "Biologic therapy" (acceptable shorthand in context) |
| Conventional synthetic DMARD (csDMARD) | Small-molecule DMARDs: MTX, leflunomide, HCQ, SSZ | "DMARD" without qualifier (ambiguous) |
| ACR20/50/70 | American College of Rheumatology response criteria (% improvement in joint count + other measures) | "Improvement" without specifying criteria |
| DAS28 | Disease Activity Score-28; joint assessment score | "Disease score" alone |
| PASI 75/90/100 | Psoriasis Area and Severity Index; % improvement | "Skin clearance" (acceptable lay summary) |
| CDAI | Crohn's Disease Activity Index | "CD score" |
| HiSCR | Hidradenitis Suppurativa Clinical Response (≥50% reduction in abscess/nodule count) | "HS response" alone |
| Injection site reaction (ISR) | Local skin reaction at injection site (redness, pain, swelling) | "Shot reaction," "skin allergy" |
| Methotrexate (MTX) | Anchor csDMARD; used in combination with HUMIRA | "Background therapy" alone |
| Real-world evidence (RWE) | Evidence generated from real-world clinical practice data | "Real-life data" (acceptable informal) |
| Patient-years | Total cumulative exposure: sum of years each patient has been followed | "Total time on drug" (acceptable informally) |
| Citrate-free | Formulated without citrate buffer; reduces injection site stinging | "No citrate" (acceptable informal equivalent) |
| Anti-drug antibodies (ADA/AAA) | Immunogenic response to adalimumab; may reduce efficacy | "Resistance to HUMIRA" (oversimplified) |
| Secondary failure | Loss of initial clinical response; may be ADA-mediated | "Drug stopped working" (acceptable in lay context with explanation) |
| Treat-to-target (T2T) | Strategy of adjusting therapy to achieve predefined clinical target | "Goal-based treatment" |
