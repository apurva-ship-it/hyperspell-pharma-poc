## 1. Brand Name Consistency Rules

### Primary Brand Name
- **US/Global Brand Name**: KEYTRUDA® (all caps, registered trademark symbol always required on first reference)
- **INN (International Non-proprietary Name)**: pembrolizumab (lowercase; no trademark)
- **QLEX SC Brand**: KEYTRUDA QLEX™ (trademark pending in some jurisdictions) — use "pembrolizumab SC injection" where trademark not cleared
- **Do NOT use**: Keytruda (mixed case without approval), pembro (abbreviation acceptable in internal documents only; never in promotional materials)

### Country-Specific Brand Names
| Country/Region | Local Brand Name | Regulatory Authority | Notes |
|---------------|----------------|---------------------|-------|
| USA | KEYTRUDA | FDA | Reference country |
| EU (EMA) | KEYTRUDA | EMA | Same brand name maintained |
| Japan | キイトルーダ (Keytruda) | PMDA/MHLW | Katakana pronunciation maintained |
| China | 可瑞达 (Kě Ruì Dá) | NMPA | Phonetic transliteration; approved by NMPA |
| Korea | 키트루다 (Kiteuruda) | MFDS | Phonetic in Hangul |
| Brazil | KEYTRUDA | ANVISA | Same name; Portuguese package insert |
| India | KEYTRUDA | CDSCO | Same name; marketed by MSD India |

### Trademark Rules
1. Always use ® on first mention of KEYTRUDA in any document or promotional piece
2. Use ™ (not ®) for QLEX in markets where trademark registration pending
3. Pembrolizumab should never be capitalized mid-sentence as a proper noun
4. Generic name (pembrolizumab) must accompany brand name on first reference in all regulated promotional materials
5. Do not create portmanteau or unofficial abbreviations (e.g., "pembro" = internal use only)

## 2. Core Messages in Simplified Form (Global Adaptation)

### Core Message 1: Proven Long-Term Survival
**Full US Version**: "In the Phase 3 KEYNOTE-024 trial, KEYTRUDA demonstrated a 5-year overall survival rate of 31.9% compared to 16.3% with platinum-based chemotherapy in patients with PD-L1 TPS ≥50% previously untreated metastatic NSCLC (HR 0.62; 95% CI 0.48–0.81)."

**Simplified Global Core**: "KEYTRUDA extended long-term survival in patients with high PD-L1 expression advanced lung cancer, with nearly 1 in 3 patients alive at 5 years."

**Key Translation Notes**:
- "PD-L1" is universally understood in oncology; retain in all languages without translation
- "5-year overall survival" — in regions without survival benchmark context (EM), provide historical chemotherapy comparison explicitly
- "HR 0.62" — in some regulatory contexts (e.g., Japan, China), confidence intervals must be expressed in local statistical format
- Avoid: "cure," "remission," "beats," "destroys" — use neutral outcome language

---

### Core Message 2: Biomarker-Driven Treatment Selection
**Full US Version**: "KEYTRUDA harnesses validated biomarkers — PD-L1 (TPS or CPS), MSI-H/dMMR, and TMB-H — to identify patients most likely to benefit, enabling precision immuno-oncology."

**Simplified Global Core**: "KEYTRUDA works best when patients are selected using biomarker testing — tumors with specific immune markers respond better to this treatment."

**Key Translation Notes**:
- "Biomarker" translates directly in most scientific contexts; verify regulatory acceptance of term in China (生物标志物) and Japan (バイオマーカー)
- In Emerging Markets (EM) where PD-L1 testing infrastructure is limited, messaging may need adaptation: "Testing can help identify which patients are most likely to benefit"
- MSI-H/dMMR: Always retain acronym with full expansion on first use in all languages
- TMB-H: Explain "tumor mutational burden" with brief description in lay materials: "the number of genetic changes in the tumor"
- Regulatory note: Tumor-agnostic messaging requires local label compliance; EMA does not have same tumor-agnostic label structure as FDA — do NOT use tumor-agnostic messaging in EU promotional materials without EMA indication confirmation

---

### Core Message 3: Broad Indication Portfolio
**Full US Version**: "KEYTRUDA has over 40 FDA-approved indications across more than 20 tumor types — more than any other checkpoint inhibitor — representing the most broadly validated immuno-oncology agent available."

**Simplified Global Core**: "KEYTRUDA is approved for use in many different types of cancer, giving oncologists a well-established treatment option for a wide range of patients."

**Key Translation Notes**:
- "40+ FDA indications" — rephrase to "X locally approved indications" in each market; FDA is US-specific authority; replace with EMA/PMDA/NMPA/CDSCO equivalents
- "Checkpoint inhibitor" — retain in HCP-directed materials; simplify to "immunotherapy" in patient-directed content
- "Most broadly approved" — competitive superiority claim; verify permissibility under local promotional code (EFPIA in EU, JPMA in Japan; substantiation required)
- In markets with fewer approvals (e.g., Emerging Markets with 5–10 indications), do not use "40+ indications" messaging — use locally applicable indication count

## 3. Cultural Considerations by Region

### United States
- **Communication Style**: Direct, data-driven; HCPs expect statistical evidence and clinical trial names
- **Regulatory Framework**: FDA; promotional materials require full ISI (Important Safety Information) or fair balance
- **Patient Communication**: Empowering language; patient as active participant in treatment decision
- **Key Sensitivities**: Avoid "cure" language (FDA enforcement risk); drug pricing sensitivity for DTC; disparities messaging for underserved populations
- **Digital**: High digital engagement; HCPs active on Doceree, Epocrates; patients on WebMD, patient forums
- **Imagery**: Diverse representation (racial, age, gender) required; LGBTQ+ inclusive representation increasingly expected

---

### European Union
- **Communication Style**: Evidence-based; ESMO guidelines heavily referenced; conservative on superiority claims
- **Regulatory Framework**: EMA; EFPIA Code governs promotional conduct; each member state has national authority (MHRA UK, ANSM France, BfArM Germany)
- **Label Differences**: EMA indication language may differ from FDA (e.g., PD-L1 thresholds may vary; EMA does not have tumor-agnostic approval for all MSI-H tumors)
- **Key Sensitivities**: No DTC advertising permitted in EU; all promotion restricted to healthcare professionals; pricing and reimbursement determined at national level (AMNOG in Germany, SMR/ASMR in France, NICE in UK)
- **Medical Education**: ECCO, ESMO, ASCO (EU chapter) — major HCP congress sponsorships; symposia at ESMO annual meeting
- **Cultural Notes**: Germany — methodological rigor expected; France — preference for French-language materials from KOLs; UK — NHS cost-effectiveness framing paramount; Nordics — high transparency expectations, access through tender processes
- **Data Privacy**: GDPR compliance mandatory; patient-level data handling requires DPA/DPO review

---

### Japan
- **Communication Style**: Formal, relationship-driven; trust built over long-term engagement; indirect communication style preferred
- **Regulatory Framework**: PMDA (pharmaceutical approval); MHLW (marketing authorization); J-code equivalent (薬価): reimbursement pricing set by MHLW biannually
- **Approval Status**: KEYTRUDA approved by PMDA for multiple indications; some indications lag FDA approval by 1–2 years
- **Key Sensitivities**: Direct comparison claims highly regulated; use absolute data; comparative claims require J-KOL consensus
- **Medical Education**: JSCO (Japanese Society of Clinical Oncology) — key annual congress; JCOG trial network — respect local evidence generation
- **Language**: All promotional materials must be in Japanese (日本語); kimono culture of detailed package inserts; physicians read full PI before prescribing
- **Pricing**: Drug price (薬価) set by government; KEYTRUDA IV and SC priced at MHLW-approved rates; re-examination every 2 years
- **Cultural Nuances**: Patient preference discussions are physician-led (more paternalistic model vs. US); shared decision-making materials adapted accordingly; emphasis on HCP confidence rather than patient activation

---

### Emerging Markets (Brazil, India, Southeast Asia, Middle East)
- **Communication Style**: Relationship and trust-based; KOL influence paramount; congress-driven education
- **Regulatory Frameworks**: ANVISA (Brazil), CDSCO (India), HSA (Singapore), SFDA (Saudi Arabia) — each with unique submission and promotional requirements
- **Key Sensitivities**:
  - Affordability is primary barrier; access programs messaging central
  - Testing infrastructure variability — PD-L1 testing availability is limited in rural settings; messaging must address
  - Generic/biosimilar alternatives may be preferred or mandated by government formularies
  - Regulatory approvals lag FDA by 2–4 years in some markets; use only locally approved indications
- **Language**: Materials required in local languages — Portuguese (Brazil), Hindi/regional languages (India), Arabic (GCC), Thai/Bahasa (SEA)
- **Digital**: Mobile-first environments; WhatsApp-based HCP communication common; digital sampling increasing
- **Cultural Nuances**:
  - India: Strong preference for published studies in NEJM, Lancet as credibility markers; Ayurvedic/integrative medicine coexistence; family-centric treatment decisions
  - Brazil: SUS (public system) vs. private payer differentiation critical; KOL networks at INCA and state cancer centers
  - Middle East: Single-payer government systems in GCC; Ministry of Health decisions drive formulary; relationship with MOH medical committees essential
  - Southeast Asia: ASEAN harmonization initiatives; Singapore as regional hub; Thailand's NLEM negotiations

## 4. Glossary of Approved Terms

| Approved Term | Definition | Do Not Use |
|--------------|-----------|-----------|
| Immune-mediated adverse reactions | Inflammation caused by overactivation of immune system | "Autoimmune side effects," "immune reactions" (too generic) |
| PD-1 (Programmed Death-1) | Inhibitory checkpoint receptor on T cells | "Death receptor" alone (misleading to lay audiences) |
| PD-L1 (Programmed Death-Ligand 1) | Ligand on tumor cells that binds PD-1 | "Cancer protein" (oversimplified) |
| Tumor Proportion Score (TPS) | % of tumor cells with PD-L1 expression | "PD-L1 score" alone (ambiguous) |
| Combined Positive Score (CPS) | PD-L1 positive cells / total viable tumor cells × 100 | "CPS score" (redundant) |
| Microsatellite Instability-High (MSI-H) | Tumor with defective mismatch repair causing repetitive DNA errors | "Unstable cancer" |
| Mismatch Repair Deficient (dMMR) | Loss of one or more MMR proteins (MLH1, MSH2, MSH6, PMS2) | "Bad DNA" |
| Tumor Mutational Burden-High (TMB-H) | ≥10 somatic mutations per megabase by Foundation Medicine CDx | "Lots of mutations" |
| Immune checkpoint inhibitor | Class of drugs that block inhibitory immune receptors | "Cancer immunotherapy pill" (KEYTRUDA is not oral) |
| Pembrolizumab | INN; generic name of KEYTRUDA | "pembro" (internal only), "PD-1 antibody" (too vague) |
| Immune-related adverse event (irAE) | AE resulting from immune system overactivation; preferred internal term | "Side effect" alone (ambiguous) |
| Overall Survival (OS) | Time from randomization to death from any cause | "Survival benefit" without qualification |
| Progression-Free Survival (PFS) | Time from randomization to disease progression or death | "Time without cancer growth" (acceptable in lay materials) |
| Objective Response Rate (ORR) | Proportion of patients with CR or PR | "Response rate" (acceptable shorthand with context) |
| Complete Response (CR) | No detectable tumor on imaging | "Cancer-free" (not approved; remission language requires caution) |
| Partial Response (PR) | ≥30% reduction in sum of target lesion diameters | "Tumor shrinkage" (acceptable in lay materials) |
| Subcutaneous (SC) | Administration under the skin | "Shot," "jab" (informal; use in lay patient materials only with MLR approval) |
