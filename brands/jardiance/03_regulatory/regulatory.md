# JARDIANCE (Empagliflozin) — Regulatory & Prescribing Information Reference
**Brand:** JARDIANCE (empagliflozin) | **Company:** Boehringer Ingelheim Pharmaceuticals / Eli Lilly and Company
**NDA:** 204629 | **FDA Original Approval:** August 1, 2014 | **Document Version:** 2024

---

## 1. Approved Indications (US FDA Label)

### Full Approved Indication Text (Current as of 2024)
JARDIANCE (empagliflozin) is indicated:

**1. Glycemic Control (T2D):**
As an adjunct to diet and exercise to improve glycemic control in adults with type 2 diabetes mellitus.

*Limitation of Use:* JARDIANCE is not recommended in patients with type 1 diabetes. It is not indicated for use in patients with an estimated glomerular filtration rate (eGFR) <30 mL/min/1.73 m² for the glycemic indication.

**2. Cardiovascular Mortality Reduction (T2D + CVD):**
To reduce the risk of cardiovascular death in adults with type 2 diabetes mellitus and established cardiovascular disease.

**3. Heart Failure (HFrEF):**
To reduce the risk of cardiovascular death and hospitalization for heart failure in adults with heart failure with reduced ejection fraction.

**4. Heart Failure (HFpEF — All Ejection Fractions):**
To reduce the risk of cardiovascular death and hospitalization for heart failure in adults with heart failure.
*(Note: This approval covers the full EF spectrum, including HFpEF/HFmrEF)*

**5. Chronic Kidney Disease:**
To reduce the risk of sustained eGFR decline, end-stage kidney disease, cardiovascular death, and hospitalization for heart failure in adults with chronic kidney disease at risk of progression.

## 2. Dosing and Administration

### Recommended Doses
| Indication | Starting Dose | Max Dose | Frequency |
|-----------|--------------|---------|-----------|
| T2D glycemic control | 10 mg QD | 25 mg QD | Once daily |
| CV mortality reduction (T2D+CVD) | 10 mg QD | 10 mg QD (approved dose) | Once daily |
| Heart failure (HFrEF/HFpEF) | 10 mg QD | 10 mg QD | Once daily |
| Chronic kidney disease | 10 mg QD | 10 mg QD | Once daily |

**Administration:**
- Take once daily in the morning, with or without food
- Swallow tablets whole; available as 10mg and 25mg tablets
- 25mg dose is only for additional glycemic control in T2D; ALL cardio-renal indications use 10mg

### eGFR-Based Dosing Restrictions
| Indication | eGFR Threshold | Action |
|-----------|---------------|--------|
| T2D glycemic control | <30 mL/min/1.73m² | Do not use for glycemic indication |
| HF indications | No eGFR restriction (down to ~20 per EMPEROR data) | Initiate at 10mg; monitor |
| CKD indication | eGFR ≥20 mL/min/1.73m² | Initiate at 10mg; may continue even if eGFR declines during therapy |
| Any indication | eGFR <20 or dialysis | Not recommended |

**Important:** JARDIANCE can be initiated and continued in HF/CKD patients with lower eGFR than previously allowed. The "eGFR ≥45 required" restriction no longer applies to HF/CKD indications.

### Perioperative Management
- **Hold JARDIANCE before elective surgery or invasive procedures** that require fasting
- Recommended hold period: at least 3–4 days before the planned procedure
- Resume JARDIANCE when the patient is eating and drinking normally and renal function is stable
- Rationale: Risk of euglycemic DKA during prolonged fasting or surgical stress

## 3. Contraindications

| Contraindication | Details |
|----------------|---------|
| Serious hypersensitivity to empagliflozin | Anaphylaxis, angioedema; use contraindicated if prior serious hypersensitivity |
| Dialysis-dependent end-stage renal disease | eGFR effectively 0; no therapeutic benefit |

**Note:** Unlike older sulfonylureas or insulin, JARDIANCE does NOT have contraindications related to cardiac conditions, hepatic impairment (mild-moderate), or age. This broad applicability is a commercial messaging point.

## 4. Warnings and Precautions

### 4.1 Diabetic Ketoacidosis (DKA)
- **Risk:** DKA has been reported in patients with T2D and T1D taking SGLT2 inhibitors, including JARDIANCE
- **Frequency:** Rare; approximately 0.1–0.2% per year in T2D patients
- **Euglycemic DKA:** Critical concept — blood glucose may be only modestly elevated (or even normal/low) while DKA is present; clinicians may not recognize DKA if they rely only on glucose
- **Risk Factors:** Reduced caloric intake, alcohol, illness, surgical procedures, volume depletion, reduced insulin dose
- **Management:** If suspected, discontinue JARDIANCE and initiate standard DKA management; check blood/urine ketones even with near-normal glucose
- **Sick-day rules:** Educate patients to hold JARDIANCE when not eating/drinking (gastroenteritis, fasting for procedures)

### 4.2 Urinary Tract Infections (UTIs)
- **Frequency:** 9–18% (any grade); serious UTIs (pyelonephritis, urosepsis) less common but reported
- **Mechanism:** Glucosuria provides substrate for bacterial growth in the bladder/urinary tract
- **Patient Counseling:** Encourage adequate hydration; prompt reporting of UTI symptoms (dysuria, frequency, urgency, fever)
- **Management:** Standard UTI treatment; JARDIANCE can usually be continued through uncomplicated UTI

### 4.3 Genital Mycotic Infections
- **Frequency:** Women: ~10% (any grade); Men: ~6% (any grade)
- **Mechanism:** Glucosuria creates warm, moist, glucose-rich environment favorable for Candida
- **Patient Counseling:** Hygiene guidance; prompt treatment with topical antifungal if symptoms occur
- **Management:** Topical antifungal; JARDIANCE typically continued; dose reduction not required

### 4.4 Hypotension / Volume Depletion
- **Risk:** Empagliflozin causes diuresis; patients with baseline volume depletion, low blood pressure, diuretic use, elderly patients are at risk
- **Assess volume status before initiating** in elderly patients, renally impaired, or those on diuretics
- **BP reduction:** ~3–4 mmHg systolic; beneficial for most HF/CKD patients but monitor for symptomatic hypotension

### 4.5 Fournier's Gangrene (Necrotizing Fasciitis of the Perineum)
- **Class Warning (ALL SGLT2 inhibitors):** Rare, serious, life-threatening infection of the genitalia/perineum
- **Frequency:** Very rare; estimated <1:10,000 exposures based on post-marketing case reports
- **Action:** Assess for perineal pain, tenderness, swelling, erythema; if suspected, discontinue JARDIANCE immediately; initiate broad-spectrum antibiotics and urgent surgical consultation

### 4.6 Lower Limb Amputation
- **Note:** No increased risk of lower limb amputation with empagliflozin
- **Contrast with canagliflozin (Invokana):** CANVAS trial demonstrated increased amputation risk with canagliflozin (HR ~2x); this led to Black Box Warning for canagliflozin
- **Prescribing talking point:** JARDIANCE does NOT carry an amputation warning or Black Box Warning

### 4.7 No Black Box Warning
JARDIANCE does **NOT** have a Black Box Warning. This is a critical differentiator from canagliflozin (Invokana), which carries a BBW for lower extremity amputations.

### 4.8 Embryo-Fetal Toxicity
- Avoid use during the 2nd and 3rd trimesters of pregnancy
- Empagliflozin may cause renal-related adverse developmental effects based on animal studies
- Use effective contraception during treatment

## 6. Drug Interactions

### Key Drug Interactions
| Drug Category | Specific Agents | Interaction | Recommendation |
|-------------|----------------|-------------|----------------|
| Insulin / Sulfonylureas | Glipizide, glimepiride, insulin | Additive hypoglycemia risk | Reduce insulin/SU dose by ~20% when adding empagliflozin |
| Diuretics | Loop diuretics (furosemide), thiazides | Additive diuresis; volume depletion risk | Monitor BP, hydration; may allow diuretic dose reduction |
| UGT inducers | Rifampin | Potential reduction in empagliflozin exposure | Monitor glycemic control; may need dose increase to 25mg |
| Lithium | Lithium carbonate | Empagliflozin may increase lithium excretion | Monitor lithium levels |
| ACEi/ARB/RAAS agents | Ramipril, enalapril, losartan | Additive BP lowering; additive renal benefit | Beneficial combination in CKD; monitor K+, BP, creatinine |

**Note:** Empagliflozin is primarily metabolized by UGT enzymes (not CYP); therefore, CYP-based drug interactions are not a significant concern — differentiating it from some other agents.

## 7. Special Populations

### Renal Impairment
| eGFR (mL/min/1.73m²) | T2D Glycemic Indication | HF / CKD Indications |
|----------------------|------------------------|----------------------|
| ≥60 | Standard dose 10–25mg | 10mg |
| 45–59 | 10–25mg; 25mg may have reduced glycemic effect | 10mg |
| 30–44 | Not recommended for glycemic efficacy | 10mg (EMPA-KIDNEY enrolled eGFR 20–45) |
| 20–<30 | Contraindicated for glycemic indication | **10mg — approved per EMPA-KIDNEY** |
| <20 / Dialysis | Contraindicated | Not recommended |

### Hepatic Impairment
| Hepatic Function | Recommendation |
|-----------------|----------------|
| Mild (Child-Pugh A) | No dose adjustment |
| Moderate (Child-Pugh B) | No dose adjustment |
| Severe (Child-Pugh C) | Limited data; use with caution |

### Geriatric Patients (≥75 years)
- EMPA-REG, EMPEROR trials included patients ≥75 years; no dose adjustment required
- Higher risk of volume depletion/hypotension in elderly; assess volume status before initiating
- Genital mycotic infections may be more challenging to diagnose in elderly patients
- eGFR monitoring more important in elderly (age-related GFR decline)

### Pediatric Patients
- Not approved for use in patients <18 years
- Safety and efficacy not established in pediatric population

### Pregnancy and Lactation
- Avoid use in 2nd and 3rd trimesters (adverse developmental effects in animals)
- 1st trimester: Consider risk-benefit; developmental toxicity risk is lower but data limited
- Lactation: Avoid; not known if empagliflozin is excreted in human milk

## 8. Monitoring Parameters

### Pre-Initiation Assessment
- [ ] Confirm eGFR (to establish baseline and appropriateness per indication)
- [ ] Assess volume status (especially elderly, diuretic users)
- [ ] Check blood pressure baseline
- [ ] Confirm absence of T1D (DKA risk much higher in T1D)
- [ ] Assess genital hygiene history / prior UTI/genital mycotic infection
- [ ] Educate on sick-day rules (hold for procedures, illness, fasting)
- [ ] Confirm not pregnant; counsel women of reproductive potential

### Ongoing Monitoring
| Parameter | Frequency | Notes |
|-----------|-----------|-------|
| eGFR | Every 3–6 months (CKD) | Acute eGFR dip upon initiation (~5% expected; reversible; long-term benefit maintained) |
| Blood pressure | Routine clinical visits | Beneficial reduction expected |
| Weight | Routine | ~2–3 kg expected reduction |
| HbA1c (T2D) | Every 3–6 months | Glycemic response assessment |
| Genital/UTI symptoms | At each visit | Patient education on recognizing symptoms |
| DKA symptoms | Patient education | Sick-day rules; hold before procedures |
| Potassium | If on RAAS agents | Additive K+ effects; monitor K+ in CKD |
