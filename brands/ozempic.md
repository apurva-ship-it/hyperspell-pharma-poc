# Ozempic (semaglutide) — Brand Intelligence

**Generic Name:** Semaglutide  
**Brand:** Ozempic (T2D injectable); Wegovy (obesity); Rybelsus (oral T2D)  
**Company:** Novo Nordisk  
**Drug Class:** GLP-1 receptor agonist (GLP-1 RA)  
**Mechanism of Action:** Activates GLP-1 receptors → glucose-dependent insulin secretion, glucagon suppression, delayed gastric emptying, central appetite suppression via hypothalamic pathways  
**FDA Approval:** December 2017 (Ozempic for T2D); June 2021 (Wegovy for obesity)  
**Formulation:** Ozempic: 0.5 mg, 1 mg, 2 mg/dose pre-filled pen (SQ QW); Wegovy: 2.4 mg/dose SQ QW; Rybelsus: 3 mg, 7 mg, 14 mg oral tablet QD  

---

## Approved Indications

### Ozempic (semaglutide injection)
- **Type 2 Diabetes (T2D):** To improve glycemic control in adults with T2D, as adjunct to diet and exercise
- **Cardiovascular Risk Reduction (2020):** Reduce risk of major adverse cardiovascular events (MACE: CV death, non-fatal MI, non-fatal stroke) in adults with T2D and established CVD

### Wegovy (semaglutide 2.4 mg)
- **Chronic weight management** in adults with BMI ≥30 or ≥27 with ≥1 weight-related comorbidity
- **Cardiovascular risk reduction (2024, SELECT trial):** Reduce MACE in adults with established CVD and overweight/obesity (BMI ≥27), WITHOUT diabetes — landmark expansion

### Rybelsus (oral semaglutide)
- **T2D glycemic control** in adults; PIONEER program (oral)

## Clinical Efficacy

### SUSTAIN-6 (Ozempic, T2D + CVD)
- **Design:** Semaglutide SQ 0.5/1 mg vs placebo, 2-year CV outcomes, T2D + high CV risk
- **MACE reduction:** 26% relative risk reduction (HR 0.74, 95% CI 0.58–0.95, p=0.016 non-inferiority; p=0.02 superiority)
- **CV death + non-fatal MI + non-fatal stroke:** 6.6% vs 8.9%
- **HbA1c reduction:** −1.1% (0.5 mg), −1.4% (1 mg) vs −0.4% (placebo)
- **Weight loss:** −3.5 kg (0.5 mg), −4.9 kg (1 mg) vs +0.4 kg (placebo)

### SUSTAIN-7 (Ozempic vs Trulicity — head-to-head)
- **Sema 0.5 mg vs Dulg 0.75 mg:** HbA1c −1.5% vs −1.1%; Weight −4.6 vs −2.3 kg
- **Sema 1 mg vs Dulg 1.5 mg:** HbA1c −1.8% vs −1.4%; Weight −6.5 vs −3.0 kg
- Semaglutide statistically superior on both HbA1c and weight at both doses

### STEP 1 (Wegovy, obesity — no T2D)
- **Design:** Semaglutide 2.4 mg QW + lifestyle vs placebo + lifestyle, 68 weeks
- **Mean weight loss:** **−14.9%** (sema) vs −2.4% (placebo); Δ12.4% (p<0.001)
- **≥5% weight loss:** 86.4% vs 31.5%
- **≥10% weight loss:** 69.1% vs 12.0%
- **≥15% weight loss:** 50.5% vs 4.9%
- **≥20% weight loss:** 32.0% vs 1.7%

### STEP 4 (Wegovy, sustained weight loss)
- Patients achieving ≥5% weight loss at 20 weeks randomized to continue sema or switch to placebo
- **Continue sema:** additional −7.9% weight loss by week 68
- **Switch to placebo:** regained 6.9% — confirms need for continuous treatment

### SELECT Trial (Wegovy, CV outcomes in obesity without T2D — 2023)
- **Design:** Semaglutide 2.4 mg vs placebo, ~17,600 patients, established CVD + BMI ≥27, **no diabetes**
- **MACE:** HR **0.80** (20% relative risk reduction, p<0.001) — landmark result for obesity without diabetes
- **Absolute risk reduction:** 2.0% over ~3.3 years
- First obesity drug with prospective CV outcomes benefit without requiring diabetes diagnosis

### FLOW Trial (Wegovy, renal outcomes — 2024)
- **Design:** Semaglutide 1 mg vs placebo in T2D + CKD (eGFR 50–75)
- **Primary composite:** 24% relative risk reduction in kidney disease progression, ESKD, or renal/CV death (HR 0.76, p<0.001)
- Trial stopped early for efficacy; first GLP-1 with dedicated CKD outcomes data

## Safety Profile

### Common Adverse Events
| AE | Ozempic | Wegovy |
|---|---|---|
| Nausea | 15–20% | 44% |
| Diarrhea | 8–12% | 30% |
| Vomiting | 5–9% | 24% |
| Constipation | 5–7% | 24% |
| Abdominal pain | 5–7% | 20% |
| Injection site reactions | 1–2% | <5% |

- Most GI events are dose-dependent and transient; peak at dose escalation phase (first 16 weeks)
- GI events are the primary reason for dose reduction or discontinuation (~5–10%)

### Serious Adverse Events
| AE | Notes |
|---|---|
| Pancreatitis | Rare (<0.3%); suspend if suspected; not clearly causal |
| Gallbladder disease | Cholelithiasis 1.5% vs 0.4% placebo (rapid weight loss mechanism) |
| Acute kidney injury | Usually GI-related dehydration; monitor renal function |
| Diabetic retinopathy (Ozempic) | Transient worsening in patients with pre-existing retinopathy; rapid HbA1c reduction |
| Tachycardia | Mean +1 bpm; no clinical significance established |
| Suicidal ideation | Under active regulatory monitoring post-market (GLP-1 class wide) |

### Black Box Warning
- **Thyroid C-cell tumors:** Semaglutide causes thyroid C-cell tumors in rodents at clinically relevant exposures. Relevance to humans unknown. Contraindicated in patients with personal/family history of MTC or MEN2.

### Key Contraindications
- Personal/family history of medullary thyroid carcinoma (MTC)
- Multiple Endocrine Neoplasia syndrome type 2 (MEN2)
- Prior serious hypersensitivity to semaglutide

## Key HCP Messages

1. **Proven cardiovascular protection — even without diabetes:** The SELECT trial demonstrated a 20% reduction in MACE with semaglutide 2.4 mg in patients with overweight/obesity and established CVD but without T2D — establishing cardiovascular risk reduction as an obesity treatment benefit independent of diabetes.

2. **Landmark weight loss with 1 in 3 patients losing ≥20%:** In STEP 1, semaglutide 2.4 mg achieved a mean 14.9% weight loss with 32% of patients losing ≥20% of body weight — outcomes approaching bariatric surgery in select patients.

3. **Kidney protection in T2D and CKD:** The FLOW trial demonstrated a 24% reduction in the composite of kidney disease progression, ESKD, and renal/CV death in T2D patients with CKD — adding semaglutide to the armamentarium for cardiorenal protection.

4. **Manageable GI profile with stepwise titration:** GI adverse events are dose-dependent and transient; the 16-week titration schedule (0.25 → 0.5 → 1 → 2 mg for Ozempic) minimizes discontinuations and allows personalized dose optimization.

5. **QW dosing supports adherence:** Once-weekly injection reduces treatment burden vs. daily GLP-1 options; real-world adherence data demonstrates superiority over daily injectables in T2D management.

## Patient Profile

### Ideal T2D Patient (Ozempic)
- HbA1c not at goal on metformin ± additional oral agents
- Established CVD or high CV risk (for CV label)
- Overweight/obese (BMI ≥27) — weight loss benefit meaningful
- CKD coexistent (eGFR >15; FLOW data for ≥50)
- Motivated for weekly injection; no needle phobia

### Ideal Obesity Patient (Wegovy)
- BMI ≥30 or ≥27 with comorbidity (HTN, dyslipidemia, T2D, OSA)
- Failed diet and exercise alone
- Established CVD (for CV label — SELECT)
- No MTC/MEN2 history

### Relative Exclusions
- T1D (not approved; insulin secretion dependent)
- History of MTC or MEN2
- Active pancreatitis or history of recurrent pancreatitis
- Proliferative or severe non-proliferative diabetic retinopathy (monitor closely with rapid HbA1c drops)

## Market Position

### Sales Performance
- **Ozempic 2023 global revenue:** ~$13.9 billion USD
- **Wegovy 2023 global revenue:** ~$4.5 billion USD
- **Combined Novo semaglutide franchise:** ~$18+ billion USD
- **Growth rate:** 150%+ YoY for Wegovy; ~50% for Ozempic
- Novo Nordisk became Europe's most valuable company in 2023 driven by semaglutide demand

### Supply Constraints
- Both Ozempic and Wegovy have faced persistent supply shortages since 2022–2024
- Novo Nordisk investing $6+ billion in manufacturing expansion (Denmark, US)
- FDA maintained Ozempic and Wegovy on drug shortage list through much of 2023–2024

### Payer Access
- Ozempic: Broadly covered on commercial and government plans for T2D (Tier 2–3)
- Wegovy: Historically poor commercial coverage for obesity; improving post-SELECT data; Medicare Part D exclusion for weight loss being actively lobbied
- GoodRx / compounded semaglutide issue: Compounding pharmacies producing semaglutide during shortage (FDA enforcement ongoing)
