**Brand:** JARDIANCE (empagliflozin) | **Company:** Boehringer Ingelheim + Eli Lilly | **Document Version:** 2024
**Therapeutic Area:** T2D, Heart Failure (HFrEF/HFpEF), Chronic Kidney Disease | **Mechanism:** SGLT2 Inhibitor

## 1. Mechanism of Action

### SGLT2 Biology
Sodium-glucose cotransporter 2 (SGLT2) is a high-capacity, low-affinity glucose transporter located in the proximal convoluted tubule of the kidney (primarily S1/S2 segments). SGLT2 is responsible for reabsorbing approximately **90% of filtered glucose** from the glomerular filtrate back into the bloodstream.

**Normal physiology:** Under euglycemic conditions, SGLT2 reabsorbs ~160–180g of glucose/day, ensuring minimal glucosuria. In T2D, SGLT2 expression is upregulated, increasing glucose reabsorption capacity and worsening hyperglycemia.

### Empagliflozin Mechanism — Direct Effects
Empagliflozin is a **highly selective, competitive inhibitor of SGLT2** (IC50 ~3.1nM; >2,500-fold selectivity over SGLT1). By blocking SGLT2, empagliflozin produces:

1. **Glucosuria:** Approximately 60–90g of glucose excreted per day in urine; primary antihyperglycemic mechanism. HbA1c reduction approximately 0.5–0.8% from baseline
2. **Osmotic diuresis:** Glucosuria drives water loss; ~400–450 mL/day increased urine volume; plasma volume contraction by ~7–10%
3. **Natriuresis:** Sodium excretion increases with osmotic diuresis; further reduces preload
4. **Reduction in preload and afterload:** Volume contraction → lower filling pressures → beneficial in heart failure; improved LV wall stress
5. **Weight loss:** ~2–3 kg over 6 months (combination of glucosuria, osmotic fluid loss)
6. **Blood pressure reduction:** ~3–4 mmHg systolic; mechanism: volume contraction + natriuresis

### Cardiovascular and Renal Benefit Mechanisms (Beyond Glycemia)
The magnitude of cardiovascular and renal benefits of empagliflozin **far exceeds what would be expected from glycemic control alone.** Benefits were seen in patients with baseline HbA1c across the full range, and in non-diabetic patients in EMPEROR trials. Proposed mechanisms:

#### Hemodynamic Mechanisms
- **Preload reduction:** Osmotic diuresis → reduced circulating blood volume → lower LV filling pressure (LVEDP) → reduced wall stress and MVO2
- **Afterload reduction:** Natriuresis → arterial blood pressure reduction
- **Neurohormonal modulation:** Reduction in renin-angiotensin-aldosterone system (RAAS) and sympathetic nervous system activation

#### Metabolic Mechanisms
- **Ketogenesis shift:** Mild ketonemia (BHB increase 0.3–0.5 mM) → shift in cardiac substrate from glucose to ketone bodies → more oxygen-efficient fuel; increased cardiac efficiency
- **Reduction in visceral adiposity:** Weight loss; adipokine profile improvement; inflammation reduction
- **Uric acid excretion:** Modest hyperuricemia; xanthine oxidase competition effect

#### Renal Mechanisms
- **Tubuloglomerular feedback (TGF) restoration:** SGLT2 inhibition → increased sodium delivery to macula densa → tubuloglomerular feedback → afferent arteriole vasoconstriction → reduction in intraglomerular pressure → long-term nephroprotection
- **Reduction in glomerular hyperfiltration:** Particularly relevant in diabetic nephropathy; reduces proteinuria
- **Oxygen availability in outer medulla:** Reduced sodium-glucose cotransport → reduced ATP demand in proximal tubule → relative hypoxia protection
- **Anti-inflammatory/anti-fibrotic effects:** NLRP3 inflammasome suppression; reduced TGF-β signaling in animal models

### PK Parameters of Empagliflozin
| Parameter | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| Oral bioavailability | ~86% |
| Time to peak (Tmax) | 1.5 hours |
| Half-life (t1/2) | ~12.4 hours |
| Protein binding | ~86% (albumin) |
| Metabolism | UGT1A3, UGT2B7, UGT1A8, UGT1A9 (glucuronidation); not CYP-dependent |
| Elimination | Fecal (~41%), renal (~54%) |
| Food effect | No clinically relevant food effect; can be taken with or without food |
| Dose | 10mg QD (all indications); 25mg QD (additional glycemic control in T2D only) |

## 2. Key Clinical Trials

### EMPA-REG OUTCOME (T2D + Established CVD, 2015)
**Study Design:** Phase 3, randomized, double-blind, placebo-controlled; empagliflozin 10mg or 25mg vs placebo; T2D with established CVD; on background standard of care; N=7,020; median follow-up 3.1 years

**Primary Endpoint — 3-Point MACE (CV death, non-fatal MI, non-fatal stroke)**
| Endpoint | Empagliflozin | Placebo | HR (95% CI) | p-value |
|----------|--------------|---------|-------------|---------|
| **3P-MACE (primary)** | 10.5% | 12.1% | **0.86 (0.74–0.99)** | **p=0.04 (superiority)** |

**Cardiovascular and Mortality Secondary Endpoints**
| Endpoint | Empagliflozin | Placebo | HR (95% CI) | RRR | p-value |
|----------|--------------|---------|-------------|-----|---------|
| **CV death** | 3.7% | 5.9% | **0.62 (0.49–0.77)** | **38%** | **p<0.001** |
| **Hospitalization for HF** | 2.7% | 4.1% | **0.65 (0.50–0.85)** | **35%** | **p=0.002** |
| **All-cause mortality** | 5.7% | 8.3% | **0.68 (0.57–0.82)** | **32%** | **p<0.001** |
| Non-fatal MI | 4.6% | 5.4% | 0.87 (0.70–1.09) | — | NS |
| Non-fatal stroke | 3.4% | 3.0% | 1.18 (0.89–1.56) | — | NS |
| CV death + HF hospitalization | 6.3% | 9.6% | 0.66 (0.55–0.79) | 34% | p<0.001 |

**Renal Composite (Secondary)**
| Endpoint | Empagliflozin | Placebo | HR (95% CI) | p-value |
|----------|--------------|---------|-------------|---------|
| Renal composite (incident macroalbuminuria, doubling SCr, initiation of RRT, renal death) | 12.7% | 18.8% | **0.61 (0.53–0.70)** | **p<0.001** |
| Incident macroalbuminuria | 11.2% | 16.2% | 0.62 | p<0.001 |
| Doubling of serum creatinine | 1.5% | 2.6% | 0.56 | p<0.001 |
| Initiation of renal replacement therapy | 0.3% | 0.6% | 0.45 | p<0.001 |

**Empagliflozin 10mg vs 25mg:** Both doses demonstrated consistent CV and mortality benefits; no significant dose-response on primary endpoint. 10mg is the recommended starting dose for all approved indications.

### EMPEROR-Reduced (HFrEF, 2020)
**Study Design:** Phase 3, randomized, double-blind, placebo-controlled; empagliflozin 10mg vs placebo; HFrEF (LVEF ≤40%), NYHA II–IV; ~50% T2D, ~50% non-diabetic; N=3,730; median follow-up 16 months

| Endpoint | Empagliflozin | Placebo | HR (95% CI) | RRR | p-value |
|----------|--------------|---------|-------------|-----|---------|
| **Primary: CV death + HF hospitalization** | 19.4% | 24.7% | **0.75 (0.65–0.87)** | **25%** | **p<0.001** |
| First HF hospitalization | 13.2% | 18.3% | 0.69 (0.59–0.81) | 31% | p<0.001 |
| Total HF hospitalizations (first + recurrent) | — | — | 0.70 (0.58–0.85) | 30% | p<0.001 |
| CV death | 10.0% | 11.6% | 0.92 (0.75–1.12) | — | NS |
| eGFR decline rate (slope) | -0.55 mL/min/1.73m²/year | -2.28 mL/min/1.73m²/year | — | — | p<0.001 |

**Diabetic vs Non-Diabetic Subgroup (EMPEROR-Reduced):**
| Subgroup | HR Primary Endpoint (95% CI) |
|---------|------------------------------|
| T2D patients (~50%) | 0.72 (0.60–0.87) |
| Non-T2D patients (~50%) | 0.78 (0.64–0.97) |
| Interaction p-value | p=0.50 (no significant interaction) |

*Key message: Benefits are independent of diabetes status*

### EMPEROR-Preserved (HFpEF, 2021)
**Study Design:** Phase 3, randomized, double-blind, placebo-controlled; empagliflozin 10mg vs placebo; HFpEF (LVEF >40%), NYHA II–IV; N=5,988; median follow-up 26.2 months

#### LANDMARK FINDING — First Drug to Show Benefit in HFpEF
| Endpoint | Empagliflozin | Placebo | HR (95% CI) | RRR | p-value |
|----------|--------------|---------|-------------|-----|---------|
| **Primary: CV death + HF hospitalization** | 13.8% | 17.1% | **0.79 (0.69–0.90)** | **21%** | **p<0.001** |
| First HF hospitalization | 8.6% | 11.8% | 0.71 (0.60–0.83) | 29% | p<0.001 |
| Total HF hospitalizations | — | — | 0.73 (0.61–0.88) | 27% | p<0.001 |
| CV death | 7.3% | 8.2% | 0.91 (0.76–1.09) | — | NS |
| eGFR decline rate | Slower with empagliflozin | — | — | — | p<0.001 |
| KCCQ-TSS at 52 weeks | +1.32 vs placebo | — | Diff 1.32 (0.45–2.19) | — | p=0.003 |

**Subgroup Analysis by Ejection Fraction:**
| EF Category | Empagliflozin HR | Interpretation |
|-------------|-----------------|----------------|
| LVEF 41–50% (HFmrEF) | 0.71 (0.55–0.91) | Strong benefit |
| LVEF >50% (HFpEF strict) | 0.83 (0.70–0.98) | Significant benefit |
| LVEF ≥60% | 0.87 (0.72–1.06) | Trending; benefit in preserved EF |

**Historical Context:** Prior to EMPEROR-Preserved, multiple drugs had FAILED in HFpEF (nebivolol, candesartan, irbesartan, spironolactone did not meet primary endpoints in major trials). Empagliflozin was the **first drug with a positive pivotal trial in HFpEF** — a major breakthrough in cardiovascular medicine.

### EMPA-KIDNEY (CKD, 2023)
**Study Design:** Phase 3, randomized, double-blind, placebo-controlled; empagliflozin 10mg vs placebo; CKD (eGFR 20–<45 with ANY cause of CKD, OR eGFR 45–<90 with urine albumin-to-creatinine ratio ≥200 mg/g); ~46% T2D, ~54% non-diabetic; N=6,609; stopped early due to overwhelming efficacy

#### Primary Composite: Progression of Kidney Disease or CV Death
| Endpoint | Empagliflozin | Placebo | HR (95% CI) | RRR | p-value |
|----------|--------------|---------|-------------|-----|---------|
| **Primary composite** | 13.1% | 16.9% | **0.72 (0.64–0.82)** | **28%** | **p<0.001** |
| Kidney disease progression (end-stage, sustained ≥40% eGFR decline, renal death) | 11.6% | 15.7% | 0.71 (0.62–0.81) | 29% | p<0.001 |
| CV death | 2.1% | 2.2% | 0.97 (0.72–1.30) | — | NS |
| HF hospitalization | 4.0% | 5.5% | 0.71 (0.56–0.89) | 29% | p=0.003 |
| eGFR annual decline | -2.93 mL/min | -4.26 mL/min | — | 31% slower | p<0.001 |

**CKD Category Breakdown (EMPA-KIDNEY):**
| CKD Subgroup | N | HR Primary | p-value |
|-------------|---|-----------|---------|
| eGFR 20–<30 (CKD G4) | ~1,200 | 0.67 | Significant |
| eGFR 30–<45 (CKD G3b) | ~1,600 | 0.73 | Significant |
| eGFR 45–<90 with UACR ≥200 | ~3,800 | 0.74 | Significant |
| Diabetic nephropathy | ~46% of N | Consistent | — |
| Non-diabetic CKD | ~54% of N | **Consistent benefit** | — |

**Key Differentiation vs DAPA-CKD (dapagliflozin):**
| Feature | EMPA-KIDNEY | DAPA-CKD |
|---------|------------|---------|
| Minimum eGFR enrolled | **20 mL/min/1.73m²** | 25 mL/min/1.73m² |
| CKD cause | All causes (diabetic + non-diabetic) | All causes (diabetic + non-diabetic) |

| CV death contribution | Not significant | Significant (HR 0.71) |

### EMPACT-MI (Acute MI, 2024 — Emerging Data)
**Study Design:** Empagliflozin vs placebo in patients hospitalized for acute MI; primary endpoint: CV death + HF hospitalization; N=6,522

**Results (2024 AHA presentation):**
| Endpoint | Empagliflozin | Placebo | HR | p-value |
|----------|--------------|---------|-----|---------|
| Primary: HF hosp + CV death | 8.2% | 9.1% | 0.90 (0.76–1.06) | NS |

**Note:** EMPACT-MI primary endpoint NOT met. Empagliflozin did not significantly reduce HF hospitalization + CV death in post-MI patients overall. However, consistent trends seen in specific subgroups (patients with prior HF, lower LVEF). Pfizer/BI continue to analyze subgroup data.

## 4. EMPEROR Pooled Analysis

A pre-specified pooled analysis of EMPEROR-Reduced and EMPEROR-Preserved (N=9,718) confirmed:
| Endpoint | Pooled HR | Confidence Interval | p-value |
|----------|----------|---------------------|---------|
| CV death + HF hospitalization | **0.77** | 0.70–0.85 | p<0.001 |
| HF hospitalization (total) | 0.70 | 0.63–0.78 | p<0.001 |
| CV death | 0.91 | 0.80–1.03 | NS (trend) |
| eGFR slope improvement | Significant | — | p<0.001 |
| Consistent in T2D and non-T2D | Yes | Interaction p=NS | — |
| Consistent across EF range | Yes | — | — |

**Conclusion:** Empagliflozin reduces the primary composite of CV death and HF hospitalization across the full spectrum of heart failure (EF <40% to >70%), diabetic and non-diabetic patients — a unique and defining dataset.

## 5. Biomarker and Mechanistic Insights

### Predictive Biomarkers for Empagliflozin Benefit
| Biomarker | Evidence | Clinical Implication |
|-----------|---------|---------------------|
| NT-proBNP | EMPEROR: Higher BNP → larger absolute benefit | Use BNP to identify high-risk HF patients for JARDIANCE |
| eGFR | EMPA-KIDNEY: Works across eGFR 20–90 | No lower eGFR cutoff below which benefit is lost (except ESRD) |
| Albuminuria (UACR) | EMPA-REG, EMPA-KIDNEY: Higher UACR → more renal benefit | Screen CKD patients with UACR before initiating |
| LVEF | EMPEROR-Preserved: Benefit across full range | No EF threshold; treat all EF patients with HF |
| HbA1c | NOT predictive of CV/HF benefit | Glucose control independent of CV/HF efficacy |
| Hematocrit | EMPA-REG: Hematocrit increase (~3%) correlates with CV benefit | Hemoconcentration as surrogate of plasma volume contraction |
