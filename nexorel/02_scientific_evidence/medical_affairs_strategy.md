### Priority 2: NEXUS-RWE Registry — Real-World Evidence Generation

**Study Design:** Prospective, multicenter, observational registry
**Enrollment Target:** 2,000 patients across 85 US sites
**Primary Endpoint:** Real-world overall survival at 24 months
**Secondary Endpoints:** Time-to-treatment discontinuation, real-world CNS outcomes, healthcare resource utilization

**Enrollment Status (Q1 2026):**
- Sites activated: 62 of 85
- Patients enrolled: 487 of 2,000
- Projected enrollment completion: Q4 2026
- Projected primary publication: Q1 2027 (target: Journal of Clinical Oncology)

**Strategic Rationale:** Real-world evidence is increasingly used by payers in formulary decisions. NEXUS-RWE data will be used in payer negotiations beginning H2 2027.

**Site Distribution:** 40% academic, 60% community — critical for payer relevance (payers scrutinize whether academic trial results translate to community setting).

### Priority 4: Pipeline Medical Support — NEXUS-ADJ & NEXUS-COMBO

**NEXUS-ADJ (Adjuvant Study):**
- Phase III trial of Nexorel in resected Stage IB–IIIA NSCLC with EGFR mutation
- Comparator: Osimertinib (ADAURA regimen)
- Primary endpoint: Disease-free survival
- Enrollment: 1,200 patients; 40 global sites
- Status: Enrollment 60% complete; projected completion Q2 2027
- Medical Affairs role: Site selection, investigator meetings, protocol development support

**NEXUS-COMBO (Combination Study):**
- Phase I/II trial of Nexorel + savolitinib (MET inhibitor) for EGFR+/MET-amplified NSCLC
- Rational: MET amplification is a primary resistance mechanism (~20% of Nexorel patients at progression)
- Status: IND filed January 2026; Phase I first patient dosed projected Q3 2026
- Medical Affairs role: Scientific rationale development, biomarker strategy

### Priority 5: Medical Education — "Think Brain First" Initiative

**Objective:** Establish brain MRI at diagnosis as the standard of care for all newly diagnosed metastatic NSCLC patients, regardless of symptomatology.

**Rationale:** 35% of EGFR+ NSCLC patients present with CNS metastases; many are asymptomatic. Current practice: brain MRI ordered in <55% of newly diagnosed metastatic NSCLC patients at community centers. Closing this gap directly expands Nexorel's differentiated patient population.

**Programs:**
1. **"Think Brain First" CME Module** — accredited online CME (1.0 AMA PRA Category 1 Credit); target 5,000 completions by year-end; launched February 2026
2. **Thoracic Oncology Fellowship Program** — educational grants to 12 fellowship programs for EGFR/CNS curriculum modules; direct influence on next-generation prescribers
3. **Radiology Partnership** — educational initiative with radiologists at academic centers to standardize brain MRI protocol reporting in NSCLC staging
