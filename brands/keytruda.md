### Mechanism of Action

Pembrolizumab is a highly selective, humanized IgG4-kappa monoclonal antibody that binds to the PD-1 receptor on T lymphocytes with high affinity, blocking interaction with its ligands PD-L1 and PD-L2. PD-1 engagement by tumor-expressed PD-L1 suppresses cytotoxic T-cell activation — a key immune evasion mechanism exploited by many cancers. By blocking this checkpoint, pembrolizumab restores T-cell effector function, enabling tumor-infiltrating lymphocytes to mount an antitumor immune response. The IgG4 backbone minimizes Fc-mediated effector function (ADCC/CDC), reducing off-target immune activation.

## 2. Clinical Efficacy

### KEYNOTE-024 — 1L Metastatic NSCLC, PD-L1 TPS ≥50% (Monotherapy)

- **Design:** Phase 3, randomized, open-label; N=305
- **Population:** Treatment-naive metastatic NSCLC, TPS ≥50%, EGFR/ALK wild-type
- **Arms:** Pembrolizumab 200 mg Q3W vs. platinum doublet chemotherapy
- **Primary endpoint:** PFS
- **Key Results:**
  - Median PFS: 10.3 months (pembro) vs. 6.0 months (chemo); HR 0.50, p<0.001
  - Median OS: 26.3 months vs. 13.4 months; HR 0.62
  - **5-year OS: 31.9% (pembro) vs. 16.3% (chemo)** — roughly doubled long-term survival
  - ORR: 44.8% vs. 27.8%
- **Clinical significance:** Established pembrolizumab monotherapy as the 1L standard of care for high PD-L1 expressors

### KEYNOTE-189 — 1L Metastatic Non-Squamous NSCLC + Chemotherapy

- **Design:** Phase 3, double-blind, placebo-controlled; N=616
- **Population:** Treatment-naive metastatic non-squamous NSCLC, any PD-L1
- **Arms:** Pembro + pemetrexed + platinum vs. placebo + pemetrexed + platinum
- **Key Results:**
  - Median OS: 22.0 months (pembro combo) vs. 10.7 months (chemo alone); HR 0.60, p<0.001
  - **5-year OS rate: 19.4% vs. 11.3%**
  - PFS HR: 0.48 (p<0.001)
  - ORR: 47.6% vs. 18.9%
  - Risk of death reduced by 40% vs. chemo alone
- **PD-L1 subgroups:** Benefit observed across all PD-L1 subgroups including TPS <1%

### KEYNOTE-407 — 1L Metastatic Squamous NSCLC + Chemotherapy

- **Design:** Phase 3, double-blind; N=559
- **Arms:** Pembro + carboplatin + paclitaxel/nab-paclitaxel vs. chemo alone
- **Key Results:**
  - Median OS: 17.1 months vs. 11.6 months; HR 0.71, p=0.0006
  - 5-year OS: 18.4% vs. 9.7%
  - ORR: 57.9% vs. 38.4%

### KEYNOTE-522 — Early-Stage Triple-Negative Breast Cancer (Perioperative)

- **Design:** Phase 3, double-blind; N=1,174
- **Population:** Previously untreated, locally advanced/high-risk early TNBC
- **Arms:** Pembro + chemo (neoadjuvant) then pembro adjuvant vs. chemo + placebo
- **Key Results:**
  - pCR rate: **64.8% (pembro) vs. 51.2% (placebo)**; difference +13.6 percentage points; p<0.001
  - Event-free survival: HR 0.65 (significant benefit at 5 years)
  - EFS at 5 years: ~81.3% vs. ~72.3%
- **Significance:** First immunotherapy approval in early TNBC; perioperative IO standard of care

### KEYNOTE-042 — 1L Locally Advanced/Metastatic NSCLC, TPS ≥1% (Monotherapy)

- **Design:** Phase 3; N=1,274; TPS ≥1%, no EGFR/ALK
- **Key Results:**
  - Median OS (TPS ≥50%): 20.0 vs. 12.2 months; HR 0.69
  - Median OS (TPS ≥1%): 16.7 vs. 12.1 months; HR 0.81
  - Benefit most pronounced in TPS ≥50% group

### KEYNOTE-158 — Tumor-Agnostic TMB-H Solid Tumors

- **Design:** Phase 2, basket trial; multiple tumor types
- **ORR in TMB-H (≥10 mut/Mb) patients: 29%** vs. 6% in TMB-low
- **Basis for FDA approval:** First biomarker-based tumor-agnostic anti-PD-1 indication (TMB-H)

### KEYNOTE-966 — Biliary Tract Cancer, 1L

- **Design:** Phase 3; N=1,069
- **Arms:** Pembro + gemcitabine/cisplatin vs. chemo alone
- **Key Results:**
  - Median OS: 12.7 vs. 10.9 months; HR 0.83; p=0.0034
  - OS benefit consistent across subgroups

## 3. Safety Profile

### Common Adverse Events (All Grades, ≥20% incidence across trials)

| Adverse Event | Incidence (All Grades) |
|---|---|
| Fatigue | 20–30% |
| Nausea | 10–25% |
| Musculoskeletal pain | 14–27% |
| Pruritus | 10–20% |
| Rash | 12–20% |
| Diarrhea | 12–20% |
| Decreased appetite | 10–16% |
| Dyspnea (combo regimens) | 15–25% |

### Immune-Mediated Adverse Events (irAEs) — Grade 3–4 Rates

| irAE Category | Grade 3–4 Incidence |
|---|---|
| Pneumonitis | 1–3% |
| Colitis/Diarrhea | 1–2% |
| Hepatitis (ALT/AST elevation) | 2–4% |
| Endocrinopathies (thyroid, adrenal, pituitary) | <1% G3/4 (but common G1/2) |
| Nephritis | <1% |
| Skin reactions (SJS/TEN rare) | <1% |
| Myocarditis | <1% (but potentially fatal) |
| Type 1 Diabetes Mellitus (new onset) | <1% |

- **Discontinuation due to AEs:** ~10–15% across monotherapy trials
- **Treatment-related deaths:** ~0.5–1% (pneumonitis, myocarditis most common causes)

### Black Box Warning

**WARNING: IMMUNE-MEDIATED ADVERSE REACTIONS AND SOLID ORGAN TRANSPLANT REJECTION**
Pembrolizumab can cause severe and fatal immune-mediated adverse reactions including:
- Pneumonitis, colitis, hepatitis, endocrinopathies (adrenal insufficiency, hypothyroidism, hyperthyroidism, hypophysitis, thyroiditis)
- Nephritis with renal dysfunction
- Dermatologic reactions (severe cutaneous reactions)
- Myocarditis, encephalitis, and other serious immune reactions
- Solid organ transplant rejection

### Key Monitoring Requirements

1. **Thyroid function** (TSH, free T4) at baseline and periodically
2. **Liver function tests** (AST, ALT, bilirubin) at baseline and with each cycle
3. **Serum creatinine** — monitor renal function
4. **Blood glucose** — monitor for immune-mediated diabetes
5. **Adrenal function** — ACTH stimulation if adrenal insufficiency suspected
6. **Chest imaging** — if respiratory symptoms develop (rule out pneumonitis)
7. **Cardiac monitoring** — troponin/BNP if myocarditis suspected

### Management of irAEs

- Grade 1: Continue therapy with monitoring
- Grade 2: Hold therapy; initiate corticosteroids (prednisone 1–2 mg/kg/day)
- Grade 3–4: Permanently discontinue; high-dose corticosteroids (methylprednisolone 1–2 mg/kg IV), consider immunosuppressants (infliximab, mycophenolate) if steroid-refractory

### Special Populations / Contraindications

- **Pregnancy:** Fetal harm demonstrated in animal studies; avoid use
- **Allogeneic HSCT:** Increased risk of GVHD and transplant-related mortality
- **Solid organ transplant recipients:** Risk of rejection; use with extreme caution
- **Autoimmune diseases:** May exacerbate underlying conditions

1. **Breadth:** 40+ FDA-approved indications across 20+ tumor types — broadest IO label in oncology
2. **Landmark long-term data:** 5-year OS data across NSCLC, melanoma demonstrating durable benefit
3. **Tumor-agnostic approvals:** dMMR/MSI-H (2017) and TMB-H (2020) — biomarker-driven approach
4. **KEYTRUDA QLEX:** Subcutaneous formulation (Sep 2025) — reduced infusion burden, ~5-minute admin vs. 30 minutes IV
5. **Established manufacturing scale:** Global supply reliability
6. **PD-L1 CDx ecosystem:** Well-validated 22C3 assay widely deployed

### Emerging Competitive Threats

- **Bispecific antibodies:** Ivonescimab (AK112, PD-1/VEGF); Tarlatamab (DLL3/CD3 for SCLC)
- **ADCs with IO combinations:** Enfortumab vedotin + pembrolizumab vs. chemo in bladder
- **Next-gen CIs:** Anti-TIGIT, anti-TIM-3 combinations in development
- **Pembrolizumab biosimilars:** Multiple programs targeting 2028+ US entry (Samsung, Celltrion, Amgen pipelines)

### Market Share Context

- KEYTRUDA commands approximately **60% of global anti-PD-1/PD-L1 market** by revenue
- Lung cancer segment: largest indication by revenue (~27% of total KEYTRUDA sales)
- In 1L NSCLC (TPS ≥50%), KEYTRUDA monotherapy commands ~70% of new patient starts

## 6. Patient Profile

### Ideal Patient Characteristics

**Highest confidence indication: 1L Metastatic NSCLC Monotherapy**
- Non-squamous or squamous NSCLC
- **PD-L1 TPS ≥50%** confirmed by FDA-authorized 22C3 assay
- EGFR, ALK, ROS1, BRAF mutation-negative (no actionable driver mutations)
- ECOG performance status 0–1
- No active autoimmune disease requiring systemic immunosuppression
- No prior systemic anti-cancer therapy for metastatic disease
- Adequate hepatic, renal, and pulmonary function

**For Combo (KEYTRUDA + Chemo) — Any PD-L1:**
- 1L non-squamous NSCLC: any PD-L1, EGFR/ALK WT
- 1L squamous NSCLC: any PD-L1
- ECOG PS 0–2

**For Early TNBC (KEYNOTE-522):**
- High-risk early-stage TNBC: Stage II–III (cT2 or higher, or N+)
- No prior therapy
- Neoadjuvant chemo eligible
- PD-L1 status is not required for treatment selection in this setting

### Biomarker Requirements by Indication

| Indication | Required Biomarker | Assay |
|---|---|---|
| 1L NSCLC monotherapy | PD-L1 TPS ≥50% | 22C3 pharmDx |
| 2L+ NSCLC | PD-L1 TPS ≥1% | 22C3 pharmDx |
| HNSCC (some settings) | CPS ≥1 | 22C3 pharmDx |
| Gastric/GEJ, Esophageal | CPS ≥10 or ≥1 | 22C3 pharmDx |
| dMMR solid tumors | MSI-H/dMMR | PCR or IHC panel |
| TMB-H tumors | ≥10 mut/Mb | FoundationOne CDx or similar |
| TNBC neoadjuvant | None required | — |
| Cervical cancer | CPS ≥1 | 22C3 pharmDx |

### Lines of Therapy

- **1L:** Multiple tumor types as monotherapy or combination
- **2L+:** NSCLC, melanoma, various other tumors post-progression
- **Adjuvant:** NSCLC post-resection, melanoma stage IIB/C/III, early TNBC, RCC post-nephrectomy

### Patient Profiles to Avoid / Use Caution

- Active systemic autoimmune disease (risk of severe irAEs)
- Active inflammatory bowel disease
- Prior immune-related colitis on IO therapy
- Organ transplant recipients (risk of rejection)
- Interstitial lung disease at baseline (risk of fatal pneumonitis)
- EGFR/ALK+ NSCLC: limited IO benefit; prefer targeted therapy

### Payer Access

- **US:** Broadly covered on commercial and Medicare Part B (infusion) or Part D (once SC QLEX processed)
- **Step therapy:** Rarely required for oncology; IO indications typically allow direct access with biomarker documentation
- **Medicare negotiation:** Not currently selected for IRA Medicare drug price negotiation (biologic, injection route)
- **Copay assistance:** Merck Access Program provides support for eligible commercially insured patients
- **International:** Reimbursed in EU5, Japan, and most high-income markets; access limited in some middle-income countries due to price

### Patent & Exclusivity Position

| Protection | Date |
|---|---|
| Core molecule US compound patent | Expected expiry 2028 |
| Method of treatment patents | Extend to ~2036–2038 |
| KEYTRUDA QLEX (SC formulation/device) | Patent coverage to ~2041 |

- **Biosimilar threat:** Multiple biosimilar developers (Samsung Bioepis, Celltrion, Amgen, Henlius) targeting IV pembrolizumab post-2028
- **Merck's counterstrategy:** QLEX SC formulation approved 2025 — requires separate biosimilar development; creates distinct product category with new patent life through ~2041
- **Revenue at risk post-2028 (IV only):** Estimated $29.5B — one of the largest single-drug patent cliffs in pharma history

### Notable Market Pressures

1. **Patent cliff 2028:** Core IV patent expiry; biosimilar entry anticipated in US
2. **QLEX adoption curve:** Success of SC transition critical to revenue defense
3. **Competing checkpoints:** Anti-TIGIT, anti-LAG-3, and bispecific combos may erode share in specific niches
4. **IRA impact:** Long-term pricing pressure on high-revenue biologic products
5. **Combination competition:** Enfortumab vedotin + pembro now approved in bladder cancer — captures the combination market share Merck already holds
6. **Global pricing pressure:** EU HTA bodies (G-BA, HAS) increasingly demanding comparative effectiveness vs. IO combinations
