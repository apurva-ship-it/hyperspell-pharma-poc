## 1. Competitive Landscape Overview

The GLP-1 RA and broader cardiometabolic market is undergoing the fastest transformation in pharmaceutical history. The GLP-1 class has grown from ~$15B globally (2021) to an estimated $55B (2025), with projections exceeding $130B by 2030. Ozempic and Wegovy collectively remain the market leader, but Eli Lilly's tirzepatide (dual GIP/GLP-1) has mounted the most significant competitive threat in the brand's lifecycle.

## 2. Competitor Profiles

### 2.1 Tirzepatide (Mounjaro / Zepbound) — PRIMARY THREAT

**Company:** Eli Lilly
**Mechanism:** Dual GIP (glucose-dependent insulinotropic peptide) + GLP-1 receptor agonist
**Dosing:** 2.5–15mg QW; same escalation schema as semaglutide class

**Approved Indications:**
- Mounjaro: T2D glycemic control (May 2022 FDA)
- Zepbound: Chronic weight management (November 2023 FDA)
- Mounjaro CV outcomes: SURPASS-CVOT results pending; MACE trial ongoing

**Key Clinical Data:**

| Trial | vs. Comparator | HbA1c | Weight Loss | Key Result |
|-------|---------------|-------|-------------|------------|
| SURPASS-2 | Semaglutide 1mg | -2.3% vs -1.86% | -11.4 vs -7.1 kg | Tirzepatide superior |
| SURMOUNT-1 | Placebo | — | -20.9% vs -3.1% | 63.3% lost ≥15% |
| SURMOUNT-2 | Placebo (T2D+obesity) | — | -15.7% vs -3.3% | Superior to placebo |
| SURMOUNT-4 | Tirzepatide (maintenance) | — | -25.3% total | Sustained benefit |

**Lilly Competitive Strengths:**
- Demonstrably superior weight loss (~20.9% vs ~14.9% for Wegovy at highest doses)
- Superior HbA1c reduction in direct comparison (SURPASS-2)
- Growing formulary coverage; aggressive payer contracting
- Zepbound vials at $399/month (unbranded) → access strategy undercutting Wegovy
- Pipeline: orforglipron (oral GLP-1, Phase 3), retatrutide (GLP-1/GIP/glucagon triple agonist), mazdutide

**Lilly Vulnerabilities:**
- No published CV outcomes data (SURPASS-CVOT ongoing; expected 2026)
- Less mature post-approval safety record (approved 2022 vs Ozempic 2017)
- No renal outcomes data (Sema FLOW gives Ozempic exclusive differentiation 2024–2027)
- SELECT data: Ozempic/Wegovy has proven CV benefit in obesity — Lilly does not yet
- Limited formulary coverage in several managed Medicaid plans

**Battle Card — Ozempic vs. Mounjaro:**

| Dimension | Ozempic 1mg | Mounjaro 15mg | Ozempic Advantage |
|-----------|------------|--------------|------------------|
| CV outcomes | HR 0.74 (SUSTAIN-6) | Not proven | YES — 7 years' data |
| Renal outcomes | HR 0.76 (FLOW) | Not available | YES — only GLP-1 with CKD data |
| Weight loss | -6.5 kg | -11.4 kg | NO |
| HbA1c | -1.8% | -2.3% | NO |
| Safety record | 7+ years | 3 years | YES |
| GI tolerability | Nausea 20% | Nausea 19% | Neutral |
| Formulary status | Broadly preferred | Growing | Slight Ozempic advantage |

### 2.2 Dulaglutide (Trulicity) — Secondary Competitor (Declining)

**Company:** Eli Lilly
**Mechanism:** GLP-1 RA (native GLP-1 analog, weekly autoinjector)
**2024 Revenue:** ~$3.1B (declining ~25% YoY as tirzepatide cannibalizes Lilly's own portfolio)
**CV Data:** REWIND trial — MACE HR 0.88 (p=0.026); broader population than SUSTAIN-6

**Key Competitive Context:**
- Trulicity has been the default GLP-1 RA for PCPs due to ease of autoinjector
- Losing share rapidly to both Mounjaro and Ozempic
- Likely to face generic entry ~2027–2028
- Not a material strategic threat; monitor for formulary preference displacement

### 2.3 Liraglutide (Victoza/Saxenda) — Legacy Competitor

**Company:** Novo Nordisk (same company — internal cannibalization context)
**Victoza:** T2D GLP-1 RA (QD injection, 1.2–1.8mg) — losing share to Ozempic intentionally
**Saxenda:** Obesity (QD injection, 3mg) — being replaced by Wegovy

**Competitive Context:**
- Novo Nordisk strategy is deliberate transition from liraglutide to semaglutide franchise
- Victoza LOE 2023; generic liraglutide entering; no patent defense planned
- Saxenda declining ~40% as Wegovy access improves
- LEADER trial CV data (HR 0.87) less compelling than SUSTAIN-6 (HR 0.74)

### 2.4 Oral GLP-1 RA Pipeline — EMERGING THREAT

**Orforglipron (Eli Lilly):**
- Small molecule, non-peptide GLP-1 RA; oral QD — no food restriction
- Phase 3 obesity: -14.7% weight loss at 36 weeks (vs ~0.9% Rybelsus-comparable)
- T2D Phase 3: HbA1c -1.9% at 26 weeks; weight -7.9 kg
- Key advantage: No refrigeration, oral, no GI peak (distinct PK from Rybelsus)
- Expected FDA filing 2025; approval 2026
- THREAT LEVEL: HIGH — could shift GLP-1 category to oral-first

**Rybelsus (oral semaglutide — Novo Nordisk own):**
- 14mg: HbA1c -1.5%; weight -4.4 kg; food restriction required
- SOUL trial: MACE HR 0.86 (p=0.003) — first oral GLP-1 with CV outcomes data
- Does not cannibalize Ozempic significantly; serves injection-averse patients

**Danuglipron (Pfizer):** Phase 2 discontinued; Pfizer discontinuing oral GLP-1 program

| Asset | Company | Stage | Mechanism | Threat Level |
|-------|---------|-------|-----------|-------------|
| Orforglipron | Eli Lilly | Phase 3/NDA | Oral GLP-1 RA | HIGH |
| Retatrutide | Eli Lilly | Phase 3 | GLP-1/GIP/Glucagon triple | CRITICAL |
| Amycretin | Novo Nordisk | Phase 2 | GLP-1/amylin | Internal |
| Cagrilintide combo | Novo Nordisk | Phase 3 | GLP-1 + amylin | Internal |
| Pemvidutide | Altimmune | Phase 2 | GLP-1/glucagon | Moderate |
| Mazdutide | Innovent/Roche | Phase 3 (China) | GLP-1/GCG | Moderate (ex-US) |
| Biosimilar semaglutide | Biocon, Sandoz | Pre-clinical/Ph1 | Generic | HIGH (2028+) |
| Compounded semaglutide | Various | Active (gray market) | Unregulated | Active threat |

**Retatrutide Threat Assessment:**
Phase 2 data: -17.5% weight loss at 24 weeks (triple agonist). Phase 3 TRIUMPH program ongoing. If CV outcomes data materializes (~2027–2028), this could represent a class-threatening event for semaglutide franchise. Novo Nordisk counter: amycretin Ph2 data showing ~20%+ weight loss; CagriSema (sema + cagrilintide) showing ~22–25%.

## 6. Key Messages for Competitive Situations

**When HCP mentions tirzepatide's superior weight loss:**
"Tirzepatide shows impressive weight data. For your patient with T2D and established CVD, Ozempic has 7 years of CV outcome data and proven 26% MACE reduction. Tirzepatide's SURPASS-CVOT is still ongoing. When you're making a decision for a high-CV-risk patient, that proven data matters."

**When HCP mentions Trulicity's convenience:**
"Trulicity's autoinjector is very accessible. Ozempic's FlexTouch pen is also highly rated in usability studies. And when you compare the outcomes data — HbA1c -1.8% vs -1.4%, weight -6.5 vs -3.0 kg — Ozempic consistently performs better across both glycemic and weight endpoints."

**When HCP raises price/access concerns:**
"Access has been a real challenge. Today, Ozempic has preferred formulary status at ESI, Caremark, and OptumRx, covering more than 180 million commercial lives. And for uninsured patients, our NovoCare program provides Ozempic for $99/month."
