## 1. Mechanism of Action (MOA)

### 1.1 Primary Pharmacodynamic Actions

Semaglutide is a glucagon-like peptide-1 receptor agonist (GLP-1 RA) with 94% amino acid sequence homology to endogenous human GLP-1. It is modified at two positions (Ala→Aib at position 8; Lys→Arg at position 34) and carries a C18 fatty diacid chain on Lys26 enabling albumin binding, which extends half-life to approximately 168 hours (7 days), supporting once-weekly dosing.

**Multi-organ effects:**

| Organ/System | Mechanism | Clinical Outcome |
|-------------|-----------|-----------------|
| Pancreatic β-cells | Glucose-dependent insulin secretion ↑ | HbA1c reduction |
| Pancreatic α-cells | Glucose-dependent glucagon suppression ↓ | Fasting glucose control |
| Hypothalamus/CNS | Appetite suppression via GLP-1R on arcuate nucleus | Weight loss |
| Stomach | Delayed gastric emptying | Post-prandial glucose blunting |
| Liver | Reduced hepatic glucose output | Fasting glucose reduction |
| Cardiovascular | Anti-inflammatory, anti-atherosclerotic effects (endothelial GLP-1R) | MACE reduction |
| Kidney | Reduced glomerular hyperfiltration, anti-inflammatory | CKD progression slowing |
| Gut | Satiety signaling via vagal afferents | Caloric intake reduction |

### 1.2 Pharmacokinetic Profile

| Parameter | Value |
|-----------|-------|
| Half-life | ~168 hours (7 days) |
| Time to Cmax | 1–3 days post-dose |
| Bioavailability (SQ) | ~89% |
| Volume of distribution | ~12.5 L |
| Protein binding | >99% (albumin) |
| Metabolism | Proteolytic cleavage; fatty acid oxidation |
| Renal excretion | ~3% unchanged |
| Dose adjustment for renal impairment | Not required (including ESRD) |
| Dose adjustment for hepatic impairment | Not required |

### 1.3 Differentiation from Liraglutide (Victoza)
- Longer half-life (168h vs 13h) enabling QW vs QD dosing
- Higher receptor binding affinity → superior HbA1c and weight reduction at approved doses
- Unique albumin-binding structure reduces renal clearance

## 2. Pivotal Clinical Trial Program

### 2.1 SUSTAIN-6 — Cardiovascular Outcomes Trial (T2D + CVD)

**Design:** Randomized, double-blind, placebo-controlled; N=3,297
**Population:** T2D adults with established CVD or high CV risk; mean age 65; mean HbA1c 8.7%; mean diabetes duration 14 years
**Treatment:** Semaglutide 0.5mg or 1.0mg QW vs. placebo; median follow-up 2.1 years
**Primary Endpoint:** 3-point MACE (CV death, non-fatal MI, non-fatal stroke)

| Endpoint | Semaglutide | Placebo | HR (95% CI) | p-value |
|----------|-------------|---------|-------------|---------|
| 3-point MACE | 6.6% | 8.9% | 0.74 (0.58–0.95) | 0.016 (non-inferiority + superiority) |
| CV death | 2.7% | 3.8% | 0.73 (0.50–1.06) | 0.10 |
| Non-fatal MI | 2.9% | 3.9% | 0.74 (0.51–1.08) | 0.12 |
| Non-fatal stroke | 1.6% | 2.7% | 0.61 (0.38–0.99) | 0.04 |
| Nephropathy (new/worsening) | 3.8% | 6.1% | 0.64 (0.46–0.88) | 0.005 |
| HbA1c change | -1.0% | -0.2% | — | <0.001 |
| Body weight change | -3.6 kg | -0.7 kg | — | <0.001 |

**Key Publication:** Marso SP et al. NEJM 2016;375:1834–1844.
**Clinical Implication:** First GLP-1 RA to demonstrate superiority (not just non-inferiority) for MACE in T2D CVD patients.

### 2.2 SUSTAIN-7 — Head-to-Head vs. Dulaglutide (Trulicity)

**Design:** Randomized, open-label, active-controlled; N=1,201
**Population:** T2D on metformin; mean HbA1c 8.2%; mean BMI 34
**Treatment:** Semaglutide 0.5mg or 1mg vs. dulaglutide 0.75mg or 1.5mg QW; 40 weeks

| Endpoint | Sema 0.5mg | Dula 0.75mg | Sema 1mg | Dula 1.5mg |
|----------|-----------|------------|---------|-----------|
| HbA1c reduction | -1.5% | -1.1% | -1.8% | -1.4% |
| Weight loss | -4.6 kg | -2.3 kg | -6.5 kg | -3.0 kg |
| HbA1c <7% | 67% | 52% | 79% | 64% |
| Nausea | 22% | 17% | 23% | 18% |

**Key Publication:** Pratley R et al. Lancet Diabetes Endocrinol. 2018;6:275–286.
**Clinical Implication:** Semaglutide 1mg superior to highest approved dulaglutide dose on both glycemic and weight endpoints.

### 2.3 STEP 1 — Wegovy Weight Management Trial (Obesity, No T2D)

**Design:** Randomized, double-blind, placebo-controlled; N=1,961
**Population:** BMI ≥30 or ≥27 with ≥1 weight-related comorbidity; no T2D
**Treatment:** Semaglutide 2.4mg QW vs. placebo + lifestyle intervention; 68 weeks
**Primary Endpoint:** % change in body weight; ≥5% weight loss responders

| Endpoint | Semaglutide 2.4mg | Placebo | Difference | p-value |
|----------|------------------|---------|------------|---------|
| Mean weight change | -14.9% | -2.4% | -12.4 pp | <0.001 |
| ≥5% weight loss | 86.4% | 31.5% | — | <0.001 |
| ≥10% weight loss | 69.1% | 12.0% | — | <0.001 |
| ≥15% weight loss | 50.5% | 4.9% | — | <0.001 |
| ≥20% weight loss | 32.0% | 1.7% | — | <0.001 |
| Waist circumference change | -13.5 cm | -4.1 cm | — | <0.001 |
| Systolic BP change | -6.2 mmHg | -1.4 mmHg | — | <0.001 |

**Key Publication:** Wilding JPH et al. NEJM 2021;384:989–1002.
**Clinical Implication:** ~1 in 3 patients lost ≥20% body weight — approaching bariatric surgery outcomes.

### 2.4 SELECT — Cardiovascular Outcomes in Obesity (No T2D)

**Design:** Randomized, double-blind, placebo-controlled; N=17,604 — largest CV trial in obesity
**Population:** Age ≥45, BMI ≥27, established CVD, NO diabetes at baseline
**Treatment:** Semaglutide 2.4mg QW vs. placebo; median follow-up 39.8 months
**Primary Endpoint:** Time to first MACE (CV death, non-fatal MI, non-fatal stroke)

| Endpoint | Semaglutide | Placebo | HR (95% CI) | RRR |
|----------|-------------|---------|-------------|-----|
| 3-point MACE | 6.5% | 8.0% | 0.80 (0.72–0.90) | 20% |
| CV death | 2.5% | 3.0% | 0.85 (0.71–1.01) | — |
| Non-fatal MI | 3.6% | 4.8% | 0.72 (0.61–0.84) | 28% |
| Non-fatal stroke | 1.8% | 2.0% | 0.93 (0.74–1.15) | — |
| Heart failure hospitalization | 1.8% | 2.3% | 0.77 (0.62–0.96) | 23% |
| All-cause mortality | 4.0% | 4.8% | 0.81 (0.71–0.93) | 19% |
| Weight change | -9.4% | -0.9% | — | — |

**Key Publication:** Lincoff AM et al. NEJM 2023;389:2221–2232.
**Clinical Implication:** Established CV benefit of semaglutide independent of glycemic effects — driven by anti-inflammatory/anti-atherosclerotic mechanisms. Supports Wegovy's CV indication approval (August 2024 FDA).

### 2.5 FLOW — Renal Outcomes Trial in CKD + T2D

**Design:** Randomized, double-blind, placebo-controlled; N=3,533; trial stopped early for efficacy
**Population:** T2D + CKD (eGFR 25–75 mL/min/1.73m²; UACR ≥100 mg/g); ~97% on RAS inhibitor
**Treatment:** Semaglutide 1mg QW vs. placebo; median follow-up 3.4 years
**Primary Endpoint:** Composite of kidney failure, ≥50% sustained eGFR decline, CV death, kidney disease death

| Endpoint | Semaglutide | Placebo | HR (95% CI) | p-value |
|----------|-------------|---------|-------------|---------|
| Primary composite | 24.5% | 29.6% | 0.76 (0.66–0.88) | <0.001 |
| Kidney failure | 11.1% | 13.9% | 0.79 (0.64–0.98) | 0.03 |
| ≥50% eGFR decline | 10.5% | 12.9% | 0.79 (0.64–0.97) | 0.02 |
| CV death/kidney death | 11.6% | 14.4% | 0.79 (0.64–0.98) | 0.03 |
| eGFR slope (mL/min/year) | -0.75 | -2.19 | — | <0.001 |
| UACR change | -32% | -3% | — | <0.001 |
| MACE | HR 0.84 | — | (0.69–1.02) | — |

**Key Publication:** Perkovic V et al. NEJM 2024;391:1892–1904.
**Clinical Implication:** Semaglutide is the first GLP-1 RA with dedicated renal outcomes data; CKD label update filed; positions semaglutide alongside SGLT2i in nephrology.

| Diarrhea | 13% | 30% | 6% |
| Vomiting | 9% | 24% | 4% |
| Constipation | 11% | 24% | 7% |
| Abdominal pain | 6% | 10% | 4% |
| Headache | 9% | 14% | 10% |
| Fatigue | 8% | 11% | 7% |
| Dyspepsia | 9% | 9% | 4% |
| Dizziness | 5% | 8% | 3% |
| Acute pancreatitis (clinical trials) | 0.1% | 0.2% | 0.2% |
| Gallbladder disease | 1.9% | 2.6% | 1.4% |
| Injection site reactions | 0.5% | 0.9% | 0.4% |

### 5.2 Cardiovascular Safety (SUSTAIN-6, SELECT)
- No increased risk of heart failure, hypotension, arrhythmia
- Modest heart rate increase: +3–4 bpm (class effect)
- Consistent benefit across subgroups (age, gender, eGFR, prior CVD)

### 5.3 Long-Term Safety
- Diabetic retinopathy complications: increased risk observed in SUSTAIN-6 (HR 1.76) — mechanistically related to rapid HbA1c reduction; clinical significance debated
- Thyroid: C-cell tumors in rodents at supra-clinical exposures; no human signal in 7+ years post-approval pharmacovigilance
