## 1. MECHANISM OF ACTION — DEEP DIVE

### 1.1 EGFR Biology and Mutant NSCLC Dependency
The epidermal growth factor receptor (EGFR) is a receptor tyrosine kinase (RTK) in the ErbB family. In ~15% of Western NSCLC patients and ~35–50% of Asian NSCLC patients, activating mutations drive constitutive EGFR signaling — creating oncogene addiction that can be therapeutically exploited.

**Key activating mutations targeted by Tagrisso**:
- **Exon 19 deletions (Ex19del)**: ~45% of EGFR-mutant NSCLC; most commonly del746-750; highly sensitive to EGFR TKIs
- **L858R point mutation (exon 21)**: ~40% of EGFR-mutant NSCLC; leucine-to-arginine substitution; slightly lower sensitivity vs Ex19del historically
- **T790M resistance mutation (exon 20)**: Acquired resistance mutation following 1st/2nd gen TKI; threonine-to-methionine; ~50–60% of post-1st/2nd gen progression cases; Tagrisso designed to overcome

### 1.2 Osimertinib Structural and Mechanistic Advantages
Osimertinib (AZD9291) is a **third-generation, irreversible EGFR TKI** with a distinctive covalent binding mechanism:

| Property | Detail |
|---|---|
| Binding mechanism | Irreversible covalent binding to Cys797 of EGFR kinase domain |
| Selectivity | Engineered to have ~200× selectivity for mutant EGFR over wild-type (WT) EGFR |
| T790M coverage | Directly overcomes T790M resistance via steric accommodation in binding pocket |
| WT-EGFR sparing | Reduced inhibition of WT EGFR = less rash, less diarrhea vs gefitinib/erlotinib/afatinib |
| CNS penetration | High CSF/plasma ratio ~2.5% (P-gp/BCRP substrate; designed for CNS penetration) |
| Metabolites | AZ5104 (desmethyl), AZ7550 (active metabolites); contribute to total pharmacological activity |
| Half-life (t½) | ~48 hours; supports once-daily oral dosing |

### 1.3 Downstream Signaling Pathways Blocked
- **RAS/RAF/MEK/ERK pathway**: Cell proliferation blockade
- **PI3K/AKT/mTOR pathway**: Survival signaling inhibition
- **JAK/STAT pathway**: Anti-apoptotic signaling reduction
- **Net effect**: G1 cell cycle arrest, apoptosis induction, anti-angiogenic effects in EGFR-dependent tumor cells

### 1.4 CNS Penetration — Mechanistic Basis
Standard EGFR TKIs (gefitinib, erlotinib, afatinib) are substrates for P-glycoprotein (P-gp) and BCRP efflux transporters at the blood-brain barrier, limiting CNS exposure. Osimertinib's molecular structure results in:
- Lower P-gp efflux: Enables meaningful CNS penetration
- CSF/plasma ratio ~2.5% vs <1% for earlier EGFR TKIs
- Clinically validated: CNS disease control rate 90%+ in T790M+ patients (AURA3); CNS PFS not reached in FLAURA 1L

## 2. CLINICAL TRIAL EVIDENCE — FIRST-LINE METASTATIC NSCLC

### 2.1 FLAURA (Phase 3 — 1L vs Standard-of-Care EGFR TKI)
**Design**: Randomized, double-blind; N=556
**Population**: Previously untreated metastatic NSCLC with EGFR Ex19del or L858R
**Comparison**: Osimertinib 80mg QD vs standard-of-care (gefitinib 250mg QD or erlotinib 150mg QD)

| Endpoint | Osimertinib | SOC TKI | HR | p-value |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **Progression-Free Survival (primary)** | **18.9 months** | **10.2 months** | **0.46** | **<0.001** |
| Overall Survival (key secondary) | **38.6 months** | **31.8 months** | **0.80** | **0.046** |
| CNS PFS | **Not reached** | **11.7 months** | **0.48** | **<0.001** |
| Objective Response Rate | 80% | 76% | — | NS |
| Duration of Response | 17.2 months | 8.5 months | — | — |
| CNS ORR (CNS met patients) | 91% | 68% | — | — |

**FLAURA PFS Subgroups**:
| Subgroup | Osimertinib PFS | SOC PFS | HR |
|---|---|---|---|
| Ex19del | 21.4 months | 11.2 months | 0.42 |
| L858R | 14.9 months | 9.6 months | 0.51 |
| CNS metastases present | 15.2 months | 9.6 months | 0.47 |
| Asian patients | 19.1 months | 9.6 months | 0.43 |

*Published: Soria JC et al. NEJM 2018; 378:113–125 (PFS); Ramalingam SS et al. NEJM 2020; 382:41–50 (OS)*

### 2.2 FLAURA2 (Phase 3 — 1L Osimertinib + Chemotherapy)
**Design**: Randomized, open-label; N=557
**Comparison**: Osimertinib + platinum-pemetrexed × 4 cycles → osimertinib maintenance vs osimertinib monotherapy

| Endpoint | Osimertinib + Chemo | Osimertinib Mono | HR | p-value |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **PFS (primary)** | **25.5 months** | **16.7 months** | **0.62** | **<0.001** |
| CNS PFS | 24.9 months | 13.8 months | 0.58 | — |
| ORR | 83% | 76% | — | — |
| OS | Immature | Immature | — | Pending |

**Safety Consideration — FLAURA2**:
| AE | Combo arm | Mono arm |
|---|---|---|
| Grade ≥3 AEs | 64% | 27% |
| Neutropenia Grade 3/4 | 26% | 0% |
| Anemia Grade 3/4 | 10% | <1% |
| Nausea (any grade) | 58% | 20% |
| Dose reductions | 23% | 5% |

*Published: Planchard D et al. NEJM 2023; 389:1935–1948*

## 3. CLINICAL TRIAL EVIDENCE — ADJUVANT NSCLC

### 3.1 ADAURA (Phase 3 — Adjuvant Post-Resection)
**Design**: Randomized, double-blind, placebo-controlled; N=682
**Population**: Completely resected NSCLC Stage IB–IIIA with EGFR Ex19del or L858R; 3 years of treatment
**PRIMARY ENDPOINT CHANGE**: Unblinded early due to overwhelming DFS benefit

| Endpoint | Osimertinib | Placebo | HR | p-value |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| **DFS, Stage II–IIIA (primary)** | **65.8% at 5yr** | **26.2% at 5yr** | **0.23** | **<0.0001** |
| DFS, overall (Stage IB–IIIA) | 60.4% at 5yr | 28.0% at 5yr | 0.27 | <0.0001 |
| **Overall Survival (secondary)** | **88% at 5yr** | **78% at 5yr** | **0.49** | **0.0039** |
| CNS DFS | **98%** at 5yr | **85%** at 5yr | — | <0.0001 |
| Time to first subsequent treatment | Not reached | 15.7 months | — | <0.0001 |

**ADAURA DFS by Stage**:
| Stage | Osimertinib 5yr DFS | Placebo 5yr DFS | HR |
|---|---|---|---|
| Stage IB | 75.0% | 45.0% | 0.39 |
| Stage II | 67.8% | 29.3% | 0.23 |
| Stage IIIA | 52.0% | 13.0% | 0.23 |

**ADAURA by Mutation Type**:
| Mutation | Osimertinib 5yr DFS | Placebo 5yr DFS | HR |
|---|---|---|---|
| Ex19del | 68.2% | 27.4% | 0.22 |
| L858R | 61.8% | 25.3% | 0.26 |

*Published: Wu YL et al. NEJM 2020 (interim); Herbst RS et al. NEJM Evid 2023 (5yr OS)*

### 7.2 Liquid Biopsy Testing — ctDNA
- **Sensitivity for Ex19del/L858R (ctDNA)**: 65–80% in stage IV (lower in earlier stages)
- **Advantage**: Non-invasive; faster turnaround; captures tumor heterogeneity
- **Limitation**: Lower sensitivity in early-stage disease, lower tumor burden, bone-only metastases
- **Guideline recommendation**: NCCN: tissue NGS preferred; liquid biopsy acceptable when tissue insufficient
- **Clinical workflow**: Test tissue first → if inconclusive or insufficient, perform liquid biopsy (ctDNA)
