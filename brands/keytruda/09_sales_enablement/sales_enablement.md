## 1. Sales Call Guide

### Pre-Call Planning
Before every call, complete the following:
- [ ] Review HCP's last 3 prescribing activity reports (CRM): TRx trend, KEYTRUDA vs. competitor, new patient starts
- [ ] Check recent biomarker testing orders (PD-L1 testing data from partner labs if available)
- [ ] Review open PA denials or patient support cases for this HCP's patients
- [ ] Check for any new publications, guideline updates, or competitive approvals in last 30 days
- [ ] Review HCP's congress attendance or recent CME participation
- [ ] Set a clear call objective: Is this a disease awareness call, data presentation call, or patient support resolution call?

---

### Call Structure: KEYTRUDA Consultative Sales Model (4-Stage)

#### Stage 1: Opening (2–3 minutes)
**Goal**: Establish rapport, gain permission to discuss, confirm time available

**Script Framework**:
> "Dr. [Name], I know you have 3 minutes. I wanted to share something I think is directly relevant to your [tumor type] patients — specifically the updated [KEYNOTE-XXX / QLEX SC / MSI-H testing] data. Would you have a moment?"

**Key Principles**:
- Lead with patient relevance, not product pitch
- Confirm available time upfront; respect if they say "2 minutes" — plan accordingly
- Reference prior conversation or shared interest to establish continuity

---

#### Stage 2: Probing Questions (3–5 minutes)
**Goal**: Understand the HCP's current patient context, challenges, and priorities

**High-Impact Probing Questions**:
1. "When you're treating a newly diagnosed stage IV NSCLC patient, what's your first question when you get the biomarker report back?"
2. "Are you finding that PD-L1 testing turnaround is impacting your treatment initiation timing?"
3. "How are you managing irAEs when they occur — do you have a protocol in place with your nursing team?"
4. "What's your experience been with getting KEYTRUDA approved for your MSI-H/dMMR patients outside of colorectal cancer?"
5. "Have you had patients express concerns about the frequency of infusion visits? How are you handling that?"
6. "Are there specific tumor types where you feel less confident about KEYTRUDA's role?"

**Listen for**:
- Biomarker testing barriers → segue to 22C3 testing support resources
- irAE concerns → segue to irAE management algorithm and MSL referral
- Infusion burden → segue to QLEX SC messaging
- Competitor mention → segue to differentiation data (stay factual and clinical)
- Access/PA issues → segue to KEYTRUDA Access team resources

---

#### Stage 3: Presenting (4–6 minutes)
**Goal**: Present 1–2 tailored messages that directly respond to identified needs

**Principle**: Never present more than 2 core messages in a single call. Tailor based on probing findings.

**Presentation Framework (SOAPBOX)**:
- **S** – State the patient scenario: "For your patient with PD-L1 TPS ≥50%, treatment-naive stage IV NSCLC..."
- **O** – Offer the data: "KEYNOTE-024 showed that KEYTRUDA delivered a 5-year OS of 31.9% vs. 16.3%..."
- **A** – Answer the implied question: "What does that mean in practice? Nearly 1 in 3 patients alive 5 years out."
- **P** – Patient relevance: "Think about your patient who wants to be around for their grandchild's graduation..."
- **B** – Benefit bridge: "KEYTRUDA gives you the best-supported foundation to have that conversation."
- **O** – Object surface: "Is there anything that would make you hesitant about starting this patient on KEYTRUDA?"
- **X** – X-tra resource: "I can leave the KEYNOTE-024 5-year reprint and connect your team with our access specialist."

---

#### Stage 4: Closing (1–2 minutes)
**Goal**: Gain commitment or next step; do NOT simply end the call without a defined action

**Closing Types**:
| Close Type | When to Use | Language |
|-----------|-------------|---------|
| **Trial Close** | HCP showing interest but not committed | "Would you feel comfortable starting your next PD-L1 ≥50% NSCLC patient on KEYTRUDA?" |
| **Next Step Close** | Need to continue conversation | "Can I set up a 20-minute lunch session with your tumor board team to walk through the combination data?" |
| **Resource Close** | HCP needs reference material | "Let me leave the QLEX SC dosing guide with your infusion coordinator — what's the best way to get that to them?" |
| **MSL Close** | Scientific/data request beyond rep scope | "Our MSL Dr. [Name] can go deep on the irAE prediction data — can I have her reach out to schedule?" |
| **Access Close** | PA/coverage issue identified | "Let me connect your office manager with our KEYTRUDA Access specialist today. Can I introduce them over phone?" |

**Never**: End a call without a defined next step logged in CRM; "Thanks for your time" is not a close.

## 2. Territory Planning Framework

### Monthly Territory Management Priorities

**Week 1: Top-Tier (Tier 1–2) Focus**
- In-person calls with Tier 1 academic KOLs and Tier 2 high-volume community oncologists
- Target: 8–12 face-to-face calls; minimum 3 Tier 1 contacts

**Week 2: Biomarker Expansion**
- Pathologist calls at key testing sites (hospital and independent labs)
- Target: 2–3 pathologist or lab director calls per month
- Goal: Ensure 22C3 testing protocols in place; address turnaround time issues

**Week 3: Mid-Tier Expansion**
- Tier 3 community oncologist calls; satellite office coverage
- APP (Nurse Practitioner / PA) calls — increasing prescribing authority for continuation therapy

- Pharmacy director visits at hospital/clinic accounts

## 3. Top 5 Objection Handling Scripts (Sales Context)

### Objection A: "I'm comfortable with what I'm using — KEYTRUDA is not my first choice."
**Trigger**: HCP using Opdivo, cemiplimab, or other ICI as default
**Response Script**:
> "I respect your clinical experience, and I'd love to understand what's driving your preference. What I can share is that in PD-L1 ≥50% NSCLC specifically, KEYTRUDA is the only agent with 5-year OS data showing 31.9% survival — that's nearly twice the rate seen with chemotherapy. I'm not asking you to change everything — just to consider KEYTRUDA as the default for your biomarker-high patients where the evidence base is deepest. Would you be open to revisiting the data?"

**Follow-up if resistant**: Book MSL consultation; do not push further in same call.

---

### Objection B: "The PA process is too burdensome — my staff doesn't want to deal with it."
**Trigger**: Office manager or HCP frustrated with PA denials or delays
**Response Script**:
> "This is one of the most common challenges I hear, and we take it seriously. Our KEYTRUDA Access team can work directly with your office staff to pre-verify coverage and begin the PA process proactively. They've helped reduce average PA approval time from over a week to under 3 days for many practices. Can I set up a 15-minute call between your office manager and our access specialist this week?"

**Resource**: Leave KEYTRUDA Access team direct line and 1-page PA checklist

---

### Objection C: "I've heard KEYTRUDA is too expensive and a biosimilar is coming."
**Trigger**: Cost concerns; awareness of patent expiry
**Response Script**:
> "Cost is a real consideration, and I want to give you accurate information. For your commercially insured patients, our co-pay support program ensures $0 out-of-pocket — they pay nothing. For uninsured patients, our Patient Assistance Program provides free drug. On biosimilars: the core IV formulation patent expires in 2028, but KEYTRUDA QLEX SC, which we launched last September, has patent protection to approximately 2041. For patients starting on SC today, they'll have consistent access long-term. Biosimilar pricing also won't necessarily translate to lower net cost given rebate structures."

---

### Objection D: "I had a patient who had a bad irAE experience — I'm nervous about recommending it."
**Trigger**: Negative personal clinical experience with irAE
**Response Script**:
> "I'm sorry to hear your patient had a difficult experience — those cases are challenging for everyone. I want to make sure you have all the tools to manage irAEs proactively for future patients. Less than 10% of patients permanently discontinue treatment due to irAEs, and with early recognition and the right management protocol, most patients can safely continue or restart. Our MSL can walk through the specific irAE management algorithm with your team and address any case-specific questions. Would that be a helpful session to arrange?"

**Leave behind**: irAE management algorithm card; nursing education resources

---

### Objection E: "My patient is on Medicare and the 20% coinsurance is a problem."
**Trigger**: Medicare access barrier for financially limited patients
**Response Script**:
> "Medicare patients face real co-insurance burdens with Part B drugs. There are several resources we can connect you with. For income-eligible patients, the Cancer Care Fund and other foundations provide co-insurance assistance. Merck also has a Patient Assistance Program for income-eligible patients regardless of insurance type. Let me connect your patient navigator or billing team with our access specialist who deals with Medicare situations daily. They may also want to explore Part D eligibility under the new QLEX SC formulation in the future."

## 5. Coaching Guide (Manager/District Manager Use)

### Rep Performance Development Framework

**For New Reps (0–6 months)**:
- Focus: Indication mastery (3 core: 1L NSCLC, TNBC, tumor-agnostic MSI-H)
- Coaching: Role-play call openings and probing questions daily; shadow Tier 1 calls
- Red flags: Overwhelming HCP with all 40+ indications; unable to articulate KEYNOTE-024 5-yr data clearly
- Milestone: Score ≥85% on KEYTRUDA Certification Assessment by Month 3

**For Developing Reps (6–18 months)**:
- Focus: Differentiation from competitors; QLEX SC conversion conversations; access program utilization
- Coaching: Field rides focused on handling objections A–E; review CRM close rates
- Red flags: Low QLEX SC discussion rate; not generating MSL co-visit requests; no speaker program sourcing
- Milestone: Achieve 90% goal attainment in at least 2 of 4 quarters

**For Senior Reps (18+ months)**:
- Focus: Territory account management, KOL relationships, tumor board access, peer-to-peer influence
- Coaching: Strategic account planning reviews; pipeline data discussions; competitive territory defense
- Red flags: Plateau in new patient starts; low Tier 1 call frequency; complacency with existing prescribers
- Milestone: Mentor new team member; contribute to regional advisory board planning

### Coaching Questions (for field rides)
1. "Walk me through how you'd open a call with a Tier 1 academic oncologist who hasn't been using KEYTRUDA for TNBC."
2. "If the HCP says 'I'm waiting for a biosimilar,' how do you respond?"
3. "You identify that a patient's PA was denied — what's your exact next action?"
4. "How do you proactively identify which patients in this practice might benefit from QLEX SC?"
5. "What's your close if you only have 2 minutes with this HCP?"
