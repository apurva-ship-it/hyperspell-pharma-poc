# Skyrizi (Risankizumab) — Regulatory & Prescribing Information Reference
**Brand Owner:** AbbVie | **Document Version:** 2024.1 | **Classification:** Internal Regulatory Affairs | **Last PI Review:** April 2024

---

## 1. Approved Indications (US FDA)

### 1.1 Plaque Psoriasis (First Approval — April 2019)

Skyrizi is indicated for the treatment of moderate-to-severe plaque psoriasis in adults who are candidates for systemic therapy or phototherapy.

### 1.2 Psoriatic Arthritis (Approval — July 2022)

Skyrizi is indicated for the treatment of active psoriatic arthritis in adults.

### 1.3 Crohn's Disease (Approval — June 2022)

Skyrizi is indicated for the treatment of moderately to severely active Crohn's disease in adults.

### 1.4 Ulcerative Colitis (Approval — June 2024)

Skyrizi is indicated for the treatment of moderately to severely active ulcerative colitis in adults.

### 1.5 EU Approved Indications (EMA)

- Moderate-to-severe plaque psoriasis in adults who are candidates for systemic therapy (April 2019)
- Active psoriatic arthritis in adults, alone or in combination with methotrexate, when response to previous DMARD therapy has been inadequate (2022)
- Moderately to severely active Crohn's disease in adults who have had an inadequate response with, lost response to, or were intolerant to either conventional therapy or a biologic agent (2022)
- UC: EMA review ongoing at time of document preparation

## 2. Dosing and Administration

### 2.1 Plaque Psoriasis — Dosing

**Dosing regimen:**
- Induction: 150 mg SC at weeks 0 and 4
- Maintenance: 150 mg SC every 12 weeks (Q12W) thereafter
- No weight-based dosing required
- Formulation: 150 mg/mL solution in a single-dose cartridge with on-body injector OR prefilled syringe

**Administration:**
- Subcutaneous injection; patient or caregiver self-injection capable (with training)
- Inject into abdomen, thigh, or upper arm; avoid skin that is tender, bruised, red, hard, or affected by PsO plaques
- Allow to reach room temperature (15–30 minutes before injection)
- Refrigerate at 2–8°C; protect from light; do not freeze

**Dosing calendar note:** After 3 doses (weeks 0, 4, then 16), maintenance is every 12 weeks = 4 injections per year. This is a key adherence and convenience advantage.

### 2.2 Psoriatic Arthritis — Dosing

**Dosing regimen:**
- 150 mg SC Q12W (with or without methotrexate)
- No induction loading doses required for PsA
- Same formulation as PsO; same injection instructions

**Note on combination with cDMARDs:** In KEEPsAKE-1, ~50% of patients were on concomitant cDMARDs (methotrexate). Efficacy was consistent regardless of background cDMARD use. No dose adjustment required.

### 2.3 Crohn's Disease — Dosing

**Induction (IV):**
- Risankizumab 600 mg IV infusion at weeks 0, 4, and 8 (three induction doses)
- Must be administered in a healthcare facility capable of managing infusion reactions
- Infusion over ≥1 hour

**Maintenance (SC):**
- 360 mg SC Q8W (starting 8 weeks after last IV induction dose)
- Patient self-injection capable
- Formulation: 360 mg/2.4 mL prefilled syringe

**Response assessment:** If no clinical response by week 12 (after 2 IV doses), consider discontinuation. Clinical remission typically assessed at week 12; endoscopic response at week 24–52.

### 2.4 Ulcerative Colitis — Dosing

**Induction (IV):**
- Risankizumab 1200 mg IV at weeks 0, 4, and 8 (three induction doses; note: higher IV dose than CD — 1200 mg vs. 600 mg)
- Administered in a healthcare facility with infusion capabilities
- Infusion over ≥1 hour

**Maintenance (SC):**
- 360 mg SC Q8W (same maintenance regimen as CD)
- Starting 8 weeks after last IV induction dose

### 2.5 Dose Modifications

**Renal impairment:**
- No dose adjustment required for any degree of renal impairment (including severe)
- Limited data in dialysis patients; use clinical judgment

**Hepatic impairment:**
- No dose adjustment required for mild to moderate hepatic impairment
- No data in severe hepatic impairment; use with caution

**Elderly (≥65 years):**
- No dose adjustment required; limited data in patients >75 years
- Overall safety profile consistent with general adult population in clinical trials

**Pediatric patients:**
- Not approved for patients <18 years old (as of 2024); pediatric PsO Phase III ongoing

## 3. Contraindications

1. **Hypersensitivity:** Known serious hypersensitivity reaction (e.g., anaphylaxis, serious hypersensitivity) to risankizumab or any of the excipients.

*Note: Skyrizi has no Black Box Warning and no contraindication related to IBD, pregnancy category restrictions (standard biologic language applies), or serious infection beyond standard precautions.*

---

## 4. No Black Box Warning

Skyrizi does **not** have a Black Box Warning. This is a key differentiator:
- TNF inhibitors (adalimumab/Humira, etanercept, infliximab): BBW — serious infections (including TB, fungal), malignancies (lymphoma)
- JAK inhibitors (tofacitinib, upadacitinib, baricitinib): BBW — serious infections, MACE (major adverse cardiovascular events), malignancies, thrombosis
- IL-17 inhibitors: No BBW, but contraindication in IBD patients
- Skyrizi: No BBW; no IBD contraindication — broadest usability profile in class

## 5. Warnings and Precautions

### 5.1 Infections

- Risk of serious infections; exercise caution in patients with a chronic infection or history of recurrent infection
- Do not initiate in patients with active, clinically important infection
- Treat active infections before initiating risankizumab
- If serious infection develops during treatment: discontinue until infection resolves
- **Tuberculosis:** Evaluate patients for latent TB before initiating therapy; do not use in active TB. For latent TB: treat per standard protocols before initiating Skyrizi.
- Fungal infections: Rare cases of serious fungal infections reported; monitor for signs; tinea infections are slightly elevated (see AE table)

### 5.2 Hypersensitivity Reactions

- Anaphylaxis and serious hypersensitivity reactions reported; discontinue immediately if reaction occurs
- Have equipment for management of anaphylaxis available, especially for first few doses
- Monitor patients during and after IV infusion (CD/UC doses)

### 5.3 Vaccinations

- Avoid live or live-attenuated vaccines during treatment
- Non-live vaccines (influenza, pneumococcal, shingles recombinant) may be administered while on therapy
- Update vaccinations prior to initiating treatment
- Interval after stopping risankizumab before live vaccine: not specifically established; allow washout ~5 half-lives (~140 days)

### 5.4 IBD Exacerbation (Informational Note)

- In contrast to IL-17 inhibitors, risankizumab is NOT contraindicated in inflammatory bowel disease; it is in fact indicated for IBD
- However: New-onset IBD in psoriasis patients has not been reported as an adverse event in risankizumab trials
- Monitor for gastrointestinal symptoms; IBD diagnosis in a PsO/PsA patient on risankizumab should prompt consultation rather than discontinuation

## 7. Drug Interactions

### 7.1 Live Vaccines
- Do not administer live vaccines concurrently with risankizumab (contraindication by labeling precaution)

### 7.2 CYP450 Considerations
- Risankizumab does not interact with CYP450 enzymes
- IL-6 pathway inhibitors (not Skyrizi) modulate CYP expression — not applicable here
- No known clinically relevant pharmacokinetic drug interactions with common concomitant medications

### 7.3 Concomitant Immunosuppressants (IBD Patients)
- Combination with immunosuppressants (azathioprine, 6-MP, methotrexate): Used in CD/UC trials; no new safety signals
- Combination with corticosteroids: Standard of care during induction phase; no dose interaction
- Avoid combination with other biologics or JAK inhibitors (not studied; theoretical increased infection risk)

## 8. Special Populations

### 8.1 Pregnancy
- Limited data; no adequate well-controlled studies in pregnant women
- Monoclonal antibodies cross the placental barrier; fetal exposure possible in second/third trimester
- Risk is theoretical; IL-23 pathway role in placental function not established
- Recommendation: Discuss risk/benefit with patients; if needed during pregnancy, use only if potential benefit justifies risk
- No BBW for pregnancy (unlike ACE-I/ARB analogues for comparison)

### 8.2 Lactation
- Unknown if risankizumab is present in human milk; caution advised
- IgG antibodies present in human milk generally in low levels; systemic absorption in infant is limited
- Risk-benefit discussion with patients warranted

### 8.3 Females and Males of Reproductive Potential
- No evidence of reproductive toxicity in animal studies
- Not teratogenic in cynomolgus monkey studies at doses up to 50x the clinical dose

### 8.4 Pediatric Use
- Not approved in patients <18 years old (as of 2024)
- Phase III pediatric PsO trial (12–17 years) ongoing; results expected Q4 2025
- No data in pediatric IBD or PsA

### 8.5 Geriatric Use (≥65 years)
- No dose adjustment; no overall safety/efficacy differences observed vs. younger adults in trials
- Immunosenescence-associated risk considerations standard for any immunomodulator

## 9. Pharmacokinetics Summary

| PK Parameter | Value |
|-------------|-------|
| Route | Subcutaneous (PsO/PsA maintenance; IBD/UC maintenance) or IV (IBD/UC induction) |
| Bioavailability (SC) | ~73% |
| Tmax (SC) | ~3–14 days |
| Volume of distribution | ~11.4 L (central) |
| Clearance | 0.31 L/day |
| Half-life | ~28 days (SC) |
| Steady-state accumulation ratio | ~2-fold (after Q12W SC; Q8W SC slightly higher) |
| PK linearity | Linear over 18–600 mg dose range |
| Metabolism | Proteolytic degradation; not CYP-dependent |
| Renal elimination | <1% of dose excreted intact in urine |
| Immunogenicity | Anti-drug antibodies (ADA) in ~4% of PsO patients; minimal impact on PK/efficacy |
