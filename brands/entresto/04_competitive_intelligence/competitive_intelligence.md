## 1. Competitive Landscape Overview

The heart failure market is characterized by a multi-drug, guideline-mandated treatment paradigm. Unlike oncology or immunology where therapies compete directly for a single indication slot, HF treatment involves four foundational pillars per the 2022 ACC/AHA/HFSA guidelines. Entresto's primary competitive challenge is not displacement by another drug but rather:

1. **Prescriber inertia** — physicians keeping patients on cheaper ACE inhibitors or ARBs
2. **Underutilization** — only 20–25% of eligible HFrEF patients receive ARNI
3. **Payer step therapy** — mandatory ACE-I/ARB trial delaying ARNI initiation
4. **Generic narrative** — low-cost lisinopril "good enough" messaging from payers/formulary committees

## 2. Key Competitor Profiles

### 2.1 ACE Inhibitors (Primary Competitive Threat)

#### Lisinopril (Multiple Generics — Zestril, Prinivil)

| Attribute | Lisinopril | Entresto |
|-----------|-----------|---------|
| Mechanism | ACE inhibition only | Dual: NEP inhibition + AT1 blockade |
| CV death reduction (vs. placebo) | 16% RRR (SOLVD-T) | 20% RRR vs. enalapril (PARADIGM-HF) |
| HF hospitalization reduction | Yes (SOLVD-T) | 21% RRR vs. enalapril |
| Guideline status (ACC/AHA 2022) | Class I (alternative to ARNI) | Class I (preferred over ACE-I/ARB) |
| Annual drug cost | ~$100–$200 | ~$8,400 (WAC) |
| Side effects | Cough 14–20%, rare angioedema | Cough 11.3%, hypotension 14%, angioedema 0.45% |
| Key disadvantage | Inferior to ARNI in head-to-head | Angioedema risk, BP drop, washout required |

**Battle Card vs. Lisinopril:**
- "Lisinopril works, but Entresto works better — 20% reduction in CV death vs. enalapril (PARADIGM-HF HR 0.80, p<0.0001). No trial has compared any ACE inhibitor to Entresto and shown equivalence."
- "Lisinopril causes cough in up to 20% of patients — nearly double Entresto's 11.3% rate."
- "For your HFrEF patient who wants to live longer, guidelines say Entresto is preferred. The question isn't whether to use an ACE-I over Entresto — the guidelines already answered that."

#### Enalapril (Generic — Vasotec)

- Historically the gold-standard HF ACE-I (SOLVD-T: 16% mortality reduction vs. placebo)
- Direct head-to-head comparator in PARADIGM-HF; Entresto demonstrated superiority on all major endpoints
- Prescribers who remember enalapril as "the standard" must be educated that the standard has now shifted

### 2.2 ARBs (Secondary Competitive Threat)

#### Valsartan (Generic — Diovan), Candesartan (Generic — Atacand), Losartan

| Attribute | Valsartan (standalone) | Entresto |
|-----------|----------------------|---------|
| Evidence in HF mortality | Val-HeFT (add-on to ACE-I) | PARADIGM-HF (head-to-head vs. enalapril) |
| Guideline status (2022) | Class I (if ACE-I intolerant) | Class I (preferred) |
| Key differentiation | One-axis RAAS blockade | Dual neurohormonal axis |
| Cost | ~$500–$1,000/year (generic) | ~$8,400/year (branded) |

**Competitive Note:** Valsartan component is present in Entresto — prescribers who choose standalone valsartan are getting only half the mechanism. Battle card: "Valsartan addresses one neurohormonal pathway. Entresto addresses two. When your patient is already on valsartan and still has an LVEF ≤40% and elevated NT-proBNP, the next step is Entresto."

### 2.3 SGLT2 Inhibitors (Complementary — Not Competitive)

#### Dapagliflozin (Farxiga, AstraZeneca) and Empagliflozin (Jardiance, Boehringer Ingelheim / Lilly)

| Attribute | Dapagliflozin (DAPA-HF) | Empagliflozin (EMPEROR-Reduced) | Entresto |
|-----------|------------------------|--------------------------------|---------|
| CV death + worsening HF (vs. placebo) | HR 0.74 (p<0.001) | HR 0.75 (p<0.001) | HR 0.80 vs. enalapril |
| HF hospitalization reduction | 30% RRR | 31% RRR | 21% vs. enalapril |
| Approved for HFpEF | Yes (DELIVER) | Yes (EMPEROR-Preserved) | Broadened label only |
| Mechanism | SGLT2 inhibition | SGLT2 inhibition | ARNI |
| Key advantage | Renal protection, weight loss | Renal protection, DM benefit | Neurohormonal dual block |
| Position in GDMT | Pillar 4 | Pillar 4 | Pillar 1 (ARNI) |

**Strategic positioning:** SGLT2 inhibitors are complementary to Entresto — both should be used in HFrEF patients (ACC/AHA 2022 Class I for both). The evidence for additive benefit comes from post-hoc analyses and mechanistic data. Entresto + SGLT2i is the preferred combination. **Do not allow SGLT2i to displace ARNI therapy — they work through entirely different mechanisms.**

**Key message vs. SGLT2i narrative:** "Entresto addresses neurohormonal remodeling — the pathophysiologic driver of progressive HF. SGLT2 inhibitors address volume and metabolic factors. Together they cover complementary mechanisms. Guidelines recommend both. The choice is not Entresto OR SGLT2i — it is Entresto AND SGLT2i."

### 2.4 Beta-Blockers (Background Pillar — Not Direct Competitor)

- Carvedilol, metoprolol succinate (Toprol-XL), bisoprolol
- All generic; Class I for HFrEF mortality reduction
- Guideline-directed use: Pillar 2 of GDMT alongside ARNI
- Not competitive but important for GDMT messaging — Entresto is prescribed alongside beta-blockers, not instead of them

### 2.5 MRAs (Mineralocorticoid Receptor Antagonists)

- Spironolactone (generic), eplerenone (generic), finerenone (Kerendia — Bayer)
- Spironolactone/eplerenone: Pillar 3 of GDMT (Class I, LVEF ≤35%)
- Finerenone: Non-steroidal MRA; approved for CKD/diabetes with cardiac benefit — not yet in HF guidelines
- Not competitive with Entresto; both recommended together

### 2.6 Ivabradine (Corlanor — Amgen)

- Sinus node If channel inhibitor; reduces heart rate without negative inotropic effect
- Approved: HFrEF (LVEF ≤35%, HR ≥70 bpm, on maximally tolerated beta-blocker, in sinus rhythm)
- Evidence: SHIFT trial (HR 0.82 for CV death or HF hospitalization vs. placebo)
- Not a direct competitor to Entresto; potential add-on therapy
- Small patient population (requires HR ≥70 on max beta-blocker)

### 2.7 Vericiguat (Verquvo — Merck/Bayer)

| Attribute | Vericiguat | Entresto |
|-----------|-----------|---------|
| Mechanism | sGC stimulator | ARNI |
| Target population | Worsening HF (post-hospitalization) | HFrEF/HFmrEF (broad) |
| Evidence | VICTORIA: HR 0.90 (p=0.02) vs. placebo | HR 0.80 vs. enalapril (active comparator) |
| Guideline status | Class IIb (modest evidence) | Class I (highest recommendation) |
| Competitive threat level | Low | — |

### 2.8 Omecamtiv Mecarbil (Cytokinetics — FDA review)

- Selective cardiac myosin activator; GALACTIC-HF trial: HR 0.92 (p=0.025) vs. placebo — modest benefit
- FDA declined approval in initial review cycle; European review ongoing
- If approved, would target the same HFrEF population; adds a fourth neurohormonal approach
- Competitive threat: Moderate if approved with strong data and positive formulary placement

## 5. Competitive Battle Cards

### Battle Card 1: Entresto vs. Lisinopril/ACE-I

**Physician Objection:** "My patient is stable on lisinopril. Why change?"

**Response:**
> "Stability on lisinopril doesn't mean optimized therapy — it means the disease is progressing at its natural rate. PARADIGM-HF showed that switching to Entresto reduced CV death by 20% and HF hospitalizations by 21% vs. enalapril — which is the same class as lisinopril. The 2022 ACC/AHA guidelines now prefer ARNI over ACE-I (Class I, Level B-R). Your patient deserves that extra protection."

**Supporting data:** PARADIGM-HF HR 0.80 (95% CI 0.73–0.87, p<0.0001); NNT=21 over 27 months.

### Battle Card 2: Entresto vs. Payer Step-Therapy Requirement

**Payer/Access Objection:** "We require ACE-I/ARB failure first."

**Response:**
> "Multiple national guidelines — ACC/AHA/HFSA 2022 — state ARNI is preferred over ACE-I as first-line neurohormonal therapy. Requiring ACE-I failure first contradicts published guideline recommendations and may expose patients to preventable hospitalizations. Our total cost of care model shows that ARNI reduces 30-day readmissions by 16% — a savings of $3,200 per avoided readmission, generating net savings even at Entresto's current price."

### Battle Card 3: Entresto vs. "SGLT2i is enough" Narrative

**Physician Objection:** "I already have them on empagliflozin. That covers mortality."

**Response:**
> "Empagliflozin and Entresto work through completely different mechanisms — SGLT2i addresses metabolic and volume pathways; Entresto addresses neurohormonal remodeling. EMPEROR-Reduced showed empagliflozin benefit on TOP OF background RAS therapy — meaning the incremental benefit was in addition to, not instead of, ARNI therapy. Current guidelines recommend BOTH — they're synergistic, not competing."

### Battle Card 4: Angioedema Concern

**Physician Concern:** "I'm worried about angioedema risk."

**Response:**
> "In PARADIGM-HF, Entresto had a 0.45% angioedema rate vs. 0.24% for enalapril — a small absolute difference. Critically, Entresto is contraindicated for patients with prior ACE-I–related angioedema — those are already screened out. For patients without that history, the absolute angioedema risk difference is roughly 1 in 500 patients over 27 months. Compare that to the NNT of 21 for preventing CV death or HF hospitalization — the benefit-risk profile strongly favors Entresto."
