## 1. Mechanism of Action — Deep Dive

### 1.1 Overview: The ARNI Concept

Entresto (sacubitril/valsartan, LCZ696) is the first and only Angiotensin Receptor–Neprilysin Inhibitor (ARNI). It is a sodium supramolecular complex comprising sacubitril (AHU377, a neprilysin inhibitor prodrug) and valsartan (an AT1 receptor blocker) in a 1:1 molar ratio. This single entity delivers simultaneous dual neurohormonal blockade — augmenting the natriuretic peptide system while suppressing the renin-angiotensin-aldosterone system (RAAS).

### 1.2 Sacubitril: Neprilysin Inhibition Pathway

Sacubitril is a prodrug converted by esterases in vivo to the active metabolite LBQ657. LBQ657 competitively and reversibly inhibits neprilysin (neutral endopeptidase 24.11, NEP), a zinc metalloprotease expressed primarily in the kidney, vasculature, brain, and heart.

**Neprilysin substrates degraded (when NEP active):**
- Atrial natriuretic peptide (ANP)
- Brain natriuretic peptide (BNP)
- C-type natriuretic peptide (CNP)
- Adrenomedullin
- Bradykinin
- Substance P
- Angiotensin II (minor pathway)

**Consequences of NEP inhibition:**
- ANP levels rise → increased cGMP → diuresis, natriuresis, vasodilation, reduced aldosterone
- BNP levels rise → antifibrotic, antiproliferative effects on cardiac myocytes; vasodilation
- CNP levels rise → vascular smooth muscle relaxation, antiproliferative effects
- Bradykinin levels rise (modest) → vasodilation; also explains risk of angioedema
- Net hemodynamic effect: reduced preload, afterload, and cardiac filling pressures

**Important note:** NEP inhibition alone increases angiotensin II (since angiotensin II is a minor NEP substrate, but more importantly, NEP inhibition reduces angiotensin 1-7 production). This is why NEP inhibition alone (e.g., candoxatril) increased blood pressure in early trials — valsartan component is essential to block the compensatory AT1-mediated vasoconstriction.

### 1.3 Valsartan: AT1 Receptor Blockade

Valsartan selectively blocks the angiotensin II type 1 (AT1) receptor, preventing angiotensin II–mediated:
- Vasoconstriction
- Aldosterone secretion
- Sympathetic activation
- Cardiac and vascular remodeling
- Sodium and water retention

The valsartan dose in LCZ696 is equivalent to approximately twice the valsartan dose in stand-alone valsartan formulations (97/103 mg sacubitril/valsartan = ~160 mg valsartan equivalent). The enhanced valsartan bioavailability is attributable to the supramolecular complex structure.

### 1.4 Combined Pharmacodynamic Effects in Heart Failure

In HFrEF, the failing heart is characterized by neurohormonal activation — excessive RAAS activity, sympathetic nervous system activation, and impaired natriuretic peptide signaling. Entresto addresses this through:

1. **Augmented natriuretic signaling** (via NEP inhibition): Reduces cardiac fibrosis, cardiac hypertrophy, and adverse remodeling. ANP/BNP restore diuresis/natriuresis.
2. **RAAS blockade** (via AT1 antagonism): Reduces vasoconstriction, attenuates aldosterone excess, prevents angiotensin II–driven cardiac remodeling.
3. **Complementary hemodynamic effects**: Reduced systemic vascular resistance, reduced filling pressures, improved cardiac output without reflex tachycardia.

Biomarker effects in clinical trials:
- NT-proBNP reduction: 47% vs. 25% for enalapril at 8 weeks (PIONEER-HF)
- Aldosterone reduction: more pronounced vs. valsartan alone in PARADIGM-HF substudies
- BNP rises (due to NEP inhibition; not a valid marker of clinical worsening on Entresto — NT-proBNP preferred for monitoring)

## 2. Key Clinical Trials

### 2.1 PARADIGM-HF (Primary Registration Trial)

**Full title:** Prospective Comparison of ARNI with ACEI to Determine Impact on Global Mortality and Morbidity in Heart Failure

**Publication:** McMurray JJ, et al. *N Engl J Med.* 2014;371(11):993–1004.

**Design:** Randomized, double-blind, active-controlled, event-driven superiority trial

**Population:**
- N = 8,442 patients
- HFrEF (LVEF ≤40%)
- NYHA Class II–IV
- Elevated NT-proBNP (≥600 pg/mL, or ≥400 pg/mL if hospitalized for HF in prior 12 months; ≥900 pg/mL if AF)
- Required prior ACE-I or ARB tolerance (run-in period)
- Excluded: prior ACE-I angioedema, eGFR <30 mL/min/1.73m², SBP <100 mmHg, K+ >5.2 mEq/L

**Comparator:** Enalapril 10 mg BID (active comparator, not placebo)

**Follow-up:** Median 27 months; trial stopped early for overwhelming benefit at prespecified interim analysis

**Primary Endpoint:** Composite of CV death or first HF hospitalization

| Endpoint | Entresto | Enalapril | HR (95% CI) | p-value |
|----------|----------|-----------|-------------|---------|
| Primary composite (CV death or HF hosp) | 21.8% | 26.5% | 0.80 (0.73–0.87) | <0.0001 |
| CV death | 13.3% | 16.5% | 0.80 (0.71–0.89) | <0.0001 |
| First HF hospitalization | 12.8% | 15.6% | 0.79 (0.71–0.89) | <0.0001 |
| All-cause mortality | 17.0% | 19.8% | 0.84 (0.76–0.92) | <0.0001 |
| Sudden cardiac death | 7.2% | 8.7% | 0.80 (0.68–0.94) | 0.008 |
| Death from worsening HF | 3.4% | 5.0% | 0.79 (0.64–0.98) | 0.034 |

**NNT for primary composite:** 21 (over 27 months)
**Absolute risk reduction:** 4.7% for primary composite

**Selected Secondary/Exploratory Endpoints:**
- Change in KCCQ clinical summary score at 8 months: +2.99 (Entresto) vs. +1.59 (enalapril); p=0.001 (quality of life benefit)
- Time to first HF hospitalization: significantly longer in Entresto arm
- Total HF hospitalizations (first + recurrent): 32% reduction vs. enalapril

**Key Safety Results:**

| Safety Event | Entresto (%) | Enalapril (%) |
|-------------|-------------|--------------|
| Symptomatic hypotension | 14.0 | 9.2 |
| Cough | 11.3 | 14.3 |
| Angioedema | 0.45 | 0.24 |
| Hyperkalemia (K+ >6.0) | 4.3 | 5.6 |
| Renal impairment | 3.3 | 4.1 |
| Discontinuation due to AE | 10.7 | 12.3 |

**Subgroup analyses (selected):**
- Women (HR 0.78, 95% CI 0.64–0.95): consistent benefit
- Age ≥75 years (HR 0.83): consistent benefit
- NYHA Class III/IV (HR 0.79): consistent benefit
- De novo HF patients (HR 0.71): numerically greater benefit
- LVEF 35–40% (HR 0.73): consistent benefit

### 2.2 PARAGON-HF

**Full title:** Prospective Comparison of ARNI with ARB Global Outcomes in HF with Preserved Ejection Fraction

**Publication:** Solomon SD, et al. *N Engl J Med.* 2019;381(17):1609–1620.

**Design:** Randomized, double-blind, active-controlled superiority trial

**Population:**
- N = 4,822 patients
- HFpEF (LVEF ≥45%)
- NYHA Class II–IV
- Elevated NT-proBNP
- Structural evidence of HF (LA enlargement or LVH)

**Comparator:** Valsartan 160 mg BID

**Primary Endpoint:** Composite of total HF hospitalizations + CV death (rate ratio)

| Endpoint | Entresto | Valsartan | RR/HR (95% CI) | p-value |
|----------|----------|-----------|----------------|---------|
| Primary composite (rate ratio) | — | — | 0.87 (0.75–1.01) | 0.059 (NS) |
| Total HF hospitalizations | — | — | 0.85 (0.72–1.00) | 0.048 |
| CV death | — | — | 0.95 (0.79–1.16) | NS |

**Key subgroup findings (post-hoc, informing 2021 label):**
- LVEF below median (<57%): HR 0.78 (95% CI 0.64–0.95) — statistically significant
- Women: HR 0.73 (95% CI 0.59–0.90) — statistically significant
- LVEF 45–57% in women: HR 0.60 (95% CI 0.43–0.83)
- These subgroup findings supported the 2021 FDA label expansion for patients with LVEF below normal with elevated NT-proBNP

**Regulatory significance:** FDA 2021 labeling change — indication expanded to "to reduce the risk of cardiovascular death and hospitalization for heart failure in adult patients with chronic HF. Benefits are most clearly evident in patients with LVEF below normal. In patients with LVEF ≥60%, effectiveness has not been established."

### 2.3 PIONEER-HF

**Full title:** Comparison of Sacubitril-Valsartan versus Enalapril on Effect on NT-proBNP in Patients Stabilized from an Acute Heart Failure Episode

**Publication:** Velazquez EJ, et al. *N Engl J Med.* 2019;380(6):539–548.

**Design:** Randomized, double-blind, active-controlled, multicenter trial

**Population:**
- N = 881 patients
- Hospitalized for acute decompensated HF
- HFrEF (LVEF ≤40%)
- Hemodynamically stabilized (SBP ≥100, no IV vasodilators/inotropes for ≥6h)
- Initiated in-hospital

**Comparator:** Enalapril 10 mg BID

**Primary Endpoint:** Time-averaged proportional change in NT-proBNP from baseline through weeks 4 and 8

| Endpoint | Entresto | Enalapril | Ratio (95% CI) | p-value |
|----------|----------|-----------|----------------|---------|
| NT-proBNP reduction (time-averaged) | −46.7% | −25.3% | 0.71 (0.63–0.81) | <0.001 |

**Secondary Endpoints:**

| Endpoint | Entresto (%) | Enalapril (%) | p-value |
|----------|-------------|--------------|---------|
| Rehospitalization for HF at 8 weeks | 8.0 | 13.8 | 0.011 |
| Death at 8 weeks | 3.1 | 4.7 | 0.17 |
| Serious adverse events | 21.1 | 27.2 | 0.043 |

**Clinical significance:** Established safety/feasibility of in-hospital ARNI initiation in stabilized acute HF patients; supported change in clinical practice guidelines.

### 2.4 TRANSITION Study

**Publication:** Wachter R, et al. *Lancet.* 2019;393(10187):2176–2188.

**Design:** Open-label, randomized, multicenter

**Population:** N=1,002; post-acute HF stabilization; prior ACE-I/ARB

**Key finding:** In-hospital vs. post-discharge Entresto initiation — both safe; in-hospital initiation non-inferior with trend toward better 10-week NT-proBNP reduction. Supported in-hospital initiation feasibility in European context.

### 2.5 EVALUATE-HF

**Publication:** Jhund PS, et al. *JAMA.* 2018;319(1):52–60.

**Design:** Randomized, double-blind, crossover

**Population:** N=464; HFrEF (LVEF ≤35%); stable; cross-over design vs. enalapril

**Key finding:** Aortic characteristic impedance (primary endpoint) reduced significantly with Entresto vs. enalapril (−3.46 dynes/cm5 vs. −0.89 dynes/cm5, p=0.07 — trended but not significant). Significant reductions in NTproBNP, LV end-systolic volume index. Provided mechanistic data on reverse remodeling.

### 2.6 TITRATION Study

**Publication:** Senni M, et al. *JACC Heart Fail.* 2016;4(10):765–772.

**Design:** Open-label, multicenter tolerability study

**Population:** N=498; stable HFrEF (LVEF ≤35%); prior ACE-I/ARB or naive

**Key finding:** Conservative titration schedule (starting at 24/26 mg BID, doubling every 2 weeks) equally tolerated as condensed schedule. 76% of patients tolerant to 97/103 mg BID target dose at 12 weeks.

## 4. Biomarker Data and Real-World Evidence

### 4.1 NT-proBNP vs. BNP Monitoring on Entresto

Critical clinical note: Entresto increases BNP through NEP inhibition (BNP is a neprilysin substrate). BNP-based monitoring will overestimate natriuretic peptide levels and cannot be used to gauge clinical response on Entresto. **NT-proBNP is the preferred biomarker** (not a neprilysin substrate).

### 4.2 Selected RWE Studies

**CHAMP-HF Registry (Chang et al., JAMA Cardiol 2019):**
- N = 3,518 HFrEF patients in ARNI arm
- Real-world ARNI use: only 13.3% of eligible patients receiving ARNI at index
- ARNI-treated patients had significantly lower 12-month mortality vs. ACE-I (HR 0.62, 95% CI 0.45–0.86)

**GWTG-HF Analysis (Fonarow et al., JAMA Cardiol 2020):**
- N = 40,000+ patients; hospital discharge data
- Entresto discharge use increased from 5.3% (2015) to 14.7% (2018)
- In-hospital initiation associated with 30-day readmission reduction of 16%

**ARNI-Real Evidence Study (Docherty et al., Eur J Heart Fail 2021):**
- 2,030 patients in the UK
- Real-world NT-proBNP reduction at 12 months: 41% (vs. 47% in PIONEER-HF)
- LVEF improvement +5.2 EF points at 12 months in responding patients
- QoL improvement (KCCQ total symptom score +8.3 points)
