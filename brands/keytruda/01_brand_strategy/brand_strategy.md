## 1. Brand Vision

**"To redefine the standard of care in oncology by harnessing the immune system's full potential — making durable, long-term survival a reality across cancer types."**

KEYTRUDA aspires to be the cornerstone of cancer treatment globally, not merely an add-on therapy, but the foundational agent around which combination regimens are built. ## 3. Strategic Imperatives (FY2026–2028)

### Imperative 1: Defend and Grow Core Tumor Types
- Reinforce first-line NSCLC leadership against Opdivo+Yervoy (CheckMate-227) competition
- Expand TNBC early-stage adoption following KEYNOTE-522 pCR and EFS data
- Solidify MSI-H/dMMR tumor-agnostic positioning as biomarker testing expands
- Target: Maintain ≥35% checkpoint inhibitor market share in 1L NSCLC

### Imperative 2: QLEX SC Formulation Launch Excellence
- Drive conversion from IV to SC in eligible patients: target 40% of new IV starts to SC by end of 2026
- Key messaging: ~30-minute administration vs. 30–60 min IV infusion; same clinical profile
- SC launch focus markets: community oncology, infusion center capacity-constrained accounts
- Healthcare system cost savings from reduced chair time and nursing resource utilization

### Imperative 3: Pipeline-Driven Lifecycle Extension Beyond 2028
- Pembrolizumab patent cliff 2028 mitigation: QLEX SC patent protection to ~2041
- ADC combinations (MK-2870, MK-1308A) in late-stage development
- Bispecific antibody combinations targeting LAG-3, TIGIT, TIM-3 co-inhibitory pathways
- Personalized cancer vaccine (mRNA-4157/V940) with pembrolizumab: Phase 3 (NSCLC, melanoma, bladder)

### Imperative 4: Biomarker Ecosystem Expansion
- Increase PD-L1 and MSI-H testing rates across all target tumor types to ≥85% (current est. 68%)
- Support liquid biopsy companion diagnostic development (Foundation Medicine, Guardant Health)
- Drive tumor-agnostic indication adoption through pathology society partnerships
- Establish next-generation biomarker (BRCA, HER2, KRAS G12C co-testing) workflows

### Imperative 5: Global Market Penetration & Access
- Accelerate approvals in Tier 2 markets: Brazil, India, China (pembrolizumab biosimilar threat horizon 2030)
- Establish price-value frameworks with HEOR data for EU HTA negotiations (AMNOG, HAS, NICE)
- KEYTRUDA China (Keyizumab) – navigate NMPA requirements and NRDL negotiations
- Target: ≥25 ex-US countries with reimbursement for ≥5 indications by end of 2027

## 4. SWOT Analysis

### Strengths
- **Market Leadership**: #1 oncology brand globally by revenue ($31.7B in 2025)
- **Unmatched Indication Breadth**: 40+ FDA indications; more than any I/O competitor
- **Clinical Evidence Base**: KEYNOTE program encompasses 1,600+ clinical trials, ~200,000 patients enrolled globally
- **Companion Diagnostics**: 22C3 pharmDx PD-L1 assay (Dako/Agilent) — gold standard in PD-L1 testing
- **KPS Delivery Platform**: QLEX SC formulation extends patent lifecycle to 2041
- **Physician Brand Loyalty**: Net Promoter Score among oncologists: 72 (industry benchmark: 48)
- **Combination Versatility**: Active in 350+ combination regimens across development pipeline

### Weaknesses
- **Core Molecule Patent Expiry**: pembrolizumab API patent expires 2028; biosimilar threat horizon imminent
- **irAE Profile Complexity**: Black box warning for immune-mediated reactions creates HCP hesitation in comorbid patients
- **IV Administration Burden**: 30–60 min infusion time limits community oncology penetration (partially addressed by QLEX SC)
- **Biomarker Dependency**: ~30% of NSCLC patients PD-L1 <1% — limited options in biomarker-low tumors without combination partners
- **PD-L1 Testing Variability**: Inter-laboratory concordance remains suboptimal (~85%); affects prescribing confidence
- **Reimbursement Gaps in EM**: Coverage rates <40% in several emerging market countries

### Opportunities
- **mRNA Vaccine Combination (mRNA-4157/V940)**: Phase 3 data in melanoma, NSCLC (2026 readout); potential paradigm shift
- **Subcutaneous Expansion**: QLEX SC approval drives access in under-resourced infusion settings
- **Tumor-Agnostic Positioning**: Increasing pan-tumor MSI-H/TMB-H screening rates = new addressable patient pool
- **Early-Stage Expansion**: Neo/adjuvant approvals in TNBC, NSCLC, RCC, bladder growing rapidly
- **ADC Combinations**: Co-development with Seagen/Pfizer ADCs (fam-trastuzumab deruxtecan, enfortumab vedotin) creates novel I/O+ADC combos
- **AI-Driven Patient Identification**: Partnerships with EHR systems for real-world biomarker-based patient ID

### Threats
- **Opdivo/Yervoy Dual Checkpoint**: BMS's combination regimen gaining in MSS/TMB-H tumors
- **LAG-3 Combinations (Opdualag)**: Relatlimab+nivolumab approval threatens melanoma share
- **TIGIT Inhibitors (Forthcoming)**: Tiragolumab, domvanalimab — potential to disrupt PD-1 monotherapy
- **pembrolizumab Biosimilar Entry (2028+)**: Up to 12 biosimilar applicants filed; price erosion 30–70% modeled
- **Skyrizi/Rinvoq I/O Pivot**: IL-23 and JAK inhibitors expanding into solid tumor indications
- **Cost-Effectiveness Scrutiny**: IRA drug pricing negotiations and EU HTA assessments threatening net price

## 7. Campaign Pillars (FY2026)

### Pillar A: "Beyond Survival — Long-Term Outcomes"
- Theme: 5-year OS data across KEYNOTE trials as proof of transformative durability
- Target audiences: Medical oncologists (tier 1–2), academic KOLs, tumor boards
- Channels: ASCO/ESMO congress symposia, peer-reviewed publications, speaker bureau programs
- Key assets: Interactive 5-year survival data visualization tools, reprint cards

### Pillar B: "Precision. Biomarker-Driven. Patient-First."
- Theme: PD-L1/MSI-H/TMB-H testing enables right drug, right patient, right time
- Target audiences: Pathologists, molecular oncologists, community oncologists
- Channels: CAP/USCAP pathology society partnerships, CME programs, EHR integration alerts
- Key assets: Testing algorithm decision aid, biomarker testing kit, co-pay support for testing

### Pillar C: "KEYTRUDA Everywhere — SC Innovation for a Broader Reach"
- Theme: QLEX SC formulation democratizes pembrolizumab access across care settings
- Target audiences: Community oncologists, infusion center directors, practice managers
- Channels: ACCC community oncology network, ASCO practice management, trade shows
- Key assets: Time-motion study data, chair time calculator, SC conversion guide

### Pillar D: "The Combination Standard"
- Theme: KEYTRUDA-based combinations as the new backbone of first-line therapy
- Target audiences: Oncologists treating NSCLC, GI, GU, gynecologic cancers
- Channels: Tumor-type specific publications, NCCN guideline updates, KOL panel events
- Key assets: Combination regimen comparison tables, dosing guides, toxicity management tools
