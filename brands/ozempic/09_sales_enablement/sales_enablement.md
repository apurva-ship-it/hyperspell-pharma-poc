## 1. Sales Call Framework

### 1.1 Recommended Call Structure (PREP Model)

**P — Profile (Pre-call, 2 min)**
Review the HCP's prescribing profile before every call:
- Current Ozempic TRx volume (trailing 13 weeks)
- Competitive switch risk (Mounjaro TRx trend)
- Open PA issues via PA Navigator dashboard
- Formulary status for the HCP's primary payers
- Last call notes: What was discussed, what was left open

**R — Rapport and Relevance (1–2 min)**
Open with something relevant to the HCP's practice — not a generic opener.
- "I know you've been seeing a lot of patients with T2D and CKD since the FLOW trial published — I wanted to share our FLOW data package for you and your team."
- "Your practice is in a high CVS/Caremark market — I have an access update that makes prior authorization a lot easier for your team."

**E — Engage on a Clinical Need (4–6 min)**
Anchor the conversation on a patient-type or clinical scenario the HCP finds challenging.
- Use the current sales aid (approved; MLR ref OZM-US-2025-SA-002)
- Present no more than 2 key messages per call
- Preferred call flow: CV benefit → Renal benefit → or Head-to-head efficacy (depending on segment)
- Ask exploratory questions: "When you're looking at a patient with T2D and heart disease who needs more A1c control — what's your current approach?"

**P — Progress and Close (1–2 min)**
- Secure a commitment: "Would you be open to starting your next T2D+CVD patient on Ozempic before we meet again?"
- Leave behind one approved piece of material only (PA guide OR clinical reprint OR patient brochure)
- Document any access barriers, competitive intel, or patient support opportunities in Veeva CRM immediately post-call

## 2. Call Guide by HCP Segment

### 2.1 Call Guide — Cardiologist

**Primary Business Objective:** Convert T2D+CVD co-management patients to Ozempic as preferred GLP-1 RA; get cardiology to initiate (not just endorse) Ozempic prescribing.

**Opening Probe:**
"In your practice, when you have a T2D patient with established coronary artery disease and their endocrinologist is managing their diabetes — what's your role in the GLP-1 RA decision?"

**Key Message:** SUSTAIN-6 CV outcomes data; 26% MACE reduction; CV death HR 0.73
**Secondary Message:** SELECT trial as context-setter for broader semaglutide franchise CV credibility

**Leave-Behind:** SUSTAIN-6 clinical reprint + Ozempic CV indication indication card

**Frequency Target:** Tier 1 cardiologist (>5 co-managed T2D+CVD patients/week): Monthly | Tier 2: Bi-monthly

---

### 2.2 Call Guide — Endocrinologist

**Primary Business Objective:** Maintain Ozempic as default GLP-1 RA; prevent tirzepatide switching for T2D+CV patients; activate FLOW data for CKD patients.

**Opening Probe:**
"How are you thinking about the FLOW data for your CKD patients? Have you started using semaglutide more broadly in that population?"

**Key Message 1 (Efficacy):** SUSTAIN-7 head-to-head superiority vs. dulaglutide; HbA1c -1.8%, weight -6.5 kg
**Key Message 2 (CV+Renal):** Ozempic is the only GLP-1 RA with both CV (SUSTAIN-6) and renal (FLOW) outcomes data
**Objection handler ready:** Tirzepatide superior weight loss — lead with CV and renal differentiation

**Frequency Target:** Tier 1 endocrinologist (>20 Ozempic Rx/quarter): Bi-weekly | Tier 2: Monthly

---

### 2.3 Call Guide — Primary Care Physician (PCP)

**Primary Business Objective:** Build confidence in initiating Ozempic (not just referring); reduce PA barrier hesitancy; establish OzempicCare program enrollment habit.

**Opening Probe:**
"When you have a T2D patient with elevated cardiovascular risk — what's your current approach to GLP-1 RA therapy? Do you start it yourself or refer to endocrinology?"

**Key Message 1 (Access):** "PA is manageable — here's your payer's criteria in one page. My PA Navigator can handle submissions for your office."
**Key Message 2 (CV):** ADA 2025 guidelines recommend GLP-1 RA for T2D+CVD — Ozempic is the guideline-concordant choice.
**Key Message 3 (Patient Support):** OzempicCare enrollment = nursing support for GI tolerance → fewer patient calls to your office

**Leave-Behind:** Access card + PA quick guide for their top payer + OzempicCare enrollment brochure

**Frequency Target:** Tier 1 PCP (>15 Ozempic Rx/quarter): Monthly | Tier 2: Bi-monthly | Tier 3: Quarterly

---

### 2.4 Call Guide — Nephrologist (Emerging Segment)

**Primary Business Objective:** Establish Ozempic as the GLP-1 RA of choice for nephrologists co-managing T2D+CKD; drive new prescribing from non-traditional Ozempic writers.

**Opening Probe:**
"Have you had a chance to look at the FLOW trial data? What's your take on adding semaglutide to SGLT2i therapy in CKD patients?"

**Key Message:** FLOW — 24% reduction in CKD composite endpoint (kidney failure + eGFR decline + CV death) on top of standard of care (97% on RAS inhibitors; 46% on SGLT2i at baseline)

**Important Note:** FLOW data is medical/scientific only until CKD label update. Reps present with a non-promotional medical summary; defer detailed Q&A to MSL. CKD is NOT currently an on-label indication.

**Action:** Connect nephrologist with Medical Science Liaison (MSL) for FLOW deep-dive if interest confirmed.

## 4. Territory Planning Framework

### 4.1 Territory Segmentation (Target Customer Grid)

**Tier 1 — Platinum Accounts** (Top 20% of accounts; 70% of volume)
- Criteria: ≥20 Ozempic TRx/quarter OR ≥5 high-CV-risk T2D patients/week
- Call frequency: Bi-weekly (primary care: monthly)
- Activities: Advisory board nominations, clinical rep + MSL tandem calls, speaker programs
- Goal: Defend share; prevent competitive switch; activate FLOW narrative

**Tier 2 — Gold Accounts** (Next 30% of accounts; 25% of volume)
- Criteria: 8–19 Ozempic TRx/quarter; moderate CV risk patient mix
- Call frequency: Monthly
- Activities: Standard rep call + access support; speaker program invitations
- Goal: Convert fence-sitters; increase Ozempic share of GLP-1 wallet

**Tier 3 — Silver Accounts** (Bottom 50% of accounts; 5% of volume)
- Criteria: <8 TRx/quarter; low engagement; inertia prescribers or injection averse
- Call frequency: Quarterly
- Activities: Digital + rep combined; CME; nurse educator session
- Goal: ADA guideline compliance education; initiate first prescribing behavior

### 4.2 New Business Development Targets

**Priority growth areas:**
1. **Nephrologists** with >50 T2D+CKD patients: Unworked accounts — FLOW data is the door-opener
2. **Hospital/Academic Discharge Lists:** Patients discharged post-MI or post-ACS represent immediate CV indication patients; engage hospitalist and cardiology inpatient teams
3. **Endocrine NPs/PAs:** Rapidly growing prescriber base; want evidence-based, easy-to-use therapy
4. **Obesity Medicine Clinics:** Wegovy/Ozempic both fit; coordinate with obesity team

## 6. Sales Coaching Guide (District Manager)

### 6.1 Coaching Framework — GROW Model

**G — Goal:** "What was your objective for today's call?"
**R — Reality:** "What actually happened? How did the HCP respond?"
**O — Options:** "What could you have said differently? What data point would have been more compelling?"
**W — Way Forward:** "What's your specific plan for the next call with this HCP?"

### 6.2 Common Coaching Opportunities

**Issue: Rep leads with features instead of clinical need**
- Coaching: Role-play probe questions — "What challenge are you solving for this HCP today?"

**Issue: Rep over-handles objections with data — loses conversational flow**
- Coaching: "One data point, then pause and ask a follow-up question. Don't lecture."

**Issue: Rep avoids cardiologists (feels out of comfort zone)**
- Coaching: Tandem call with MSL or specialty rep; debrief and practice CV story independently

**Issue: Rep misses OzempicCare enrollment conversation**
- Coaching: Build enrollment step into standard close; provide 30-second enrollment pitch card
